Thomas Aquinas (c. 1225–1274) was an Italian Dominican friar, philosopher, and theologian, known as the *Doctor Angelicus*. His *Summa Theologiae* is the foremost systematic work of medieval scholastic theology, integrating Aristotelian philosophy with Catholic doctrine across questions of God, creation, ethics, and law. He was declared a Doctor of the Church in 1568 and remains the preeminent authority in Thomistic philosophy.
