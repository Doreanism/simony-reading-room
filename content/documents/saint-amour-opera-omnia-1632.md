This volume collects the known works of William of Saint-Amour (c. 1200–1272), doctor of the Faculty of Theology of Paris and member of the Sorbonne. Published pseudonymously in Constance in 1632 under the imprint "Ad Insigne Bonae Fidei Apud Alithophilos," the edition was assembled by Joannes Alithophilus to defend the hierarchical structure of the Church against the mendicant orders. It opens with an extensive prefatory history of William's controversies and relevant papal documents, followed by William's preface to the Psalms, his major treatise *De Periculis Novissimorum Temporum* (On the Dangers of the Last Times), his *Collectiones Catholicae et Canonicae Scripturae ad instructionem et praeparationem Simplicium Christi Fidelium contra pericula imminentia Ecclesiae Generali per Hypocritas et Pseudo-Praedicatores*, a table of signs for detecting false preachers, and a sermon for the feast of Saints James and Philip.
