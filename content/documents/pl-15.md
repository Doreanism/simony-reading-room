Volume 15 of J.-P. Migne's *Patrologiae Cursus Completus, Series Latina*, published in Paris in 1845. This volume is the second part of the first tome of Ambrose of Milan's *Opera Omnia*, gathering his major exegetical works on Scripture. It contains the *Expositio in Psalmum CXVIII* (a sustained commentary on Psalm 118 in twenty-two sermons), the *Expositio Evangelii secundum Lucam* (Ambrose's homiletic commentary on the Gospel of Luke in ten books), the *Commentarius in Cantica Canticorum* (a posthumous compilation drawn from Ambrose's writings on the Song of Songs), the *De Excidio urbis Hierosolymitanae libri quinque* (a Latin paraphrase of Josephus on the destruction of Jerusalem traditionally attributed to Ambrose, also known as Pseudo-Hegesippus), and an *Anacephalaeosis* of the same. The text follows the Maurist Benedictine recension, collated against Vatican, Gallican, and Belgian manuscripts, and is printed in the standard Patrologia Latina two-column format with sequentially numbered columns running from 1193 to 2290.
