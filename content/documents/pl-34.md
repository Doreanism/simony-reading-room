Volume 34 of J.-P. Migne's *Patrologiae Cursus Completus, Series Latina*, published in Paris in 1861. This volume is the third tome of Augustine of Hippo's *Opera Omnia* and gathers his major exegetical writings on Scripture. It contains the *De Doctrina Christiana* (four books on Christian hermeneutics and rhetoric), *De Vera Religione*, *De Genesi contra Manichaeos*, the incomplete and complete versions of *De Genesi ad Litteram*, the *Locutionum libri* and *Quaestiones in Pentateuchum* (grammatical and interpretive notes on the Old Testament), *Annotationes in Job*, the *De Scriptura sacra Speculum* (a scriptural anthology), *De Consensu Evangelistarum* (a harmony of the Gospels), and *De Sermone Domini in Monte* (two books of commentary on the Sermon on the Mount). The text is printed in the standard Patrologia Latina two-column format with sequentially numbered columns running from 1 to 1308.
