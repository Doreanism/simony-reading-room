A digital edition of Dante Alighieri's *Commedia* prepared for the *Letteratura italiana Einaudi* series, based on the critical text edited by Giorgio Petrocchi (Milan: Mondadori, 1966–67). The poem, composed in Italian terza rima between roughly 1308 and 1321, recounts the narrator's allegorical journey through Hell (*Inferno*), Purgatory (*Purgatorio*), and Paradise (*Paradiso*), guided first by Virgil and then by Beatrice. Within the *Inferno*, the third bolgia of the eighth circle (Canto XIX) is devoted to simoniacs — popes and clergy who trafficked in sacred offices — whom Dante depicts buried upside down in stone sockets with flames burning the soles of their feet.
