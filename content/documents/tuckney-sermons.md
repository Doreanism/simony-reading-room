A posthumous collection of forty sermons preached by Anthony Tuckney, D.D., Master of Emmanuel and St John's Colleges and Regius Professor of Divinity at Cambridge. The sermons, expounding texts from Philippians, Second Peter, Proverbs, Isaiah, Luke, the Psalms, Genesis, Matthew, and Philippians again, were published at London in 1676 by his son Jonathan Tuckney, M.A., transcribed faithfully from the author's own manuscript notes. The collection represents Tuckney's characteristic combination of careful exegesis, Reformed doctrine, and practical application to the Christian life.
