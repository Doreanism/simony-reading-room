One of the most comprehensive works of early thirteenth-century moral theology, covering simony, usury, oaths, vows, tithes, and related cases of conscience. Robert of Courson (ca. 1160/70–1218), an English-born theologian trained under Peter the Chanter at Paris, composed the Summa c. 1208–1214. He later became a cardinal legate of Innocent III and a central figure in the preparatory councils for the Fourth Lateran Council (1215). BnF Latin 14524 is a thirteenth-century manuscript copy, 186 folios, in two-column Gothic script.
