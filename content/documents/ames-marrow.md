An English translation of William Ames's *Medulla Theologica* (1627), printed at London by Edward Griffin for Henry Overton in 1639. The work presents Reformed theology in two books: Book 1 on Faith (the knowledge of God and salvation) and Book 2 on Observance (the practice of obedience to God). Ames composed the original Latin as a compact systematic theology for students at Franeker, and it became one of the most widely used Reformed textbooks of the seventeenth century, influential in both English Puritanism and New England Congregationalism. This edition includes a table of hard words explained and synoptic diagram-tables summarizing each chapter.
