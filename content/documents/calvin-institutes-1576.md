Calvin's _Institutio Christianae Religionis_, first published in 1536 and substantially revised in 1559 into its definitive four-book Latin form, is the foundational systematic theology of the Reformed tradition. Organized according to the structure of the Apostles' Creed, the four books treat the knowledge of God as Creator, God as Redeemer in Christ, the manner of receiving the grace of Christ, and the external means by which God invites us into fellowship with Christ. This 1576 Lausanne edition was printed by Franciscus le Preux for the Bernese authorities and prepared by Nicolas Colladon, who added a topical index and marginal cross-references, together with two scripture concordances collected by Augustin Marlorat in 1562.
