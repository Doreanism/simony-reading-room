Peter Auriol's commentary on the fourth book of Peter Lombard's *Sentences*, covering the sacraments. Originally lectured at Paris c. 1316–1318 as part of Auriol's broader *Scriptum super Sententias*, the fourth-book commentary engages the standard sacramental questions of scholastic theology — baptism, confirmation, eucharist, penance, extreme unction, orders, and matrimony — with Auriol's characteristic critical rigor, particularly in dialogue with Scotus. This 1605 edition was printed at Rome by Aloisio Zannetti under the patronage of the Societas Bibliopolarum Urbis, dedicated to Francesco di Bel Gioioso, Commissary General of the Friars Minor Observant, and issued *superiorum permissu*.
