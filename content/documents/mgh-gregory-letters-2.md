The *Registrum Epistolarum* of Gregory I (Gregory the Great, r. 590–604) is the largest surviving corpus of late antique papal correspondence, comprising some 854 letters organized into fourteen books by indiction year. This volume (Tomus II, Libri VIII–XIV) contains letters from the eighth through fourteenth indiction years of Gregory's pontificate (597–604). Books VIII–IX were edited by Paulus Ewald; Books X–XIV and the critical apparatus (preface and indices) were completed after Ewald's death by Ludovicus M. Hartmann. The edition is part of the Monumenta Germaniae Historica (*Epistolae*, Tomus II) and remains the standard critical text.
