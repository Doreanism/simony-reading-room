Volume 30 of the Borgnet edition of Albert the Great's *Opera Omnia*, edited by Augustus Borgnet (a priest of the diocese of Reims) with the approval of Pope Leo XIII, and published in Paris by Ludovicus Vivès in 1894. This volume contains the second half of Albert's Commentary on Book IV of Peter Lombard's *Sentences*, covering Distinctions XXIII–L — treating the sacraments of extreme unction, orders, and matrimony, as well as the doctrine of the resurrection, the Last Judgment, and the final states of the blessed and the damned. The edition is drawn "ex editione lugdunensi religiose castigata" — carefully corrected from the older Lyon edition of Pierre Jammy (1651) — and supplemented from the editions of Peter of Prussia, Rodolph of Nymwegen, and others.
