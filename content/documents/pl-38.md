Volume 38 of J.-P. Migne's *Patrologiae Cursus Completus, Series Latina*, published in Paris in 1845. This volume is the first part of the fifth tome of Augustine of Hippo's *Opera Omnia*, following the Benedictine Maurist recension collated against Louvain, Vatican, Gallican, and Belgian manuscripts. It gathers three of the four classes of Augustine's *Sermones ad Populum* as organized in the Benedictine edition: *Classis Prima* (*Sermones de Scripturis*, cols. 23–994), *Classis Secunda* (*Sermones de Tempore*, cols. 995–1246), and *Classis Tertia* (*Sermones de Sanctis*, cols. 1247ff.), together with accompanying apparatus of variant readings, annotations, and indices. These sermons were preached by Augustine over the course of his episcopate at Hippo (396–430) and range from sustained exegetical homilies on Scripture to liturgical sermons tied to the liturgical calendar. The text is printed in the standard Patrologia Latina two-column format with sequentially numbered columns.
