The *Libelli de Lite Imperatorvm et Pontificvm* (Tomus I) is the first volume of the Monumenta Germaniae Historica's critical edition of polemical tracts written during the investiture controversy of the eleventh and twelfth centuries. Edited by E. Dümmler, F. Thaner, L. de Heinemann, and K. Francke, and first published in Hannover in 1891, it collects nine works directed at the conflict between imperial and papal authority: Wido of Ferrara's letter on simony, the anonymous *De ordinando pontifice*, Peter Damian's *Liber gratissimus* and *Disceptatio synodalis*, Cardinal Humbert of Silva Candida's *Libri III adversus simoniacos* (pp. 95–253), the Pseudo-Udalric letter on clerical celibacy, Gebhard of Salzburg's letter to Bishop Hermann of Metz, the letter of Wenric of Trier, and Manegold of Lautenbach's *Ad Gebehardum liber*. The volume also includes an index of sources and authorities cited throughout.
