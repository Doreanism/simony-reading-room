Book II, Chapter 16 of Ames's *Marrow of Sacred Divinity* treats justice and charity as the two principal virtues governing human relations. Ames defines justice as conformity to the second table of the Decalogue, distinguishing its distributive, commutative, and corrective forms, and differentiating justice strictly taken from equity and charity. He discusses the nature of obligation in contracts, the basis of restitution, and concludes by examining how ill will and unjust discord can culminate in schism when directed against fellow believers.
