Gerhoch of Reichersberg's *Libellus de eo, quod princeps mundi huius iam iudicatus sit*, also known as the *Liber de Simoniacis*, is a polemical treatise addressed to Bernard of Clairvaux in which Gerhoch argues that the simoniacal clergy of his day embody the "prince of this world" whose judgment Christ announced in the Gospel of John. Drawing on scripture, canon law, and patristic authority, he contends that bishops and priests who obtained their offices through simony are spiritually dead and that the sacraments they perform are invalid or gravely suspect. The work opens with a dedicatory prologue to Bernard and proceeds through a sustained argument that the reform of the church requires the rooting out of simony at every level of the ecclesiastical hierarchy.
