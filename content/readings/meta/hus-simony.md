Hus's complete treatise on simony, written in Czech in 1413 and completed on Candlemas (2 February). In ten chapters he attacks the buying and selling of spiritual goods — from sacraments and benefices to monastic letters and episcopal fees — as a heresy against the Holy Spirit. Drawing on Wycliffe and scripture, he condemns simony at every level of the Church, from parish priests to the papacy, and calls on secular rulers and the lay faithful alike to resist it.
