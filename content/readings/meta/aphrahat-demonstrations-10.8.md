A passage from Aphrahat's tenth Demonstration (*De Pastoribus*, "On Pastors") meditating on the inexhaustibility of the Lord's treasury of wisdom, freely shared with prophets, apostles, and pastors. Aphrahat invokes Matthew 10:8 — "Freely you have received, freely give" — to argue that those who received gratuitously must likewise give gratuitously; if they hoard or sell what was given, it will be taken from them. A key patristic locus for the theological argument against simony.
