Albert the Great's commentary on Distinction 25 of Book IV of Peter Lombard's *Sentences*, which treats the dispensation of the sacrament of orders. Opening with the question whether heretics cut off from the Church can confer valid orders (and whether those they ordain must be reordained on return to Catholic unity), Albert surveys the conflicting patristic authorities and sides with the "moderns": so long as the minister is validly ordained and preserves the form and intention of the Church, the sacrament is effected, whether the minister is heretical or schismatic. He then turns to simony — defining it as the willing exchange of temporal price for spiritual gift in imitation of Simon Magus — and works through a series of articles on whether simoniacal ordinations are valid, whether an inferior can commit simony with a superior (and notably with the pope), the fourfold classification of "spiritual" things that can be made the object of simony, the three modes of simoniacal exchange (gift of hand, tongue, and service), the status of those knowingly and unknowingly ordained by simoniacs, and the canonical ages required for ordination.
