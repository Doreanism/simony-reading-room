Paragraph 49 of Book IV, Chapter 17 of Calvin's Institutes marshals patristic evidence that both elements of the Eucharist were always administered to all communicants in the ancient church. Calvin cites Gregory I, Gelasius, Cyprian, and others to show that withholding the cup from the laity was considered sacrilege. He argues that the Roman practice of communion under one kind violates both the explicit command of Christ and the universal custom of the church for over a millennium.
