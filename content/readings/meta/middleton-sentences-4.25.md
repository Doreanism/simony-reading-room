Richard of Middleton's commentary on Distinction 25 of Book IV of Peter Lombard's *Sentences*, which addresses ordinations performed by heretics and simoniacs. The commentary proceeds through articles and questions: Article I asks whether a non-episcopal priest can ordain; Articles II–III treat simony directly — defining it, asking whether simoniacal ordinations are valid, and examining the different modes (gift of hand, tongue, and service); subsequent articles address canonical impediments to ordination including irregular clerics, heretical bishops, and age requirements.
