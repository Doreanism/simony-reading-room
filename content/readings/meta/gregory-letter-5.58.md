In this letter of August 12, 595, Gregory grants Virgilius, bishop of Arles, the apostolic vicariate over the Gallic churches under the Frankish king Childebert II, following ancient custom and at the king's request. Gregory transmits the pallium and instructs that bishops may not travel far without Virgilius's authority; disputes among bishops are to be settled in a synod of twelve, with unresolved cases referred to Rome. The letter then turns to the twin abuses Gregory has heard are common in Gaul and Germany: simony (condemning the sale of ordinations as the heresy of Simon Magus) and the sudden tonsuring of laymen for vacant sees, without due passage through clerical orders.
