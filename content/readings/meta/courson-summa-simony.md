Robert of Courson's simony treatise from his Summa (c. 1208–1214). Structured as a series of questiones, the treatise begins from the question of whether general absolutions and remissions granted by pope and bishops truly benefit the recipients, then proceeds through a sweeping treatment of simoniacal heresy: its definition and varieties; the validity of sacraments conferred by simoniac priests; remedies for those who have unwittingly received orders or sacraments from them; the proper election and installation of clergy; simony in patronage rights, oblations, and tithes; and canonical procedures for purging and penalizing simoniacs, including appeals to Rome. The treatise draws throughout on Gratian's Decretum, the papal Decretals, Scripture, and the Parisian theological tradition. BnF Latin 14524, ff. 18v–118r.
