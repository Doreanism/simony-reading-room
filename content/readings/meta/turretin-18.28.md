Quaestio XXVIII of Turretin's treatment of the Church asks whether ministers of the church are owed some stipend, and answers affirmatively against the Anabaptists. Turretin marshals scriptural arguments from both Testaments to show that those who labor in the Gospel have a right to be supported by it, then surveys the legitimate sources of ministerial income—the labor of preaching, congregational provision, and endowments—while warning against the excesses of greed and luxury. He also traces the historical origin of ecclesiastical benefices and properties, acknowledging how an originally legitimate institution was corrupted by papal patronage and monastic accumulation.
