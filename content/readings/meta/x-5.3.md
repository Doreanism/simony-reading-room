The forty-six canons of Decretales Gregorii IX, Book V, Title III, compiled by Raymond of Penyafort and promulgated by Pope Gregory IX in 1234. This title gathers papal decretals from Gregory I through Gregory IX on simony — the buying and selling of spiritual goods, ordinations, and ecclesiastical offices — and on the broader prohibition against demanding payment for the sacraments, burial, monastic profession, or other sacred acts. The canons address the definition of simony, the penalties for simoniacal ordination and election, and the obligations of bishops, abbots, and other clergy to confer spiritual benefits freely.
