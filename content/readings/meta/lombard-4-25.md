Lombard's Distinctio XXV of Book IV of the Sentences addresses ordinations performed by heretics and simoniacs. Chapter I asks whether orders conferred by heretics are valid. Chapter II provides the foundational definition of simony — buying or selling sacred things, named after Simon Magus — and Chapters III–V distinguish who counts as a simoniac, whether buying material rather than spiritual goods is excused, and the tripartite classification of simoniacs derived from Nicholas I. Chapter VI addresses those forcibly ordained by simoniacs; Chapter VII closes with the canonical age requirement for ordination.
