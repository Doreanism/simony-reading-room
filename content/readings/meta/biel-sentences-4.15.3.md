Gabriel Biel's extended treatment of the various kinds of theft and the obligations of restitution, introduced as the third question of Distinction XV. The question proceeds through three articles: (1) whether all thieves and robbers are required to restore specifically to each defrauded owner; (2) noting special classes of theft—peculatus (embezzlement of public property), abigeatus (theft of dispersed livestock), plagiatus (sale of a free person), and rapina (violent seizure)—and defining their conditions; and (3) resolving a series of seven dubia concerning cases where restitution obligations may be mitigated or excused, including extreme necessity, the rule that found property whose owner is unknown may go to the poor, the status of goods seized in shipwreck, and the excommunication decreed at the Fourth Lateran Council against those who plunder Christian castaways.
