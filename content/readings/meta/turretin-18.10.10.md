Section X of Quaestio X in Turretin's treatment of the Church provides the positive response to the Roman Catholic challenge demanding to know where the Reformed church was before Luther and Zwingli. Turretin argues that the question must be understood under four aspects—doctrine, persons, location, and form of governance—and that under the first and most important aspect (doctrine), the substance of saving faith has been preserved in every age through Scripture, the Apostles' Creed, baptism, and the Lord's Supper, even when the church lay hidden and was mixed with the corruptions of Rome, as God has always providentially conserved his elect until the Reformation.
