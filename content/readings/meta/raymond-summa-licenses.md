Raymond's treatment of teaching licenses in Book 1, Title 3 of the Summa. Drawing on papal decretals of Alexander III (Prohibeas, Quanta), it prohibits masters and chancellors from demanding any payment for the licentia docendi, treating such licenses as annexed to spiritual goods and therefore subject to the law against simony. Discusses what compensation teachers may lawfully receive (voluntary offerings, expense recovery), addresses the chanceries of Paris and Bologna, and considers the bishop who withholds a cleric's license to study unless paid.
