Thomas of Strasbourg's commentary on Distinction 25 of Book IV of Peter Lombard's *Sentences*, which treats the conferral and reception of holy orders. The distinction opens with an overview of the Lombard's structure and a resolution stating that while only a bishop can confer orders by common law, a heretical or censured bishop still validly ordains when the Catholic form and intention are observed. Article I examines the power of the ordainer, establishing that episcopal authority alone suffices to confer the sacrament of order. Subsequent articles address simoniacal ordination: whether orders conferred or received simoniacally are valid, the three modes of simoniacal exchange (gift of hand, tongue, and service), and the conditions under which a transaction involving spiritual or annexed goods constitutes simony.
