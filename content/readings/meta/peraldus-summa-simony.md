Peraldus's treatment of simony within his influential treatise on the vices. Covers the detestation of simony as a heresy, twelve insults simony does to God, the multiple harms it causes, simoniacs as worse than Judas, the difficulty of their salvation, and the divisions of simony (fourfold: by money, flattery, carnal relations, and service).
