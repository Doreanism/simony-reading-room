Book V, Chapter 42 of Ames's *Conscience with the Power and Cases Thereof* treats the law of contracts. Ames defines the conditions required for a lawful contract — fit persons, free and promissive consent, and outward expression — and examines the matter, form, and end of contracts. The chapter addresses simony as the paradigmatic case of an unlawful contract, covering its extension to sacraments and ecclesiastical benefices, the three modes of simoniacal gift (hand, tongue, and observance), and the analogous corruption involved in the sale of public offices of jurisdiction.
