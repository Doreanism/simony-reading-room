Causa 1 of the *Decretum* is Gratian's comprehensive treatment of simony, structured around a hypothetical case of a man ordained through payment. Seven questions explore whether simoniacally ordained clergy can function validly (Q. I), whether they must be deposed (Q. II), whether they must surrender their benefices (Q. III), whether the sin is mortal (Q. IV), whether ordaining bishops are excommunicated (Q. V), whether simony can be committed through prayer or service rather than money (Q. VI), and whether lay investiture constitutes simony (Q. VII). The causa draws on patristic authority, papal decretals, and Roman law, and represents the most systematic canonical treatment of simony produced in the medieval West.
