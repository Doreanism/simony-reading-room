A single section from Book IX of Ambrose's *Expositio Evangelii secundum Lucam*, on Christ's overturning of the seats of those who sold doves in the Temple (Luke 19:45–46; Matt. 21:12). Reading the gesture spiritually rather than literally — for "what dignity is there among doves?" — Ambrose connects the dove with the Holy Spirit descending at Christ's baptism, and so identifies the dove-sellers as those who "would peddle the grace of the Holy Spirit" (*qui sancti Spiritus gratiam nundinentur*). He invokes Christ's command "Freely you have received, freely give" (Matt. 10:8) and Peter's rebuke of Simon Magus — "Thy money perish with thee, because thou hast thought that the gift of God may be purchased with money: thou hast no part nor lot in this faith" (Acts 8:20–21) — to bar simoniacs from the company of the Church.
