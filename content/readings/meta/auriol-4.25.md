Peter Auriol's commentary on Distinction 25 of Book IV of Peter Lombard's *Sentences*, which treats those receiving and conferring the sacrament of orders. Auriol poses three questions: first, whether bishops alone can minister the sacrament of orders; second, whether simony is a vice directly opposed to justice — working through the tripartite classification of spiritual things (sanctifying grace, freely given grace, and temporal emoluments annexed to spiritual offices), the three modes of simoniacal exchange (by word, by service, and by money), and concluding that exchanging sacraments for temporal goods is against justice as well as against religion, since it renders saleable what nature ordains to be given freely; third, whether the Pope can commit simony in any case.
