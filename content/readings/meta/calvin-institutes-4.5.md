Book IV, Chapter 5 of Calvin's Institutes demonstrates that the papal system of church governance has nothing in common with the ancient church. Calvin surveys how the early church elected its bishops by clergy and people together, how no bishop claimed primacy over others, how councils were conducted without papal domination, and how clerical offices were held without the pluralism, non-residence, and luxury of the contemporary papacy. He concludes by contrasting the ancient diaconal care for the poor with the current diversion of church revenues to ornamentation and episcopal display.
