Wido of Pomposa's letter to Archbishop Heribert of Milan is one of the earliest systematic polemical treatments of simony from the reform period. Written in the early eleventh century, the letter argues that simony is not merely an irregular transaction but a fundamental violation of the nature of holy orders: spiritual gifts cannot be bought or sold, and those who receive ordination through payment receive nothing valid, since the grace of ordination is not transmissible by money. Drawing on Gregory the Great and the Acts of the Apostles account of Simon Magus, Wido situates simony as a heresy that corrupts the entire sacramental order of the church and demands active resistance from the faithful. The letter was preserved and edited by Friedrich Thaner for the MGH *Libelli de Lite*, vol. I (1891).
