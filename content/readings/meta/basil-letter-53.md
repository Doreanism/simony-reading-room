Letter 53 of Basil of Caesarea, addressed to the chorepiscopoi (country bishops under his jurisdiction), addresses two concerns. The first section defends the full equality of the Holy Spirit with the Father and Son against any attempt to subordinate, elevate, or otherwise displace the Spirit from its proper rank within the Trinity. The second — and for this project, central — section denounces the taking of money from candidates for ordination, even when disguised under the name of piety. Basil identifies the practice as worse than ordinary simony because it corrupts the good as an instrument of sin, condemns it with Peter's curse upon Simon Magus, and declares covetousness to be the root of all evils and equivalent to idolatry. He threatens any minister who continues the practice with expulsion from the altars of the diocese.
