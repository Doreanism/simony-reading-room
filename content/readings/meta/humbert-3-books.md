Cardinal Humbert of Silva Candida's *Libri tres adversus simoniacos* (ca. 1058) is the most radical and systematic of the eleventh-century treatises against simony. In three books, Humbert argues that simony is not merely a canonical irregularity but a formal heresy: simoniacal ordinations are utterly invalid, and those ordained by simoniacs must be reordained rather than simply reconciled. The first book surveys the canonical and patristic evidence against simony; the second argues that simoniacal bishops are not true bishops at all but heretics masquerading in ecclesiastical office; the third extends this logic to lay investiture, arguing that the entire system of royal appointment to episcopal and abbatial offices is itself simoniacal. This last argument made the *Libri tres* a foundational text of the Gregorian Reform's most aggressive wing and contributed to the theoretical framework of the Investiture Controversy. Edited by Friedrich Thaner for the MGH *Libelli de Lite*, vol. I (1891).
