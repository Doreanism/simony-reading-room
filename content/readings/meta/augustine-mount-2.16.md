Chapter XVI of Book II of Augustine's *De Sermone Domini in Monte*, comprising sections 53–55. Augustine comments on Matthew 6:25–34 ("nolite esse solliciti animae vestrae"), arguing that Christ's prohibition on anxiety about food and clothing rests on the argument from the greater to the lesser: since God has already given the soul — the greater good — he will surely provide for the body's lesser needs as well. The chapter turns on the distinction between seeking temporal necessities as an ultimate end and seeking them as means subordinated to the kingdom of God, closing with the exhortation to seek first the kingdom and its justice, trusting that all else will be added.
