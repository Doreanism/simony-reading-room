Book IV, Chapter 25 of Ames's *Conscience with the Power and Cases Thereof* addresses the call to the ministry. Ames argues that a singular calling is required for the ministerial office, distinguishing it from the general duty of all Christians to teach. He examines what integrity of life, knowledge, and divine assurance are required of candidates, how a vocation is confirmed by the judgment of the Church, and on what grounds a minister may lawfully leave one congregation for another.
