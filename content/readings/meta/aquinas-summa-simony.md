Aquinas's treatment of simony in the Summa Theologiae (II-II, Q. 100), comprising six articles: whether simony is a sin; whether it is always a mortal sin; whether simony can be committed by thought, word, or deed; whether things annexed to spiritual goods may be sold; the penalties incurred by simoniacs; and special cases and dispensations.
