A complete transcription and translation of Henry Burton's 1624 treatise against simony — the buying and selling of ecclesiastical offices and spiritual goods. Comprising fourteen chapters, Burton systematically defines simony from canon law and Scripture, examines its various forms (in presentation, institution, and ordination), refutes objections, and concludes with a protestation addressed to Prince Charles, calling for legal reform. The work draws on Gratian, scholastic theologians, and civil law to argue that contemporary English practices of purchasing church livings, tithes, and patronage rights stand condemned by the universal church.
