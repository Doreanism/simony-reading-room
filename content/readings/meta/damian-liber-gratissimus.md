Peter Damian's *Liber Gratissimus* (1052) is a sustained canonical and theological argument on one of the most contested questions of the Gregorian Reform: whether clerics ordained by simoniacal bishops received valid orders. Written in response to the reforming synods of Clement II and addressed to the archbishop of Ravenna, the work argues that ordinations performed by simoniacs — though gravely sinful — are nonetheless valid and need not be repeated, since sacramental efficacy derives from God and not from the moral state of the minister. This position set Damian in direct conflict with Cardinal Humbert of Silva Candida, who argued for the invalidity and heretical character of simoniacal ordination. Damian's more moderate position eventually prevailed in practice, and was confirmed by the synodal decisions of Pope Nicholas II. Edited by L. de Heinemann for the MGH *Libelli de Lite*, vol. I (1891).
