Wycliffe's complete treatise on simony, written c. 1379–1380. In eight chapters he attacks the buying and selling of spiritual goods as a heresy rooted in clerical endowment, treating papal provisions, annates, benefice trafficking, and the corrupt practices of religious orders. He argues that simony offends the entire Trinity and that secular lords have a duty to suppress it.
