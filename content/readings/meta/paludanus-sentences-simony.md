Peter of Palude's full treatment of Distinction 25 in his commentary on the Fourth Book of the Sentences, covering all five questions. Question I asks whether all bishops — including heretics and those cut off from the Church — can confer orders, treating the sacramental power of order as distinct from jurisdictional standing. Question II concerns the proper times for ordinations, with conclusions on canonical ages, ordination days, and the obligation of benefice-holders to receive orders. Question III addresses impediments to holy orders in general — including sex (women cannot be ordained), age, and bodily defect. Question IV asks whether the Pope can commit simony, arguing from the will-based nature of the sin and distinguishing penal from moral dimensions. Question V examines the essence of simony and its three species — munus a manu, munus a lingua, and munus ab obsequio — and discusses when transactions involving sacred things, relics, burial, patronage rights, and benefices become simoniacal.
