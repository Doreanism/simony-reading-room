Peter of Palude's treatment of simony within his commentary on Lombard's Distinction 25 (on holy orders). Question IV asks whether the Pope can commit simony, arguing from the plenitude of papal power in spiritual matters and the will-based nature of the sin that even the Pope can incur it. Question V examines the essence of simony and its species — munus a manu, munus a lingua, and munus ab obsequio — and discusses when transactions involving sacred things, relics, burial, patronage rights, and benefices become simoniacal.
