Gabriel Biel's discussion of the matter of the sacrament of penance, engaging the view attributed to Jean Gerson in his *Flores morales* that the penitent recipient is himself the matter of the sacrament. Against this, Biel presents the standard tripartite scheme: the remote matter is sins committed, the proximate matter is confession, and the form is sacramental absolution. He works through the question of whether the sacrament requires any act on the part of the recipient, compares the structure of penance to that of baptism and the Eucharist, and examines the conditions under which the sacrament produces grace—concluding that the sacrament confers grace on one who receives it without the obstacle of mortal sin, whether actual in deed or persistent in the will.
