Chapters 25–40 of Peter the Chanter's *Verbum Abbreviatum*, the section of the work devoted to simony. Chapter 25 opens against those who exercise ecclesiastical offices or spiritual functions for temporal reward. Subsequent chapters work through the distinctions, circumstances, and borderline cases of simoniacal exchange: receiving benefices in hope of temporal gain, the role of episcopal intent in benefice resignations, gratis ordination, and the limits of what counts as simoniacal. Together these chapters constitute the most concentrated treatment of simony in the Verbum Abbreviatum, drawing on Scripture, canon law, and Patristic authority.
