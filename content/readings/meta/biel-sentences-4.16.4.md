Gabriel Biel's fourth question under Distinction XVI, treating almsgiving as a form of penance-satisfaction. Invoking James 5 and the principle that the whole Church is one commonwealth—so that whoever spends on those in need receives in turn what is necessary for Christian life—Biel argues by reason, example, and authority that almsgiving is obligatory on those with surplus, citing the mendicant exemplars of Alexius, Benedict, Dominic, and Francis. Five dubia follow: whether healthy beggars are obligated to work; whether almsgiving (as satisfaction) is superior to fasting and prayer; whether spiritual alms outrank bodily alms; whether venial sin can be remitted while mortal sin persists; and whether venial sin can become mortal. The question's explicit closing—"Et tantum de illa questione ac tota distinctione"—ends Distinction XVI.
