Raymond's treatment of simony in Book 1, Title 1 of the Summa. Defines simony as the deliberate will to buy or sell a spiritual thing (§. I), covers the various modes of committing it — by gift, service, or flattery — and distinguishes licit from illicit transactions involving ecclesiastical offices, ordinations, benefices, and patronage rights. Addresses penalties for simoniacs and conditions for dispensation, with extensive citation of the Decretum and Decretals.
