Calvin's prefatory letter to Francis I, King of France (originally Basel, 1 August 1536), defending the Reformed faith against charges of sedition and heresy. Written when the persecution of French Protestants was intensifying, Calvin argues that the reformers are falsely accused of promoting disorder and licentiousness, that the true disorder comes from those who suppress the Gospel by fire and sword, and that the king should grant the Reformed cause a fair hearing. He answers the main slanders against evangelical doctrine—on original sin, justification, the sacraments, and Christian liberty—and closes with a prayer for the king and an appeal to divine judgment.
