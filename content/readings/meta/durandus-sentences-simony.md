Durandus's commentary on Lombard's Distinction 25, which addresses simony in the conferral of holy orders. The "In generali" section defines simoniacs and distinguishes those who knowingly receive orders from a simoniac bishop (whose ordination is wholly invalid) from those who do so unknowingly (whose ordination is mercifully tolerated). Question I then asks specifically whether a bishop sins mortally by ordaining for payment and whether such ordinations are valid, arguing from natural law, canon law, and patristic authority.
