Durandus's commentary on Lombard's Distinction 25, which addresses simony in the conferral of holy orders. The "In generali" section defines simoniacs and distinguishes those who knowingly receive orders from a simoniac bishop (whose ordination is wholly invalid) from those who do so unknowingly (whose ordination is mercifully tolerated). Question I asks whether every bishop can confer orders, distinguishing between episcopal power, its exercise of right, and its exercise of fact. Question II asks whether a woman or child can be ordained, arguing from defects that can occur in the conferral of orders. Question III asks whether it is licit to buy or sell spiritual things for a temporal price. Question IV asks whether simony is committed in three customary modes: by gift of hand, by gift of tongue, and by gift of service. Question V asks whether the pope can commit simony.
