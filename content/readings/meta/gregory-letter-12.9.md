In this letter of March 602, Gregory writes to Victor, primate of Numidia, about grave complaints lodged by the clergy of Paulinus, bishop of Tegesis, concerning both his personal conduct and the charge that he has been conferring ecclesiastical orders for payment. Gregory instructs Victor to conduct a careful investigation together with his fellow bishop Columbo and other priests, correcting Paulinus according to canon law should the complaints be verified and ensuring that his own primatial authority is not slighted. Gregory is particularly urgent about the simony charge, warning that if confirmed it must be punished and the illicit ordinations voided of all benefit to those who sought them; the letter concludes by directing Victor to summon Gregory's chartulary Hilary if needed to help resolve the matter.
