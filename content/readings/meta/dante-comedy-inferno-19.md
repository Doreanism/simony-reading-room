Canto XIX of Dante's *Inferno* is devoted to the simoniacs — popes and prelates who trafficked in sacred offices — whom Dante places in the third *bolgia* of the eighth circle. They are buried head-down in stone sockets with flames burning the soles of their feet. The canto opens with an invective against Simon Magus and his followers, culminates in Dante's dialogue with Pope Nicholas III (who mistakes him for his successor Boniface VIII), and closes with a prophetic denunciation of the Donation of Constantine as the root of the Church's corruption.
