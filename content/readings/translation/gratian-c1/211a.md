Gratianus. Item, it is read that the treasures of Jericho were anathematized on account of the sin of the citizens. Hence Achor, who stole a golden bar and certain other precious things, is said to have taken from the anathema. §. 1. Item, on account of the sin of the Amalekites their animals were commanded to be slain; and because Saul spared them, he heard from Samuel: “Do you not know that rebellion is as the sin of divination, and stubbornness is as iniquity and idolatry?” §. 2. Item, on account of the sin of the Egyptians their possessions were delivered to the hail, and their cattle and their firstborn were consumed by death. §. 3. Item, on account of the sin of the Israelites the ark of the Lord was delivered to the Philistines. If therefore things that lack sense — such as the church, the altar, the ark of the Lord, the stones of Jericho — are either cursed, or delivered to sinners, or anathematized, or devastated by hail; if things that have no understanding — such as the animals of the Egyptians and the Amalekites — are destroyed by death on account of the sins of men: it is plain that ignorance of a crime excuses no one, especially when a benefit accrues to the ignorant person from it; just as if anyone should gather riches for his unknowing son from theft and rapine, ignorance would not excuse the son, but after his father’s death he would be compelled to return what had been wrongly acquired. §. 4. Item, on account of the sin of Achor the people of Israel were delivered into the hands of their enemies. §. 5. Item, on account of the sin of the sons of Heli the people fell by the hand of the Philistines. §. 6. On account of the sins of the people, their chosen leaders were often impeded, and at last the prophets were led into captivity together with them.

III. Pars. §. 7. To these arguments it is replied as follows. Insensible and irrational things are either cursed or anathematized or destroyed by death on account of the sins of men — not by participation in the sin, but by reason of sacrament, or in detestation of the sin, or as a scourge upon the offenders.

By reason of sacrament, as with the treasures of Jericho and the animals of the Amalekites. For Jericho is interpreted as “moon” and signifies this world. The Amalekites are interpreted as “those who lick blood” and signify the covetous, the avaricious, and the demons. The Israelites are interpreted as “men who see God” and signify the multitude of the saints. The stones and treasures of Jericho are therefore anathematized, and the animals of the Amalekites are ordered to be slain, in order to test the covetousness of the Israelite people, and to signify that the walls of vices and the treasures of this world — which are designated by the stones and treasures of Jericho — and also the irrational impulses of vice — which are understood by the animals of the Amalekites — ought not to pass into the portion of the saints.

The animals of the Egyptians, and their possessions, and their firstborn were consumed by hail and death, so that they might be punished in those things by whose prosperity they rejoiced, and so that it might be given to understand that the world will perish along with its substance.

Moreover, the land of the Israelites was turned to saltiness, adverse rains fell upon their crops, and their animals were consumed by sterility and disease, so that they might at least feel their Maker amid punishments, whom they had refused to acknowledge through benefits.

In detestation of sin, the money of Symon was condemned, to whom it was said: “Your money perish with you, because you thought the gift of God could be possessed for money.” In detestation of the same crime, an altar and a church that are consecrated through simony are said not so much to be consecrated as to be cursed, because one who pours out the oil of consecration through the mediation of money must be believed not to consecrate but to curse.

Hence it is that the blessed Gregorius commanded churches consecrated by Arians to be consecrated again, because those who lacked the faith could not bestow the sanctification of consecration, which is accomplished only in the faith of the Trinity. But the ark and the prophets were delivered to enemies on account of the sins of their fellow citizens. And the chosen leaders who were impeded by their murmuring suffered this not as a punishment for crime, but as a trial and example of patience. §. 8. Item, the people who were delivered into the hands of enemies on account of the sin of Achor and the sons of Heli signifies that subjects are corporally scourged, so that amid those very scourges the sins of their superiors may be struck down — just as Achor, discovered by lot, was crushed by stones. §. 9.
