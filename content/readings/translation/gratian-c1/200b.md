### C. CVI. Nothing shall be demanded for the dedication of churches or the conferring of other sacraments.

From the Council of Chalon, in the time of Charlemagne.

We decree that, just as nothing is to be received for the dedication of churches or the conferring of orders, so too priests coming to receive chrism shall give nothing for the balsam or for the purchase of candles. Let bishops therefore buy the balsam from the resources of the church, and each one the candles for his own churches.

XIII. Part. Gratianus. Just as these sacraments, even when administered simoniacally, nonetheless convey the effect of blessing to the recipient, so also sacerdotal anointing, even if simoniacally administered, ought not to lack its proper effect. But, as was said above, those sacraments pertain to necessity while this pertains to dignity; and therefore the privileges of the former cannot establish a common law for the latter. Simoniacs therefore have a wounded head through the wicked imposition of simoniacal hands. This, however, is understood of those who are excused neither by ignorance nor by the violence of coercion — those who are ordained by men they know beyond doubt to be simoniacs, whether they themselves are ordained simoniacally or not. For, as Pope Nicolaus distinguishes, some are ordained simoniacally by simoniacs, others simoniacally by non-simoniacs, and others non-simoniacally by simoniacs. He intimates this distinction beginning at chapter II, saying:

### C. CVII. Concerning the multiple kinds of simoniacs.

We establish a decree concerning simoniacs who are simoniacal ordainers or ordinands, concerning simoniacs simoniacally ordained by non-simoniacs, and concerning simoniacs non-simoniacally ordained by simoniacs. §. 1. Simoniacs simoniacally ordained or who ordained simoniacally shall be deposed from their own rank according to the ecclesiastical canons. Simoniacs who were simoniacally ordained by non-simoniacs shall likewise be removed from the office they so obtained. §. 2. Those simoniacs, however, who were ordained non-simoniacally by simoniacs we mercifully permit, in view of the needs of the time, to remain in office.

Gratianus. But this is to be understood of those who are ordained by simoniacs of whose simony they were ignorant. Such persons are made simoniacs not by the guilt of the crime but by ordination from a simoniac. Of these, Pope Urbanus says:
