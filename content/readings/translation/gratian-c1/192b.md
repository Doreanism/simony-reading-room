### C. LXXIII.

The priests of heretics are not held worthy of the honors of Christ.

Also Innocentius, chapter VII:

As for Arians and the other plagues of this kind, since we receive their laity who have converted to the Lord under the form of penance and the sanctification of the Holy Spirit through the laying on of hands, their clerics do not appear worthy to be received with the dignity of the priesthood or of any ministry. For to those whose baptism alone we permit to be valid — which is received in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit — we do not think they have the Holy Spirit from that baptism and from those ministries, since, when their founders departed from the catholic faith, they lost the fullness of the Spirit which they had received, and they cannot give the fullness of it, which works chiefly in ordinations, \[those\] who through the faithlessness of their impiety have lost the faith of the Lord. For this reason it cannot be that we should deem their profane priests worthy of the honors of Christ, since we receive their laity — imperfect, as I have said — under the form of penance for receiving the grace of the Holy Spirit.

Also §. 1. It is written: “Whoever touches what is unclean shall be unclean.” How then shall dignity be granted to one accustomed to receiving uncleanness and filth? And below: §. 2. How one who has been made a partner of the condemned ought to receive honor, we cannot determine.

Gratian. Moreover, from the fact that the laying on of hands is commanded to be repeated, it is shown that it is not a sacrament. Hence Augustinus, On Baptism, book III, against the Donatists:

### C. LXXIV.

The laying on of hands is not a repetition of a sacrament.

The laying on of hands cannot, like baptism, be repeated. For what is it other than a prayer over a person?

Gratian. Since, therefore, Innocentius permits only baptism to be valid among heretics; since Gregorius calls the consecration of Arians sacrilegious; since Ciprianus judges whatever is done by heretics to be carnal, empty, and false; since Ieronimus asserts that all things offered by them are defiled in the sight of the Lord; since Leo testifies that outside the Church neither priesthoods are valid nor sacrifices true: it is clear that the ecclesiastical sacraments other than baptism (as has been said above) cannot be administered by heretics.
