### C. II. Concerning those who sell churches or ecclesiastical benefices.

The same, to the Bishop of Rouen and to all the Bishops of Gaul.

If anyone, whether giver or receiver, has obtained the churches of God or ecclesiastical benefices (which some call prebends) through the mediation of money — whether by giving to purchase them or by receiving to sell them — he is not excluded from the perdition of Simon. But if he persists, let him be punished with perpetual condemnation. For whoever has wished to serve God under the guise of religion, if he has received anything, both loses his merit and is defrauded of the benefit he accepted. He is therefore compelled by the force of reason to restore what he unjustly received and not to retain whatever he received for the sake of base gain.

### C. III. A bishop who sells benefices or ecclesiastical offices shall be suspended from office.

Item, Pope Gregorius VII.

If any bishop shall have sold prebends, archdeaconries, provostships, or any ecclesiastical offices, or shall have arranged them otherwise than the statutes of the holy Fathers prescribe, let him be suspended from office. For it is just that, as he received his bishopric freely, so he should distribute the members of that bishopric freely.

### C. IV. Bishops may not sell either altars or prebends.

Item from the Council of Pope Urban II, held in Auvergne.

A question was raised concerning bishops who frequently compelled monasteries that had been given altars to redeem them with money, because a certain custom of simoniacal wickedness has long taken root in the regions of Gaul, whereby churches or tithes (commonly called altars among them) given to monasteries are frequently sold by bishops under the cloak of avarice — namely when the clerics whom they call “persons” die or are changed. We, expelling all venality from ecclesiastical properties and ministries with God as our author, forbid by apostolic authority that this be done any further, just as we also prohibit the sale of all prebends. Furthermore, whatever altars or tithes monasteries are known to have possessed for thirty years or more under such redemption, we confirm as henceforth to be held by them quietly and without any molestation. §. 1. With due regard, of course, for the annual census of the bishops, which they are accustomed to receive from those same altars.

### C. V. Whatever is acquired in sacred orders through money given or promised shall be held void.

The same, at the Council of Piacenza.

We confirm with our own judgment and apostolic authority the statutes established by the holy Fathers concerning simoniacs. Whatever, therefore, has been acquired in sacred orders, or in ecclesiastical matters, through money given or promised, we declare to be void and to have no force whatsoever.
