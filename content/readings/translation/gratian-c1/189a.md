Just as if someone’s hand were withered, it would be a true member, though without blood it would be ineffectual and dead — so too faith, though it be true, is yet dead without works.

### C. XLVIII.

The baptism of Christ, even though it is not corrupted by any perversity, nonetheless lacks its effect among heretics.

Item, Augustinus speaks to the contrary in the book on baptism against the Donatists, saying:

Even those who are slow of understanding recognize that the baptism of Christ cannot be corrupted by any perversity on the part of the one who gives or receives it. But since the sacrament is one thing and the effect of the sacrament another, and since it is established that those who live wickedly within the unity of the Church can neither give nor possess the remission of sins — for it was not to the wicked but to the good sons that it was said: “Whose sins you shall forgive, they are forgiven them; whose sins you shall retain, they are retained” — it is therefore clear that heretics give and receive only the sacrament of baptism.

### C. XLIX.

Item, on the words of the Lord, concerning blasphemy against the Spirit.

Bodily sacraments, which even those separated from the unity of the body of Christ carry and celebrate, can have the form of piety. But the invisible and spiritual power can no more be in them than sensation follows a member when it is severed from the body.

### C. L.

On the same.

Item, Ambrosius in the book on initiating the uninstructed.

The baptism of the faithless does not heal, does not cleanse, but pollutes, because in the flood of waters they will not draw near to God — for as the Apostle says, “whatever is not from faith is sin” — and it not only profits nothing but leads to judgment, both for those who perform it and for those upon whom it is performed.
