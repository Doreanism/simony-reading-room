III. Part. Gratianus. From this authority it is to be understood that whoever has desired \[to enter\] the society of heretics \[in abandonment of\] the unity of the catholic faith, even if he afterward, renouncing his heresy, returns to the Church, cannot nonetheless exercise the office he received. By the authority of Ciprianus, moreover, those are forbidden from ecclesiastical administration who, having been ordained within the catholic church, slip away to the assault of the faith.

Further, simonists are neither ordained by heretics for the subversion of the faith, nor, having been ordained within the catholic church, do they rise up against the faith. For even though some ordain others for money, they do not thereby assault the faith, nor do they preach the grace of the Holy Spirit as something for sale.
