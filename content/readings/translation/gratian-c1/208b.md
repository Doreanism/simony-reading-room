For just as the sellers of doves represent those who attempt to sell the sacred laying on of hands, so the money-changers represent the sellers of ecclesiastical benefices, who — as the Gospel testifies — make the house of God a den of thieves. §. 3. Wherefore I, Alexander, bishop of the holy Roman Church and apostolic see — or rather its unworthy minister — beholding so many and such great evils being done in many churches and especially in the Church of Lucca, out of wicked concupiscence, lest the blood of the wicked be required of our hand by the strict Judge, have decreed to uproot and entirely eradicate them. We therefore establish and confirm by the present decree (as our predecessors are known to have done aforetime) that no bishop shall henceforth dare to confer on any cleric a benefice of the Church (which some call a canonry, or prebends, or even holy orders) for any price or gift; but that they take diligent care to ordain all ministers and servants of the Church freely and without any venality in the holy Church. Let them not choose in the house of the Lord those who bring the largest purses of money, but those who, rich in character and learning, are able to sustain the Church itself in its office. We establish and confirm in the same way that no cleric of whatever rank shall dare to offer anything for a benefice of the Church, whether to the fabric of churches, in gifts to churches, or even for distribution to the poor — for, as scripture witnesses, he who receives something wrongly in order to dispense it rightly is burdened rather than helped. But if anyone, unmindful of the divine precepts and the salvation of souls, shall have presumed in a rash attempt, led by wicked cupidity, to buy or sell the aforesaid benefice of the Church, we decree — as was defined in the Council of Chalcedon — that he be subject to the peril of losing his rank, that he may not minister in the Church whose venality he coveted, and moreover, pierced by the sword of terrible anathema, let him, unless he repent, be cut off in every way from the Church of God which he has injured.

### C. X. Those who sell or buy the gifts of God are alike condemned by God.

Item, Gregorius, Homily XIII.

Christ expelled the sellers and buyers from the temple because he condemns both those who confer the laying on of hands for a gift and those who strive to buy the gift of God. Item: §. 1. Those who sell in the temple are those who bestow upon others for a reward what rightfully belongs to certain persons by right. For to sell justice is to observe it only upon receiving a reward.
