### C. XXXVIII. The wicked can possess the sacraments, though they cannot possess the virtue of the Holy Spirit.

The same \[Augustinus\], Book III \[On Baptism\].

There are many operations of the Holy Spirit, which the same Apostle, having enumerated in a certain passage, concludes thus: “But all these are the work of one and the same Spirit, distributing to each one individually as he wills \[1 Corinthians 12\].” Since therefore the sacrament is one thing — which even Symon Magus could possess — and prophecy another, which is wont to occur even in wicked men as in Saul, and the operation of the same Spirit yet another, which only the good can possess, that is, charity: whatever heretics and schismatics may receive, “charity, which covers a multitude of sins \[1 Peter 4\],” is the proper gift of catholic unity and peace, and this gift does not belong to all, for not all belong to it.

### C. XXXIX. Although the wicked administer the sacraments, they do not confer remission of sins.

The same \[Augustinus\], the same book.

The avaricious do not confer remission of sins, which is given through prayer, that is, through the moaning of the dove, whoever may baptize — provided those who receive it belong to that peace.
