### C. XIV. He who bestows tithes for money sells the Holy Spirit.

Item, Pascalis II.

No member of the faithful is ignorant that to give an altar and tithes for money is to sell the Holy Spirit and constitutes the simoniac heresy.

### C. XV. Let him who has attempted to acquire ecclesiastical benefits for money forfeit the honor so wrongly obtained.

Item, Pope Innocentius II, in the Roman Synod, chapter 2.

If anyone has acquired prebends, or a priorate, or a deanship, or an honor, or any ecclesiastical promotion, or any ecclesiastical sacrament — such as chrism, or holy oil, consecrations of altars or of churches — through the execrable heat of avarice and for money, let him forfeit the honor so wrongly obtained; and let the buyer, the seller, and the intermediary each be struck with a mark of infamy; and let nothing be exacted before or after, either for sustenance or under the pretext of any custom, nor let him presume to give anything, since it is simoniacal; but let him freely and without any diminution enjoy the dignity and benefit conferred upon him.

Gratianus. What the sacred canons define on the second and third questions has been made clear by the authorities brought forward on both sides.

## QUESTIO IV.

GRATIANUS.

I. Pars. Now therefore the fourth question must be treated: whether the father’s crime is to be imputed to this person \[the ordinand\], given that it is established to have been committed without his knowledge? That ignorance excuses this person is proved by the authorities of many.

For Augustinus says, Letter XLVIII, to Vincentius:

### C. I. A person who did not know of the crime of another is not stained by it.

The crime of another does not stain one who did not know of it.

### C. II.

Item, in the same letter.

What room is left for innocence, if the crime of another stains one who did not know of it?

### C. III. Concerning infants baptized by heretics.

Item, it is read in the African Council, c. 14:

It has been decided concerning infants who were baptized by heretics — since this was not done by their own choice — that when they have returned to the Church of God with wholesome intent, the error of their parents should not hinder them from being promoted to the ministry of the holy altar.
