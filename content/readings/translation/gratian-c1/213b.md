## QUESTIO VII.

GRATIANUS.

I. Part. Now we must treat the seventh question: whether one who renounces his heresy is to be received back into episcopal dignity or not?

Cyprianus writes on this matter to Stephanus, Book II, epistle 1:

### C. I. Those returning from heresy are to be received back in their orders.

If any presbyters and deacons were previously ordained in the catholic Church and subsequently became faithless and rebellious against the Church, or were promoted among heretics by pseudo-bishops and antichrists through a profane ordination contrary to the disposition of Christ, and attempted to offer outside \[the Church\] false and sacrilegious sacrifices against the altar and the divine word — these also are to be received on this condition when they return: that they communicate as laypeople, and consider it sufficient that they are admitted to peace, having shown themselves to be enemies of peace; nor should they, upon returning, retain among us those arms of ordination and honor with which they rebelled against us.

### C. II. Concerning those who confer ordination for gifts, and those who return from heresy and are received back in their orders.

Likewise from the Seventh Synod \[Second Council of Nicaea, 787\].

If anyone violates any ecclesiastical tradition, whether written or unwritten, let him be anathema. §. 1. Every bishop, or presbyter, or deacon, convicted of having given or received ordination for gifts, shall fall from the priesthood. §. 2. But some say: “We do penance for sin, and God forgives it.” So also I say that God receives every penance and forgives through penance the sins that have already been committed. For I know that God received David, a fornicator and murderer, through penance, and said of him: “I have found a man after my own heart.” I know likewise that the same man heard from God: “You will not build a house for me, because you are a man of blood.” I know that Manasseh had his impiety forgiven through confession. I know that prostitutes and tax collectors were received through penance — yet none of those who sinned before baptism was numbered among the priests. I know that certain persons, while they were in the world, remained in great fornication, but when made monks shone like luminaries of the world; yet none ascended to the priesthood.
