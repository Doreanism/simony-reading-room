Since therefore, according to Leo, simony is not brought to a good end; according to Gregorius, no superstructure of good work follows from it; nor is what is distributed to the poor from the price of simony to be considered charity; when the pastor is wounded, medicine is not applied to heal the sheep; from an infected root no fruit is produced; and a greater calamity is to be feared in the future — what happy outcome can be hoped for from so evil a beginning? Hence Augustinus says:

### C. XXIX. The sacraments of simonists lack the effect of grace.

Just as the eunuch who purchased Ioseph was a eunuch, so one who buys grace has no living seed in his dry genitals. So also the fire of sacrifice, which had survived for seventy years of the Babylonian captivity under water, was extinguished when Antiochus sold the priesthood to Iason, which signified that the fire of the Holy Spirit does not shine in simoniacal sacraments.

Gratian. What did Augustinus wish to signify by these likenesses, if not that the sacraments of simonists, like those of other heretics, are genuine as to form but empty and false as to effect, since they cannot beget or kindle heavenly grace in the hearts of men?

V. Pars. §. 1. But the following text of Augustinus is raised in objection against heretics:

### C. XXX. Whether the minister is good or evil, through either God confers the effect of grace.

If the minister is just, I reckon him with Paulus, who does not seek his own glory, saying: “I planted, Apollo watered, but God gave the growth \[1 Corinthians 3\].” But if the minister is proud, he is reckoned with the devil; yet the gift of Christ is not contaminated, because through him a pure stream flows and comes to fertile ground. I know that a stone cannot bear fruit from water, and water passes through stone channels to garden beds — in a stone channel it generates nothing; but the garden produces abundant fruit. For the spiritual virtue of the sacrament, like pure light, is received by those who are to be illuminated; but passing through unclean vessels it is not defiled.
