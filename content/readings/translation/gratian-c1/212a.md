PALEA.

### C. XIII. The Emperor Valentinianus Augustus.

If anyone has gone so far in the audacity of his fury as to violently seize possession of property belonging to the treasury or to any persons whatsoever before the outcome of judicial arbitration: if he was the lawful owner, he shall restore to the possessor the possession he has taken away and shall forfeit ownership of that thing. If, however, he invaded the possession of another’s property, he shall not only return it to those in possession, but shall also be compelled to restore the estimated value of the same property.

Gratianus. In this case \[of the young person whose father committed simony\], however, there was ignorance not of law but of fact — and of that kind of fact which he was not obliged to know. Therefore, ignorance of the paternal offense excuses him from the guilt of the crime; for even if he had been obliged to know it, he could not have discerned it on account of his youth.

## QUESTIO V.

GRATIANUS.

I. Part. The fifth question asks whether it is lawful for someone to remain in the Church or to exercise the ordination he obtained through his father’s money. This is settled by the authority of Urbanus \[II\], who, writing about simoniacs, declares among other things in the Council of Piacenza, c. 5 and following, and likewise in the Roman Council:

### C. I. Concerning young children who, through their parents’ greed, obtain churches.

If any persons, through the greed of their parents, obtained churches or parts of churches by means of money while they were still children, we grant them, out of mercy — once they have relinquished those churches entirely — to remain in those same churches, if they wish to live canonically in them; nor do we remove them from holy orders on this account, provided they are found otherwise worthy. §. 1. Those, however, who, being of more mature age, were led by wicked greed to purchase churches themselves, we permit — out of great mercy — to minister in other churches in which they wish to live canonically, retaining their own orders. But if perhaps they cannot be transferred to other churches, and promise to live canonically in those same churches, they shall be content with minor orders and shall not advance to holy orders, with the authority of the apostolic see preserved in all things. §. 2. If any persons, however, were ordained in the catholic Church before the purchase, when they have relinquished what they purchased and have lived the canonical life, they shall be permitted to remain in their grades, unless perhaps the church is of such a kind that they ought to hold the first place in it. For we do not permit them to hold the first, singular, or provost’s place in purchased churches.
