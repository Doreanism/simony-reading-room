Gratian. Others observe the form of the Church in baptizing. But when they are baptized by those who share their heresy or schism, they do indeed receive the sacrament of baptism from them, yet they do not obtain its power, which no one can obtain without faith. Hence it is said of the Lord that in his homeland he could not perform a miracle on account of their unbelief.

### C. LIV.

When the word is added to the element, a sacrament comes to be.

Item, Augustinus in homily LXXX:

Remove the word — what is water but water? The word is added to the element, and a sacrament comes to be. §. 1. Whence comes this great power of water, that it touches the body and washes the heart, if not through the action of the word? Not because it is spoken, but because it is believed. For in the word itself, one thing is the transient sound, another the abiding power.

Gratian. Concerning those also who are baptized in heresy or schism, Augustinus writes to Petrus the Deacon, saying:

### C. LV.

Those baptized outside the Church who have not returned to the Church heap destruction upon themselves.

Hold most firmly and doubt not at all that destruction is heaped upon those baptized outside the Church if they do not return to the Church. For the communion of ecclesiastical fellowship has such power for salvation that baptism does not save one to whom it is not given where it ought to be given.

### C. LVI.

Gifts that are held apart from the unity of the Church are worthless.

The same, in tractate XIII on John 3:

Let us hold fast to unity, my brethren. Apart from unity, even one who works miracles is nothing; the people of Israel were within unity and did not work miracles; the magicians of Pharaoh were outside unity and worked the same signs as Moses. The people of Israel, as I said, did not work miracles. Who were those who were to be saved in God’s sight — those who worked miracles, or those who did not? The apostle Petrus raised the dead. Symon the magician did many things. There were certain Christians who could not do what Petrus did, nor what Symon did. But they rejoiced in this: that their names were written in heaven.
