Gratian. That the other sacraments cannot be administered by heretics is attested by Hieronymus in the Prophet Haggai:

### C. LXI.

What is offered by heretics is not pleasing to God.

Thus is this whole people of all heretics in God’s sight, says the Lord: everything they do, everything they offer to me — whether vows, or offerings for salvation, or peace offerings, or sin offerings, or burnt offerings, or alms, or fasts, or bodily chastity — shall be defiled in my sight. For although what is offered may appear holy in its outward form, yet, because it has been handled by one who is polluted in soul, all things are polluted.

### C. LXII.

On the same.

Also in the Prophet Amos:

God hates the sacrifices of heretics and casts them away from himself; and as often as they gather in his name, he loathes the stench of them and closes his nostrils.

### C. LXIII.

Also in Hosea:

They offer sacrilegious bread, and give alms, and appear to pursue humility. These things, if done truly, I would count as whole burnt offerings. But since they have forsaken the knowledge of God, they boast in vain that they possess the other members of faith while the head is cut off.

### C. LXIV.

Without faith and charity, neither the gospel is held nor do any good works profit.

The same, in the letter to the Galatians:

Marcion and Basilides, and the other plagues of the heretics, do not possess the gospel of God, because they do not have the Holy Spirit, without which the gospel that is taught becomes merely human. And let us not suppose that the gospel consists in the words of scripture, but in the meaning; not in the surface, but in the marrow; not in the leaves of words, but in the root of reason.

### C. LXV.

The same, on chapter 5 of the same letter:

See how great a good is charity. If we suffer martyrdom in such a way that we wish our relics to be honored by men, or if, following popular opinion, we pour out our blood without fear, and give away our substance even to personal beggary — for such a work there is due not so much a reward as a punishment, and it merits the torments of faithlessness rather than the crown of victory.

### C. LXVI.

The blessing of heretics is a curse.

Also from the Council of Laodicea:

It is not fitting to receive the blessings of heretics, since they are curses rather than blessings.
