### C. XC.

It is not the words but the merits of a priest that make the eucharist.

Priests who serve the eucharist and distribute the blood of the Lord to his people act impiously against the law of Christ, thinking that the words of the one pronouncing the prayer make the eucharist and not \[his\] life, and that only the solemn prayer is necessary, and not the merits of the priests — concerning whom it is said: “A priest, whatever blemish he has upon him, shall not approach to offer oblations to the Lord.”

### C. XCI.

Priests are heard all the more readily the more worthy they are before God.

Item, Alexander:

The priests themselves intercede for the people and consume the sins of the people, because they blot them out and destroy them by their prayers; and the worthier they are, the more readily are they heard in the needs for which they cry out.

### C. XCII.

The Holy Spirit is not present at sacraments administered through criminal ministers.

Item, Gelasius, to Bishop Elphidius:

The most sacred religion, which contains Catholic discipline, claims for itself such great reverence that no one dares to approach it unless with a pure conscience. For how will the heavenly Spirit, when invoked, come to the consecration of a divine mystery, if the priest who prays for him to be present is found full of criminal deeds?

### C. XCIII.

An unclean hand defiles what it touches; it does not cleanse it.

Item, Gregorius, in the Register:

It is necessary that the hand which takes care to cleanse filth be itself clean, lest it make everything it touches worse, if, being dirty itself, it afterwards holds to the mud — for it is written: “Be clean, you who carry the vessels of the Lord.” For those carry the vessels of the Lord who receive the souls of neighbors into the example of their own conduct, to be led toward inward things.

### C. XCIV.

The word of Christ and the body of Christ are to be guarded equally.

Item, Augustinus:

I ask you, brothers or sisters — tell me, which seems greater to you: the body of Christ or the word of Christ? If you wish to answer truly, you ought to say that the word of God is not less than the body of Christ.
