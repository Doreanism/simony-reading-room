### C. XXII. Both the giver and the receiver of payment for holy orders shall be anathema.

Likewise from the Second Council of Braga.

It was resolved that bishops shall accept no gifts for the ordination of clerics, but, as it is written, that what they have received freely from God who gives freely they shall give freely \[cf. Matthew 10:8\]. Let not the grace of God and the laying on of hands be sold at any price, for the ancient definition of the Fathers has thus decreed concerning ecclesiastical ordinations, saying: “Let the giver and the receiver be anathema.” For this reason — because some men, weighed down by many crimes and ministering unworthily at the holy altar, obtain their positions not by the testimony of good works but by lavish gifts — it is proper to ordain clerics not through the favor of gifts but through diligent examination first, and then through the testimony of many witnesses.

Gratian. From this authority of Ambrosius and Gregorius it is clear that simoniacs, like other heretics, deviate from the faith, and therefore what is decreed concerning others is consistently understood to apply to them as well.
