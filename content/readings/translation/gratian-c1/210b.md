“The soul that has sinned, it shall die.” But that one may be regenerated through the act of another’s will, when one is offered for consecration, this is the work of the one Spirit from whom the one offered is regenerated.

Hence also Gregorius, in Book XXVI of the Moralia, c. 8, on Job chapter 35:

### C. IX. The wickedness of another harms only the one who imitates it.

“Your wickedness will harm a man who is like you, and your righteousness will help a son of man.” Human wickedness harms one whom it corrupts by perverting him, and in turn our righteousness helps one whom it changes from wicked actions. For those things that can neither corrupt a person from good nor change him from evil do not know how to harm or help.

Gratianus. Against this the Lord says to Moses: “I am a jealous God, who visit the sins of fathers upon children to the third and fourth generation” — although Augustinus asserts that this saying rests not on inequality of judgment but on the greatness of mercy, saying thus: “In the third and fourth generation the Lord threatens in the law to repay the sins of fathers to children — not with inequality of judgment, as if some should sin and others be punished, but out of the greatness of mercy, since he always awaits repentance and does not correct and amend what was transgressed in the first generation until the third and fourth generation has come.” In the Digest, under the title On Penalties, c. VI: “The crime or penalty of a father can inflict no stain upon a son. For each person is subjected to his lot on account of his own offense, nor is anyone constituted the heir of another’s crime. And this too the divine brothers \[Emperors Marcus Aurelius and Lucius Verus\] rescript to the inhabitants of Hierapolis.” Since therefore Innocentius writes of those baptized by heretics: “It is the law of the church for those who were baptized by heretics to be given only lay communion by the laying on of hands, and none of them is to be substituted for the honor of the clergy, even the least” — it is plain that infants who were baptized by heretics are excused by ignorance alone, so that the name of the error does not hinder them, in accordance with the African Council, from receiving the office of the clergy.

Item, as Augustinus writes to Bishop Auxilius, Letter 75:

### C. X.

“The sins that parents commit after the birth of their children are not imputed to the children; nor are children to be afflicted with spiritual punishment for the sins of their parents.”

Gratianus. To be deprived of an ecclesiastical office or benefit is a spiritual punishment; therefore a son does not incur this on account of his father’s sin.

Item, if one who is ignorantly ordained by a simonist is excused, as is read above in the canon of Urbanus, then this person too is to be excused, who was ignorantly ordained through simony.

II. Pars. But that saying of Gregorius is raised against \[this\]:

### C. XI. Concerning a church consecrated by pact.

A church that has been consecrated by pact ought to be called cursed rather than consecrated.
