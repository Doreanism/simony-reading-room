Sometimes, by the merits of superiors, the life of subjects is corrupted, so that subjects who favor their superiors in evil may fall together with them — just as the children of Israel, long bearing the sins of the priests, at last fell with them into the hands of the enemies. Item, David numbered the people; on account of which sin the sword of the Lord raged against the people. “But the glory of a king is in the multitude of his people; in the diminishment of the people is the ruin of the prince.” He therefore who took pride in the multitude of his people was justly punished in its diminishment. §. 10. Item, the ignorance of the infants of Sodom did not excuse them from being consumed by heavenly fire on account of their fathers’ wickedness; but by this fire the punishment was diminished for those from whom the succession of their fathers’ wickedness was taken away. It has likewise been shown that children are sometimes corporally scourged on account of the sins of their parents. The same is to be understood of the little ones who, on account of the sin of Datan and Abiron, went down alive into the underworld. §. 11. Item, with Cham sinning, his son Canaan is cursed; with Gehazi sinning, leprosy is transmitted to his descendants. When the Jews cried out: “His blood be upon us and upon our children,” the remnant of them was adjudged to the penalty of the death of Christ. With Achab sinning, his descendants lost the throne of the kingdom, and those of his seed who were born in the kingdom of Judah were excluded from the genealogy of the Lord until the stain of that family was purged. The hatred of Esau too (as Malachias \[Malachi\] testifies) passed over to the Idumeans.

From all of these it is gathered that ignorance of a sin does not excuse anyone. §. 12. But by these examples it is not proved that any are held bound by the sin of others except imitators of their wickedness. For whoever of the seed of Esau and the others were converted to God and detested their fathers’ malice experienced not hatred but the mercy of God.

Item, as Ambrosius says in Sermon XI:

### C. XII.

“The boat of Petrus was troubled, in which Judas was present, because one who was firm through his own merits was troubled by the merits of another.”

Gratianus. Petrus, however, did not know the sin of Judas. His ignorance therefore did not excuse him \[from the trouble\]; but this disturbance is to be understood not as a pollution of sin but as a bodily persecution, because through the merits of evil men the good are sometimes so pressed that their weakness is disturbed.

Therefore by these authorities simonists are not proved in the cases of those on whose behalf money was given without their knowledge. Nor therefore can this person be proved guilty of a crime of which he had no awareness.

IV. Pars. §. 1. It must also be noted that not every ignorance excuses anyone. For there is one kind of ignorance of fact and another of law. Of fact: one kind concerns what he was not obliged to know, another concerns what he was obliged to know.

What he was not obliged to know — as the Apostle says: “If any of the unbelievers invites you to dinner, eat whatever is set before you, asking no questions. But if someone says to you, ‘This has been offered to idols,’ do not eat.” Behold, the one invited to dinner was not obliged to know that the meat set before him had been offered to idols.

What he was obliged to know — such as the ignorance of the Jews and the rest of the unbelievers, who are still ignorant that the things announced by the prophets concerning Christ have been fulfilled, for which reason the one who is to come is still awaited by the Jews. This \[ignorance\] excuses no one.

§. 2. Item, ignorance of law is of two kinds: natural and civil. Natural \[ignorance of law\] is culpable for all adults; civil law, however, certain persons are permitted to be ignorant of, and others are not. Ignorance of civil law harms no one in avoiding a loss, if a transaction arises from it — as when a minor has given money to a son-in-the-power-of-a-family \[filiusfamilias\], he may recover it; but in an adult it is as if a wrongdoing. §. 3.
