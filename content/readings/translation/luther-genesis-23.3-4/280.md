But Peter understands gifts given by God in the passive sense \[*passive*\], not in the active sense \[*active*\]. This is therefore the fallacy of composition and division, as the dialecticians say, when these things are confused and mixed together. Yet how rightly could they teach on this matter? — they take no care for the gifts of the Holy Spirit and are themselves submerged in simony and in gifts given to God, that is, in what are commonly called “spiritual goods.”

And I have often lamented the abuse of these terms — *geistliche Güter*, *geistliche Person* \[spiritual goods, spiritual persons\] — by which name they designate their sacrificers \[*sacrificos*\]. I would gladly have retained these terms in their true and proper use, but they have now been ruined for us by abuse. To the papists, “spiritual goods” now means revenues, rents, buildings, towns, territories — things that are thoroughly political in nature. A “spiritual person” is one who believes and is baptized, whether layman or one holding an ecclesiastical office; it does not mean a shaved and anointed sacrificer ordained to offer masses for the dead.

I make these points so that you may know how utterly perverse the abuse is, and how horrible the blindness in canon law disputes about simony. The buying and selling — not of the gifts of the Holy Spirit in the active sense, but of human gifts given to God or the Church, such as prebends, towns, and the like — this the canonists call simony. For example, if someone sells what they call a “spiritual benefice” — a parish, an altar benefice, a Mass benefice. Or if a bishop receives money and ordains a priest. They then go on to dispute whether a simoniac ordained by a simoniac is truly ordained or not. On these questions whole cartloads — indeed whole seas — of books exist.

But if their definition holds true, then all papist bishops and canons are simply simoniacs, all condemned to hell and the devil — and they condemn themselves not by evangelical law, but by their own canon law. The Bishop of Mainz counts out twenty thousand gold coins to the pope for his pallium. The Bishop of Würzburg, ten thousand. Canons pay a hundred gold coins each. Are they not all simoniacs? Therefore the entire papacy is submerged and condemned to hell, because all are simoniacs — not by our law, but by their own.

On this matter the canonists are silent and cannot bear our criticism. But they condemn and kill them if our ministers and pastors of the churches take wives. What is this but iniquity upon iniquity?

Four hundred years ago, if a bishop had charged someone with this crime \[of simony\], everyone would have fled his company; indeed he would have been excommunicated on the spot. But now everything is for sale. Meanwhile they command us to observe the canons and decrees, from which they themselves are entirely free. They rail at us that the papal decrees are being neglected, that ecclesiastical order is being scorned and dismantled — why do they not themselves observe these things? Among them no one sins except the Son of God; no one is righteous except the devil.
