He neither seeks nor seizes any of those things that are the true gifts of the Holy Spirit. They are indeed gifts of God, but they are political, economic, and bodily gifts.

We therefore absolve the Pope of this crime according to the true definition by which he has entangled himself in his own law: because he does not sell spiritual things in the true sense — indeed, he does not possess them — but sells certain dignities and seizes the wealth of the world for himself. He does not sell the ministry; on the contrary, he takes no care for the ministry of God and the Church. Therefore, the crime of simony is not properly charged against him, but rather the crime of a Verres or a Dionysius. He ought to be called the plunderer of all plunderers, the thief of thieves, the robber of robbers — for with insatiable and unquenchable voracity and greed he devours and swallows up all bodily goods.

Simony is truly an ecclesiastical vice, as Peter defines it, when we accept money for the gifts of the Holy Spirit, not for bodily gifts. These two things the canonists confuse, being utter and complete asses. For simony has no place in economic or political goods, but only in the gifts of the Holy Spirit.

As for the objection that those bodily goods are annexed to spiritual ones: quite right — economy and polity are annexed to the Church. But this life cannot be sustained without food and drink; does that make everything that believers use spiritual?

Simony is when I sell at a price a spiritual thing that ought to be given freely. But the temple, the tomb, and all the gifts offered to the saints are not spiritual but simply bodily; these can be seized, sold, and disposed of. Spiritual gifts, however, we cannot steal, sell, or buy, because they are not ours but belong to the Holy Spirit.

Now, if a minister or pastor of the Church were unwilling to teach you about the forgiveness of sins and the benefits of the Gospel, and denied you absolution unless you paid him a hundred gold coins, that would be simony. And in this sense the Pope is a simoniac: for he offers to all the grace and forgiveness of sins from God, if they pay him a fixed sum of money. That is selling a spiritual thing and the grace of the Holy Spirit — though it cannot actually be sold, even if you were to count out a thousand gold coins or more for one absolution. First, because the pope does not possess it. Second, because it is impossible to sell spiritual things — except that a pretense can be made for the ignorant, and a certain semblance of selling can be enacted.

But the gifts of the Holy Spirit and the blood of Christ are not for sale, nor is their sale possible — except by fraud and a kind of appearance, according to the choice and will of men. When the Pope declares that one who brings five groschen will redeem a soul from purgatory — there is simony in the proper sense, but not a true one, because the liberation of the soul from purgatory does not follow, even when the price, the purchase, and the sale are all present.
