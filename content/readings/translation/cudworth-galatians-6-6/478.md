## V.

In the necessary unfolding of the meaning of certain passages of Scripture which cannot be grasped without philosophy. In such a case we may employ human learning in descending to the reach and capacity of the audience, and teaching heavenly things by earthly things — as our Savior Christ taught regeneration by the similitude of the wind (John 3). Yet all such things must be used: first, sparingly; second, when there is just cause; third, without ostentation; and fourth, delivering nothing to the people the ground of which is not in the word.

This shows that the chief scope of the ministry is to preach the word purely and to apply it powerfully to the consciences of men. It condemns all deceitful handling of the word and all huckster-like dealing — mingling wine and water, wheat and chaff, gold and dross; perverting it with feverish and foolish conceits; wresting it with allegories, tropologies, and anagogies; and wringing the text until they make it bleed, and so (as an ancient writer says) pressing the two dogs of the Scriptures — the Old and New Testaments — so that instead of milk they drink nothing but blood. This also teaches hearers to rest content with the plain words, without hungering after new doctrines as the Athenians hungered after news, or being drawn away from the simplicity that is in Christ by the subtlety of seducers or by the devices and quiddities of human invention. Let those, therefore, who loathe the manna and long for quails remember that if God grants their desire, he will likewise give them their desert. Let them take heed lest he punish them with a famine — not of bread, nor a thirst of water, but of hearing the word (Amos 8:11). And let those who have itching ears, heaping up for themselves a multitude of teachers, take heed also that they have not tingling ears when they hear of God’s judgments for the contempt of his word.

Furthermore, whereas Paul says that he who teaches is to be maintained: this is a good caution to all idle drones who will not labor and all dumb dogs who cannot bark — they may not expect the maintenance that is due to ministers.
