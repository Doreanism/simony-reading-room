It is further called the word of the kingdom (Matthew 13:19) because it teaches what the kingdom of grace and glory is, and because, when believed — or as the Apostle says, when mingled with faith in our hearts — it makes us free citizens of the kingdom of grace in this life and advances us to the kingdom of glory in the life to come. Secondly, it is called the word of God because he is its author, and no creature, man, or angel (2 Thess. 3:13). Thirdly, the word of salvation (Acts 13:26) because it shows the way and means of attaining salvation. And lastly, the word of life (Acts 5:20) because it not only shows the narrow way that leads to eternal life but is itself a living word and mighty in operation (Hebrews 4:12). For just as the powerful word of God in the beginning gave being to things that did not exist, so the Gospel — being the power of God unto salvation to everyone who believes — makes new creatures by the immortal seed of the word.

*Make him that taught him.*] That is, catechized him. Yet as before, this must be understood generally of any kind of teaching or instruction, for the word is so used elsewhere in Scripture: Luke 1:4; Acts 18:25; Romans 2:18; 1 Corinthians 14:19. This therefore gives us no just occasion to speak of the origin, manner, use, and profit of catechizing.

*Partaker of his goods.*] By goods he understands food, clothing, lodging, books, and other necessaries without which a minister of the word cannot pursue his calling; for Paul uses the word *goods* in accordance with the common opinion that so judges of these things. Luke 12:19: *Soul, thou hast much goods laid up for many years.* Luke 19:25: *Son, remember that thou in thy lifetime receivedst thy good things.*

*Make them partaker.*] That is, communicate, afford, and give to them these temporal things, seeing that they give you spiritual ones.

*Of all his goods.*] Not by giving everything away, but by imparting to their teachers what they stand in need of, counting nothing too dear for them. The Papists, on this basis, take tithes of all as the priests did under the Levitical law — whereas Paul says nothing of tithes.
