The Holy Ghost calls the ministry “the work of the ministry” (Eph. 4:12) — nay, a worthy work (1 Tim. 3:1). Therefore Paul says that those elders are worthy of double honor who labor in the word and doctrine (1 Tim. 5:17), and he exhorts the Thessalonians to know those who labor among them, and to hold them in singular love for their work’s sake (1 Thess. 5:12–13). Hence it is that Scripture habitually compares the work of the ministry to the most toilsome labor imaginable: to the work of the husbandman, to setting, plowing, sowing, reaping, laboring in the vineyard; and the minister to a builder, to a shepherd watching his flock, to a soldier fighting in the wars, and so forth. Moreover, one must not judge the painfulness of a calling by its outward appearance; for by that standard a king would seem to have the easiest life of all, when in truth the toil he bears and the cares that possess him exceed all others. If a man knew the travail required to wield a scepter and the pain taken in wearing a heavy crown, he would scarcely stoop down to take the one into his hands or set the other upon his head. The master builder does not hew the stones, nor work the mortar, nor carry the rubble, nor engage in any such drudgery; he only stands by and directs the workmen — and yet his labor is double that of any of theirs. The master of a ship (a man would think) is idle and does nothing: he does not attend to the rigging, he does not stir the pump, he does not drive the oars, he does not sound the deep, he does not ride the ropes — but only sits still at the stern, looks to the pole star, and guides the compass; yet his labor surpasses all the rest. Were it not for him, the ship would run herself under water, or strike upon the rocks, or be split upon the sands, or fall foul upon another (as mariners say). Even so it is, in every respect, with the ministers of the word: they seem to sit still, to be at ease, to do nothing — and yet their labor is double and treble that of other men’s bodily labor, unless they be unfaithful and do the work of the Lord negligently.
