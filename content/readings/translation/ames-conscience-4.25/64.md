# Chapter XXV. On Calling to the Ministry.

## Question I. Is a Calling Necessary?

1. *A.* 1. A singular calling is not required in order to teach and preach the Gospel on occasion, Acts 8:4, 11:19–21, 18:24–25.

2. The reason is: First, because it is the duty of all Christians to advance the kingdom of God as much as they can, and thus to instruct others in matters of divinity.

Secondly, because it is sometimes necessary that people be convicted of errors and instructed in the truth before any lawful vocation can exist.

Thirdly, because a congregation sometimes left without a minister cannot, without harm to its edification, be helped in any other way unless it admits the teaching of those who have not yet been rightly called to the Ministry.
