And to the same point speaks St. Remigius\[26\] on the same passage as St. Gregory, and says as follows: Hear and take heed, you priests of the holy church — that is, of the Christian assembly — and take care lest you turn the house of God into a den of robbers. For he is a robber who seeks gain from the law and is devoted to the service of the sacraments \[for\] the purpose of commerce. Therefore they must fear lest they be cast out of the spiritual temple, as those others were cast out of the physical temple; for every day the Lord visits the house of his Father — that is, the holy church — and drives out those occupied with wicked gains, and by one sin removes both buyers and sellers. The sellers are those who give ordinations for reward; and the buyers are those who give money for that right, and by giving money to the sellers, purchase sin. It must be noted that the reading says:\[27\] He overturned the tables of the money-changers and the tables of those who sold doves. What is meant by the tables of the money-changers? — only the altars, which through the greed of wicked priests become tables of money-changers. And what by the seats of those who sell doves? — only the offices of masters in the holy church are meant, which offices, when they are turned to profit, will be turned to nothing.
