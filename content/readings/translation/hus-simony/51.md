And that it is simony to consecrate a church, chapel, or altar for money, St. Gregory says: “A church which has been consecrated under a negotiated agreement ought rather to be called desecrated than consecrated.”\[92\] And Pope Innocent\[93\] says: “If anyone has obtained prebends, or a priorate, or a deanship, or any other spiritual dignity, or any sacred thing — such as chrism or holy oil, and the consecration of altars or churches — through the accursed desire of avarice, with money, let him not hold the dignity so wickedly obtained; and let both the buyer, the seller, the negotiator, and the go-between be condemned with the mark of disgrace. And neither for food nor by any custom beforehand or afterward shall anything be demanded, nor shall he dare to give: for it is simony!”

Behold, the pope speaks this word for word. And this is a holy ordinance in canon law, for it is founded on this word of Christ: Freely you have received, freely give.\[22\] But alas! this holy ordinance has already died among bishops; for they have now established for themselves a rule to take no fewer than two kopě \[sixty groschen each\] for an altar, and for a church, as much as they can extract beyond two kopě. And this they now hold more firmly than the commandment of Christ.
