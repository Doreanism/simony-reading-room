For when one thus receives a bishopric, being of holy life, full of learning to teach the people, elected by God through men, and consecrated and confirmed without gifts — then he enters the bishopric rightly. But for anyone to enter a bishopric in such manner nowadays, being so well-ordered himself, and those who elect him so well-ordered, and the pope confirming him in such order: sooner would the Charles Bridge of Prague collapse than anyone would enter the Prague bishopric by such a holy course!\[82\]

### \[B\]

Nevertheless I say that the Lord God can bring this about whenever he pleases; for although it is very difficult within the ordinary course of human affairs, yet for almighty God it is very easy to arrange. And that such a bishop ought to be, St. Paul shows:\[83\] that “every bishop, chosen from among men, is appointed for men in those things which pertain to God, that he might offer gifts and sacrifices for sins, and who could suffer with those who are ignorant and err, because he himself is also compassed with infirmity; and therefore he ought, as for the people, so also for himself, to offer sacrifice for sins.
