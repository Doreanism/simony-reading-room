And he will say: What you did not do for the least of mine, you did not do for me.” On this word, St. Augustine says: \[202\] Since the one who did not give of his own will go into everlasting fire, what will become of him who seized what belonged to another? And since those mentioned above take away alms from the poor, where then will such people be on Judgment Day, unless they repent and return to the poor what they have withheld? And because they are murderers of the poor, Scripture says: \[203\] “He who cheats the poor is a man of blood; he who takes away bread earned by sweat — know this, he is guilty as one who kills his neighbor.” And that they are plunderers is certain, for they take away the property of the poor; and that they are thieves is evident, for they steal what belongs to others; and that they are despoilers of sacred things — sacrilegious — is because they steal the holy alms, which are called holy for this reason: that they are for the Lord God, who is holy above all that is holy; and so that a person may become holy, he gives to the poor. And from this a reasonable person can observe how densely people sin in those alms: both those who give them, and those who are trustees of them.
