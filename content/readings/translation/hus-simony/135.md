As happened this very year in our archbishopric, where some gave with the intent that those who preach the word of God and rebuke their sins might be driven out; others gave out of fear lest they be brought to the gallows, for a penalty had been imposed upon them that they should not enter church until they laid down money for Simon \[i.e., in payment of the simoniacal fee\]. But a third group, moved by the Holy Spirit, or perhaps fearing thereafter the same subjection, being unwilling to give, appealed to the pope — yet it would have been better had they appealed boldly to God, refusing consent to participation in simony, and besought the lords and the community to take action and not allow them to be pressed into that sin and into bodily harm.241)

There is a second perversion, namely that although certain parties have the right to elect a bishop, the canons have so firmly established it among themselves that they alone are to elect — and they add further that they should elect from their own chapter, that is, from their own corporation.242) And in both these stipulations they block the path of the Holy Spirit, as though to say: We ourselves are worthy of election; the Holy Spirit has given this right to no others! The second \[stipulation\] is as though to say: Among us alone is one worthy of the bishopric; to no one outside has the Holy Spirit given worthiness! And when they elect, they do not look to choose from all men the best in virtues, but rather one who would protect them in their properties, from whom they would have bodily benefit, one who would conform to their wicked customs!
