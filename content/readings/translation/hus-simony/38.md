Calling them to him, he said to them: “You know that the princes of the Gentiles lord it over them, and those who are greater exercise power over them. It shall not be so among you; but whoever among you desires to be the greatest, let him be your servant; and whoever among you desires to be first, let him be your servant — even as the Son of Man came not to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many.”

O dear servant, precious Lord of all the world! Now have we priests fallen away from following you; your service we disregard, we grasp at dominion excessively — more than even the pagans — transgressing all your commandments, and yet we boast that we are lords of the world, that we are the most worthy in the holy Church! Not so the apostles, nor St. Gregory the pope, who, living holily, always called himself unprofitable, and was the first to devise and to style himself the servant of the servants of God, yet laid down that no one should so style himself unless he showed by his deeds that he was in God’s service. Therefore I do not know whether every pope truly writes that he is the servant of the servants of God. If he follows Christ in virtues, then he writes truly; but if he lives contrary to Christ, then he writes falsely.
