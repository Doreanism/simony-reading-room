Perhaps you still say: But others commonly give money. And I answer: others commonly sin, yet you ought not to sin on that account. You say further: But it is an ancient custom. And I say that a wicked custom, the longer it endures, the greater punishment it draws from God, as I shall demonstrate later. And I marvel why we do not observe the older good custom of the holy apostles, which they received from Christ when he said to them: *You have received freely, give freely*. And why do they not fear the blessing which Saint Peter gave to Simon, saying: *Your money be with you unto damnation, because you thought to obtain the gift of God for money*. And why too they are not terrified by the leprosy which the holy prophet Elisha brought upon Gehazi and his sinful offspring. And third, why they do not fear the curse which the saints have pronounced upon simoniac \[clergy\]. And finally, why they do not fear eternal damnation.

### \[C\]

A second way a parish priest may be a simoniac: by compelling people to give \[payment\] for baptism, for confession, for burial, for the Body of Christ, for holy oil, for the solemnization of marriages, for the thirty masses \[*gregorianae*\], for the churching \[of women after childbirth\]; and when he compels them, refusing to hear confession unless the sick person first bequeaths something to the church.
