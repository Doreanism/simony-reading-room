From this discourse, and from the customs we openly see in the clergy, it can be recognized that there are few priests who have not been ordained simoniacally and who have not obtained their benefices simoniacally, and thus that these on the one side, and their bishops on the other side, have fallen into simony. And since simony is heresy, therefore if they examined themselves rightly, they would find that many of them are heretics; and there is no distinction except that those who are higher in spiritual offices are more abundant, more constant, and more thoroughgoing simonists and thus heretics; nor is there any doubt that simonists are heretics, as the learned\[20\] say. And Pope Pascal\[23\] says that “it is clear that simonists, as the foremost and highest heretics, must be condemned by all the faithful; and if, having been admonished, they do not desist, they must be suppressed by secular powers; for all sins compared to the heresy of simony are reckoned as nothing.” So says that pope.
