## CHAPTER II.

\[A\] Heresy has, at the first level, three roots, so that there are three kinds of heresy: apostasy, blasphemy, and simony. Apostasy is turning away from the law of God. Blasphemy is the reviling of divine power, and this occurs in three ways: First, when a person ascribes to God what does not belong to him — as if someone were to maintain that God is a liar, or that he can sin. The second way of blaspheming belongs to one who stubbornly diminishes God’s power in his mind and thus says or teaches that God cannot, whenever and wherever and whomever he wills, satisfy. The third way of blaspheming belongs to one who attributes to human power or to another creature what belongs to God alone.
