In this blasphemy also are those who say that the pope is an earthly god, such that he may do here on earth whatever he pleases and rule over all people as he wills; and those who say that he can establish laws contrary to the law of God, or command something honorable against the holy apostles. And they also say that no one in the world may speak against him, whatever he does. But blessed be the mighty Lord God forever, who permitted Peter to fall into great sin for our instruction, that we might know — since that first vicar of his, chosen, appointed, confirmed and established by him, sinned so grievously, and afterward, even after the sending of the Holy Spirit, erred again and was rebuked by St. Paul, as St. Paul says:\[10\] “I opposed Peter to his face, for he was to be blamed” — what pope, then, is elevated above St. Peter so as to be incapable of sinning here?
