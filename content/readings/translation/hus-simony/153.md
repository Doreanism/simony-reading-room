And since the merciful Lord God grants to the simple the knowledge of truth — to those who in humility keep His commandments — and conceals it from the wise of the world, Christ therefore praised the Father and confirmed this truth, that such are to be believed, saying: \[Matthew 11:25\] “I praise you, Father, Lord of heaven and earth, that you have hidden these things from the wise and prudent, and have revealed them to the little ones — \[that is, to the humble\] — and to those shut away from the world.” And would people not have had more reason to believe the twelve apostles — poor, simple, and shut away from the world, who went the way that Christ went — more than all the bishops, masters, priests, and scribes of the pagans and Jews? Therefore people must exercise this discernment: that whoever accords more closely with the law of Christ and with His life is more to be trusted, even were a thousand others \[against him\] who did not accord with His law and life.

By way of example: when people play at bowls, the jack is thrown first and then the bowls are rolled, and whoever bowls closest has won; and when it happens that they cannot distinguish whose bowl is nearest the jack, they take a measure and measure from the jack to the bowls, and the measure shows which bowl is closest. Something similar applies to Christ and His truth: Christ stands before us as the jack
