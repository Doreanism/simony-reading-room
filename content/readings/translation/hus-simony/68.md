Likewise, whoever wishes to enter an order in order to have carnal pleasure, to be guaranteed a bread-loaf \[i.e., to live without want\], to avoid labor, or to be held in greater esteem in the world than he would be without the order — such as to become an abbot, a provost, or some other official — is, in so entering the order, a simoniac, because he receives a spiritual dignity or a religious life in a disordered way, for non-spiritual gain.

### \[B\]

Therefore it is necessary for one who wishes to enter religious life to take counsel with the Holy Spirit, so that he may discern with what intent he wishes to enter; for if he goes in for the reasons just mentioned, he will fall into simony. He must also weigh what he wishes to do in the order, so as to be of benefit to it and to holy Church; and if he finds that, as a priest living in the apostolic manner and leading the people, and living in sobriety, he would be of greater benefit \[outside\], he ought not to enter the order — especially if he would be suited to the people in preaching the word of God, so as not to place the light of Holy Scripture under a bushel.

Now, being already in an order, members fall into simony in various ways — by obtaining churches for their order from the pope or from lords by money or other gifts. And the proof that they do this out of greed is this: they seek only those churches that have large revenues, but will not have poor ones.
