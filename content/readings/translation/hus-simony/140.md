The second thing that draws them away is their alliance with the laity: for they are bound together with them in worldly pleasure and favor, so that the laity enjoy their company and they flatter one another mutually. The third thing that repels the laity from this good spiritual remedy is worldly preoccupation with temporal affairs, so that they are more concerned with growing wealthy, with being honored by men, with playing, gambling, and providing worldly entertainment, than with faithfully serving the Lord God.

Therefore the third remedy, and the most immediate, is that the community should withhold revenues from those who openly traffic in simony and live wickedly, so that they cannot use them for trade; but the community would have to steel itself for a time against their summonses and their curses, by which they defend their wickedness. Let us beseech the merciful Lord God that He may deign in His power to dispose His Christendom, and by these means or whatever others He may see fit, to overthrow that simony!
