\[N\] And as for leasing, I do not know when it can be done fittingly, and this on both sides. The parish priest, if he truly cannot remain with his flock, should entrust it to a good priest. For since he is entrusted with souls, for which he must render account to the chief Shepherd, why does he not trust him with the sheep’s droppings \[i.e., the material revenues\]? And I do not know how he can fittingly lease it out! As for the one who leases, I do not know how he could fittingly do so otherwise than with this intention: that, seeing in the people a lack of instruction in the word of God, and seeing a lack of parish priests, and perceiving that he could be of benefit, he would take up the labor — but guarding himself very carefully lest greed draw him anywhere, or afterward ensnare him, and lest he give any occasion of offense to that people — perhaps he would not be called before Christ a hireling, but a shepherd; for he would be feeding Christ’s sheep with good example and with his word, guarding himself from sin in every direction, and being diligent for the salvation of that people.
