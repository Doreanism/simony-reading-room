## CHAPTER VIII.

\[A\] Having demonstrated the simony of the clergy, it is now fitting to touch upon the simony of the laity as well; for they too have been corrupted by simony in many ways: first and most crudely, those who, receiving gifts — whether money, goblets, horses, or other things — or having revenues or services pledged to themselves, give out bishoprics, provostships, canonries, or parish livings. These have King Jeroboam as their father, of whom Scripture says: \[1 Kings 12:31\] “Whosoever wished to fill the hand of Jeroboam, he was made a priest over the high places.” And Scripture immediately says that “for this cause the house of Jeroboam sinned, and was overthrown and wiped from the earth.” “House,” that is, his lineage and kingdom; for afterward it stands written \[1 Kings 14:1–18\] that Jeroboam sent his wife to the prophet named Ahijah, to ask whether his son would live and reign after him.
