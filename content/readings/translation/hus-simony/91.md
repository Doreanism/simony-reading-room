### \[E\]

And truly parish priests are very shameful in this: that they let their brethren, who share their labors, suffer want, when they ought to share \[the revenues\] together for their bodily needs; for in very truth, he who labors more earnestly with the people through grace, living well \[himself\], and benefits them, is more worthy of the revenues which those people give. And so there are perhaps many chaplains who are more worthy of the church’s revenues than their parish priests. And to this the Savior bears witness, saying: “Provide neither silver nor gold nor money for yourselves, for the laborer is worthy of his food.” And so a faithful and industrious priest is worthy of both clothing and food; and a parish priest who does not labor is unworthy to enjoy \[the revenues\], unless he worked before and can no longer do so, or has some other impediment that merits excuse. Therefore Saint Paul says to the citizens of Thessalonica: “You know how you ought to follow our example, that we were not disorderly among you, nor did we eat bread from anyone for nothing.” And then he says: “When we were with you, we commanded you this: that if any man would not work, neither should he eat.” — Oh, this will move the canons, parish priests, bishops, and other priests and prelates, who eat, gorge, guzzle, feed themselves richly, and profit nothing in the spiritual life!
