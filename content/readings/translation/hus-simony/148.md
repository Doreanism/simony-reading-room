And this was the practice of pagan kings, who always compelled all their subjects not to oppose their gods but to serve them. Therefore Saint Augustine says: \[Augustine, *Sermon* 302\] Mark well, beloved, what I am saying: that it befits Christian kings to protect in their times their holy mother the Church, from whom they were born, in peaceful tranquility. And a little later he says: King Nebuchadnezzar commanded, saying: Whoever shall speak blasphemy against the God of Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego shall perish, and his house shall be demolished! Behold now, how a pagan king commanded, under penalty of death, that no one should oppose the God of Israel who delivered those three young men from the fire — Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego — whom he had cast into the great fire because they refused to transgress God’s commandment by bowing down to the golden idol. \[Daniel 3\]

And since priests who commit simony and other manifest sins oppose and blaspheme God — as Saint Peter says in his prophecy, \[2 Peter 2:1–3\] “there will be among you false teachers,” and then he says, “and many will follow their fornication, through whom the way of truth will be blasphemed, and in covetousness they will make merchandise of you with fabricated words” — and the apostle Saint Jude says in his epistle \[Jude 1:7–8\] that “just as Sodom was kept for eternal fire, so too those who defile their flesh, despise dominion, and speak blasphemy against the divine majesty” — behold, since wicked priests so blaspheme God, the king must with his power, in the duty of avenging God’s honor, restrain these priests
