And to the same end princes and lords are likewise bound, since they have power from God to avenge the wrongs that people do to God; for otherwise they would be unfaithful servants of the highest King. For it is ordained: \[*Decretum Gratiani*, causa 23\] Whoever is able to stand against evildoers and trouble them and does not do so is nothing other than consenting to their wickedness; and he is not without the taint — that is, without the stain — of secret complicity who is unwilling to confront manifest wickedness.

### \[G\]

There are many other arguments, which I shall cut short, saying this: that if kings, princes, lords, being instructed in the law of God, would not allow priests to practice simony, and would themselves refrain from simony, then the Lord God would grant them peace in the land, honor and wealth, and thereafter the heavenly kingdom. O faithful kings, princes, lords, and knights, wake from the slumberous sleep with which the priests have lulled you, and drive the heresy of simony from your dominions; for otherwise without doubt there will be storm and true peace will not be — that peace which was given on earth to people of good will. And remember that the governance of the people has been entrusted to you by God according to His law; therefore defend them against practicing simony and other sins; do not allow the devil’s cunning to plunder the people, do not allow it to be carried out of your dominions to your own harm. O if you dare to stand against those who take cattle, how much more stand against those who, by secretly wheedling petty coins from the poor, are tearing out both their own souls and those of the poor!
