But observe the magnitude of the trafficking: he pays a grosch for a document covering ten people, and collects a grosch from each one, thus gaining nine groschen on a single grosch! And if he assesses a higher rate — two or three groschen from the wealthier — he gains still more, according to whatever cunning he can devise. And so he sells doves in the temple of God, making a den of thieves, as was demonstrated above, that those who sell doves are those who give money for the gift of the Holy Spirit, laying hands upon heads.

And his excuse, when he says: I was obliged to pay for a document — this incriminates him more than it excuses him; for it denounces him as guilty of Simon’s sin: having desired a faculty and having given money in order that he might lay hands on people to absolve them and enable them to receive remission of sins, and thus the Holy Spirit as well. And I do not know what that document is for, except that money may be taken on both sides. But it ought to be thus: that a parish priest with holy intent should say to the bishop: Priest! I have people who have strayed into great sins; give your blessing, that I may absolve them. And the bishop, having examined whether the priest or chaplain is learned, ought to say: God give the blessing! Freely you have received, freely give. And know, priest! if you demand money for confessions, I shall take vengeance on you according to the holy ordinance.
