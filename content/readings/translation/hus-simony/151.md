### \[I\]

But there is a question: since there are many simonists, as is well known from the earlier arguments and from present-day practice, and since the clergy have established for themselves that no one shall be called a simonist unless it is proved before them according to their own statutes — how then can anyone take vengeance on simonists, seeing that they are themselves the judges, and they have the power to judge whom they will, and by producing false witnesses they both imprison, curse, torture, and sometimes even burn — just as Annas and Caiaphas with their masters and priests murdered the innocent priest Jesus Christ, the true God, as a heretic, producing testimony and saying: \[John 19:7\] “We have a law, and by the law he ought to die!” And so those heretics, wrongly interpreting Scripture and bringing forward false witnesses, crucified Jesus. —

To this question I will say first of all to everyone in general, as Saint Paul says to Titus: \[Titus 3:10–11\] “A heretical man, after the first and second admonition, shun, knowing that he is subverted.” And the Lord Jesus says: \[Matthew 18:17\] “Let him be to you as a heathen and a manifest sinner.” And if we held to this ordinance of God, who would dare to practice simony or otherwise openly commit mortal sin? — Second, I say that princes and lords, being themselves free from simony, and the community likewise, should not admit simonists to office. And third, as was said before: if they refuse to cease from sin, let revenues be withheld from them.
