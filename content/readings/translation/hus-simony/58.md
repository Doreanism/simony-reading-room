As for the second excuse, the behavior of the bishop’s servants answers it; for sometimes they make their justifications \[for why payment is required\], sometimes they give admonishments, and the servants and scribes even take pledges, and those who have nothing or are unwilling to give they drive away regardless! Therefore they are very far from the pardon of St. Gregory, who extended pardon only to the case where someone gave, neither under agreement, nor under compulsion, nor having been asked, something for letters or for a robe — that is, for a stole. But, alas! already the bulls and letters for a bishopric, and the stole which the bishop wears when celebrating Mass, are sold far too dearly, so that instead of the bull one could buy a Bible, and instead of the stole one could buy cloaks for the twelve apostles — were they still living as bishops here! And already without bulls and without stoles they will not admit \[a candidate\] to a bishopric.

### \[G\]

There are also those suffragan bishops who excuse themselves, saying they would have nothing to live on and could not sustain themselves if they did not collect in this way from ordinations. But the first answer is: that since he is a deputy bishop or assistant, let the bishop provide for his necessities — since the bishop feeds others, pages and many \[servants\] who have nothing to do with the episcopal office.
