And the simony is not excused by the fact that he sometimes takes only a little; for just as the poor widow, as the Savior bears witness, gave more than all the others, having given two small copper coins, because she gave out of her great longing: so also a priest or scribe, taking a penny or two out of great longing, will be convicted of grievous simony. Therefore St. Gregory, the great pope, says: “Just as it is not fitting for a bishop to sell the laying on of hands, so also a servant or scribe ought not to sell his voice or his writing at the bishop’s ordination.” From these words you have it: that just as a bishop is a simonist if he ordains for money, so also is a scribe if he writes for money.

But someone says: Must the scribe write for everyone free of charge? Where is he to get his necessities? Here the answer is simple: that the bishop ought to provide for him; for it is for this purpose that he is endowed with revenues — so that, having the means to live with his necessary servants for the spiritual administration, and giving them their necessities, he may faithfully serve the holy Church, distributing spiritual gifts without money.

But there is an excuse put forward in the bishop’s defense, saying that this happens without his knowledge.
