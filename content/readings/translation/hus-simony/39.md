For the archbishopric of Prague, sureties stood on behalf of Puchník even after his death — Kbel and Malešic and others — and they were compelled to pay, though he had died without receiving any benefit from the bishopric or even entering upon it! And let it be noted that the pope does not exact \[payment\] beforehand, neither directly nor through intermediaries; but it was already established by Pope John XXII how much each bishop must give. For that pope was the first to devise and command that he be given the first-fruits of all benefices — that is, all that each \[holder\] can enjoy in the first year — and that avaricious pope amassed thereby a great store of treasure into his treasury.

Then, regarding the third point — that what the pope decrees, no one should speak against — a faithful Christian might concede this, were the pope to decree nothing evil. But if he decrees something evil, what faithful person would consent to it? Even his own heralds must concede \[its wrongness\], for they teach in their own schools that what one pope decrees, another annuls, and then others in turn reject those annulments. And what faithful person would say that it is a good papal decree that the word of God should not be preached in chapels, nor anywhere else except in parish churches and monasteries? Yet that decree is directly contrary to the decree of Christ. This decree was issued in a bull by Pope Alexander V, and shortly after issuing it he died. Against that bull I took a stand and appealed, so that the word of God might not be suppressed.
