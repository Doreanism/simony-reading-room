Their excuse is that they are masters of Holy Scripture, and especially doctors — that is, teachers — of papal law, and they suppose that on account of their mastership the Lord God will not hold their sin against them, so that, holding many benefices, they may nourish pride and pleasure, so that people may honor them in their estates, in the schools, and in the streets; and therefore they have great cloaks with white fur, with ermine, with sable, and with silk — long and wide — and all this so that they may be seen by men! Believe the truth of Christ, who says: “The masters and the scribes have sat in the seat of Moses; therefore whatever they tell you, observe and do; but do not do according to their works, for they speak and do not act. They bind heavy burdens and grievous to be borne — that is, such as they themselves cannot bear — and lay them upon men’s shoulders; but they themselves will not move them with so much as a finger. And all their works they do to be seen by men, for they make their phylacteries broad and their hems great, and love the chief seats at feasts and the first seats in the synagogues, and greetings in the marketplace, and to be called masters!”

Now you have the sins of the masters beautifully described. Understand that the Savior says: “The masters have sat in the seat of Moses.” When he says “they have sat,” he does not mean those who lived before him; rather, Christ means those who come after him.
