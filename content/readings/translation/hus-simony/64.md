### \[L\]

And so now you have it: how a bishop can be a simonist — first by entering the bishopric, second by abiding in it; and now, thirdly, he can be so by departing from it, and that in two ways. The first is that he surrenders it to a friend or to another for money, and so having entered in a disordered manner, he departs in a disordered manner. The second is that he entered the bishopric wickedly, does not repent, and thus dies \[in that state\]. And a great sign of this is when, knowing within himself that he entered wrongly, he does not repent of it, and he is opposed to the end to those who speak against such simony. And concerning this, St. Gregory writes as follows: “If anyone is not distinguished by holy conduct, has not been called by the clergy and the people, has not been pressed by supplication, and — being already defiled by some sin — shamelessly takes up the priesthood of Christ, \[driven\] by a base desire of the heart, or by impure supplications of the mouth, or by tumult, or by bodily service, or by a deceitful gift; not for the gaining of souls, but clothed in the desire for vain glory, and during his life does not willingly relinquish the episcopal or priestly dignity, and death does not find him in harsh penitence — he will without doubt perish forever.”
