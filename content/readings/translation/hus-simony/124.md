\[E\] Then by counsel a person is a participant in simony — whoever gives advice to one who wishes to purchase something unlawfully, or to one who wishes to sell. And there are a great many such persons at the papal court, who devise for the pope and for those who seek benefices all manner of clever tricks, all so that the pope and they may collect more money, and so that the purchasers may pay more; and so it comes about that one benefice will be sold several times. And to set down all those devices — only as many as I myself know of — would take far too long. But this much I used to say to those who had been cheated: that if I were to want to petition the pope for a benefice, I would put it thus in the bull that so the Holy Father wishes it, that no one could devise anything to surpass this grant. In this form of participation stand the lawyers; and who else could one enumerate?
