### \[F\]

Then by confirmation or by confirming simony all are participants who assent to it, saying: It is well done! And especially guilty of this are the powerful kings, princes, and lords, the bishops and other men of high rank, who ought to abolish it — and instead they confirm it or take no heed, as if it were none of their concern! And what excuse can they make before God, who gave them power and elevation above others so that they should govern them and not permit them to be led into wickedness, as far as they are able? Nor do they have any excuse, as I will show later, when they say they take no heed of what the clergy does. For I know that if someone were to slander a secular prince, or steal from him, or lie with a woman, he would not tolerate it — he would take vengeance at once, if he could; and yet he will not trouble himself that a clergyman commits a greater sin! Is simony not a greater sin than theft, or fornication, or slandering a secular prince, inasmuch as through simony a man especially dishonors the Holy Spirit, by selling or buying his gifts, and thus treating the servants of the Holy Spirit as slaves? Therefore it is written that simony is a more grievous heresy than that of Macedonius, who said that the Holy Spirit was a servant of the Father and the Son; and these simonists in practice bring it about that the Holy Spirit serves a creature!
