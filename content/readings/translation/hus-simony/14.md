Likewise, a preacher or master may rightly exchange teaching for bodily necessities; for St. Paul says:\[19\] “Since we give you spiritual things, it is no great matter if we take your earthly things.” Therefore, that you may know that simony has its root or nest in wicked will, it was said that simony is wicked consent to the exchange. From this you also understand that simony is not only when the transaction has taken place, or when there is a silent exchange of a spiritual thing for a non-spiritual thing, but when a person has a disordered will toward such an exchange; just as fornication is first a mortal sin in the soul, and afterward in the act.

### \[C\]

But here there is one difficulty in understanding what a spiritual thing is, since every person who sins mortally is a simonist, for he sells his soul to the devil and the dignity of a human being, which is a spiritual thing. Therefore know that simony specifically denotes the disordered exchange among people according to the spiritual office of the house of God.
