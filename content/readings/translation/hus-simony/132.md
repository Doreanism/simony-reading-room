## CHAPTER X.

\[A\] It remains yet to set forth concerning this sin how one might best guard against it. And there is no more fitting path than that elections to bishoprics or parishes should be made according to God’s will; for so the apostles proceeded when, having no revelation, they were to receive someone in the bishopric in place of Judas. To this purpose tends the word of Saint Jerome, who says: 235) “Since so great and such a Moses is not permitted to choose a prince of the people by his own deliberation, nor to appoint a successor after himself, who then is he who would dare — from among the people, who are often stirred by clamor toward pride or gain — or even from among the priests, who would judge himself worthy of this, except the one to whom, while he prays and beseeches God, it has been revealed by God?
