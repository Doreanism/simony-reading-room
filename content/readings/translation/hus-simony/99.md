The fifth custom is that they keep fine horses, outfit them with proud trappings, and keep many hunting dogs as well, with which they squander the alms of the poor.

Even a very simple person can recognize that the Savior did not leave these five vain customs to priests, and that they are not at all helpful to the priestly office — rather, they are the devil’s bonds by which he binds the priests and drives them toward pride, leading them like the blind to damnation. And so these spiritual servants of the devil are so struck with spiritual blindness that when asked: Why do you act in this way? they answer that the world — or worldly honor — demands it. And since the devil is far superior to that worldly honor, it would be a somewhat more fitting and truthful answer if they said that the devil demands it. Therefore whoever gives such an answer at once recognizes in his folly that he has been overcome by the devil — so much so that he invokes the world in defense of his sin, the world through which the devil, using evil to his advantage, has overcome him; for we know that the devil fights against us through the world and the flesh. Therefore just as a man has no excuse for fornication in saying that the flesh desires it, so he has no excuse in saying that the world desires it. For would a similar excuse serve a knight before his king, if he had let himself be captured by the king’s enemy of his own free will, and the king asked him: Why did you let yourself be captured? and he answered that the enemy wished it so?
