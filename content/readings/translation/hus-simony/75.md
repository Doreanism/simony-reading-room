And it is not I alone who say this, but St. Bernard — and they themselves say it when they wish to lure someone into the order, as they lure: children, boys, and little girls who have no judgment yet. Oh, if only they would remember what Pope Boniface says, declaring: “We never read that the Lord’s disciples — that is, Christ’s apostles — or those who were converted to the faith by their preaching, ever enticed anyone toward the faith of God by any gift, except that someone might give food in common to some of the poor; for to no one, of whatever condition he might have been, was bodily food denied.”

Behold, now you know that with wealth and bodily comforts the apostles and other saints did not entice anyone, nor summon \[anyone\] to the faith of Christ — as indeed Christ himself did not summon \[people with wealth\], but rather called them to suffering and to poverty. To suffering, saying: “If anyone wishes to come after me, let him take up his cross each day and follow me.” And calling to poverty, he said: “Unless one renounces all his possessions, he cannot be my disciple.” Therefore, just as it is not fitting to enter the faith or an order for the sake of wealth and pleasure, nor for gifts, so likewise it is not fitting to entice anyone into the faith or into an order with gifts, wealth, and pleasures. Therefore I confess my own evil desire: that when I was a student, I had a mind to become a priest quickly, so that I might thus have a good life and clothing, and be held in esteem by people; but I recognized that evil desire when I came to understand Scripture.
