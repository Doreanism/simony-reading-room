Therefore whoever is a hireling not for human salvation and his own, but for his own greed, is a thief and a robber. He has no care for the sheep, for they are not his concern. One robber has leased the sheep to another: this one holds them, milks them, and hands them over to that one, who feeds himself by plunder without labor! O that one of them might take thought, as that thief on the cross \[did\], that Christ without fault of his own redeemed the sheep, and let him grieve, saying: We two are both guilty, for we have so purchased these sheep of Christ!

But one says: I must lease out the church, I cannot make a living from it. O fool! How then shall the hireling make a living from it? Still more plunder will fill both bellies! Again he says: I cannot sit at my church, I must be at another benefice. O miser! You cannot do enough for one, and yet you dare to hold two. Again you say: I must attend court. And I say that by this you intend to serve the devil; if you are a shepherd, tend the sheep and leave off attending court! Again you say: I have a bad patron. And I say: Perhaps the patron is good and you are bad; therefore leave off your wickedness, and settle things with the patron and the sheep. But if the patron is bad, let your goodness overcome his badness; and if you still cannot overcome it, seek help from God and from good people. But if nothing avails — perhaps the people are not worthy of you, or you of them — then resign and go elsewhere.
