And they have this excuse: “The smith has tongs so that he won’t get burned.” But to this a plain man will answer, saying: “The devil, leaving the faggots, grabbed hold of the smith!”

They have a second defense from the \[legal\] maxim: “He who acts through another is as if he acted himself.” Know, however, that whoever does something good through another, without abandoning his own office out of vanity, levity, or sloth — so that he adds his own diligence in order to live well according to his own station, and so he lives, and sometimes being unable to suffice in like manner by himself, he appoints another — such a one has a worthy helper. Likewise in evil: whoever prepares another to sin through assistance, command, or negligence — by failing to prevent it — is guilty as if he had done it himself.

And so Scripture says that the Jews crucified Jesus, that Pilate scourged \[him\], and that King Herod slaughtered the children \[of Bethlehem\]. And by this argument such men show that, not fulfilling their office and having others do the work in their place, they will receive no reward for another’s labor. Therefore St. Bernard says: “Whoever has a deputy in toil will have a deputy in reward.” Why? Because Scripture says that each man will receive reward according to the labor he had in his own life. Therefore the Savior speaks rightly \[Matthew 20\], saying that the lord of the vineyard commanded the laborers to be called and each to be given \[wages\] for the labor he had in the vineyard.
