who preach for money and \[live\] for money — like those mountebanks who give indulgences for money, of whom the Savior prophesied, saying: “False prophets shall arise and shall lead many astray.” And St. Peter also says: “There were false prophets among the people, even as there will be false teachers among you, who shall bring in sects of perdition.” And then he says that they deny him who redeemed them, bringing upon themselves swift destruction. “And many will follow their licentiousness, by reason of whom the way of truth shall be reviled; and through covetousness with feigned words they will make merchandise of you, whose judgment of old does not linger, and their damnation does not sleep.” And then he says: “Having hearts trained in covetousness, children of cursing, forsaking the right way, they have gone astray, following the way of Balaam, who loved the wages of unrighteousness.”

Thus St. Peter describes the sons of Balaam, and there are many of them. Recognize them: first, he says they will be liars; second, that they will lead companies after them, that they will be false teachers; third, that they deny him who bought them; fourth, that they will be unchaste and many will follow their licentiousness; fifth, that from them the way of truth will be reviled; sixth, that with invented speeches in covetousness they will traffic among people and profit from them, having hearts trained in covetousness; seventh, that they have forsaken the right way and are walking the way of Balaam; eighth, he grants them indulgences, saying that they are children of cursing, and that their judgment does not linger and their damnation does not sleep — that is, it does not tarry.
