## CHAPTER III.

\[A\] For a fuller understanding of simony it is fitting to show further where it had its origin. We have in Scripture that this heresy existed both in the Old Testament and in the New. And although there are two fathers of this sin — one in the Old Testament, called Gehazi, and another in the New, called Simon — the first being he who accepted gifts for the healing of the leper Naaman, the second being he who offered money to the apostles, wishing to possess the power to lay hands on people so that they might receive the Holy Spirit — nevertheless I wish to make the simonists more plainly known, those who, like sons with wicked fathers before them, put on their fathers’ shoes.

\[B\] Know that just as from Simon they are called Simonians or Simonites, so from Gehazi they are called Gehazites, from Balaam Balaamites, from Jeroboam Jeroboamites, and from Judas Judaites.
