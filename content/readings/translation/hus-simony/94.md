And the Germans, having introduced an evil custom, refuse to depart from it at all, saying: Ist unze recht — that is: This is our right. And so people have settled on this, that they will not depart from these customs at all, and defend them to the death; and they defend their customs more than the plain commandment of the Lord God.

But faithful Christians guard themselves against all customs that are contrary to the customs of their Savior and contrary to his counsel or his commandment; and if they see any custom that is contrary to truth, they immediately reject it. Therefore Saint Augustine says: “Vainly do some who are overcome by unreasoning argument set custom before us, as though custom were greater than truth, or as though in spiritual matters one ought not to follow what the Holy Spirit has made manifest as better.” The saint means that people in vain defend themselves by evil custom, as though it were worthier than truth. Yet reason prevails: that truth must overcome custom. And again: when something good is done in ecclesiastical matters and the Holy Spirit reveals something better, we must immediately embrace it. Saint Augustine says again: We must follow truth more than custom, for reason and truth always drive out custom.
