Of these thieves and robbers the Savior himself says: “As many as have come, all of them are thieves and robbers!”

And that we must guard against this participation, Saint John writes in his Revelation, saying: “I saw an angel descending from heaven, having great power, and the earth was illumined by his glory. And he cried out with might, saying: Fallen is great Babylon, and it has become the habitation of devils, and the hold of every unclean spirit, and the hold of every unclean and abominable bird. For all nations have drunk of the wine of the wrath of her fornication — that is, of Babylon — and the kings of the earth have committed fornication with her, and the merchants of the earth have grown rich through the power of her delights.” — Babylon, the great city, is the assembly of wicked men; for Babylon is interpreted as disgrace and transfer: for all those wicked ones have been disgraced and transferred — from the grace of God into wrath, and from holy living into cursedness. The great angel who descended from heaven with great power, by whom the earth was illumined, is the gracious Christ.
