### \[D\]

Therefore, although St. Paul shows the Corinthians, and likewise other good Christians, that the whole world is theirs, yet he did not on that account, being more worthy than they, exalt himself so as to wish to rule over their possessions; rather, he was unwilling to take their wealth, and he preached to them, but worked with his own hands, so as to give no cause for scandal to his preaching, as he himself says. And when he says that the whole world is theirs, hear how he says it: “Let no one boast in men; for whether Paul, or Apollos, or Peter — all things are yours, whether death or the world, whether life or things present or things to come: verily all things are yours, and you are Christ’s, and Christ is God’s.”

Behold, now you have it that the whole world belongs to a righteous man, and so also to popes, if any one of them is ever righteous.
