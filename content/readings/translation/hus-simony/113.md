### \[F\]

And since this right of presentation is a spiritual power, it is therefore not fitting to sell it. It is called spiritual also because it pertains to the spiritual order — that is, to the ordinance for the salvation of souls — and also because in itself it is not a material thing. But crude people, who think that the church, altar, or chapel is the right of presentation, therefore buy and sell the right of presentation as \[if it were\] an ox or a cow, unworthily, for material gain, or for honor, or so that they may present \[benefices\] to their friends or servants. Know this: when you say “I hold the right of presentation to a church,” you hold the power to give the church to a good priest and to govern it, so that, together with the priest and the people, it may stand well to the glory of God. This power is held also by children and other relatives as an inheritance, and sometimes by bequest, or by transfer, so that one gives it to another.
