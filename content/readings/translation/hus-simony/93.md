Just as the men of Sodom, when the holy man told them not to sin with the unspeakable sin, \[answered\] saying: He came to dwell among us — you came to us, not to give us laws! And alas, fornication is now excused with the words that it is natural; but this is a lie. For no sin, nor any wickedness contrary to virtue, is natural or inborn, even though by his nature one man may be more inclined to one sin than another. And so generally people are inclined to fornication — one more than another; likewise to wrath.

### \[G\]

Therefore keep this well in mind: that neither custom, nor nature, nor natural inclination can excuse sin so that it ceases to be sin — though nature and natural inclination may in one man, more than in another, make sin heavier or somewhat lighter. Thus one man has a heart readier than another, and he flares up, and the other will also grow angry; therefore the one who flares up so quickly sins more lightly, all other things being equal. The same holds for fornication. One man has a body very lustful by nature, and another not so much; therefore the first, when he falls, may sin more lightly — but still, in falling, he sins mortally through fornication.
