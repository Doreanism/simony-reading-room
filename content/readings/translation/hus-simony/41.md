### \[F\]

Know finally that the law of God, the law of nature, and the decrees of the saints — which are grounded in Holy Scripture — bind papal power: the law of God, so that \[the pope\] does nothing contrary to it, and thus dispenses spiritual things freely, as the apostles did; the law of nature, which is the knowledge implanted in rational creatures by which they are to govern themselves, so that \[the pope\] commits nothing unworthy; and the decrees of the saints, who, understanding Holy Scripture, founded \[their decrees\] upon it. May God grant that the pope — whether the present one or those who come after — bestow true Christian teaching freely for God’s sake, solely for the glory of God and for the salvation of the people, and not entangle himself in wars, and not grant benefices for money to the unworthy, and follow Christ! But it seems to me that it will not be soon that any pope walks Christ’s path in humility, poverty, and labor, until Christ’s Scripture is \[fully\] fulfilled; for the wealth of this world has miserably blocked the path of Christ and bred all manner of disorder in the clergy, on account of which simony, greed, and strife abound. Therefore simony will not be driven from the holy Church so long as wealth and lordship are not abandoned by the clergy.

And since greed ever increases, especially among the clergy, it is therefore necessary for the faithful to take heed, lest they fall into greed and simony, and to be vigilant against the devil’s cunning arguments by which he justifies simony and greed, so that they might refute them.
