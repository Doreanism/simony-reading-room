But now the pope gives one man on account of money, not knowing who he is or what 160) he is; a king or prince gives another who is unworthy; the canons elect another for gifts or for other reasons, and not straightforwardly for the praise of God; likewise lords and squires and townspeople elect not according to the teaching of Scripture, nor according to sincere desire. Therefore there is disorder in Christendom, many scoundrels in the clergy, and Christendom is filled with all manner of wickedness; and so Antichrist has so firmly established his will in these elections, appointments, and simoniacal transactions that it surpasses human power to be able to rebuke and drive out that wickedness.

\[D\] Nevertheless, I observe three things that might serve as remedies against that wickedness, should the Lord God show his grace in them: The first remedy would be if the Lord God gave some pope who would abolish all those simonies according to Scripture and according to the ordinances of the holy bishops. But where is such a one to be found? Truly it would be a great wonder if such a pope were to appear now; and I know well that his apostles would not allow him to live long!
