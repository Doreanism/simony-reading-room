These books I have written, knowing that I shall receive neither praise, nor favor, nor any bodily benefit from greedy priests or from other worldly persons; for I do not seek these things from them, but from God a reward and their salvation. And if their reproach or affliction should befall me, I have resolved that it is better to suffer death for the truth than to receive temporal reward for flattery. And Saint Paul also says: \[Galatians 1:10\] “If I were pleasing men, I should not be the servant of God” — understand: if I were pleasing men by flattery, I should not be the servant of God — therefore I guard against flattery, lest I should lose both them and myself and corrupt them by flattery. Plainly and simply have I set forth my speech, to see whether I might in some measure strike down and root out simony. Toward that end may the merciful Savior be pleased to help! In His year one thousand four hundred and thirteen, on the day of His presentation in the temple by the Virgin Mary, of His being received into the arms of Simeon — which day the Czechs call Hromnice \[Candlemas\] — \[these books were completed\].

Whosoever shall read \[this\], mark that my intent is not that the good should be summoned or compelled, but that those who are \[good\] should guard themselves from wickedness, and that the wicked should repent. Amen.
