Perhaps someone else says: The pope must have much money, in order to compel the disobedient and rebellious by force to obedience; he must therefore obtain money however he can, and there is no more fitting means than to receive it for benefices, since people give willingly; and also that he may grant indulgences, so that people, by giving money, might purchase for themselves the heavenly kingdom and assist \[the effort\] against evil men. — To this argument, the poor life of Christ and the holy apostles gives the answer, who were not permitted to hold much money, nor wished to drive the disobedient and rebellious to obedience by force. Behold the Savior, who with a single word could have struck down the disobedient and the rebellious and turned them to himself, when they came against him with swords, clubs, and other weapons, humbly said to them: “As against a thief you have come out with swords and clubs, and I was daily among you, teaching in the temple, and you did not seize me.” And again he said to them: “Whom do you seek? And they answered him: Jesus of Nazareth. And he said to them: I am he; and since you seek me, let these others go.” Peter likewise, being in Rome, did not press the disobedient to the faith by force, but by the preaching of God’s word. So too did the other apostles, who sealed the holy Church with their own blood.
