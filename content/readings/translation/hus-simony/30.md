## CHAPTER IV.

### \[A\]

Let us examine whether a pope can be a simoniac. It seems he cannot, for he is the lord of the whole world, to whom it belongs to take as he pleases and to act as he pleases; and also because he is the most holy father, whom sin cannot touch. Know, then, that many popes have been heretics and otherwise wicked, and have been deposed from the papacy — and it would take long to write them all down. Therefore, have no doubt that a pope can indeed be a simoniac. And whoever holds that a pope cannot commit simony or otherwise sin mortally, would be placing him above Peter and the other apostles. As for the argument that he is the lord of the whole world, to whom it belongs to take as he pleases and to act as he pleases: the answer is that there is only one Lord of the whole world who cannot sin, to whom it belongs to rule the world and act as he pleases, and that Lord is almighty God.
