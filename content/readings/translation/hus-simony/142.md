Second, I say: since every king has power from God over his kingdom to govern the entire realm in truth and justice, and since priests are in the kingdom, the king is therefore obliged to govern them in truth and justice; and he would not be governing them in truth and justice if he allowed them to live in manifest wickedness against the supreme King, like a negligent servant, thereby failing to discharge his royal office faithfully. The proof of this argument is the bull of Saint Peter, \[1 Peter 2:13–14\] in which he commands that all should be subject to the king as to the supreme ruler. And why? Because kings and other lords are sent by God to punish the wicked and to honor the good. Therefore Saint Peter says: “Submit yourselves to every human institution for the sake of God: to the king as supreme, and to governors — \[that is, to dukes\] — as sent by him — \[that is, by God\] — for the punishment of evildoers and the praise of those who do well.” — Behold, now you see that not only to the king, but also to dukes and to every man, are people to be subject for God’s sake — either by obeying them in good things, or by suffering at their hands for the sins that
