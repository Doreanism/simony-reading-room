In truth I have heard in the schools such words from masters concerning humility, patience, poverty, and fortitude and other virtues, that they excused themselves so diligently and forcefully, as if it could not be better, and as if they had fulfilled all those virtues; and yet in their deeds I found none of those virtues — but pride, avarice, impatience, and cowardice in abundance! And as the dear Christ has described, they lay great burdens upon men, issuing their statutes, pressing for the first dignities in the ecclesiastical order, and when men do not bow to them as to gods, they grow angry; and when they are not seated at the first place at the table, they puff themselves up strangely, and they quarrel greatly about the first place in the schools. I know that one master, Mařík the monk, obtained from the pope a bull that he should be given precedence over the other masters; and \[the pope\] commanded in his bulls — that is, in his letters — that they should give him precedence under penalty of forfeiture of benefices and priesthood and all dignities, and under anathema — yet he could not obtain their assent! They wickedly refused out of pride, and he wickedly sought it; and so pride was fulfilled on both sides. For the Savior says that they love the first seats in the synagogues, and make their hems broad on their gowns, their cloaks and their tabards and their hoods.
