Judas was therefore a worse heretic, saying to the Lord Jesus: “Hail, Master!” when he betrayed him, than Peter, who swore and cursed, saying that he did not know that man whom they meant.\[5\]

From this argument you understand that everyone who obstinately commits mortal sin is a heretic, because he obstinately holds error contrary to Holy Scripture — first against the article “I believe in God,” and if he is a fornicator, then against the Scripture “You shall not commit fornication,” and if he holds any other mortal sin, he stands immediately against some Scripture. O Lord, you know how many are in deed heretics who pass for true Christians and call other good people heretics!

Furthermore, know from this argument that, were there no Holy Scripture, there would be no heresy, just as there would be no night had day not preceded night. And further, just as a person cannot recognize heresy unless he knows the Holy Scripture against which the heresy stands, \[D\] so too no one ought to call his neighbor a heretic unless he knows the Holy Scripture against which that person speaks or acts obstinately; and since he does not know that person’s intention, he ought not rashly — *temerarie*\[6\] — call him a heretic.

Moreover, from this argument you understand that everyone who holds error obstinately against Holy Scripture, whether by word or by deed, is a heretic.
