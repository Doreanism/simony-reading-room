## CHAPTER VI.

### \[A\]

How members of religious orders \[zákonníci\] may become simonians — by entering an order, by remaining in it, and the third way by departing from it. First, in entering: both the one who receives \[a candidate\] and the one who is received \[may sin\]. The former, receiving someone for money, for carnal favor, or for some other non-spiritual reason, already has an evil will, and thereby sells the religious life, which ought to be a spiritual matter. And the same applies to admission into an order by agreement involving money, or by custom of giving food, or clothing, or a ring, or a silver goblet, or a bolt of cloth. Therefore, so that members of religious orders might avoid this, Pope Urban commands that no abbot dare demand money or receive it from those who wish to enter the order. For he says: the old law is to be followed, so that no one takes anything — they have established this as a law. And there are many other ordinances and arguments, but for the sake of brevity I will leave them aside. But members of religious orders must take heed — those who eagerly hasten toward higher office
