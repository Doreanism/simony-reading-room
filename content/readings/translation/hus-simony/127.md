who in simony is the heretic above heretics, or the prince of heresy: the one who commits simony, and who defends those who commit simony with him. And under these are his lesser ones who defend him; and there are very many of them. And for better understanding, take this example: that there was a certain city of some king, which he himself had established, and afterward purchased back at the cost of his own life by fighting against enemies; then whoever, without the will of that king, surrendered that city to someone for money — and the one who received it for money would be guilty, and the one who brokered the transaction between them, and the secretary of the one who surrendered the city, and the secretary of the one who received it, and the messenger of the one and the messenger of the other, and the surety on both sides, and the negotiator on both sides, and the one who did not cry out against both or against one of them when he could, and the one who defended both or one of them when he could, and the one who advised one or both to do it, and the one who lent money for it, and the one who provided the paper for the documents, and the one who drew up the letters, and the community that submitted voluntarily, and the herald who did not cry out against it, and everyone who would gladly have contributed to helping that city be given to someone contrary to the will of that king — so it is in simony, when a bishop wishes and is installed in simoniacal fashion into a city — that is, into a people — whom the Savior himself created, and fighting against the devil at the cost of his own life, even unto death on the cross, has freed.
