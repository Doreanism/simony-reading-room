For St. John writes that it was said to him: “To the angel — that is, to the bishop — of the church of Pergamum, write: Thus says he who has the sharp two-edged sword: I know where thou dwellest, even where Satan’s seat is, and thou holdest my name and hast not denied my faith. And further he says: But I have a few things against thee, because thou hast there those who hold the doctrine of Balaam, who taught Balak to put a stumbling block before the sons of Israel, to eat and to commit fornication. Repent likewise; or else I will come unto thee quickly and will fight against them with the sword of my mouth.”

### \[H\]

O Lord! With a sword sharp on both sides thou wishest to fight — with the sword of thy mouth against those Balaamites? They fear not the sword of thy word; they preach boldly and lyingly and commit fornication! And there is no bishop, thine angel, to refuse to tolerate them; for the bishops themselves are fornicators! But thou knowest when thou wilt fight against them with the sharp sword, when thou drawest it against them, saying: “Depart, ye cursed, into everlasting fire!” Thou didst lay a curse upon Gehazi and his lineage through Elisha thy servant, that leprosy should cleave to him for ever. Thou didst give Judas and his progeny a blessing through David, saying: “Set a sinner over him, and let the devil stand at his right hand. When he is judged, let him go out condemned, and let his prayer be turned to sin. Let his days be few, and let another take his office.
