But let faithful Christians know this: that every bishop and parish priest and other prelates must themselves render an account for the sheep of whose pastors they called themselves; and since they must do so, then — if they are to be saved and be excused — they must themselves feed the sheep. Therefore those who do not feed them themselves have a greeting from God, who says: \[Ezekiel 34\] “Woe to the shepherds who feed themselves and do not feed my sheep!” And how is that a good shepherd whose sheep neither hear him nor see him, whom he does not know, and whom he does not feed? For the shepherd above all shepherds says \[John 10\] that “the good shepherd knows his sheep, and the sheep hear his voice, and he goes before them and leads them to pasture, and they follow him.” And he also said three times to Peter \[John 21\]: “Do you love me? Feed my sheep, feed my sheep, feed my sheep!”

### \[I\]

Feed \[them\] first by good example; feed \[them\] second by my word; feed \[them\] third, when you have the means, with bodily sustenance. And how do those shepherds feed, who, living wickedly, do not teach, and do not even feed the sheep with bodily sustenance — but rather shear them? Even a peasant can judge that one who never himself tends the sheep cannot be called a shepherd of sheep.
