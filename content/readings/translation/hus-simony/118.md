## CHAPTER IX

\[A\] So that people may guard themselves against simony on every side, we must also know that everyone who consents to simony sins thereby and is a participant in simony. This consent takes place in six ways: by assistance, by defense, by counsel, by confirmation, by failure to help, and by failure to punish. One is guilty of simony by assistance — by interceding, by brokering deals among simonists, or by providing occasion in other ways, such as: by letter, by messenger, by lending money, by supporting a case in court, and by other such means. The second way is by defense — that is, by defending simony.
