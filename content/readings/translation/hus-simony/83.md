## CHAPTER VII.

### \[A\] How parish priests and chaplains likewise sin through simony

can be discerned from what has been said before and from their deeds. For one may be a simoniac in entering a parish, as was said earlier: when one enters a parish for worldly honor, for wealth, or for bodily pleasure, and perhaps also being unworthy on account of other sins and for want of learning to govern the people; and likewise, if one gives money or any gifts, or served \[a patron\] for that purpose, or obtains \[the living\] by force, or receives it through carnal favor — entering thus, one is tainted by simony, for one has a wicked will to exchange a non-spiritual thing for a spiritual one, and that is simony. If one has given money to the pope, to a bishop, or to another person, then the simony is very manifest; for one has given money, just as Simon offered \[money\], in order to have the power of laying hands on people — as in confession, in baptism, in the sacrament of \[confirmation by\] the laying on of hands, for these belong to the parish priest; and thus one is a simoniac.
