And concerning this gross monetary simony he speaks thus: If a priest has obtained a church by money, let him be stripped not only of the church but also of his priesthood. O Saint Gregory! Strip them now of their churches and rob them of their priesthood — and few parish priests will remain!

### \[B\]

For though there are some who have not given money to the pope and to patrons, that is to say to lords of patronage, yet they have given \[money\] for confirmation to bishops or their officials; and \[the bishops\] are unwilling to give them the *cura animarum*, that is to say spiritual authority over souls, unless they give money. Is there not a spiritual matter in this transaction, since \[the bishop\] refuses to grant the power to baptize, to hear confession, to preach, and to lead the people out of their sins, until he gives money? A remarkable thing! The Lord gives a priest, without payment, the right to enjoy bodily alms, that living by them he may lead the people to salvation; and \[the bishop\] is unwilling to give freely the spiritual power by which he might benefit his neighbors in their souls!
