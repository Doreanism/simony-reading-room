many there are — of rulers, masters, priests, and others — who, fearing the curse, dare not confess Christ’s truth and thus Christ himself; and how many there are who, fearing to lose their miserable goods, dare not confess the truth; and above all those who, fearing to surrender their bodily life unto death, flee from the truth!

These three fears — the first, to lose human praise and favor; the second, to lose one’s goods; the third, to lose bodily life — drive men who are lacking in fortitude away from confessing the Savior Jesus Christ, who has promised \[to reward them\] both for honor, and for goods, and for life. For honor, saying: “Whoever confesses me and my words before this adulterous and sinful generation, the Son of Man also will confess him when he comes in the glory of his Father with his angels.” Then for goods he promised under an oath, saying: “Truly I say to you, there is no one who has left house, or brothers, or sisters, or father, or mother, or children, or lands for my sake and for the Gospel, who shall not receive a hundredfold!” And then for life he promises, saying: “And in the age to come, eternal life.” And elsewhere he says: “Whoever loses his life for my sake and for the Gospel, I will save it.” How? In that he will live forever in joy; for he says: “Whoever hates his life — that is, who does not yield to the will of the flesh — in this world, he shall preserve it unto eternal life.” O woe to us! How miserable we are, that we do not value infinite praise more than the temporal, wretched, and fleeting, and that we do not value eternal goods more
