And this the apostle also commands at the appointment of a priest, saying: 239) that “he must have a good report of them which are without.” But I see something more in this, namely that it is said that Moses assembled the whole congregation — meaning, understanding it spiritually, to gather all the powers of the soul together and unite the virtues, so that in the priest who is to be ordained all the powers of the soul may be wakeful and full, and that in him there may be no lack of wisdom, understanding, and discernment, but that the full abundance of senses may be in him, and the whole gathering of holy thoughts, so that he, laying up in the mystery of his heart what a bishop is, what anointing is, what vestments of the mass are, may be able to perceive \[them rightly\].” This all Saint Jerome says.235)

From this holy word a man may perceive what great perversion there is today and in our times in the appointment of bishops. For formerly the custom was that either the dear Lord God gave a bishop by revelation, or the people who were to be led elected for themselves a bishop or leader; but now the power of the people to request and elect for themselves a worthy priest has been taken away, and the path has been opened for the greedy and otherwise wicked to buy themselves men at the pope as though they were cattle, for their bodily profit!
