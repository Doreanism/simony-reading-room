## CHAPTER V.

### \[A\]

It is now fitting to examine how a bishop may fall into simony. Know that he may be a simoniac in entering a bishopric, second, in residing in it, and third, in departing from it. First, in entering: he may be a simoniac whenever he receives a bishopric primarily for temporal gain, or for worldly glory, or for bodily pleasure, rather than for the meriting of blessedness, for the glory of God, for the salvation of his own soul, and for the souls of his neighbors — that he might lead them to God by good example and by the word of God. And if he enters through gifts, or through service, or through carnal favor, then simony has already laid hold of him, for he already has a disordered will toward exchange. And truly, one who enters a bishopric in this way has made a shameful exchange, having given his soul to the devil for nothing — that is, for sin.
