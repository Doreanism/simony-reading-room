Such were those recently who were lying, unchaste, and covetous, who by their wicked deeds denied Christ, who mocked the true way of Christ, who robbed the people with false indulgences, devising wondrous speeches and absolutions, granting indulgences from all sins and from all punishments; and masters confirmed it for them, defending them and writing that the pope may justly wage war and grant indulgences from sins and from punishments. And so those, having masters to support them, plundered the people all the more boldly with their lying words, and lied as they pleased. Yet the gracious Lord God gave to good priests the Holy Spirit, so that they preached boldly against those liars, and to faithful laypeople, so that they bravely risked their lives and offered their three lives to God: Martin, Jan, and Stašek, who were beheaded in Prague for renouncing those lying sermons, and others were torn, flogged, and beaten with clubs at the Prague church by the choral clerks, and others were cursed, dishonored, and imprisoned. Praise be to thee, dear Christ, that thou still givest thy faithful such gifts, that they may confess thy truth.
