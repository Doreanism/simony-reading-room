For since he is bound to feed them by good example, by the word of God, and by bodily sustenance when he has the means — and this is his burden, as when he receives the priesthood and the office declares this to him, and sometimes he swears an oath upon it:

### \[K\]

therefore if he does not bear that burden, woe to him — and especially to him who lives wickedly and abandons the sheep of Christ, caring nothing for them, or who installs a manifestly wicked or incompetent man, or who abandons his office and seizes upon worldly \[pursuits\]. And many may suppose that such a shepherd or prelate has the devil for his deputy, who keeps account of what sins are incurred through his negligence, so that he may then confess before the supreme shepherd how much he is guilty of. Therefore woe to him who feeds himself and not the sheep; who seeks money and not souls! For God says: “Woe to the shepherds who feed themselves and do not feed my sheep.” \[Ezekiel 34\] St. Bernard took note of this; and therefore he says to Pope Eugenius that his office is to feed souls, not to rule, and that he must answer for all souls.

Already from these words you have it: how episcopal simony runs its course in wicked acquisition, and also in wicked expenditure. But perhaps you say: Do you want bishops to go begging?
