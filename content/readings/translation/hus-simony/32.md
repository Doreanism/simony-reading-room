Therefore the saints acknowledge that the priesthood, and likewise the episcopate, is the most honorable office in this world — but woe to him who has defiled it with any mortal sin! And I acknowledge that the apostles, after Christ, were the most holy fathers here on earth, and that people could rightly say so to them, but the apostles themselves forbade it. Likewise, if any pope were to live as the apostles lived, then he would be a holy father; but people ought to guard against flattering him, and he against pride, so that both sides might keep themselves from sin. For what profit is it that people call a man holy, when before God he is accursed? What profit is it to Antichrist that he exalts himself above God, since Christ will cast him down into hell? And conversely, what harm does it do a man that the world curses him, when the Lord God glorifies him?

### \[B\]

Having established that a pope can be a simoniac, let us see in what ways he can be one. The first way is by desiring the papal dignity for the sake of wealth and worldly advancement. For there is no station in Christendom more prone to falling. For when he does not follow Christ and Peter in his life above all others, he ought to be called not the apostolic successor but the apostolic adversary. Therefore, anyone who hastens and rushes toward that dignity for temporal gain and worldly esteem is already inwardly overthrown
