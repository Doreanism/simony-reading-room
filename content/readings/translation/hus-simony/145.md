And Saint Augustine says as follows: \[Augustine, *Epistle* 185\] Who with wisdom would say to our kings: Do not trouble yourselves in your kingdoms with who holds to the faith, who fights against the assembly of God; it does not belong to you who chooses to be a Christian or a sacrilegist. To them it may be said: But it does belong to you to take care in your kingdom who chooses to be honorable and who dishonorable. That is Saint Augustine.

And since simonists are already simonists without shame, how then should a king or other prince not stand against them and drive them out of his lands? And that they are worthy of this, says one pope by name Pascalis: \[Pope Paschal II\] “It is manifest that simonists, as the foremost and most particular heretics, should be rejected by all the faithful; and if admonished they do not desist, they may be suppressed even by outside powers.” The commentary on this word explains: “by outside powers” — that is, by worldly laymen, who have that authority.
