Gregorius hands down, *Moralia* book 10, saying: The highest place is then well governed, when a man rules not over men, but over vices. He therefore who does not know how to curb his own vices, how shall he restrain those of others?

From which it is gathered that Balthasar de Cossa was rashly elected to the supreme Pontificate, since by experience he was not in the state of exercising perfection, but promised that he would take up the state of acquiring perfection — which is repugnant to prelacy.

The same is clear from the example of both testaments. For in the old law, shepherds of flocks are called to the rule of men because of their experience. And in the new, fishermen are likewise taken up.

That the ignorant, moreover, are barred from ecclesiastical promotion, is clear by authority, reason, and example.

By authority, I say. Because prelates of souls in the old law are called watchmen, Ezekiel 3. But in the new law, bishops, Acts 20: Take heed to yourselves and to the whole flock, in which the Holy Spirit has placed you as bishops — that is, overseers, according to blessed Augustinus, City of God book 19, chapter 19: The name “episcopate” is Greek, and is a name of work, not of honor. Hence on account of the defect of knowledge and oversight, the ignorant are barred from the priesthood, the Lord saying: Because you have rejected knowledge, I also will reject you, that you may not exercise the priesthood, Hosea 4. And the Prophet assigns the cause or reason, saying: The lips of the priest guard knowledge, and they seek the law from his mouth, because he is the angel of the Lord of hosts, Malachi 2. An angel, I say, of the divine will, sufficiently conveying the embassy to men. Because the works of God are perfect, Deuteronomy 32. Whence He also says: I willed knowledge of God more than burnt offerings, Ezekiel 17 \[Hosea 6\].

Hence the same is clear from the example by which Christ refused to set the Apostles over the churches, when He was about to ascend to heaven, except as most sufficiently instructed by the mission of the Holy Spirit, saying: Sit here in the city, until you are clothed with power from on high, Luke, last chapter.
