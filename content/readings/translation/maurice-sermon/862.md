For although they do not deny it, as Cain did, still they seek to refer it onto another — something they did wrongly, as though anything could be set before God, who must be trusted, of such a kind \[as an excuse\]. Hence it is not an excusing but rather a true accusing, where there is an open transgression of the divine command. Thus Augustinus.

Nor does the plea of fear, which may have come in during the schism, offer any help. Augustinus, on John 12, commenting on “He who loves his soul loses it,” says: When this moment comes, that something must be done contrary to God’s precept, or else one must depart from this life because the persecutor threatens death, then let a man choose rather to die with God as his friend than to live with him offended. Thus Augustinus.

And because the bearers of the red hat are the electors of the Pope, by the sign of their redness they are indeed in the state of martyrs before the Church. Yet it is to be feared that fear of death should be ascribed to martyrs. In this matter there is no escaping the fact that seculars — who are likened to the body, as the prelates of the Church are to the spirit — are able to rend the Church. This is gathered from the condition of the state of innocence in Adam, in whom the universal Church then existed, the Apostle saying in Romans 6 \[actually Rom. 5:12\]: “in whom we all sinned.” According to Augustinus, in *De civitate Dei* book 16, chapter 26, if the spirit of Adam, as he had received it at his making, had by obedience preserved itself single, it would never have felt any rupture in what descends from it, nor could his body have rebelled against the spirit — from Augustinus’s sentence, *De civitate Dei* book \[14\], chapter 2, likewise book \[13\], chapter 1 \[sic, reading uncertain\]. But when the spirit of Adam split itself from God through sin, immediately also the whole that descends from him felt the perversity of the schism, as the Prophet says: “All have turned aside, they have together become useless, corrupt, and have become an abomination,” Psalm 13.
