and gives not to every one their portion in due season but limits it to the seasons and measure of his gain; nor does he dispense the bread and water of life indifferently to all, but withholds or distributes, impairs or augments the portion of the children according to the prospect of benefit or favor he may thereby reap. Such a one is indeed but a base simoniacal peddler, making merchandise of the gifts of God. But such is not he who requires only his allowance of those due accommodations which diligent attendance to his duties calls for, and in which he exercises himself without any base design, giving unto every one their portion in due season — nay, this is the faithful and wise steward; and blessed shall he be when, at the coming of his Lord, he shall be found so doing.
