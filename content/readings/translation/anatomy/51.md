Thus Absalom by his fair tongue bribed the affections of the men of Israel, and by stealing away their hearts thereby ensnared them in a course of rebellion and treason against his father. And thus, by the people’s tongues, was Saul bribed to spare Agag and the fat cattle of the Amalekites, and so was ensnared in the sin of rebellion against God — fearing that if he had not hearkened to their voice he would have lost their favor and incurred their hatred.
