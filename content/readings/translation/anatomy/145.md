And yet I think Leo did right not to re-ordain them, but rather, upon their repentance, to reinvest them with better authority in the exercise of their office. For although they had once received orders — however ineffective, through the want of divine concurrence with a simoniacal ordainer — the church had, by the external seal of ordination, once invested such a person with an ecclesiastical authority to transmit the same to others; and it would not have been fitting for the church to call into question the character of her own ordination.
