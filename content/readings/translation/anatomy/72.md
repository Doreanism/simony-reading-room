Ahab, *Shouldst thou help the ungodly?* — what he who does so will answer to him who has said, *Thou shalt not countenance a poor man in his cause*, I do not know. For even though his poverty might plead for our charity, our charity must not lead us to lend our patronage to an evil cause. And he may reflect on what he can expect from the hand of the Almighty, who has threatened severe wrath against those that *strengthen the hands of evildoers*.

Thus have I shown the several ways whereby men may be guilty of Simony, either as the principal agents, or as instruments and accomplices with them in their sin. By which ways, whoever enters into the sacred offices of the church is intruded by the artifices of man;
