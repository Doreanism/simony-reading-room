called a warfare; and as it is in secular warfare, so it is in the case of the Church Militant. There are many soldiers in the war; all have their wages. But in some, the design of their service is the safety of their country; in others, the design is only their own gain, and upon this account they enlist in the war. They both require, both exact, both receive their wages; yet he who designs his service for the preservation of his country is not to be reputed a mercenary soldier, because he hazards himself not for his wages but for his country, however he does require his wages as well as the other, that thereby he may be sustained to attend to his duty.
