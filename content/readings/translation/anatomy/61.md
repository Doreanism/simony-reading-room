Yet I do not say that any man may not lawfully prefer to an ecclesiastical charge a friend or relative who is well qualified for the office. For, as I have already said, religion does not weaken but rather strengthens the bonds of natural affection; so that it would be unjust to deal worse with relatives than with strangers, or — all else being equal — not to prefer them to others. But to do so merely on a carnal account, because they may better serve our temporal ends and designs, or to prefer a person unfit for the office because he is a friend, betrays an eye more to carnal interest than to God’s glory.
