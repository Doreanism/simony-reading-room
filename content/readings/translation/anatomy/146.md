And therefore the Canon Law states: *Simoniacos non Simoniace a Simoniacis Ordinatos, misericorditer per Manus Impositionem, pro temporis necessitate, in officio concedimus permanere*: “Those whom we regard as Simoniacs on account of their ordination by Simoniacal persons, but who have not themselves practiced simony, we do graciously permit, by the imposition of hands, to continue in their office, given the necessity of the times.” By this it appears that it was not thought fit to re-ordain such persons, but only to confirm their former ordination by the imposition of hands, without performing the full rites of consecration anew.
