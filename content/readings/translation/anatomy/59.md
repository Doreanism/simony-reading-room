Therefore the Canon Law declares: *Simoniacum est, pro temporali obsequio Beneficium Ecclesiasticum promittere* — “To promise an ecclesiastical benefice for temporal service is Simony.” And again: *Pro habendis Spiritualibus homagium facere, Simoniacum est* — “To do homage for the enjoyment of spiritual things is Simony.” I cannot see, therefore, how those who pay their servants’ salaries by means of a spiritual benefice, or who serve any man with the design of earning a church as their wages, can wash their hands of the loathsome stain of this sin.
