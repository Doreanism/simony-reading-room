And therefore Dionysius Areopagita (or whoever else is the author of *The Ecclesiastical Hierarchy*) says of the person who confers orders that he is but Εκφαντορικος, *the proclaimer* or *declarer of the divine election*; “who,” says he, “by his own gift does not advance those who are consecrated to the honor or power of ecclesiastical order” — this he ascribes only to God, whom he calls there Τελεταρχου, *the Prince of Consecration* or *chief Ordainer*; and he pleads the authority of Jesus Christ for ascribing this power to God alone and his Holy Spirit.
