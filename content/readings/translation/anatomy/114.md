But having given this short hint, I shall proceed rather to show how, by the judgment of the Catholic Church and the joint suffrages of many Fathers together, simony has been accounted a crime deserving the highest censures, and how laws and canons have been made for inflicting those censures upon persons guilty of it, who, according to their different qualities — whether ecclesiastics or laics — have been decreed liable to deposition or excommunication, and to be stigmatized and branded with a mark of infamy.
