Nay, and though carnal affection was no ingredient in our Saviour’s choice of his disciples, yet it is manifest that John, his cousin according to the flesh, was taken into their number, and is more than once called “the disciple whom Jesus loved.” But though the person for whom one intercedes be ever so worthy, yet if his intercessions proceed not from a religious but only from a carnal principle, to promote a worldly design, then the intercessor is guilty of this Simony by the tongue — his end therein being not to serve the Lord Jesus Christ, but only a carnal interest.
