*mercedem Dispensationis a Domino* — “They shall receive their necessary sustentation from the people, but the reward of their dispensation from the Lord.”

Thus it is evident that the pastors of the Church are not to be reputed hirelings or simoniacal merchants of spiritual things on account of the requiring and exacting of their stipends and the fruits of their benefices due from the people for their maintenance — provided that the benefice be not the end they aim at, nor their temporal advantage the design of their labors, but that they feed the flock for love of Christ and not for filthy lucre, not making the matters of their calling the subject of transactions for worldly gain.
