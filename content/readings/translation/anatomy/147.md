*Secondly,* it being undeniable that the entire efficacy of the ministry depends upon God, and that its spiritual effects flow wholly from that power by which he infuses and enables men to administer the mysteries of the Gospel, and from the concurrence of his Holy Spirit with them — for “the excellency of the power is of God, and not of man; neither is he that planteth anything, neither he that watereth anything, but God that giveth the increase” — it will then undoubtedly follow that the external rites of ordination can transmit no virtue or efficacy into the ministry of one who receives them without the call and concurrence of the Holy Ghost, and without any promise of divine influence upon his administrations.
