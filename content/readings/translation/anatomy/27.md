Likewise, under the name of *Spiritual Things*, this definition encompasses those which the former descriptions call *Things annexed to Spiritual Things*; because, by virtue of that annexation or connection — as I have shown — they become, through God’s appointment, sacred and spiritual. And this definition may be well gathered from the text, which shows that the sin of *Simon* was concerning a spiritual thing, *The Gift of the Holy Ghost*; and that his transgression in this regard was that the gift of God, which could not be valued or purchased with sums of money, he would have subjected to a price and offered money for. And since all *Simony* is but a transcript of his sin, it may rightly be defined in these terms.
