The common definition given by the *Scholastics* and *Canonists* is that it is, *Studiosa voluntas, seu cupiditas, emendi vel vendendi Spiritualia, vel Spiritualibus annexa*: “A deliberate or earnest will, or desire, of buying or selling Spiritual Things, or Things annexed to Spiritual Things.” But because this defines *Simony* only as it exists in the disposition — though it may by consequence reach to the external action and effect — yet since it does not expressly state the same, I find that the *Canonists* therefore give also another definition, calling it, *Pactio circa Spiritualia, aut Spiritualibus annexa*: “A pact, or bargain, concerning Spiritual Things, or Things annexed to Spiritual Things.” But this again reaches only to the effect, whereas the guilt of *Simony* may be incurred in the disposition and endeavor,
