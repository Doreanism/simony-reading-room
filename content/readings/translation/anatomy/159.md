For, as Calvin rightly observes, *Contigit omnes fere juvenes turgidos esse, fastuosos, tanquam nubes transvolare possent*: “It is incident to almost all young men to be puffed up with self-conceit, and proudly to raise their feathers, as if they could mount above the clouds.” And so, like Phaeton, they must needs be set in their father’s chariot, though the outcome of their preposterous undertaking should be to precipitate themselves to their own ruin and to risk setting the world on fire.
