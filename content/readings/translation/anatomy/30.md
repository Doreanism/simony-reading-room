### *First,*

If we consider these things in their own nature, as separate from the spiritual office and use to which they are appointed, they are temporal things indeed, and as such are vendible; yet by virtue of the annexation and dedication to the ministry of such an office, they become spiritual things, and therefore not vendible — for this changes their nature. Just as the bread and wine in the Eucharist are in their own nature, and while separate from the sacrament, temporal things that may be bought and sold; but when by consecration they are set apart for that spiritual and sacred use of the sacrament, they change from the nature of temporal things and are no longer vendible.
