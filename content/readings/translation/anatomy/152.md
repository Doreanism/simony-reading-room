And it is no doubt people’s contempt of the Gospel and their unfruitfulness under the labors of faithful pastors that provokes God to remove such pastors from them and to give them over to such thieves, robbers, and hirelings, whose ministry can do them no good. And therefore the only counsel I can give to those in such a condition is seriously to reflect upon this sin and, with sorrow, to humble themselves before God on account of it, and by firm resolve to devote themselves for the future to a greater love and regard for the Gospel, to a more reverent esteem of and obedience to faithful ministers, and to a better improvement of the means of grace. This is the most hopeful means and method I know for a speedy removal of this judgment and for rescuing them from that snare
