*Secondly*, it is a well-established rule that in interpreting the law of God, under the prohibition of any one sin, all sins of the same kind are to be understood as prohibited, whether more or less heinous; and likewise all things that are the proper instruments, aids, occasions, accessories, and inducements to that sin. Thus under the seventh commandment, the prohibition of adultery forbids fornication and every kind of sexual immorality; and moreover, the company of prostitutes, unclean conversation, excess, drunkenness, making provision for the flesh, and all things whatsoever that may occasion or directly minister to such a sin, are prohibited and condemned.
