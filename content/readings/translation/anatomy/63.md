And it may not unreasonably be said that those who traffic in such persons do so not to gain ministers for God, but slaves for themselves. But far more deeply does he incur this guilt who, for the purchase of such preferment, implicitly enslaves his conscience to follow the dictates of his promoter; for when a man is advanced on the condition or expectation of such blind obedience, I am certain God has no hand in the bargain. But I think I need say no more for the discovery of those rocks upon which the principal agents in such spiritual trafficking may chance to split, in the way of entrance upon sacred offices.
