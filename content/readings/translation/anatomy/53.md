Yet this is not to be understood as though all intercessions in affairs of this nature were either simoniacal or sinful; rather, it must be considered whether the person on whose behalf friends intercede and entreat is a worthy person — that is, holy and well qualified for such a sacred function — or unworthy and unsuitable for it. If he is unworthy, then certainly he sins grievously who interposes on his behalf, for he sacrifices the glory of God and the blood of poor souls to the interest of his unworthy carnal friend or favorite; and so does he who, to gratify man, is moved by entreaties to invest him with such an office.
