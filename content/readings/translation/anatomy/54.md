But if the person be worthy, then he who intercedes for him must consider upon what principle he does so. If it be out of a pious affection for the good of the Church and a well-grounded hope of his being a good instrument for the glory of God, then upon such grounds one may not only without sin but very commendably recommend such a person and even use entreaties and solicitations for his advancement to such a holy function — nay, and so much the more if he is a person in whom one has an interest of natural relation. (For religion does not in any way weaken but rather strengthens the bonds of natural affection.)
