But he whose design is gain, and not the glory of God nor the edification of souls, but who exercises the office only that he may have the benefice, is the mercenary man who makes merchandise of his office. *Mercenarius quippe est in Evang.* (says Gregory) *qui locum quidem Pastoris tenet, lucra autem animarum non quærit, sed terrenis commodis inhiat* — “He truly is the mercenary man who holds indeed the place of a pastor, yet seeks not the benefit of souls, but gapes after earthly commodities.” And so much the more mercenary is he, if, beyond the allowance of that maintenance which is set apart for him as a steward in God’s house, he also makes advantage of those spiritual things which are committed to his dispensation,
