Wherefore, as long as that spirit of Christ which made the zeal of God’s house eat him up possessed the governors of the Church, it moved them to make very strict and cautious laws and canons for stopping all those channels through which this corruption might be feared to enter, and to inflict severe censures upon such as they found guilty of this sin. But when that primitive fervour began to decay, and the spirit of the world to take hold of the lukewarm clergy, then Simon Magus began again to lift his head, being animated by the secret favour of a strong party in the Church, of whom — though he knew they would not avowedly own his interest — he was confident they would prove clandestine abettors of his designs.
