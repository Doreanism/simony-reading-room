It is for this reason that Gregorius calls the communion of Arius an execration, and Innocentius calls the ordination of Bonosus a condemnation — not because they are such in themselves, but because they render those who give or receive them thus; just as Hieronymus calls their sacrifices the bread of mourning, not with respect to what they are in themselves, but with respect to their effect.

Still others hold that those heretics who were ordained within the Church retain the right to ordain and to consecrate even after they have been separated from it; but those ordained and anointed by such persons while they were already in schism or heresy lack that right, and therefore, when they attempt to ordain, they inflict a wound rather than confer grace.

## Chap. II. On Simony: Its Name and Nature.

Concerning Simoniacs, however, there is no doubt that they are heretics; yet they do ordain and consecrate before the sentence of degradation has been passed. And although in the strict sense Simoniacs are those who, after the manner of Simon Magus, wish to purchase with money the grace that is beyond price, and those who receive payment for sacred ministry in the manner of Gehazi are to be called Gehazites, nevertheless all — both those who give and those who receive — are called Simoniacs, and both are struck by the same sentence.

## Chap. III. On Those Who Are Ordained by Simoniacs Knowingly, or in Ignorance.

There is, however, a difference between those who knowingly allow themselves to be ordained by Simoniacs and those who do so in ignorance.

“Those who have knowingly allowed themselves to be consecrated — or rather, execrated — by Simoniacs: their consecration is altogether null and void. But those who are ordained by Simoniacs without knowing at the time of their ordination that they are Simoniacs, and who were at that time regarded as Catholics, their ordination is mercifully upheld.”
