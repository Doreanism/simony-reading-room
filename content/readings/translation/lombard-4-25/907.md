And when it seems expedient to the Church that their leaders, coming to Catholic fellowship, should not exercise their offices there, nonetheless the Sacraments of ordination themselves are not taken from them, but remain upon them; and therefore hands are not laid upon them again, lest the injury be done not to the man but to the Sacrament itself. Just as in baptism there is something that can be given through such persons, so in ordination there is the right of conferring: both indeed to their own destruction. But it is one thing not to have, another to have it to one’s destruction, and another to have it wholesomely.”

The same: “Concerning those who have been separated from the unity of the Church, there is now no question that they both have \[the Sacraments\] and can confer them; but they have them to their destruction and confer them to destruction, because they are outside the bond of peace. No injury is to be done to either Sacrament. Just as one who withdraws from unity does not rightly have it, yet nevertheless has it, and therefore it is not given back to him when he returns; so also one who withdraws from unity does not rightly confer it, yet nevertheless does confer it; and therefore what he has conferred upon one who comes to unity is not repeated.”

The same: “It is one thing not to have something, another to have it without right or to usurp it illicitly. The Sacraments of Christ and the Church are therefore not absent merely because not only heretics but all the wicked make illicit use of them; rather, those persons are to be corrected and punished, while those \[Sacraments\] are to be acknowledged and venerated.”

Likewise Gregorius: “What you say — that those who have been ordained should be ordained again — is quite ridiculous. For just as one who has been baptized once ought not to be baptized again, so one who has been consecrated once cannot validly be consecrated again in the same order.”

From these and other authorities it seems to be affirmed that the Sacraments of Christ remain in all the wicked, even in heretics who have been cut off and condemned, together with the right to confer them: for they are able to confer them, though to destruction; and those upon whom they have conferred them need not be ordained again. These authorities appear to stand in direct opposition to those cited before.
