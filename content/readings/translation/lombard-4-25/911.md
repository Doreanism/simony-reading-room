## Chap. VI. On Those Who Are Ordained by Heretics or by Other Simoniacs Under Compulsion.

Likewise, where Alexander decrees “that Simoniacs are to be utterly condemned and deposed,” it must be understood with the qualification: unless someone was forcibly drawn in. For concerning these \[ordained under compulsion by Simoniacs\], and those violently ordained by any heretics whatsoever, Innocentius says that they may have some color of excuse, provided they depart from them at once and renounce their wicked assembly.

## Chap. VII. On the Age of Those to Be Ordained.

Concerning the age of those to be ordained, Pope Nicolaus decreed as follows: “The sacred canons have established,” he says, “that a subdeacon is not to be ordained before the age of fourteen, nor a deacon before \[twenty-\]five, nor a priest before thirty. Thereafter, if he shall be found worthy, he may be elected to the episcopate” — which we likewise command to be observed in the same manner.

Likewise Fabianus \[declares\]: “If anyone shall not have completed thirty years of age, he is by no means to be ordained a priest, even if he be very worthy” — “because the Lord himself was baptized at thirty years of age, and thus began to teach.”
