It was conceded to the rural priest to celebrate \[Mass\] three times on account of the solemnity \[of Christmas\] and as a kind of Sabbath, and also on account of the mystery. But a greater prelate on that day celebrates once in one church, twice in another; yet now by multiplying Masses we celebrate the Nativity of the Lord every day. Item: if assiduous preaching does not bear fruit but rather grows cheap, how much more does this sacrament, when repeated in a single day, grow cheap and turn to a weariness and a detriment to devotion? Item: in those days the word of the Lord was precious because it was rare, and so likewise the confection of the Eucharist because it was rare; but now it is cheap, because so often repeated. Item: if, in prefigurement, a single Lamb was immolated in a single house, offered to the Lord once at Passover, immolated, eaten whole, and eaten in haste (Exodus 12); how do you, sinner, dare irreverently and constantly to double, triple, and multiply the very truth of that figure under the well-known appearance of venality, “since those who serve the tabernacle,” \[that is,\] the body, “have no right to eat from this altar” (Hebrews 13)? Item: if the levitical priest entered the Holy of Holies once a year with blood, and the holy place once daily with incense (Exodus 30; Hebrews 9), how do you, sinner, evangelical priest, dare to enter daily — and, what is abominable, twice in a day — into the most holy of holies, the holy and heavenly pledges of all the saints, so irreverently and with so little fear because so constantly, and perhaps without blood and incense? “With blood,” that is, with faith in the Passion and with sorrow of heart, from which \[sorrow\] a kind of blood flows through the eyes, “and with bitter herbs” (Exodus 12), with the incense of prayer, let approach be made to these holy things. For tears make this cup the sweeter. Item: if the holy order of the Carthusians, offering a holocaust here also of those purified by abstinence and mortification of the flesh from the stain of the world, “and now flying upon the wings of the winds” (Psalm 17), dares to celebrate \[Mass\] only on feasts and observed days designated by the spiritual office, how do you, sinner-priest, dare fearlessly to handle \[it\] so often so irreverently — twice in a day? Item: a certain just man, observing and considering that the Lord was placed in a new tomb on the sixth day of the week, wrapped in a clean shroud (Matthew 27), and did not change his sepulcher but rested in it throughout the Sabbath, and rose on the Lord’s Day and the third day, resolved that he would celebrate \[Mass\] only on the third day, with one day of rest interposed, in which he might cleanse his shroud — that is, his conscience — with tears and penance, so that he might rise more strongly and devoutly on the third day to the confection of the Lord’s body. Item: if in fasting one approaches the bodily table only once in a day, how much more should this be so at the spiritual table? If at that \[table\] there is a single food, the spiritual \[food\] is now spiritually multiplied through certain external condiments inwardly, to the point of nausea, to the point of drunkenness. Item: certain persons interpret badly that place of the Lord’s Prayer: “Give us this day our daily bread” (Luke 11) — it does not say twice “today.” Item: the Hebrew in place of “daily” has “sogolla,” which is rendered ἐπιούσιον, and has a twofold inter\[pretation\].
