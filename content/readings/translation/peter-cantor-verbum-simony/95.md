# CHAPTER XXV.

Against simony present in the exercise of ecclesiastical offices, or of any spiritual acts whatsoever, which are forbidden from being performed for temporal gain.

Justice and the administration of public affairs consists not only in the settlement of cases, but also in the celebration and conferral of the sacraments together with the exercise of ecclesiastical offices, or of any spiritual acts whatsoever. Sacred Scripture forbids these from being done for temporal gain, branding in many passages the greed of those who receive payment unlawfully and who, together with Judas, sell justice and God. First, in the Gospel, thus: “God is spirit, and those who worship him must worship in spirit and in truth” (John 4) — that is, one must worship for spiritual and true goods, so that worship embraces the performance of all spiritual things. Scripture does not say: for temporal things, but for eternal ones. Otherwise, “if your eye is not single, your whole body will be in darkness” (Luke 11).

Likewise Tullius \[Cicero\] in the *De Officiis* (book II, after the midpoint): No vice is more foul than greed in princes and those who govern public affairs. For to treat public affairs as a source of profit is not merely base, but criminal, wicked, and sacrilegious. And thus the oracle that Apollo at Delphi delivered — that Sparta would perish by nothing other than greed — seems to have been a prophecy not for the Lacedaemonians alone but for all prosperous peoples. For those who preside over public affairs can win the goodwill of the multitude by nothing more easily than by abstinence and self-restraint.

Those, however, who wish to ingratiate themselves with the people and grasp for the administration of public affairs in order to pursue profit and glory, undermine the foundations of public life. Likewise the same author: Continence and abstinence and the flesh are born from the same womb of nature. \[Cato:\] How much more, then, ought continence and abstinence and the Christian people to be born from the same womb of grace and evangelical teaching? Likewise the Apostle: “Seek the things that are above, not the things that are upon the earth” (Colossians 3) — not for earthly things.
