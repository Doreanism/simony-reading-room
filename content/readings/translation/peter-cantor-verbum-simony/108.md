Those others \[who crucified Christ in the flesh\] crucified the Lord through ignorance — hence he prayed for them: “Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do” (Luke 23). Likewise: if John, who had lived in the wilderness from childhood, who was sanctified in his mother’s womb (Luke 1), and who was even invited by the Lord, did not dare to touch that crown of the Lord’s head — so dreadful to angels and worthy of reverence — (Mark 1): how is it that you, a sinner and one defiled, dare not merely to touch but to enclose and imprison within the prison of your polluted body not just the crown of Christ’s head, but the whole Christ, God and man? Or if he feared to baptize Christ, by whom he himself ought rather to have been baptized, how is it that you, a sinful soul, by whom you ought entirely to be inhabited, do not shrink from enclosing him wholly in the squalid little lodging of your body? Likewise: if it was said to Mary, who loved the Lord with such great fervor and who attended him so devoutly both in life and in death, “Do not touch me, for I have not yet ascended to my Father” (John 20) — \[meaning\] in your heart. If, I say, it was not permitted to her to touch “the footstool of his feet” (Psalm 109) in the outward sense, because she did not believe him equal to the Father, \[not believing\] that “his going out is from the highest heavens” (Psalm 18): how do you, a sinner, believe it lawful and permitted to you not only to touch the whole God, but to place him in your entire unclean body? Fear lest it be said to you not only “Do not touch me,” but also: “Friend, how did you come in here” — namely to the heavenly banquet — “not having a wedding garment” (Matthew 22), that is, charity? Likewise, 2 Kings 6: if Oza, because he stretched out his hand to the symbolic ark lest it tip when the oxen kicked, was struck dead by the Lord, how much more should he be struck by the Lord who celebrates or receives the true ark — namely the body of the Lord — unworthily? Yet Oza approached to support the ark in the performance of his proper duty, since he was of the tribe of Levi and a priest, though he did so unworthily. For the Hebrews report that on the preceding night he had lain with his wife. How much more, then, will those be punished who approach the body of Christ as men who have lain with males, concubines, or harlots? Likewise, 2 Chronicles 26: Ozias, who presumed to offer incense to the Lord contrary to the office of the priests, was struck with leprosy by the Lord; how much more will one be struck with leprosy — indeed with eternal death — by the Lord who presumes to offer the heavenly victim to the Lord unworthily? Likewise, from the institution of the primitive Church, after the Gospel was read it was said to the catechumens: “Go out, go out.” If therefore it was not lawful for members of Christ — that is, those who have charity — to be present at the confection of the body of the Lord, it is all the more to be feared **84** by members of the devil, and by those who do not have charity, \[that they dare\] to celebrate or receive so great a sacrament. I say without doubt that ministers of the altar who celebrate or receive \[the Eucharist\] in such a state, or who serve at the altar in this condition, sin mortally. It is written: “Be clean, you who carry the vessels of the Lord” (Isaiah 52).
