But, they say, we are not burdened with toil as Moses was, even though we are attached to several dignities and offices. For we discharge them through vicars, through hired substitutes, through concubinaries.

## Hence there is no need to distribute them, for in those \[substitutes\] we rest.

To such men the answer must be: Therefore, because you do not labor, neither shall you eat (2 Thessalonians 3). To one such bishop a certain people said: “Do not appoint over us a concubinary priest, that you may wander and be carried about through the land, given over to idleness.” Likewise: if Jethro, in giving counsel, said to Moses “Divide your burden among many,” etc. (Exodus 18), today it is said in the Church: “Gather burdens and many offices into one, and the Lord will depart from you.” Likewise in Numbers: the leaders of the families of Gilead from the tribe of the sons of Joseph approached Moses, saying: “The Lord commanded you to divide the land among the children of Israel by lot, and to give the inheritance due to their father to the daughters of Zelophehad; but he forbade them to be taken in marriage by men of another tribe, lest the distribution of the lots be confounded and the possession of some pass to others.” To them Moses replied: “Let them marry only men of their own tribe, that the possession of the children of Israel may not be transferred from tribe to tribe; let these remain as the Lord has assigned them” (Numbers 27; 36). How much more, then, ought churches to say to a prelate: “Give our church a man from its own tribe, and let our church \[keep its minister\], lest the minister of one church be transferred to the ministry of another — a dean in one transferred to an archdeaconry in another, and so on — so that the distribution of offices and personages is thrown into confusion, and one who was useful to one church becomes useless to both.” Likewise: See that Christ had no place to lay his head (Luke 9). And do you seek various resting places — personages, that is — in which to lay your head, in which to rest? These resting places will bring you rather to eternal punishment than to repose. Likewise in the Psalm (Psalm 67): “The king of virtues, of the beloved, of the beloved” — of the beloved, I say, that is “to divide the spoils of the beauty of the house” — that is, to make the house beautiful. Hence: “O Lord, I have loved the beauty of your house” (Psalm 25). This division of the spoils of the beauty of the house, the Apostle teaches in the Epistle to the Ephesians chapter 4: “And the Lord himself gave some to be apostles, others prophets, others evangelists, others pastors and teachers,” etc. Likewise Luke in the Gospel chapter 2: “When a strong man armed guards his courtyard, all his possessions are at peace. But if one stronger than he comes and overcomes him, he takes away all his armor in which he trusted and distributes his spoils.” These spoils are faithful souls, ecclesiastical dignities, and offices.
