# CHAPTER XXIX.

## Against Masses of Many Faces

*All falsehood seeks corners, etc.:*

*While fools flee one vice, they run into its opposite.*

(Horace, *Satires*, book I, 20.)

So while certain persons, seeking a refuge and a covering for their greed, flee the plurality of Masses, they fall not merely into a vice but into a monstrosity, fabricating monstrous double-faced and triple-faced Masses. And, while in the Apostle one reads that the Church consists of persons of many aspects, nowhere does one read of a Mass of two or three faces (2 Corinthians 1). Item: among the poets we read of two-headed Janus, the seven-headed Hydra, hundred-handed Briareus, two-bodied Geryon, and another three-bodied; nowhere do we read of a two-headed, double-faced, or many-faced Mass. For to make Masses double-faced, triple-faced, and the like is more monstrous than any monster. Item: such persons work against the mystery of the Mass, because:

*The office expresses sighs, glories, praises,*

*Kyrie eleison, thrice tripled, and prayers.*

But now in the Mass the \[Kyrie\] is tripled seven or more times, and the mystery and order of the Mass is thrown into confusion. Item: to which of the two introits will the double-faced Mass be assigned, and from which of them will it take its name? If from the second, which is for the faithful, then the kiss of peace ought not to be given in it; or if it is given, it will conflict with the general institution and custom of the Church. If from the first, which is in memory of the sending of the Holy Spirit, then the one so celebrating does not celebrate Mass for the faithful, and thus is not released from an anniversary or trental to be celebrated; and if he confess this, saying: because under one canon he celebrates two Masses — but of what kind? Is it only the Mass of the catechumens? It seems so, since the offered host has not yet been represented as sent. So it should rather be said that under two canons one Mass is celebrated — since there is one representation of the host sent — than that two Masses are celebrated under one canon. Item: such persons conflict with the institution of a synod or council, provincial though it was, but celebrated by authority of a legate of the Roman See, in which it was commanded that no one should celebrate twice in a day except in the greatest necessity — as when a body were present, or a married woman to be purified, or another case of necessity arising. It was altogether forbidden that, even under pressing necessity, Masses should be made double in this way; rather, the chasuble being set aside after the host had been sent, the introit of the Mass of the catechumens was to be begun, one and another at the priest’s pleasure. Item: this conflicts with the mystery of the Mass usefully approved by the Church. For the priest standing at first at the right horn of the altar signifies the coming of Christ to the Jewish people, \[his\] right \[hand\] and his own.
