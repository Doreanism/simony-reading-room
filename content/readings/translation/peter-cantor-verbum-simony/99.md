Moreover, clerics are not present for the singing of the hours and even of the hours of the Blessed Virgin; though present for others, they flee from these, so that the Holy Spirit may claim no work for itself in the Church, since nothing is done there freely, but money claims everything for itself. And in sum: just as, before this wretched commerce was established, it was honorable and praiseworthy to enter the choir, to celebrate the ecclesiastical offices, to be assiduous in these things — so now it is dishonorable and unpraiseworthy, since in our assiduousness at these rites we are reckoned greedy rather than devout; and so now:

*The Church’s noble name, once venerable,*
*Prostitutes herself, and sits for hire like a harlot.*

# CHAPTER XXVII.

## Against Simony in the Substance of the Sacraments, Especially the Eucharist.

If venality — the leprosy of Gehazi and the simony of Simon — is so shameful and damnable in the appendages of the sacraments, as has been shown above, how much more so in the very substance of the sacraments, and especially in the Eucharist? For there the whole **75** Christ is received, the source and origin of all graces, whereas in the other sacraments graces are distributed.

We nowhere read that the Lord was so angered and inflamed by any shameful enormity of sin as by the selling of the dove — above all the eucharistic dove. For the sake of purging and driving out this plague from the Church, we read that the Lord, stirred by the ardor of the Spirit to a wrath inborn in him and to an ineffable meekness and patience (which he could not, however, put aside, because he is greatly merciful \[Joel 2\], etc.) — as though setting aside his gentleness — “drove from the temple, with a whip made of cords, those who were selling and buying sheep and doves, and overturned the tables of the money-changers (John 2; Matthew 21).” He calls the altars of God such things on account of the avarice of the priests.
