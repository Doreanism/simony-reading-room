# Book IX, §19

Why he overturned the seats of those who sold doves I do not understand according to the letter; for the peddlers of birds could hardly claim for themselves the dignity of any place of honor in a marketplace. For what prerogative of dignity is there among doves? But the form of the Lord’s baptism gives us instruction, when the Holy Spirit descended in the likeness of a dove (*Luke* 3:22), \[for it shows\] by the figure of those traders driven out of the temple that they cannot have fellowship in the Church of God who would peddle the grace of the Holy Spirit. *Freely, he says, you have received; freely give* (*Matthew* 10:8). Hence to Simon, who supposed that the office of sanctifying could be conferred upon him by a price, Peter answered: *Thy money, he says, perish with thee unto destruction; because thou hast thought that the grace of God may be possessed by money: thou hast no part nor lot in this faith* (*Acts* 8:20–21).
