furnishings. What \[will they say\] to that famous declaration of the Synod of Aquileia, in which glorious poverty is praised in the priests of the Lord? For that precept which Hieronymus gives to Nepotianus — that his modest table should be known to the poor and to strangers, and with them to Christ as their guest — they will perhaps reject as too austere. But that which he immediately adds they will be ashamed to deny: that it is the glory of a bishop to provide for the needs of the poor, and the ignominy of all priests to be devoted to their own enrichment. Yet they cannot accept this without all condemning themselves to disgrace. But there is no need to press them more harshly here, since my sole purpose has been to demonstrate that the legitimate order of the diaconate has long since been abolished among them — so that they may no longer boast under this title for the commendation of their Church. Which I think I have done abundantly enough.
