# DISTINCTION XXV.

## On Those Receiving Holy Orders.

*It is also customary to ask, whether heretics,* etc. In this Twenty-Fifth Distinction the Master treats of those receiving the Sacrament of Order, and also of the ministers who confer it. For the elucidation of these two matters, therefore, I raise three questions.

The first is: Whether it belongs to bishops alone to administer the Sacrament of Order.

The second concerns simony, which is common to both minister and recipient: Whether simony is a vice directly opposed to justice.

The third concerns the same: Whether the Lord Pope can in any case commit simony.

## Single Question.

### Article I.

Whether it belongs to bishops alone to administer this Sacrament of Order.

WITH RESPECT TO the first question, I argue first that it does not — indeed, that it can also belong to simple priests and others — as follows: That to which a greater \[power\] is communicated can also have a lesser \[power\] communicated to it; but to a simple priest there is communicated something greater than the conferring of orders, namely the confecting of the Body of the Lord; therefore \[a simple priest can also confer orders\].

In opposition stands the argument that it belongs to the prince alone in the Church to arrange and dispense rank and station in the Church; but bishops alone are princes in the Church; therefore \[it belongs to bishops alone\].
