# Canto XIX

O Simon Magus, o you wretched followers
who prostitute the things of God, which ought
to be the brides of righteousness, and you, rapacious,

for gold and silver make them your adulterers —
now it is fitting that the trumpet sound for you,
since you are lodged in the third pouch \[of Hell\].

We had already reached the next tomb,
having climbed up that stretch of the ridge
which overhangs the middle of the ditch.

O supreme Wisdom, how great is the art
you display in heaven, on earth, and in the evil world,
and how justly your power apportions its judgments!

I saw along the sides and across the floor
the livid stone pierced full of holes,
each one the same width, and every one round.

They seemed to me no less wide or larger
than those in my own beautiful San Giovanni,
made as places for the baptizers to stand;

one of which, not many years ago,
I shattered to rescue one who was drowning inside —
and let this be my seal to undeceive every man.

From the mouth of each there protruded
the feet of a sinner, and his legs
up to the thigh, while the rest remained within.

The soles of every sinner were ablaze;
for which reason his joints twitched so violently
that they would have snapped ropes of willow or hemp.

As the flame on oiled things is wont
to move only along the outer surface,
so it ran there from heels to toes.

“Who is that one, master, who writhes
more than his other companions,”
I said, “and whom a redder flame sucks at?”
