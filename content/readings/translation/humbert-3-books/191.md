Hence Christ, about to depart to a distant region in the guise of a certain nobleman, says to his servants: “Trade while I am coming” \[Luke 19:13\]. And of the holy Church it is said in Proverbs: “She has become like a merchant’s ship, bringing her bread from afar. She has made linen and sold it, and delivered a girdle to the Canaanite” \[Proverbs 31:14, 24\]. Christ and his own are therefore merchants; antichrist and his own are likewise merchants — to whom the Lord speaks through Ezekiel in the guise of the king of Tyre: “You were the cherub that covers, and I placed you on the holy mountain of God; you walked among the stones of fire, perfect in your ways from the day of your creation, until iniquity was found in you, in the multitude of your trading, your interior was filled with iniquity.” And a little further: “In the multitude of your iniquities and the iniquities of your trading you have defiled your sanctification” \[Ezekiel 28:14–16, 18\]. He says also to that city: “In your trading you were filled, and you were glorified exceedingly in the heart of the sea; in many waters your rowers brought you” \[Ezekiel 27:25–26\]. It is plain, then, that human life here is conducted through nothing but trading. But the faithful, under the mark of Christ, sell perishable goods and buy eternal ones. The unfaithful, likewise under the mark of antichrist, sell perishable goods and buy eternal evils. The former give their temporal life and receive eternal life; the latter give their temporal life and receive eternal death. The former sell themselves and all they have, and receive them back a hundredfold with full interest in eternal life; the latter sell themselves and all they have, and afterward find nothing accumulated from it except the interest of eternal death — in whose dealings no one serving God will entangle himself \[2 Timothy 2:4\]. For perfidy, or impiety, is the general mark of all those: pagans, Jews, heretics — as alien children who have lied to God. But what of those impious ones who bear the mark of the cross on their right hand and forehead? Are they then to be counted among the merchants of Christ on account of this mark? Are they not thieves and robbers \[John 10:8\]? Whose cross, then, do they display as the mark of their perfidy? Without doubt that of the blaspheming and insolent robber who, through his crucified son — that is, his fellow thief — insolently abused the Lord crucified alongside him. But why do these robbers display the mark of the cross while invoking the name of Christ? No wonder — for they do this to the reproach and injury of our Christ and to the glory and profit of their robber. For that robber’s accomplice and fellow pupil, though he says to God: “If you are the Son of God, save yourself and us” \[Matthew 27:40\], was assailing and disparaging the omnipotence of Christ and providing occasion to his enemies to mock him. Taunting him with the punishment of the cross — as though he were no better than a robber — that impudent man drunk with the dragon’s gall felt neither present nor future shame arising from Christ’s shame. But when his former companion rebuked him, and did not hesitate to believe in his heart and confess with his mouth that Christ was king and lord even while dying on the cross, he exchanged the robber’s mark, to which he had been bound, for the mark of Christ — under which, from the punishment of a robber, he merited the triumph of a martyr. So whatever simoniacs appear to do rightly or to have that is excellent, it is all the work of antichrist — achieved, as it were, through recognition of his mark, that is, perfidy, impressed on the right hand and forehead — even though they, with the Jews, call their antichrist by the name of Christ.

## CAPUT XLIII.

### By What Mark Simoniacs Are Known to Be Antichrists

Just as the Arians are known by the mark of antichrist — inasmuch as they preach that the Son is less than the Father and the Holy Spirit less than both — so too the simoniacs are no less known by the mark of antichrist, inasmuch as they sell and buy, or believe and assert that the Holy Spirit can be sold and bought. They do not traffic under antichrist only in secret, as other heretics do, but moreover openly and in a worldly fashion they put the Holy Spirit up for sale like a cheap slave, and bargain for and receive a secular price for him. No one can sell or buy him except one marked with the mark of antichrist — that is, with perfidy — on the right hand and forehead, namely in the principal part of his thought and action, so that he may continually enrich antichrist through his trafficking.
