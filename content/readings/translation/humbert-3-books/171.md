Let them also attend to how great a penance they must undergo who know \[what they have done\], since they observe that not even the Jews who were to be baptized were excused from penance, even though they acted in ignorance. And certainly, by human judgment, ignorance ought to have excused them. But it was inexcusable, because, as the Apostle says: “He who is ignorant will be ignored.” And O how many and how great crowds of simple and rustic folk, seeking the truth in ignorance, have been seduced by heretics — crowds whom we believe to have justly perished, though how it was just we cannot fully comprehend. And truly, “whoever touches pitch will be defiled by it, and whoever associates with the proud will put on pride.” “He who is wicked toward himself — to whom else will he be good?” Let them acknowledge, even when warned, that all who are thieves and robbers “do not come except to steal and to destroy.” From whose blood-stained hands they have received not only nothing of sanctification but also a very great measure of defilement, so much so that the soul that was once faithful has become a harlot, “full of judgment.” And in that place where justice once rested there now dwells a murderer; its silver has been turned into dross and its wine mixed with water. And why is this? Because all its princes are faithless, companions of thieves, by whom those persuaded in ignorance of the purity of the Catholic faith and unwary in their trust receive, in place of Christ, that seditious robber who said in his heart, “I will place my throne in the north; I will be like the Most High” — and that murderer who was such from the beginning. If those who were heretofore ready praisers and devoted receivers of the Lord have become not his wicked blasphemers and importunate petitioners for the robber, their intention seeks that man alone, even when their voice feigns to invoke God. Whom, I ask, do they invoke, if not Mammon — those who would not utter a word or open their mouths or lift a hand for God or for their neighbor, unless they were receiving or expecting money? But “the kindly spirit of wisdom will not free the evil-speaker from his lips.” Indeed, since God is a witness of his reins and the true searcher of his heart and a hearer of his tongue, he has not freed the accursed heretic from his lips — for whom there is nothing other than blessing himself in his own voice than condemning himself and cursing himself. And even if the Spirit of the Lord is a hearer of his blessing tongue, because he fills the orb of the earth and contains all things, yet because he has knowledge of the voice, the searcher of reins and heart does not care what a man says but toward what end he speaks in saying it. The Holy Spirit himself, spurning voices and outward devotions of this kind, bears witness through Isaiah: “When you stretch out your hands, I will turn my eyes away from you; and when you multiply prayer, I will not hear. Your hands are full of blood.” Surely companions of thieves and robbers are themselves thieves and robbers, as Solomon says: “He who keeps company with the foolish becomes like them; and he who participates with a thief hates his own soul.” Moreover, thieves and robbers are men of blood and deceit, and whoever is their partner must be blood-stained and fraudulent. Hence, because his hands are full of blood and of the fellowship of associated robbers, his prayer is repelled by the most pure majesty. As the wise man says: “He who turns away his ear so as not to hear the law — his prayer is an abomination.” And if this happens to those who do not hear the law commanding that the Lord be honored from one’s own and home-grown substance, what will happen to those who impiously sell the alms of another entrusted to them to be given freely, since they have freely received? Whose prayer or blessing is not only not heard or received, but is even turned into sin and a curse, since “there is no truth in their mouth, their heart is vain; their throat is an open sepulcher, with their tongues they have dealt deceitfully; the poison of asps is under their lips. Their mouth is full of cursing and bitterness, their feet are swift to shed blood. Destruction and misery are in their ways, and the way of peace they have not known; there is no fear of God before their eyes.” “Will not all those who work iniquity understand — those who devour my people as they eat bread? They have not called upon the Lord.” And who more devours the people of God as one eats bread than those who carry all the sacraments of the Christian religion as merchandise
