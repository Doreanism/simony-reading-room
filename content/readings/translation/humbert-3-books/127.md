A foolish and untaught people have taken for themselves such a priest. If you were to question them faithfully — who made them priests? — they will presently answer and say: ‘I was recently ordained bishop by the archbishop, and I gave him a hundred solidi so that I might deserve to obtain episcopal grace; had I not given them, I would not be bishop today. For which reason it is better for me to bring gold from my purse than to lose such a great priesthood. I gave gold and bought the episcopate. Those solidi, if I live, I am confident I will shortly recover. I ordain priests, I consecrate deacons, and I receive gold; for I am also confident of extracting a profit from ordinations to the other orders as well. Behold, the gold I gave, I have received back in my own purse, and I have obtained the episcopate for free.’ This is precisely what I grieve over: that the archbishop made a bishop in a carnal way. For on account of money he ordained one who is spiritually leprous. ‘Your money,’ he says, ‘perish with you, because you have bought the grace of the Holy Spirit’s gift at a price, and you have consummated a wretched commerce to the destruction of souls’; and ignorant and untaught people cry out at their ordinations and say, ‘You are worthy and just,’ while the wretched conscience says, ‘You are unworthy and unjust.’ For the bishop of this sort pronounces to the people: ‘Peace be with you.’ To carnal eyes he appears as a great bishop; to the divine gaze he is seen as greatly leprous. By money he acquired an undeserved rank, and before God he lost the inner man: the flesh received dignity and the soul lost its honor. The flesh, a handmaid, became mistress of the soul, and the soul, which was mistress, became a servant to the flesh. The flesh rules over peoples and the soul serves the devil. He purchased the priesthood for the flesh and prepared loss for the soul. And what does it profit such a man if he gains the whole world yet suffers the loss of his soul? or what exchange will a man give for his soul \[Matthew 16:26\]? What he gave when he was ordained bishop was gold, and what he lost was a soul. When he ordained another, what he received was money, and what he gave was leprosy. These are the merchandise of the wicked, to their ruin. Yet I question our brother and fellow bishop — for I too am a bishop, and I speak with a bishop. Tell me then briefly, brother bishop: when you gave money, what did you receive?’ ‘I received episcopal grace.’ ‘Then I ask you: why is this grace called by such a name?’ ‘Why? Because, I think, it is called grace \[*gratia*\] for the reason that it is given freely \[*gratis*\].’ ‘Therefore, if grace is given freely and is not valued in gold, why do you purchase grace with money?’ ‘Because,’ he says, ‘it would not be given to me if it were not bought with money; nor would I have been ordained bishop if I had not given money.’ Therefore, as appears from your own answers, you did not receive grace when you were ordained, because you did not merit it as a free gift. And therefore, brother, if you did not receive grace, how could you have become a bishop? For the Lord says to the disciples: ‘Freely you have received, freely give’ \[Matthew 10:8\]. Why then did you think you could possess a freely-given grace by paying a price? For, as I see it, in giving gold you lost it and did not obtain holy grace. Yet let me ask the brother bishop once more, so that we may not seem to have passed over any of the true proofs. Who gives episcopal grace, brother — God or man?’ ‘Without doubt God,’ he answers; ‘yet God gives through man: man imposes hands, God bestows grace; the priest extends a suppliant right hand, and God blesses with a mighty right hand; the bishop initiates the rank, and God confers the dignity.’ ‘O justice, O equity! If money is given to the man, who in the ordination accomplishes nothing more than the service entrusted to him, why is everything denied to God, who bestows the very rank on you? Does it seem just to you that the servant be honored while the Lord suffers injury? And does the priest unjustly receive money while God suffers injury from man? But because God expects nothing from you for the rank he has granted, the Lord was willing to grant it freely to man; yet a rapacious bishop exacts money from man as if it were free: God, being genuinely generous, gave freely to man, and an unworthy priest plundered him without cause. “For what do you have that you did not receive?
