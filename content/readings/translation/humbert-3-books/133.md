Therefore, as long as ruin and uncleanness are what is possessed, and they in turn possess their holder, what can be given and received but ruin and uncleanness — the only things they have acquired? And so, because ruin and leprosy are necessarily concluded to be what was sold and bought, it follows that merchants of this kind are improperly called priests and clerics, but properly called the ruined and the leprous; and their corrupt laying on of hands confers only the evil it alone acquired, granting no good whatsoever, since no good was acquired by trafficking in this way. For what is unjustly taken can never be justly given. Hence such men bring ruin and defilement upon others as well as themselves — they do not sanctify or purify — as scripture cries out plainly: *Whatever the unclean touches shall become unclean.* And: *The Spirit of discipline will flee the deceitful and will withdraw from thoughts that are without understanding, and will be overtaken by oncoming iniquity.* He is called the Spirit of discipline because, upon whomsoever he breathes, he makes them disciplined, and through them distributes the gifts of his grace, as he wills, to each for their formation. But the undisciplined he drives far from himself — or rather, since they are deceitful, he flees them. And who is more devastatingly exposed as deceitful than the one who, contrary to Catholic discipline — of whose special administration the Holy Spirit is held to be the steward — pretends to be what he neither is nor can in any degree become: namely, that a bishop or a cleric of whatever other order may be obtained for money?

## CHAPTER XX.

### That simoniacs are without faith, and that they deprive those who assent to them of faith.

Indeed, other hypocrites are less harmful in comparison with these, for they scarcely injure anyone but themselves; but these men slaughter Christians in crowds along with themselves, continually infecting them with their leprosy and making them participants in the damnation or ruin that was sold or bought, under the pretext of ordaining or sanctifying them. These open pretenders and crafty men provoke, rather than appease, the wrath of God above all others, because these sold vendors daily sell the sheep and oxen of Christ — that is, the simple and the clever, or indeed the weak and the strong. And to whom do they sell them, if not — as blessed Augustine says — to the devil? For where the deceived believe that priests can be made for a price and that the Holy Spirit can be sold and bought, the firstborn of their hearts, that is, their faith, is shown to be corrupted; and with faith corrupted, whatever goods may have existed are necessarily corrupted to the point of being utterly destroyed, because the sole virginity of the soul is incorrupt faith — that faith which the apostle feared would be corrupted in the Corinthians by false apostles, when he said to them: *I am jealous for you with the jealousy of God, for I betrothed you to one husband, to present you as a pure virgin to Christ. But I fear that, as the serpent seduced Eve by his cunning, so your minds may be corrupted and fall away from the simplicity that is in Christ Jesus.* This very faith the same apostle, according to the grace given to him as a wise architect, laid as a foundation for them. Which foundation — as he himself goes on to say — *no one can lay other than that which has been laid, which is Christ Jesus.* Now Christ Jesus through faith dwells in hearts strengthened by the Holy Spirit, as he himself suggests to the Ephesians: *I bow my knees to the Father of my Lord Jesus Christ, from whom all fatherhood in heaven and on earth takes its name, that he may grant you, according to the riches of his glory, to be strengthened with power through his Spirit in the inner man, that Christ may dwell through faith in your hearts.* If therefore, according to so eminent a mystic and one caught up to the third heaven or even into paradise itself, faith is the foundation of Christianity, and Christ Jesus is the foundation, it remains that faith is Christ, who, dwelling in us as we are strengthened by the Spirit of God the Father, governs us. Hence it is beyond doubt clear what sort of person among Christians must be reckoned the one who holds wrong views about the Holy Spirit. For it is established that he has neither the Son nor the Father. For as it is proclaimed in Catholic teaching, the works of the Trinity — of Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — are inseparable; and whoever is deprived of the visitation of any one of the three is necessarily deprived also of the other two.
