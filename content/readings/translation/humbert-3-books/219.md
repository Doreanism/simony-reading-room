Indeed, had not the crowd of priests favored Pilate with the persistence of their outcry, Pilate would have released the Lord Jesus, and the princes themselves would not have accomplished their will in the capture of the Lord. But granted that by necessity the weak and base common people follow the will of their princes in some manner — let them at least take care that this very necessity does not become for them at once the will and the liberty to do evil. Those whom \[princes\] ought to restrain from harming all other men, they should restrain all the more from presuming upon ecclesiastical matters, bearing in mind that “if a man sins against a man, God can be appeased for him; but if a man sins against God, who will pray for him?” Let them also bear in mind that peoples are punished more numerously — and sometimes even while their princes escape temporal punishment — because they themselves are the greater number, and their princes fewer; or because it is from these peoples as from their roots that the presumption and wickedness of princes chiefly springs. Hence it is that Pharaoh perished together with all his people; and that 70 men and 50,000 of the populace of Beth-shemesh \[were struck down\] because they had looked upon the ark of God; and that when David, who had ordered the people to be numbered, escaped, 70,000 men were cut down by the angel over three days. And it should be noted that when princes with their people have offended more specifically in a divine matter, they have perished together with the people; whereas when they have transgressed in a human matter, the princes have escaped while the people were cut down.

## CAPUT XVII.

### On the merits of Israel and Judah as a type of heretics and Catholics.

But since we have taken some care to deter and at the same time to invite the princes and peoples of our own time by the examples of the princes and peoples of the Israelites, it remains that anyone who wishes to discern the worth before God and his true Israelites of the prayer and conduct of the two peoples — Israel and Judah — should read through the prophets, and especially Hosea and Amos, and then Abijah king of Judah, who in the role and voice of Catholics cries out against Jeroboam and his people — who were beyond doubt heretics — as follows: “Hear, Jeroboam and all Israel: do you not know that the Lord God of Israel gave the kingdom over Israel to David forever, to him and to his sons by a covenant of salt?” And a little after: “Now therefore you say that you are able to resist the kingdom of God, which he holds over the sons of David; and you have a great multitude of people and the golden calves which Jeroboam made for you as gods; and you have cast out the priests of the Lord, the sons of Aaron and the Levites, and have made priests for yourselves as all the peoples of the lands do: whoever comes and fills his hand with a bull and seven rams becomes a priest of those who are not gods. But as for us, the Lord is our God, whom we have not forsaken, and the priests who minister to the Lord are of the sons of Aaron and the Levites in their order. They offer burnt offerings to the Lord every day, morning and evening, and incense prepared according to the precepts of the law, and the loaves are set out on the most pure table, and with us is the golden lampstand and its lamp, that it may be lit at evening. We indeed keep the commandments of the Lord our God, whom you have forsaken. Therefore God is the leader in our army, and his priests who sound and resound the trumpets against you. Sons of Israel, do not fight against the Lord God of your fathers, for it will not prosper you.” Hence if anyone wisely considers the type of the heretics’ priesthood in the Israelite people separated from Judah, he will understand more clearly than light what their doctrines and ordinations accomplish.
