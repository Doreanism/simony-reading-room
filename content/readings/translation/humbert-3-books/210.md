## Chapter X.

### How Much Freer the Church of the Greeks Is than That of the Latins from the Power of Laymen.

Therefore, as has been said, these statutes of the holy fathers and devout rulers concerning ecclesiastical persons and properties are to this day observed inviolably in the empire overseas \[i.e., Byzantium\], and the disposition of them is left solely to metropolitans and other bishops. Hence any administration of a church is arranged solely at their discretion, whether they wish to entrust it to some clergyman freely or not freely, and it is not customarily sold save by metropolitans or bishops or members of their households. Although this is heretical and utterly detestable on account of the sale alone, it is nonetheless more tolerable for those churches of God than for ours, which, as is shown above, are continuously sold four times over. For the arrogance and greed of the rulers of our age and empire does not allow them to be restrained within prescribed limits; but transgressing divine and human laws, which fall silent before arms, they presume to possess everything as their own, including ecclesiastical properties, so that no clergyman can dwell in them or live from them without receiving them by grant or purchase from these rulers. The result is that those who ought to be the freest of all people — whose very inheritance is God himself, and who in turn are God’s own portion — are made the most contemptible of all. For even the lowliest servant or hired laborer of any layman is compelled to serve none but the one to whom he belongs, and is subject to him alone with all his possessions, perfectly free in relation to all others. Likewise the possession of any layman, whether of modest or great means, is subject only to the lordship of its owner and is not seized or sold by any of the rulers. Priests alone, the church alone, bear the assault of every man’s contention, violence, power, and rapine — to such a degree that if laymen chance to donate anything to the churches of God for the redemption of their souls, it soon becomes a matter of litigation and sale, that which was previously held peacefully by the donors themselves. And by whom above all are these things claimed and sold, if not by those who profess themselves advocates and defenders of God’s churches? By the emperors themselves, by the rulers themselves and their minions. Under the name and pretext of defense they arrogate to themselves, alienate, or sell not only the churches but everything that belongs to them, both in goods and persons and in offices. Let those who are reproved take heed of how much less they reverence God than men of whatever condition subject to their power, whose possessions they fear and beware to seize. They think they must yield to men beneath them, while not thinking they must yield to God above them. They fear and are ashamed before men, for whom they preserve earthly and civil right, while they neither fear nor blush before God, for whom they reserve neither earthly right nor heavenly law. And though they do not hesitate to show themselves sacrilegious toward God, whose inheritance they claim for themselves, they desire to be called and held as devout and Catholic princes by men, when they are truly enemies of God, against whom they conspire in unison to possess his sanctuary as an inheritance. Hence, according to the prophecy the psalmist utters in imprecation, they are to be reckoned like Oreb and Zeeb and Zebah and Zalmunna \[Psalm 83\], who were indeed princes of foreigners and enemies of the Israelites.
