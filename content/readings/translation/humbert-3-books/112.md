## Chapter VIII.

### Corruptor.

Hence, from what is above in the canons of the apostles, which Saint Isidore confirms are to be received: “If any bishop, presbyter, or deacon receives a second laying on of hands from someone, let him be deposed, and likewise the one who attempted to ordain him. For it is impossible for those who have been baptized or ordained by such a person to be either faithful or clerics.”

### Corrector.

When he says “hence, from what is above,” it is clear that he joins the preceding chapter of Saint Gregory to the supposed \[spurious\] chapter of the apostles in order to assert that there is no difference between the ordination of Catholics and that of heretics — while \[in fact\] what Saint Gregory testifies specifically concerning the ordination of Catholics, this man attempts to transfer generally to the side of heretics, as if the apostles had forbidden those ordained by heretics, as they say, from receiving a second laying on of hands from Catholics. Hence, from the canons of the apostles, we know that only the fifty chapters \[*capitula L*\] are to be received — not so much on the authority of blessed Isidore as on that of the most holy Pope Clement and of his very many successors. But as can be proven from authentic and ancient exemplars, the present chapter is established to have been deprived and stripped, in its own proper sense and integrity near its end, and a foreign fabrication appended to it under the appearance of truth. For in the older and more reliable canons it is found thus: “If anyone has received a second laying on of hands, let him be cast down, along with the one who attempted to reordain him — unless he can demonstrate that the person was ordained by heretics.” Now as for what that most wicked tavern-keeper mixed in from the waters of a broken cistern after removing the wine of Sorek \[*vinum Soreth*\] — namely, saying that those baptized or ordained by such persons cannot be either faithful or clerics — this is found neither in these canons of the holy apostles nor in any canon of the Catholic fathers. In this his fraudulent presumption and the novelty of his profane wording, it shines clearer than the sun how much he is to be received or heard in the preceding or subsequent parts of his disputation. For he manifestly attacks and, so far as lies in him, nullifies and strives to void the holy Nicene synod, which contains the very chapter that that frenzied man supposed he would throw at us in our defense in what follows — the chapter namely in which the Cathari, those heretics closer to the Catholic faith, when they come over to the Catholic \[Church\], are decreed to receive the laying on of hands and so to remain in the Catholic clergy. Nor is it to be supposed that the holy fathers decreed this concerning the laity of the Novatians, who had received no laying on of hands for the attainment of orders, but concerning their clergy, upon whom hands had been laid by the superiors of their sect so that they might be promoted to any ministry of the altar. When these men come to the Catholic Church and Catholic priests lay hands on them, are they not known to be receiving a second laying on of hands? They receive this from Catholics because they are proven to have been ordained by heretics — as is read in the aforesaid canon of the apostles according to the ancient true text, which this erroneous corrector did not shrink from suppressing, and is thereby found to have publicly wronged the holy Nicene council as well. Indeed let his conclusion also be considered, in which he defined that those baptized or ordained by such persons can be neither faithful nor clerics. If this were so — setting aside the others for the moment — it would be established that Novatian heretics passing over to the Catholic Church flee as it were from the face of a lion, but a bear meets them: since through the laying on of hands by a Catholic priest this is conferred upon them to their destruction — that henceforth neither they themselves, nor those baptized or ordained by them, can be considered faithful or clerics. By this reasoning it would have been more tolerable for them to remain as they were than to come to the Catholic Church and there receive a laying on of hands after the one they had received from their own.
