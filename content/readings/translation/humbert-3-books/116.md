The sacrament of the laying on of hands is to be held in great reverence. It cannot be performed by others than the highest priests, and in the time of the apostles it is read or known to have been performed by none other than the apostles themselves; nor can it ever be performed or ought it ever to be done by any others than those who hold their place. For if it is presumed otherwise, let it be held null and void, and let it never be reckoned among the ecclesiastical sacraments.” And from the same source, from the Council of Arles, whose author is said to have been the blessed Pope Silvester: “If anyone comes to the church from heresy, let them be asked about the creed, and if it is seen that he was baptized in the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, let only the hand be laid upon him, so that he may receive the Holy Spirit.” By these and many other similar testimonies of the holy fathers we are plainly instructed how highly the founders and preachers of the Catholic faith regarded both the baptism of heretics and the laying on of their hands — neither of which was performed without chrism. Indeed, those coming from the heretics to the Catholic Church receive both the laying on of hands with the invocation of the Holy Spirit from Catholic bishops and are anointed with holy chrism, as if they had hitherto received none of these things.

## CAPUT XI.

Furthermore, from other rules of the holy fathers now to be adduced, we shall endeavor to show how highly they esteemed — or rather, how they decreed — heretics to be regarded in any of their actions or conduct, judging them worse than pagans and Jews, as we are taught most clearly by John Chrysostom’s commentary on Matthew. He says: “When the King sees in the church of Christ one not clothed in the wedding garment — that is, in true faith — he commands his ministers, that is, the evil spirits of seductions (for he renders evil through evil spirits), such as was the spirit of error which God sent to seduce Ahab, as it is written in the Third Book of Kings \[1 Kings 22\], or such as the working of error which God will give to those who have not received the love of God, that they may believe a lie \[2 Thessalonians 2:11\]. And a little later: ‘And seizing him by his very works and counsels, they cast them into the outer darkness’ — that is, into the error of pagans, Jews, or heretics. And perhaps the darkness of pagans is closer, the outer darkness of Jews is further out, but the outer darkness of heretics is the worst, because pagans despise the truth they have not heard, Jews \[despise\] the truth they did not believe, but heretics \[despise\] the truth they heard and learned.” From this judgment of Chrysostom, heretics are proven to be worse than pagans and Jews, although among heretics themselves some are closer to the truth, that is, to the Catholic faith, and others more distant. Moreover, in what matters heretics are to be shunned by Catholics and kept far away as if they were pagans or Jews, we are taught both by many writings of the holy fathers and by the following chapters of Catholic councils.

### From the Council of Laodicea, Chapter VI.

Heretics who remain in heresy are not to be granted entry into the house of God.
