For why did Jesus come in the flesh, if not to present to himself — through the exceeding love by which he laid down his life for them — a glorious Church from persons of diverse qualities and from all nations of the peoples, having neither spot nor wrinkle nor anything of the kind, in which his one dove, his friend, sister, and immaculate bride, might rest at peace, and in her reigning he the immaculate bridegroom might himself reign, and so, with all his own consummated in himself who is one, he himself the one God might be all in all? It is above all against this wonderful and desirable union that those men contend and scheme who do not shrink from selling the Holy Spirit — by whom so great a diversity of members is gathered together, compacted, and united under the one head Christ in an arrangement beyond reproach and comprehension. For the Spirit, like the most powerful and fervent pitch or glue, by the charity which he pours out in the hearts of the saints, conglutinates and joins the body of Christ, shaped and prepared with fitting bonds and joints, and makes it grow unto the increase of God, where out of people of diverse calling and way of life he effects one body and one spirit, according to the vocation by which they are called, in one hope. When these heretics presume to snatch away this most effective glue of God’s building, what great and pitiable ruin do they cause? In such ruins, as is typical of crumbling walls, nothing will be found in order, nothing that is not confused. In them there is no longer any beauty, only the squalor of nettles and briars — that is, of lust and avarice — and the horror of a serpent’s lair.

## CAPUT XXXII.

Concerning how heretics who fornicate from God cause others to fornicate as well.

See how the profane simoniacs, fornicating from God, stain and destroy that glorious bride whom Christ cleansed, that she might be without spot, by the washing of water in the word, and stretched out her wrinkles on the scaffold of the cross, that she might be made without wrinkle. Whom now — alas! — fornicators stain with impunity, contaminating by the contagion of their uncleanness the baptismal bath and the other forms of the divine sacraments granted to her from heaven to preserve her beauty, and emptying them by their trafficking; and they wrinkle her no less when they deform her simple intention, like a fair skin, with the duplicity of their craftiness. Thus deservedly does that bridegroom, beautiful in form above the sons of men, threaten his most beautiful bride, whose beauty pleases him above all things, lest she begin to wander among the flocks of his companions — that is, of the heretics — who, by feeding their flocks of goats, pretend to be friends of the bridegroom, yet, jealous of the bride not for the bridegroom but for themselves, meditate nothing but adultery. Hence, according to the exposition of blessed Hieronymus, the bridegroom threatens his bride in the Song of Songs and says: “Either know yourself, for you are the bride of a king, and beautiful, and made beautiful by me — inasmuch as I myself presented to myself a glorious Church having neither spot nor wrinkle — or know this: that if you do not know yourself and do not know your dignity, you will suffer what follows.
