## CAPUT XXXVIIII.

### What are the signs and wonders of Catholics.

Now, how heretics cause the earth and its inhabitants to worship the first beast, blessed Jerome shows as follows, as was already cited above: *Just as idols are made by the hand of a craftsman, so the perverse doctrine of heretics turns whatever it counterfeits into an idol, and causes Antichrist to be worshipped in place of Christ.* Since those heretics seduce the inhabitants of the earth — that is, the Church — small and great, rich and poor, free and slave, by the signs they perform in the sight of that beast, the Lord also foretells this to his own in the Gospel: *False christs and false prophets will arise and will give signs and wonders, so as to lead astray, if possible, even the elect. And then if they say to you, Behold, here is Christ, or behold, there, do not believe, do not go, do not follow them* \[Matthew 24:24–26\]. For just as the holy prophets before the first coming of Jesus Christ, and the holy apostles and their imitators before the second, have given and give many and great signs and wonders, so also the heretics — who are called by the Lord false prophets and false christs, as it were Antichrists — perform theirs before their prince Antichrist. But let it be inquired what signs and wonders the worshippers of Christ have given or give, and what the worshippers of Antichrist give. Indeed, the heralds of Christ’s first coming set forth and proclaimed the sacraments of Christ and his Church through all their acts and words: as Moses, in figure of our high priest, led the people of Israel across the Red Sea, fed them with manna from heaven and gave them water from the rock to drink, and purified the priests and the tabernacle with all its furnishings not with blood alone, but also sanctified them with chrism. This image or shadow of Catholic truth did not cease until Truth itself — Christ, who is also the Way and the Life — came. Then Truth, having risen from the earth, transfers all nations through the heralds of his second coming by true baptism, feeds them with the true and living Bread, gives them new wine to drink, and not only redeems his priests but without ceasing sanctifies his whole Church, first redeemed by his blood, with the anointing of his grace. And how he continues to do this, the apostle shows in commending the bread and wine of Christ: *As often as you eat this bread and drink this cup, you proclaim the death of the Lord, until he comes* \[1 Corinthians 11:26\]. Therefore, leaving aside for now the curing of the sick and the raising of the dead and the other benefits and punishments, which were and are also signs and wonders, let us say something about baptism, sacrifice, and unction alone, which seem to us to be the principal and greatest signs and wonders, because without these no one can live to God. In these things, then, he is with us from his first coming until his second, which will be at the end of the age. These are signs in such a way that they are also realities. For what they are and contain in themselves, they signify. Thus the visible font of baptism is a sign that nonetheless contains within itself the reality — namely, the Holy Spirit — who provides spiritual and invisible cleansing to the inner person in the mind through the bodily and visible sign shown to the eyes of the flesh from the immersion of the outer person in the water.
