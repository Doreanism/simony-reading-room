Why this, if not to make known that heretics and their supporters have lost the true sacrifice and sanctification? For in the harvests and vineyards the sacrament of the body and blood of the Lord, which is prepared from them for the life of the faithful people, is not unfittingly understood. In the olive groves, however, is the oil of sacred unction. With these emptied out, there remains to them from all the sacraments of the Church only the water of baptism alone, which alone they can administer — and only to perdition — as was amply demonstrated above. And these great evils are said to have been done, or to be done, by our Samson Christ, not because he commanded such things, but because he justly permitted them to come to pass. Paul also warns the Philippians to beware of these foxes who walk alongside and, with pretext or envy, so preach Christ crucified as to strive to make his cross void, saying: “Be imitators of me and observe those who walk according to the pattern you have in us. For many walk, of whom I have often told you, and now tell you even weeping, as enemies of the cross of Christ.” Showing that the fire of their perdition dwells in their tails, he adds: “Whose end is destruction.” Furthermore, as the naturalists \[*physiologi*\] assert, a hungry fox throws itself into a filthy place and feigns death, and so suddenly seizes and kills the incautious birds that believe it. This is known to be the practice of heretics as well: hungering for earthly gain or popular favor, they feign death to this world with a certain counterfeit continence, and strangle souls that are spiritual indeed but still carnal and rendered heedless of dangers, with their treachery, and incorporate the devil together with themselves. Among these was also Herod, who pretended that he desired to see Jesus for the sake of his signs, when in fact he sought to kill him. Hence the Lord himself, when this was reported to him, answered: “Go, tell that fox” — which is plainly shown in the Passion, where Jesus is recounted to have been mocked and despised by Herod and his whole army.

## CHAPTER XXIII.

### A comparison of the acts of Judas and the simoniacs.

But let us now consider the manner of the betrayal and of the betrayer of Christ, and from a comparison with his act and merit weigh the act and merit of those who betray the Holy Spirit. Judas, that most wicked trafficker, said to his accomplices: “Whomsoever I shall kiss, he it is; seize him and lead him away carefully.” The simoniacs likewise say to theirs: “Upon whomsoever among you we lay hands in prayer, without delay the Holy Spirit comes upon him, and he must receive him and keep him for himself for the free exercise of his ecclesiastical office.” In this presumption they become far worse than Judas himself, who neither supposed that the Lord, once betrayed, had remained with him, nor gave careless instructions but warned that he should be led away carefully — whereas these betray the Holy Spirit in such a way that they believe he has remained with them, and they command that the grace poured out upon others through betrayal be handled at will and by their authority. And Judas indeed, by greeting and kissing, simulating peace and love by mouth, nod, or gesture, though he had none in his heart, betrayed the Lord Jesus for a price; but these, by supplicating, praying, laying on hands, and performing the other things proper to lawful priests as a show before others, betray the Holy Spirit for a price, as it is supposed. What answer more fitting could be given by the Holy Spirit to their words and outward displays of reverence than that which Christ gave to Judas? “Judas, do you betray the Spirit of the Father and Son with a kiss?” — as if saying ironically: “Do you suppose me to have been so granted to you that you may even falsely hand me over and sell me to whomever you will? But woe to him through whom the Holy Spirit shall be betrayed for a price; it would have been better for him had he not been born.” And not without reason is this simoniac here called Judas, which is interpreted as “confession,” because just as that man betrayed his master by confessing with his mouth and saying “Rabbi,” so this man tears apart the Holy Spirit by confessing and invoking the holy Trinity with his mouth, calling him by name God and Lord.
