The bread in their belly is turned into the gall of asps within them, because they turn the drink of life into a cup of poison for themselves, and die from the very thing from which they appear to be nourished unto life. Falling as they do into the error of heresy, it befalls them — as with the gall of asps — that these unhappy men die by their own bread. They find death in their sacrifice, because they seek life not at all in the sacraments of life or in the words of life. And it should be noted that the bread of such men is called in the book of blessed Job not the bread of God, but his — that is, the heretic’s — bread.

## CAPUT XIII.

That the Subverters of the Sacraments and of Christ’s Words Are of One Kind, and That They Neither Ask Nor Obtain Anything from Him.

Following the thought of blessed Gregory in this verse — who referred it to those who pervert the sacred scriptures — we have not inappropriately transferred it to those who pervert the sacraments of the altar, since the word of God and the bread of God are equally, indeed of the same, dignity and co-eternity. For the word of our God, according to Isaiah, “shall stand forever” \[Isaiah 40:8\]; and the word co-eternal with God the Father says in the Gospel: “My words shall not pass away” \[Matthew 24:35\]. Of him blessed Peter also says: “You have the words of eternal life” \[John 6:68\]. In the same way the bread of God — as the Word himself, yea, that very Bread, teaches in the Gospel — “descends from heaven and gives life to the world. And I am the living bread that came down from heaven; if anyone eats of this bread, he will live forever” \[John 6:33, 51\]. Hence it appears that the word of God and his bread are co-equal and co-eternal, because just as the former from the sacred scriptures, so the latter from the sacraments, grants eternal life. Nor is this surprising, since our one and the same Lord Jesus Christ is both the word of God in the holy scriptures and the bread of God in the sacraments. It follows, therefore, that with equal abhorrence we should shun the subverters of the divine eloquence and the subverters of the heavenly sacraments. Even if these men ask the heavenly Father, as it seems, for bread, yet they are given a stone; if for a fish, a serpent; if for an egg, a scorpion — for, as the Psalmist prophetically pronounces: “The Lord renders to them according to their works and according to the wickedness of their devices; according to the works of their hands he requites them, he renders to them their desert. Because they have not understood the works of the Lord, he shall destroy them and shall not build them up” \[Psalm 28:4–5\]. Nor is this surprising; for they do not ask what is heavenly from the heavenly Father, and therefore they obtain nothing heavenly from him, but by the just permission and judgment of God the Father himself, they receive from the devil — as wicked children from the worst of fathers — instead of the bread of charity or of the sacrament, the stone of obduration; instead of the fish of faith, the serpent of faithlessness; instead of the egg of hope, the scorpion of utter despair.
