For since they would not use reason to distinguish good from evil, they were admonished and rebuked to come to their senses not by words but by signs: for admonition and rebuke were administered to them alike when the rods of Moses devoured their rods, when the water turned to blood, and when the frogs that had been brought forth returned to what they were through Moses alone, while those sorcerers could only add to the plagues for themselves and others, but were not able to remove them. When they had afterward lost even the possibility of deceiving and harming in the third plague, they said: “This is the finger of God.” From this it is clear that the Holy Spirit — against whose working in Moses they were resisting — first emptied their deceptions of power, and then, in the third plague, which was the sixth in sequence, on account of their obstinacy, afflicted and condemned even them along with their cattle with boils and festering sores, so that they could no longer stand before Moses. Now the Lord himself in the Gospel indicates that the finger of God is the Holy Spirit when he says: “If by the finger of God I cast out demons.” Another evangelist, explaining this, says: “If by the Spirit of God.” From this let heretics consider what they are likened to, and let them beware of likening themselves and their secret rites — or, as they themselves presume to call them, their sacraments — to the sacraments of Catholics, with whom the finger of God cooperates, lest for their irrational obstinacy they be struck irrecoverably by the Holy Spirit, whom they do not fear to resist defiantly, as the irrational beasts were struck. Let them also consider that those sorcerers, through none of their invocations and curses, were able to remove or diminish the afflictions of their people and themselves, but rather increased them; and, having at last confessed the finger of God, let them begin to spare themselves and their followers by humbly declining ecclesiastical orders, rather than bringing ruin by arrogantly usurping them. If they do not do this, let them know without any doubt that they are more wicked than Jannes and Jambres — for these, feeling that the finger of God was against them, yielded and confessed it, even though, being unrepentant, they did not merit pardon. But the simoniacs possess nothing of all this beyond mere obstinacy: they are so much the more wicked as those sorcerers were the lesser in crime — namely in that those were magicians of Pharaoh, a mortal man after all, whereas these themselves, under the name of religion, are panders for the ancient serpent. Therefore, terrified by the examples of Cain, Balaam, and Korah — of whom we have spoken above — and by those of these sorcerers of whom we now speak, let all who desire to rejoice forever in the peace of the catholic church beware presumption, flee avarice, and shun obstinacy, always keeping before their eyes that sentence of the blessed Paul: “Whatever was written was written for our instruction. And all these things happened to them as a figure; but they were written for our correction, upon whom the ends of the ages have come.”

## CAPUT VI.

### In which Christians are warned to avoid the supplanting of heretics, whose evil and punishment are shown herein.

And now, “O priests, O kings of the earth and all peoples, understand; be instructed, O princes and all judges of the earth; young men and maidens, old men with the younger — embrace discipline, lest at any time the Lord be angry and you perish from the just way.” Love justice. Reflect on who has given you the pledge of the Spirit, and do not grieve that same Holy Spirit of God, in whom you are sealed for the day of redemption. “Do you not know that your bodies are the temple of the Holy Spirit, whom you have from God, and that you are not your own?” “For you are the temple of the living God, and the Holy Spirit dwells in you. If any man violates the temple of God, God will destroy him; for the temple of God is holy, which you are.” You were bought at a great price — do not sell yourselves again to the devil for the smallest coin, which until you have paid back you may hope in vain to leave the prison of the strict judge. “Do not make the house of God the Father a house of business,” nor make “the house of prayer a den of robbers.” “Have sound thoughts of the Lord in goodness,” presume on him to begin, do, and complete what is good, “and seek him in simplicity of heart” — not in any duplicity, for “you cannot serve” or “cleave to God and mammon.” Indeed, since the Holy Spirit himself is simplicity
