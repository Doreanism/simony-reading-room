He has not the Son either, beside whom no one can lay another foundation for Christians — for lest we fail beneath the weight of so great a foundation, we are upheld and strengthened by the Holy Spirit. It remains, therefore, that whoever does not have the Holy Spirit — upon which the foundation that is Christ Jesus rests — does not have the foundation itself. If he does not have the foundation, he does not have Christ. If he does not have Christ, he does not have faith, through which Christ might dwell in his heart. It follows accordingly that whoever does not have faith is an unbeliever; and an unbeliever, because he does not have the Spirit of Christ, does not belong to him. To whom, then, does he belong? Surely to the one who was a murderer from the beginning, who did not stand in the truth, because truth is not in him — who is a liar and the father of lies, who, since he does not gather with Christ, scatters; and because he is not with him, is against him. Hence it is manifest that simoniacs have strayed from faith through greed — or rather have apostatized and made shipwreck of the faith — as the apostle says to Timothy: *The root of all evils is greed, which some, by pursuing, have strayed from the faith and have pierced themselves with many sorrows.* These wandering apostates and shipwrecked survivors of faith must plainly be called and held to be neither bishops nor clerics. For if merchants of earthly and temporal goods sometimes stray from faith through greed — when they rashly or cunningly swear and perjure themselves, taking the name of their God in vain and endangering their souls, in order to scrape together a little profit — how far, we may ask, do those stray from faith who not only hold their own souls for sale, but even strive to sell the Holy Spirit, God and Lord? They truly stray from faith in every direction, these men whose abominable trafficking from the beginning the Holy Spirit execrates; he flees from them far away. With his flight, the foundation that is Christ Jesus cannot remain in them, because the Spirit himself — whose office it is to support and strengthen — withdraws. Thus it comes about that, while these most greedy of mortals contend to purchase and sell the incomparable, they render malignant spirits all the more eager to destroy from without not only the walls of their good works, but also from within to undermine faith itself — the very foundation — and as though spurring one another on toward total destruction they say what the psalmist intimates in his lament: *Raze it, raze it, down to its very foundations.* Indeed, once the foundation — that is, Christ, who ever rests upon the Holy Spirit — is torn from human hearts, it is certain that such a house, forsaken and utterly destroyed, is abandoned by God and made into a den of robbers, who at once claim it for their sport; so that already bearing the empty name “Christian,” there remains in him nothing of faith, that is, of Christ at all; and thereby he is shown to be without God and worse than a pagan, because this man is at once an unbeliever and an apostate — whereas a pagan, though he is an unbeliever, cannot rightly be called or held an apostate from the Christian faith or way of life that he never possessed. From his thoughts too, as was said before, the Holy Spirit withdraws himself, because they are without understanding. And what thoughts are more utterly without understanding than those which suppose the Holy Spirit can be sold and bought? For whatever is irrationally thought concerning the creature is less harmful than what is irrationally thought concerning the Creator.

## CHAPTER XXI.

### Refutation of those who say that the Holy Spirit can be possessed and rightly given by one who received it unjustly.

Now if the Holy Spirit withdraws from those who suppose he can be sold, far more so does he withdraw from those who not only suppose this, but believe it so firmly that they preach it in words and writings to the ruin of many.
