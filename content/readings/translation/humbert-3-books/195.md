This is also intimated by the fact that from his time the dragon and the first beast appear in the Apocalypse as if on holiday \[Revelation 13:11–12\]. For the dragon and beast, already at ease since the pagans have been possessed by the devil from the beginning and the Jews pervaded by antichrist, give way, because the pseudo-prophet alone is not doubted to be able to prevail in seducing Christians and subjugating them to himself. The Babylon built by these three unclean spirits — composed of persons of three professions — is given by the Lord, as the prophet says, into the possession of that hedgehog \[Isaiah 34:11\] who did not stand in the truth \[John 8:44\], and into the swamps of the waters — that is, into the assemblies of profane crowds, whose minds the mists of errors possess, and whose bodies the stenches of every kind of lust possess. In these, nothing is produced except uncontrollable anger and vain pomp, hypocrisy and treachery, dullness and barren and shameless verbosity; and among these there are also the two daughters of the leech who say: “Give, give” \[Proverbs 30:15\]. For avarice is that most savage leech that will not release the skin until it is full of blood. Its two daughters are cupidity and rapine — which, though they cling to all the carnal, do so more vividly and manifestly in simoniacs. For these do not fail to say to one another: “Give, give.” For example: the greedy buyer says to the seller, “Give me the invisible grace”; and the rapacious seller in turn says to the buyer, “Give me the visible money.” Or alternatively: the buyer says, “Bring me the Holy Spirit,” and the seller responds: “Bring me sound coin.” Whence it is clear that they are inhabitants of the swamps of Babylon, who can confer nothing without a bite, without a wound, without blood. Unless they have first expelled the poison of their innate avarice by drinking some bitter draught, they can do nothing but invite pestilence.

## CAPUT XLVII.

That it profits heretics nothing toward the effect of faith or salvation, even if they chant the Catholic creed with loud voices.

Let the simoniacs proceed, then, and with loud voices confess that Jesus has come in the flesh — he whose body they are not satisfied to harass and tear apart, on whose death they feed. Let them, caught and convicted, stubbornly and shamelessly, as is their custom, recite the Catholic creed; let them cry out and proclaim with all the instruments of the human voice, as they say, that they believe in Jesus Christ, the only-begotten Son of God, who for us human beings and for our salvation descended from heaven and was incarnate of the Holy Spirit and the Virgin Mary and became man, and so forth. Let them also cry aloud, if they can and prefer, with an iron or bronze voice, and let their chests breathe out a hundredfold sounds by which they profess belief in the Holy Spirit, lord and life-giver, who proceeds from the Father and the Son, who together with the Father and the Son is adored and glorified, who spoke through the prophets, and in the one holy catholic and apostolic church, and so forth. Do they not, by inflicting the above-mentioned wounds on the body of Christ, which is his Church, deny that he has come in the flesh? Do they not, by heaping the above-mentioned blasphemies and injuries upon the Holy Spirit, deny that he is lord and to be adored and glorified together with the Father and the Son, and that he spoke through the prophets? And what the Novatians are said to deny — should it be proclaimed that the simoniacs confess? For although the Novatians are heard to chant our creed with clear and loud voices and to subscribe to it, they are nonetheless convicted of denying that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh — not because they wound his healthy members, but because they despair that his wounded members can be healed by the Church of God. And should simoniacs be listened to in any way when they cry out that they believe Jesus has come in the flesh or that the Holy Spirit is to be adored with the Father and the Son — they who not only do not heal the wounded members of Christ but also wound even the healthy ones unto death?
