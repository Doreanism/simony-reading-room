Christ. Those who wish to learn at what peril to their own bodies and at what loss of their property Mattathias and his sons and certain others avoided their communion may read the books of Maccabees. For many thousands of people of both sexes were crowned with glorious martyrdom at that time, not only in cities but also in the mountains and wilderness, for the law of God which they zealously upheld. Animated by this zeal, the priest Mattathias and his five sons also, abandoning whatever they had in the city, fled into the mountains and withdrew to a desert place, and there lived among the wild beasts in the mountains, remaining there and subsisting on grass, lest they become partakers in defilement. And in what respect have our less holy fathers in Christianity avoided communion with heretics any less than these men had already avoided it in Judaism? Let us also consider at the same time that as long as the priesthood of the Jews flourished, no vessels or money of the temple defended by angelic watches could be plundered or taken captive, even though Heliodorus under the pontiff Onias attempted it with stubborn determination. This is established as having then been accomplished without any punishment only where the priesthood was sold or presumed upon by one not of its proper tribe. Nor is it less worthy of consideration in the actions of the legitimate priests of that time to see with what zeal and in what manner the things they found defiled by heretics are to be expiated by Catholics. For we are instructed there concerning these matters as follows: Judas chose priests without blemish, having devotion to the law of God, and they cleansed the holy places and removed the stones of defilement to an unclean place. And he deliberated about the altar of burnt offerings that had been profaned, what should be done with it; and a good counsel came to him, to demolish it, lest it become a reproach to them, because the Gentiles had defiled it; and they tore it down and stored the stones in a fitting place on the mountain of the house, until a prophet should come and give an answer concerning them. And after purging the temple they took whole stones according to the law and built a new altar like the former one, and they rebuilt the holy places and the interior of the house and the courts, and they consecrated them. And they made new sacred vessels and brought in the lampstand and the altar of incense and the table into the temple and burned incense on the altar and lit the lamps that were on the lampstand, and they shone in the temple. And they set the bread of the Presence on the table and hung up the curtains, and when all this was done they rose before dawn. And from firestones from which fire had been struck they offered sacrifice according to the law on the new altar of burnt offerings that they had made; and having done these things they fell prostrate on the ground and prayed to the Lord that they might not fall into such evils any more, but that if they had ever sinned, they might be chastised more gently by him and not be handed over to blasphemous or barbarian nations. And on the very day on which the Gentiles had polluted the temple, on that same day the purification came to pass, on the twenty-fifth \[day\] of the new month of Chislev, and they celebrated with joy for eight days in the manner of the Feast of Tabernacles. And they decreed by common precept and decree of the whole Jewish people that these days should be observed every year for eight days. And they adorned the front of the temple with golden crowns and small shields and rededicated the gates and the chambers and fitted them with doors, and there was very great rejoicing among the people. And the reproach of the Gentiles was turned away. These outlines of the legal shadow the truth of the gospel retraces with a firm step, since the Catholic fathers expiate in almost the same manner, according to the circumstances of their own time, the churches defiled by heretics — which it will be our study to demonstrate with clear examples in another place. But let this also be considered in the meantime: that before the first captivity, under priests who lived badly, neither the altar of the Lord nor its vessels nor its fire were wholly renewed, but were purified from the filth of idolatry and other contaminations by the hands of legitimate priests with the water of expiation alone. That this was done differently after the captivity on account of the sale of the priesthood is shown sufficiently above, to the extent that even the stones of the once-holy altar were removed from it, and new fire was struck from firestones — whereas from the priesthood of Aaron down to the treacherous Jason the fire of the altar of burnt offerings had never failed, and no king had built upon it the abominable idol of desolation.
