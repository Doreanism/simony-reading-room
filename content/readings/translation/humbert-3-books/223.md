This third reconciliation of the altar and temple was preserved through certain legitimate priests — of whom Zechariah, the father of the Lord’s forerunner, was one, even in the reign of Herod — until, with the Lord Jesus Christ already preaching, Annas and Caiaphas once again made the priesthood venal, and indeed annual, through their own ambition and money. By their dealings and those of men like them, the house of prayer had been made a den of robbers, as the Lord proclaimed; and shortly after, exalted on the cross as upon a pontifical throne, he condemned and irrecoverably deposed them, and tearing the veil of the temple from top to bottom through the middle, he stripped bare all their sacraments, and transferred their priesthood with all its rites and law to himself and to his co-heirs in a spiritual sense — as to the tribe of Judah which confesses his name — those having been rejected who denied him by crying out: “We have no king but Caesar.” Consequently, whatever was thereafter assumed and performed there of priestly office by them and by their like and followers was the worship of the devil and not of God, in accordance with what the blessed pope and doctor Leo affirms, with the angelic powers also crying out and saying: “Let us depart from these seats.” Thus with the continual sacrifice removed, the abomination and desolation persists there unto the end, in accordance with what the blessed Jerome, discoursing on the three and four crimes of certain nations, says: The prophet Amos declares to those nations that are comprehended in his reading that, for the reasons he mentions, the wrath of God is coming against them. And because he goes on to say: “for three impieties and upon four I will not convert him,” he signifies that God can grant pardon for a third commission of sins, but declares that to those who commit the same sins a fourth time he will grant neither repentance nor pardon. For through the likeness of the fire that he proclaimed would be sent upon the aforesaid nations, he wished to demonstrate the grave and irrevocable wrath and punishment of God. By the testimony of so great a man, let the simoniacs be roused and learn how much less they themselves are to be converted — they who not merely three or four times but even a hundred or a thousand times commit the same crimes without ceasing by their trafficking in the grace of the Holy Spirit. And though these things are to be severely punished in buyers, they are yet far more severely so in sellers, because it sometimes happens that a buyer, by buying, sins only once, so long as he refuses to sell to anyone else what he thinks he received. But what grace remains for the seller, or what wrath does not threaten him, who is accustomed to do nothing but sell? But lest it be thought that this is said only of other crimes and not of the simoniac heresy, let us attend to what the same prophet says of Israel, whose ambition, as a type of heretics, produced the kingdom divided in two, and it will be seen more clearly than the sun: first their trade, by which they sell unjustly, and second, by which they more unjustly buy the tongues of those who would reprove them as if they were prophets. For he says: Thus says the Lord: For three crimes of Israel and for four I will not convert him, because he has sold the righteous man for silver and the poor man for sandals; and they reclined upon pledged garments beside every altar, and drank the wine of the condemned in the house of their God. And I raised up some of your sons as prophets and some of your young men as Nazirites, but you gave the Nazirites wine to drink and commanded the prophets, saying: “Prophesy not.”
