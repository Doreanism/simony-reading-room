For our layman, if necessity demands, baptizes in the remission of sins through the Holy Spirit — something that no heretic, even if he is called a bishop, ever performs for anyone. This the blessed Leo himself professes in his other decretals, declaring that even the baptism administered by heretics in the name of the Holy Trinity has only the outward form of baptism without the power of sanctification. “Those,” he says, “who have received baptism from heretics, since they had not been baptized before, are to be confirmed only by the invocation of the Holy Spirit through the laying on of hands, because they have received only the form of baptism without the power of sanctification. And this rule, as you know, we declare must be observed in all churches, that the washing once begun be violated by no repetition, as the Apostle says: ‘One God, one faith, one baptism’ — whose ablution is to be violated by no repetition; but, as we have said, only the sanctification of the Holy Spirit is to be invoked, so that what no one receives from heretics may be obtained from Catholic priests.” It is clear, therefore, from these words of the most blessed Leo that the baptism of heretics has only the form or appearance of true baptism, and that the washing or ablution is administered to bodies alone, not also to souls — because it is given and received without the power of sanctification or the sanctification of the Holy Spirit, which no one at all receives or obtains from heretics. But if the baptism of our Lord Jesus, according to the testimony of John the Baptist, is in the Holy Spirit and fire — so that by it the remission of all sins may be effected and our old self renewed — it is apparent that the baptism of heretics, since it consists without the sanctification of the Holy Spirit, works no remission of sin, no renewal of the old self, but only a visible ablution of the body. Certainly Catholics have consented not to repeat this not out of any holiness or reverence for it, but prudently avoiding the scandal of the contentious and ignorant multitude, so that they might bring as many as possible back to Christ; they have permitted the washing — defective as it is in its better part — to be counted as valid, yet on the understanding that those confirmed by Catholic priests through the laying on of hands and the invocation of the Holy Spirit receive the sanctification for the cleansing of sins and the renewal of what is old, which they had not previously possessed at all. And so that you may more clearly observe how great an imperfection they are judged or considered to have even after this reception into the Church, it is established by a universal canon that none of them is to be advanced to the Catholic clergy; and if one comes from among the clergy as it is reckoned among heretics, he is to be content with lay communion only — except for the Cathari, whom the holy Nicene synod alone permits to remain in their clerical rank when received into the Catholic Church, yet not without the laying on of hands, even though they are of great and admirable conscience and have not deviated in any respect from the confession of the Catholic faith and the unity of the apostolic Church, except only in the matter of excluding and refusing communion with criminals — a practice in which they persist, even as they continually exhort such persons to bring forth fruits worthy of penance and to seek and await indulgence from God alone.
