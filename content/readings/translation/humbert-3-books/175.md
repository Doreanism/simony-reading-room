And that no sacraments believed to exist among heretics benefit their hearers, he shows by saying: “The threshing floor and the wine press shall not feed them, and the wine shall lie to them. They shall not dwell in the land of the Lord. My God will cast them away, because they have not listened to him, and they shall be wanderers among the nations.”

Furthermore, since Ephraim, who is also Israel, having been separated from Judah, bore the type of heretics, and Judah \[bore the type\] of catholics, let us now learn how much the works of each are valued by God. “Judah still walks with God and is faithful with the holy ones. Ephraim feeds on wind and pursues the east wind. All day long he multiplies lies and devastation, and he makes a covenant with the Assyrians and carries oil into Egypt” — that is, his sanctification or mercy toward the poor, \[turned\] from interior to exterior darkness. “The judgment of the Lord, therefore, is with Judah” — that is, discernment and truth. And because heretics both invoke the Lord and do not invoke him, it is said: “They shall call on me: My God, we know you, O Israel. But they did not cry to me from their heart, but they wailed upon their beds; they ruminated on grain and wine.” To wail, therefore, is the act of wolves. Hence it appears that heretics do not pray to God but wail. This applies above all to simoniacs, who, like rapacious and famished wolves, have no utterances to God except to fill their belly with the profit of their wickedness; who also do not pray over the grain and wine of the oblation, but ruminate — because they turn nothing over in their mind there except their customary food, that is, money. Thus the simoniac thief, having entered the house of the Lord, plunders the petty thieves outside by selling them the visible sacraments and stripping them even of their earthly goods.

## Chapter XXIX.

### From the same author and source as above: by what means the Spirit of God is known.

But now let the pen of blessed Augustine be restored to the question stated above, and let it briefly touch what that doctor, with his customary expansiveness, pours out for the people in orderly and detailed fashion. If, he says, the Holy Spirit is signified by the name of water, and this epistle tells us: “Do not believe every spirit, but test the spirits, whether they are of God” — let us understand that it was also said: “Abstain from foreign water, and do not drink from a foreign spring.” What is “do not drink from a foreign spring”? Do not believe a foreign spirit. There remains, then, the examination by which we may prove which spirit is of God. He indeed set a sign, and this perhaps is difficult. Let us see it nonetheless. We shall return to that love. It is love that teaches us, because it is itself the anointing. But here what does it say? “Test the spirits, whether they are of God; for many false prophets have gone out into this world.” Already there are all heretics and all schismatics. How then shall I test the spirit? It follows: “By this the Spirit of God is known: every spirit that confesses Jesus Christ has come in the flesh is of God, and every spirit that does not confess Jesus Christ in the flesh is not of God; and this is the antichrist, of whom you have heard that he is coming, and is now already in this world.” Therefore the spirit that is among heretics is of God, because they confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh? Now from this they strongly raise themselves up against us and say: You do not have the Spirit of God, but we too confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh. For he denied the Spirit of God to those who do not confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh. Ask the Arians: do they confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh? Ask the Macedonians: do they confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh? Ask the Cataphrygians \[Montanists\]: do they confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh?
