thus: “Amen, amen, I say to you, he that entereth not by the door into the sheepfold, but climbeth up another way, the same is a thief and a robber.” And even if he had said this without an oath, who, unless a liar and an antichrist, would not believe the Truth itself, Christ himself speaking? But with an oath he took care to profess it because he wished it to be heard more attentively and held more firmly. And so the proverb of truth is true according to the letter, and no less true according to the intent of the speaker. Among men, thieves and robbers are those who do not enter the sheepfold through the door but climb in from elsewhere; and no less are they thieves and robbers before God who do not enter the Church through Christ — who, as he himself proclaims, is the door of the Church — but come in from another way. These beyond all doubt are all heretics, Jews, and pagans, for they alike do not enter the Church through Christ, whom they deny to have come in the flesh.

If it is asked how all heretics deny that Christ came in the flesh, when some of them openly confess that Christ came in the flesh, the answer is to be found in the teaching of blessed Augustine in the third book of this work. For now, let us see how they climb in from another way. Undoubtedly the Arians climb in from another way — for example, because they so distinguish the trinity of persons as also to divide the unity of the divine substance. The Sabellians climb in from another way, because they so assert the unity of the divine substance as also to confuse the trinity of persons. It would be long, and beyond the powers of our understanding, to run through each of the plagues of the remaining heresies one by one. Leaving others aside, therefore, let us show how simoniac promoters — whom our work targets most directly — climb in from another way. Indeed they too, despising Christ the door and his doorkeeper the Holy Spirit, climb in from another way: with their money as ladders and siege-engines, they assault and storm the Lord’s wall — that is, the united consent and solidarity of the people of God — in order to overthrow it, and they either scramble over it or force their way through it by subversion or sheer force. For they neither respect the law of the door, which says “freely you have received, freely give,” nor do they await the good pleasure of the doorkeeper; but, driven by headlong ambition for pre-eminence, having scaled, undermined, or broken the wall, they invade the sheep as the worst of thieves and robbers. The wall of God’s sheepfold is indeed scaled by them when, abusing secular powers, they prevail in exercising temporal lordship over the sheep; it is undermined when they extract and attach to themselves ecclesiastical persons who are joined to the foundation of the Church; and it is broken through when they detach persons of middling or lay rank from the charity of Christ and attach them to themselves. By them the roof is also stripped away, when they plunder the prelates of the Church of the integrity of their office. Whether, therefore, they rush in by scaling, undermining, or breaking through, it is manifest that they climb in from somewhere other than the door. In sum, they either buy off secular powers with money, or persons of lower ecclesiastical rank, or their superiors, or laypeople of moderate influence. For against the lesser people — weaker sheep, as it were — these four orders are stationed as a bulwark: the secular powers as a wall against visible incursions, and the ecclesiastical persons as a roof against invisible ones. It is established, therefore, that simoniac robbers, in order to prevail in slaughtering and destroying the sheep, have gained entry from within through those who were appointed as defenders from without. And these defenders do not escape punishment: like tiles or stones removed from a building, they now lie neglected and useless upon the face of the earth, outside the edifice of Christ. And the very walls and roof appear to stand or survive to no good end — now that the doorkeeper has been driven away and the sheep extinguished — for they are left to desolation and ruin, and even to wild beasts and unclean birds.
