## CAPUT XVIII.

### On the difference between the priesthood of Catholics and heretics, and on the first fruits of the simoniacs.

Furthermore, how greatly Catholic priests — even if living badly — differ from heretics is gathered from this: that as the Babylonian captivity was approaching, the fire of the altar was hidden in a pit by priests who were worshippers of God, and after seventy years, even under water and in water, it is found to have lived on, along with the wood and the sacrifice placed over it; this fire, restored to the altar in accordance with the law and continually tended by the work of devout priests from the days of Cyrus king of Persia, did not fail until the reign of Antiochus, who was beyond doubt a type of Antichrist, whose prophets the heretics are deemed to be. And their first and greatest \[precursors\] in both testaments are the simoniacs. This is confirmed not only from other scriptures but also from the deeds of the Maccabees. For the typological Antichrist Antiochus was the first to sell the priesthood of the Jews to Jason, who prefigured Simon Magus. This is related there as follows: “After the departure of Seleucus from life, when Antiochus — who was called the Noble — had taken up the kingdom, Jason, the brother of Onias, sought the high priesthood; he approached the king, promising him 360 talents of gold and from other revenues 80 talents, and over and above these he promised another 150, if authority were granted him to establish a gymnasium and an ephebeum for himself and to register those who were in Jerusalem as Antiochenes. When the king agreed and he had obtained the rule, he immediately began to transfer his compatriots to the Gentile manner.” And a little after: “But this was not the beginning, but a certain increase and advance of Gentile and foreign ways, because of the nefarious and unheard-of crime of the impious Jason — who was no priest — so that the priests were no longer devoted to the duties of the altar.” Do you see how by the simoniac heresy even the Jewish priesthood was so destroyed that, on account of the crime
