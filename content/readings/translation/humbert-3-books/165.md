In doing this they acquire for themselves not only no reward but the greatest increase of damnation, when by the contact of their unclean offering they pollute the sanctuary and all things that belonged to it, as the Lord establishes through Moses: “Whatever an unclean person touches shall become unclean.” To prevent this, the same Moses in Deuteronomy forbids it thus: “You shall not bring the wages of a prostitute nor the price of a dog into the house of the Lord your God, whatever it is that they have vowed, for both are an abomination before the Lord your God.” By this precept of divine law, an offering acquired through vice is rejected, because “the Lord hates robbery in a burnt offering.” He who rightly disdains the wages of a prostitute — which a profane harlot earns from profane men by fornicating, through the corruption of her own and another’s flesh — how much more will he disdain the \[price\] which a simoniac earns by fornicating away from the Christian faith, through the prostitution of his own soul and of many others. Likewise he abhors the price of a dog — not because any animal’s nature is abominable to him, but because he abhors an offering which someone has earned, like a mute dog fawning and showing favor, by flattering and complying with others’ vices. Or certainly the price of a dog before God is whatever one who has returned to the vomit of sin after repentance offers. For either is an abomination before God — whether you vow to him the price of your fornication or the price of your compliance — however great and precious that price may be. How then will God be appeased by a price through which the virginity of innumerable souls, that is, the uncorrupted faith betrothed to him, is prostituted, or through which persistence and impenitence are ministered to the wicked, whenever a sinner is praised in the desires of his soul and the unjust is blessed? But if they have acquired for themselves and their kin and friends possessions, households, and other things useful only for human worldly life — like another Gehazi — they will nonetheless reap no fruit from these things, because the leprosy of the simoniac curse will so cleave to them and to their seed forever that their children, staggering, will be removed and will beg and be cast out of their dwellings. Whether, therefore, they spend those prices spiritually or carnally, it is established that they labor entirely in vain, because one who takes unjustly never gives justly or fruitfully to anyone. Hence these men, by their trafficking, defraud one another and take from one another what they had. For the seller, by demanding money, at once steals from the buyer both faith and money. The buyer, by tendering \[the money\] to the seller, likewise steals \[his\] faith and the grace of the Holy Spirit; and it comes about by a wondrous and wretched manner that neither has what he stole from the other, but moreover each loses what he previously had, and each receives from the other only the damnation which he did not previously have. If both are wretched, more wretched nonetheless is he who lost both his money and his faith, and the spiritual grace which he demanded from the other in exchange for the price given — he took it away from the other and did not receive it for himself; indeed, in place of a blessing he incurred a curse. Whence it comes about that, just as when someone takes a soul from another he takes it from that person but does not thereby have two souls — indeed he rather loses his own — so too do these men take faith and grace from one another, but neither of them retains either \[faith or grace\], and moreover they fall horribly into the hands of the living God, where sin, brought to completion, begets death for them. Hence they are straightway delivered by God’s judgment to the devil, who had first entered their hearts to possess them — delivered to him to possess forever in peace; and thus, like another Judas after the dipped morsel, Satan again enters them when he claims as his own the betrayers of their faith, convicted by their mutual fraud. For the dipped bread signified the mind of the betrayer infected by deceit. Finally, not everything that is dipped is cleansed — sometimes it is rather defiled.
