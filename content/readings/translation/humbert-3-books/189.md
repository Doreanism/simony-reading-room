## CAPUT XL.

### What are the signs and wonders of heretics.

Yet the signs and wonders before the first coming of Christ differ from the signs and wonders performed by Catholics before his second coming in this: that the former signified in such a way that they were not realities at all, whereas the latter are signs in such a way that they are also essentially and truly realities. It remains, therefore, to examine what are the signs and wonders of that beast which is the universal type of heretics, and what they signify or prophesy; and above all it should be noted that this beast is called the false prophet in what follows. But if it is a false prophet, its signs and wonders must necessarily be false; and what is false necessarily signifies nothing but falsehood and lies. It is therefore clear that nothing true or right is to be found among those whose signs signify nothing but falsehood. But what are the signs that this beast presumes to enact? Certainly water, bread, wine, oil, and the other visible instruments of the sacraments of the Catholic religion — which, outwardly and in appearance, are permitted by God to such people against whom he is angry until the end of time, without any spiritual grace or signification being imparted to them. Would that these visible instruments of the sacraments could at least be held among them in the purity of their substance and nature and be free of an unclean spirit! But, what is worse, they are spurned by the Spirit of God and desired by the spirit of the devil, with which they are imbued. In these things, although outwardly water, bread, wine, and oil appear, together with the other trappings of the sacraments as if the food and clothing of sheep, yet within lurks the dreadful rabies of wolves. Wherefore the Lord, through Hosea, complains over the ten tribes who maintained the outward semblance of heretics, saying: *She did not know that I gave her grain, wine, and oil, and multiplied her silver and gold, which they used for Baal* \[Hosea 2:8\]. And a little later: *And I will destroy her vine and her fig tree, and I will turn her into a wilderness, and the beast of the field shall consume her* \[Hosea 2:12\]. Thus, by this art and these pretenses, this beast creates an image of the first beast, so that just as the holy fathers, by their rites before the law and under the law, prefigured Christ who was to come, so heretics by their frauds prefigure Antichrist. For just as in our ancient fathers there then worked, and now in Catholics works, the mystery of the eternal justice and equity which is Christ, so in heretics, as in children of unfaithfulness, there works the mystery of injustice and iniquity which is Antichrist. If he were not now secretly and secretly veiled within them, the apostle would not speak of his manifestation, saying: *That he may be revealed in his time; and then the lawless one will be revealed* \[2 Thessalonians 2:6–8\]. For just as Catholics retain certain signs of the Hebrews in which they essentially bear and prophesy the Lord veiled within them, so also heretics present certain usurped signs of Catholics, in which they bear and prophesy Antichrist veiled within them. They also give spirit to his image, when they commend his imitation by the lies of their preaching and defense, as best they can. And among the other signs they counterfeit, they also make fire come down from heaven to earth whenever, laying their hands on someone in imitation of the apostles, they presume that he receives the grace and consecration of the Holy Spirit. But this they appear to do upon earth — which they call their church — and in the sight of human beings, that is, of carnal men.
