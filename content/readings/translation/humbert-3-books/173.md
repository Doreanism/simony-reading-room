If we are in unity, why are there two altars in this city, why are households divided, marriages divided? Why is there a shared bed and a divided Christ? The truth admonishes us to confess what is true: either they have gone out from us, or we from them. But God forbid that we have gone out from them; for we hold the testament of the Lord’s inheritance. We recite it, and we find ourselves in it: “I will give you the nations as your inheritance, and the ends of the earth as your possession.” We hold the inheritance of Christ; but they do not hold it, nor do they communicate with the whole world, nor do they communicate with the totality redeemed by the blood of the Lord.

And a little later: If therefore they have gone out from us, they are antichrists; if they are antichrists, they are liars; if they are liars, they deny Jesus Christ. We return again to the difficulty of the question. Question each one individually and they confess that Jesus is the Christ. Our understanding is confined by the narrowness of this epistle. You see clearly the question — this question troubles both us and them, if it is not understood. Either we are antichrists, or they are antichrists. But this epistle has identified the antichrists: whoever denies that Jesus is not the Christ, he is the antichrist. Let us now ask, then, who denies — and let us not pay attention to the tongue, but to deeds. For if all are questioned, all confess with one mouth that Jesus is the Christ. Let the tongue be quiet for a moment; question the life. If we find this, if scripture itself has told us that denial occurs not only with the tongue but also through deeds, then surely we have found many antichrists who profess Christ with their mouth and dissent from Christ in their conduct. Where do we find this? In scripture. Question Paul and hear what he says, when speaking of such people: “They profess that they know God, but they deny him by their deeds.” We find also these antichrists: whoever denies Christ by his deeds is an antichrist. I do not listen to what one says, but I look at how one lives. Works speak, and we seek words. For what wicked person does not want to speak well? But what does the Lord say of such people? “Hypocrites, how can you speak good things, since you are evil? You bring your voices to my ears; I examine your thoughts” — I see there an evil will, and you display false fruits. I know what I shall gather from them. I do not gather figs from thorns, nor grapes from brambles. Every tree is known by its fruit. That antichrist is the greater liar who professes with his mouth that Jesus is the Christ, yet denies it by his deeds. Therefore the liar says one thing and does another. From the words of so great a doctor, lest simoniacs press upon us shamelessly and presume that they live with us in catholic unity — because we share one altar and the same marriage feast, and are found to differ from us in no aspect of their conduct — let them know that this is to be imputed to our ignorance or negligence, and to their own fraudulence and shamelessness. Since they are truly antichrists, they pretend to be Christ’s — and are all the more to be guarded against the more perniciously they brood over Christ’s sheepfold under the cover of fellowship, feigning themselves to be milk-white sheep, while in the sacraments of the catholic \[church\] as in sheep’s clothing they do not cease to plot against catholics and “with open jaws work their raw slaughter among the lambs.” And following the example of their father Judas, under the sign of peace or a kiss, under the symbol of a single communion, they plan and execute all the more numerous betrayals and slaughters within the church, the more their wickedness preserves itself in concealment. For like assassins they pretend to be fellow travelers, and suddenly drawing the dagger from hiding they attack the unwary — who suspect nothing of the sort — amid friendly conversation, trained to make war under the guise of peace; comparable to the unclean Manichaeans, who — lest they be detected — even presumed with sacrilegious mouths to receive catholic communion, though they were sometimes detected in this: that, abstaining from the wine of Christ’s blood, which they did not believe was shed for the remission of sins, they withdrew from it. Similarly we cannot detect these \[simoniacs\] either, except when we find them attempting to sell the grace of the Holy Spirit. But however much they conceal themselves and pretend, they are nevertheless, together with the Manichaeans and other heretics, truly heretics — first among all to have been anathematized and cut off from the church — whether they are falsely within or truly outside; they are antichrists.
