# HERE BEGIN THE CHAPTER HEADINGS OF THE BOOK OF HUMBERT, CARDINAL BISHOP OF THE HOLY ROMAN CHURCH, IN HIS REPLY AGAINST THE SIMONIACS.

I. Invective against the Corruptor, charging him with falsehood.

II. On pseudo-bishops, whom a decretal chapter of Pope Leo marks out.

III. That simoniacs are worse than Arians and even than their own master Simon.

IV. The wrath of the Holy Spirit against Simon, and a comparison of him and his followers, and what they receive.

V. That that chapter of Saint Leo is to be understood not of heretics but of Catholics.

VI. On the distinction of pseudo-bishops and their ordinations.

VII. On the chapter of Saint Gregory prohibiting reordination, where it is also conceded that simoniacs may be at least equated with Arians.

VIII. On the canons of the Apostles and the second laying on of hands.

IX. In what sense the baptism of heretics is permitted, and what permission means.

X. On the imperfection of the baptism of heretics, according to the sentences of the holy fathers.

XI. Where the sentences of the holy fathers are set forth, in which heretics are to be avoided.

XII. On the ordinations of heretics, according to the blessed Innocent.

XIII. Further on this, according to the blessed Gregory.

XIV. Recapitulation of the above sentences of the blessed Gregory against false arguers.

XV. On proper and improper terms, and the vanity of simoniacs.

XVI. The sentence of Saint Ambrose on the ordinations of simoniacs.

XVII. Recapitulation of the above. Then the sentence of the blessed Augustine.

XVIII. The sentences of the holy fathers on those who traffic in ecclesiastical dignity.

XIX. Refutation of those who say that simoniacs possess grace from which they may be deposed.

XX. That simoniacs are without faith, and that those who agree with them are deprived of faith.

XXI. Refutation of those who say that the Holy Spirit can be possessed and justly given by one who has received it unjustly.

## HERE END THE CHAPTER HEADINGS OF THE FIRST BOOK \[AGAINST THE SIMONIACS\].

## HERE BEGINS THE FIRST BOOK AGAINST HERETICS AND ESPECIALLY AGAINST SIMONIACS, IN THE FORM OF A DIALOGUE UNDER THE NAMES OF THE CORRUPTOR AND THE CORRECTOR, COMPOSED BY HUMBERT, CARDINAL BISHOP OF THE HOLY ROMAN CHURCH.

### CHAPTER I.

Corrector.

At the very threshold of his controversy, having prefixed certain chapters for the defense of himself and his followers — set like mousetraps or nets spread to ensnare the unwary — the subverted subverter and the seduced seducer at last emerges violently from the tunnels of his ambush and suddenly bursts forth into these arguments to suffocate those caught in his net.

Corruptor.

Therefore, having re-read these chapters, O lovers of justice, you have heard more clearly that an ordination performed by pseudo-bishops could stand as valid.
