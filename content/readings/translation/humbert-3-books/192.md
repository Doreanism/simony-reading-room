Hence the prophet, lamenting Judea, says: “Her enemies have become her masters, her foes are enriched, because the Lord has spoken against her” \[Lamentations 1:5\]. And another, rebuking the maker of an idol, says: “A foolish heart will worship it, and it will not deliver its soul, nor will it say: ‘There is a lie in my right hand’” \[Isaiah 44:20\]. But this business cannot be carried out unless both parties — seller and buyer alike — are found to bear the mark of antichrist. Since Peter did not bear that mark, he could not sell \[the Holy Spirit\], although the magus, bearing that mark, wished to buy \[Acts 8:18–20\]. Here it is worth noting how far this heresy has advanced from its initial weakness to its present strength. At first it could find no seller; then, long afterward, it found a seller, but one who sought payment only from the buyer and from no one else. Now the seller is not ashamed to demand from the buyer not only his own funds and those of his household, but also church money of every kind. Woe is me! I have learned from those present of so horrifying a deed: after costly marble had been stripped from the walls and ornamental panels of basilicas for the purpose of this transaction, even the tiles were torn from their roofs at the seller’s bidding and command, so that the sum of the promised price might be paid to him by the most wretched buyer, already lamenting what had been done too late. Let anyone who can, say what heresy has ever contrived such desolation for the churches of God as this one has. And yet wise men proclaim that simoniacs should be treated more mildly than other heretics have been, and that the rigor of the canons should be tempered for them more than it was for the Novatians, toward whom the Catholic Church has shown itself more clement and indulgent. I am willing to spare wolves, thieves, and robbers, if they themselves have ever spared the sheep or do so now, or if they only seize and steal them so as not to slaughter and destroy them. Let them be permitted to grow and beget offspring without penalty — but let it be considered whether this is expedient for the little flock of lambs. Let a kid be cooked in its mother’s milk \[Exodus 23:19\], even contrary to the Lord’s prohibition — but let care be taken lest thereby a he-goat be made subject to irrevocable cursing. Let surgical instruments be withheld from diseased sheep, and biting salt, vinegar, and live sulfur from their mangy hides — but let it be seen lest dire contagion gradually spread through all. Let sacrilegious men too be spared — men whom no law spares — but let it be considered lest this leniency toward them prove impiety toward God and his own, and that sacred places, if anything remains to them, lose it. Let fratricide Cain be spared, if even he, when warned, refrained from fratricide. Let murderers be spared, if they have not sinned unto death \[1 John 5:16\]. Let robbers be spared, if they have not only ceased to rob but have also duly restored what they still forcibly retain.

## CAPUT XLIIII.

### How Guilty Simoniacs Are before the Church, and on the Number of Christ’s Name

O how many and how great fratricides and murders simoniacs are held guilty of above all other heretics — they who, beyond the hands of the common people, daily slaughter with immense sacrilege so many cohorts of God’s household: clergy, monks, and consecrated women. For while they alienate their stipends by selling and giving them to secular persons, they destroy both their bodies through intolerable hunger and want of all things, and at the same time drive their souls to the sins by which they perish. For when those who were to lead a celibate and spiritual life find that none of their allotted income on which to subsist has been left to them, they are driven to seek the support of secular people; and in their attempt to relieve their bodies, they are compelled to enslave their souls. O how many poor, widows, and orphans who ought to be fed from church alms have they killed! O how many pious establishments have they destroyed, where they have perpetually sold or given to secular persons the due share of tithes and offerings belonging to the poor and to the churches! Hence it has now come about that anyone who wishes to serve Christ must do so at his own expense, contrary to the privilege of Christian military service. This is why no one dares to compel clergy, monks, or consecrated women to a regular way of life, or to summon them freely, except according to the will of each individual.
