For it is without doubt a schism or heresy to attempt to divide God and his own from one another — as blessed Paul testifies to the Corinthians, saying: “I beseech you, brethren, by the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, that you all speak the same thing, and that there be no schisms among you; but that you be perfectly joined together in the same mind and in the same judgment. For it hath been declared unto me of you, my brethren, by them of the house of Chloe, that there are contentions among you. Now this I say, that every one of you saith: I am of Paul, and I of Apollos, and I of Cephas, and I of Christ. Is Christ divided?” — the answer being: certainly not. For otherwise — God forbid — that prayer of his would be fruitless which he poured out to God the Father as he went to his passion; wherein, among other things, he besought especially for his own, that they might be one in him, saying: “I have given them thy word; and the world hath hated them, because they are not of the world, even as I am not of the world. I pray not that thou shouldest take them out of the world, but that thou shouldest keep them from the evil. They are not of the world, even as I am not of the world. Sanctify them through thy truth; thy word is truth. As thou hast sent me into the world, even so have I also sent them into the world. And for their sakes I sanctify myself, that they also might be sanctified through the truth. Neither pray I for these alone, but for them also which shall believe on me through their word; that they all may be one; as thou, Father, art in me, and I in thee, that they also may be one in us; that the world may believe that thou hast sent me. And the glory which thou gavest me I have given them; that they may be one, even as we are one. I in them, and thou in me, that they may be made perfect in one.” Do you see, O blaspheming divider, that the things that are Christ’s cannot be divided, for they are indivisible? Indeed Christ and his ministers are indivisible. And what should astonish you further: pure men are indivisible from God the Father — who is God alone — through the mediation of Christ, who is both God and man, as he himself says: “I in them, and thou in me, that they may be made perfect in one.” Yet this wonderful and desirable unity is owed to him \[Christ\] as to the only-begotten by nature, while it is granted to man as to an adopted son by grace alone. Finally, just as Christ Jesus appears as a singularly faithful mediator between God and man, uniting both parties: to his divine nature and holiness, whereby he was in accord with God, he united the human nature and sanctification by which he was born holy and without sin — so that through the one he might accord with man conceived in sin, in order to counsel him, and through the other he might come to his aid in such a way as also to restore his lost sanctification. This sanctification, divinely bestowed on the son of virgin earth and diabolically taken away, is restored by the Son of the Virgin Mary; by it the same true man was predestined to be the Son of God in power according to the Spirit of sanctification. He himself, the Son of God and man, beseeching this Spirit of sanctification, says: “Sanctify them in the truth” — that is, in me, who am the truth, who for their sakes sanctify myself in my humanity, though I am holiness itself in my divinity — “that they also might be sanctified in me.” From which it is truly gathered that it is by sanctification alone that human things are joined to divine. Therefore, if the ministers and servants of Christ become one with Christ in God, because they are sanctified in him, it is manifest that whatever belongs to the ministers of Christ is one with them in Christ, because it has been sanctified in them through Christ. And if the peculium \[the private property\] of a carnal slave is united to and passes to the lord through the slave who belongs to him, how much more is everything belonging to Christ’s own united to and passes to Christ — especially those for whom there is no possession except what is re-given to them from his own gifts, and whose share of their inheritance is furthermore the Lord himself, to whom they in turn are an inheritance.
