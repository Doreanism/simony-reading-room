For he is a murderer from the beginning. He killed the first man, and thereafter has killed as many as he could and will. Nor indeed did he come to our first parent armed with iron or poison or any other instrument of death. He sowed an evil word and killed. Therefore no one who counsels evil should doubt that he is a murderer; and lest he think that, since he himself has not killed another, he still lives, let him take note of what the apostle John says: “You know that no murderer has eternal life abiding in him.” Lest we hedge and twist his statement as we pleased, he prefaced it by saying: “Whoever hates his brother is a murderer” \[1 John 3:15\]. “But you are all brothers,” says Christ \[Matthew 23:8\]. Those he knowingly suggests evil to, he without doubt hates. Hence the one who does so is held guilty of a murder all the greater in proportion as he more closely imitates the first murderer — the devil — and as the soul is more excellent than the body. And yet the apostle omitted no kind of homicide when he said: “You know that every murderer.”

### How Vipers Correspond to Simoniacs.

## CAPUT XIII.

Now what we have said typologically about the nature of vipers in reference to the devil, even though it can be applied to all his members, yet more particularly fits his chief members — that is, heretics — for just as between God and the seraphim no spirits are interposed, so between the devil and heretics no men are interposed. But since we are not equal to naming so many monsters, let us see only how these apply to simoniacs, about whom we are presently speaking. Whatever person of Christian profession, when her soul’s natural impulse — her concupiscence, rightly used for good — is vitiated, is seized, so to speak, by an ambition for honor: she is first deprived of the love of God, and then, made ambitious, does not wait for the Lord to glorify those who glorify him. Hence, impatient of rest and delay, she seeks out eagerly, here and there, a match for her concupiscence. Once found, she breathes into him with open mouth the vast flame in her breast, thereby manifestly exposing the wealth she has kept in her heart and with her heart until now. For “where the treasure is, there also is the heart” \[Matthew 6:21\]. So that wretch, like a second adulterer seduced by the devil, beholding the money — like the hidden but now revealed avarice of another’s heart — out of mad love for money condemns his own head to the same, where, oblivious of his enemy to his life and of everlasting death, he plunges his faith and reason — which he had received from the Lord as his chief member — faithlessly and irrationally into the faithlessness and irrationality of the other. But having thus lost his head, he dies; yet that other viper does not profit from the head of her mate for her own advancement or anyone else’s, but only for destruction, for the ambitious person does indeed lose her companion — by holding out to him the desired coin, she robs him of his faith, that is, his chief member — yet she does not herself possess the faith she has taken from him, but only the faithlessness she has greedily swallowed down like the viper’s poison as the seedbed of her own destruction and others’. For faith cannot become faithlessness, but when faithlessness enters, faith departs, as from something with which it has nothing in common. And just as the male viper, in offering his head to be severed, has no more a head — neither for himself nor for his unspeakable mate; for him it is death, for her the seedbed of death — so these wicked simoniacs, when they bring spiritual grace to be sold and bought, are indeed deserted by grace on the part of the seller, and yet grace does not come to the buyer; instead, the wrath of God horribly pervades both. They kill one another with their tongues like vipers, for the one by demanding, the other by offering, saleable grace, they become heretics and perish. And from these, since they are venomous, only venomous offspring are born, as a certain wise man attests: “Sons of abominations become sons of sinners, and those who dwell near the houses of the impious. The inheritance of sinners’ sons shall perish, and reproach shall cleave constantly to their seed.
