## CAPUT XLVI.

### How Satan Constructs a Profane Trinity against the Divine

Therefore the ancient dragon, ever desirous of doing harm and skilled in counterfeiting, among so many and so great things that he is shown above to attempt and simulate, also strives by every means to accomplish this: just as there are three who bear witness to the truth in heaven and three who do so on earth \[1 John 5:7–8\], so he too in turn transforms himself into three who bear witness to his falsehood in this turbid air which he is still permitted to occupy, and into three who do so on earth. For his pride has surged to the point that he now seeks to become like the Most High to such a degree that he nonetheless exalts himself above everything that is called \[God\] or that is worshiped \[2 Thessalonians 2:4\]. Hence, in rebellion against God, he sets up in opposition to the blessed and undivided Trinity his own abomination: himself, his antichrist, and the pseudo-prophet of both — so that he as father of lies may resist the Father of truth, and antichrist may resist Christ as falsehood resists truth, and the pseudo-prophet may resist as the conspiracy of the liar and falsehood resists the Spirit of truth and verity. These three likewise, against the three who bear witness to the truth on earth, vomit from their mouth three unclean spirits in the form of frogs \[Revelation 16:13\], which defile, invade, and sell those whom water has cleansed, the Spirit has imbued, and the blood of Christ has redeemed. These unclean spirits are fittingly compared to frogs, which avoid clear and flowing waters and are drawn to muddy swamps — in which they croak with improper and discordant noise and multiply and rear their offspring. In these swamps there is nothing to stand on while walking or resting, but only something to sink into, defiling and absorbing; from them nothing is produced except mists, stenches, sharp sedge-grasses, marsh reeds, bulrushes, venomous serpents and destructive worms, and barren trees. Such indeed is the multitude of the reprobate, which like stagnant and bitter water turns land once dry and habitable into squalid and impassable swamps — in which now nothing is found that does not deserve the unquenchable fire, in which nothing is heard but the inept and contentious babble of the carnal and garrulous, defending their drunkenness and disturbing by their impudence the sobriety of the spiritual and the silence of the reserved — such as the poets and philosophers of the pagans, the fabulators of the Jews and heretics, who like muddy frogs in their slimy swamps declaim their lamentations irrationally and stubbornly. Over these three authors presiding over them and inspiring their songs: Satan over the pagans, antichrist over the Jews, and the pseudo-prophet over the heretics. For by means of these three the devil endeavors to hunt, snare, and fish for the three sects of all humankind: through himself as behemoth for the pagans, through antichrist as a bird for the Jews, and through the pseudo-prophet as leviathan for Christians.
