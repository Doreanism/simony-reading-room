## CHAPTER XX.

### How many curses simoniacs share with Judas, and how they sell the whole Church.

Not otherwise do these heretics, seeking by the ambition of their wicked trafficking to burst the bonds of Christian law and cast off from themselves the yoke of apostolic discipline — he who dwells in the heavens laughs at them and mocks them, when in their shameless trafficking concerning God they find nothing in their hands but what Judas and the Jews found in theirs: namely, perpetual curse and damnation. By this, after the example of Judas, their children become orphans and their wife a widow, because the blood of Christ remains upon them and upon their children to be avenged. For their children become orphans from the very moment of their conception and birth, because they are without God the Father; and their wife — that is, their congregations — is a widow, because she is without her lawful husband Christ. Hence it is apparent that heretics die by generating \[offspring\], whose wife remains a widow and whose children are orphans. And truly they die to God completely when, by perversely ordaining others, they do not shrink from selling the grace of his sacraments. Thus their people, by consenting and lending support to them, are as a wife widowed from God, so that the children of both are born only as orphans, since they are straightway deprived of their eternal Father when they are initiated into those \[rites\] which are called sacraments among heretics. To these \[miseries\] is added in equal measure, as an accumulation of their damnation, that from their generation the blasphemy of the Holy Spirit — who is the remission of sins — is required, just as from mad Judas and the faithless Jews the blood of Christ, which was shed for the remission of sins, is required. That treacherous money-lender Mammon scrutinizes their entire substance for the coin of faithlessness lent to them, and strangers plunder their labors — those strangers whose voices the sheep of Christ do not know. And what more \[need be said\], since the psalm from which we have briefly plucked these \[observations\] pursues such men at greater length? If a faithful reader examines it with a Christian understanding, he will without delay recognize, under the title of Judas the betrayer, how many, what kind, and how great curses and punishments are owed to simoniacs. For the prophetic imprecation of that psalm directed at the seller of Christ and his buyers cannot be more fitly applied to any other heretics than to these, who make the Holy Spirit a commodity in the grace of all his sacraments. To enumerate some of these \[sacraments\] in particular: these men sell catechism, baptism, and the seal of perfect Christianity — that is, from the first nourishment of consecrated salt all the way to confirmation by the bishop, which is the seventh of the sacraments of regeneration, according to the same sevenfold Spirit, for the remission of all sins. And they sell these whenever they ordain bishops, priests, and others for a price — those whose offices serve the regenerative grace in the Catholic Church. Likewise in deacons, whatever of the sacraments pertains to deacons; in subdeacons, whatever pertains to subdeacons; in acolytes, whatever pertains to acolytes; in exorcists, whatever pertains to exorcists; in readers, whatever pertains to readers; in doorkeepers, whatever pertains to doorkeepers; in clerics, whatever pertains to clerics; in stewards, whatever pertains to stewards; in churches, whatever pertains to churches; in monasteries, whatever pertains to monasteries; and likewise in all other things of the kind. Since in this way they leave no order of the Christian religion untouched by sale, they leave nothing unsold in the entire world of Christendom.
