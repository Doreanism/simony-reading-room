For in coveting a priesthood not rightfully his, he both presumed and in presuming he coveted. Moreover, greed, as blessed Gregory says, is not only for money but also for advancement. It is rightly called greed when greatness is sought beyond due measure. For if the seizure of honor did not pertain to greed, Paul would never have said of the only-begotten Son: “He did not consider equality with God a thing to be grasped.” It was therefore in this that the devil drew our parent into pride — that he incited him to greed for exaltation. The obstinacy of Korah is shown in this: that neither by rebuke nor by admonition did he return to his senses. Finally, as the history recounts, when Korah was contending bitterly and shamelessly and hurling accusations, the most gentle of men, Moses, answered him: “In the morning the Lord will make known who belongs to him and who is holy, and will bring that person near to himself; and whoever he chooses he will bring near to himself. Do this: let each of you take his censer — you, Korah, and all your company — and tomorrow put fire in them and place incense on them before the Lord; and the man whom the Lord chooses shall be the holy one. You have gone too far, sons of Levi!” He said again to Korah: “Hear, sons of Levi: is it too small a thing for you that the God of Israel has separated you from the congregation of Israel, to bring you near to himself, to do service in the tabernacle of the Lord and to stand before the congregation and to minister to them? And he has brought you near him and all your brothers the sons of Levi with you — and yet you seek the priesthood too? And your whole company has banded together against the Lord.” When Moses then sent messengers to summon them and learned of the insolent defiance of Dathan and Abiram, he was very angry and said to the Lord: “Do not regard their offering. You know that I have not taken one donkey from them, nor have I harmed any one of them.” And he said to Korah: “You and all your company be present before the Lord tomorrow, you and they and Aaron. And let each one of you take his censer and put incense on it, and each one of you bring before the Lord his censer — two hundred and fifty censers; you also, and Aaron, each his censer.” When they had done so, with Moses and Aaron standing there, all the congregation gathered against them at the entrance of the tent of meeting, and the glory of the Lord appeared to all the congregation. And the Lord spoke to Moses and Aaron, saying: “Separate yourselves from among this congregation, that I may consume them in a moment.” They fell on their faces and said: “O God, the God of the spirits of all flesh, shall one man sin, and will you be angry with all the congregation?” And the Lord said to Moses: “Say to all the congregation, get away from around the dwelling of Korah, Dathan, and Abiram.” So Moses rose and went to Dathan and Abiram, and the elders of Israel followed him. And he said to the congregation: “Depart, please, from the tents of these wicked men, and touch nothing of theirs, lest you be swept away with all their sins.” So they got away from the dwelling of Korah, Dathan, and Abiram on every side. And Dathan and Abiram came out and stood at the entrance of their tents, together with their wives, their sons, and their little ones. And Moses said: “By this you shall know that the Lord has sent me to do all these works, and that it has not been of my own accord. If these men die as all men die, or if they are visited by the fate of all mankind, then the Lord has not sent me. But if the Lord creates something new, and the ground opens its mouth and swallows them up with all that belongs to them, and they go down alive into Sheol, then you shall know that these men have despised the Lord.” And as soon as he had finished speaking, the ground under them split apart. And the earth opened its mouth and swallowed them up, with their households and all the people who belonged to Korah and all their goods. They went down alive into Sheol, the earth closed over them, and they perished from the midst of the assembly. And all Israel who were around them fled at their cry, for they said: “Lest the earth swallow us up!” And fire came out from the Lord and consumed the two hundred and fifty men offering the incense. From this terrifying judgment of divine severity — nearly all of which we have judged should be inserted into this little work — we may infer even from the surface of the story what they deserve or what they will suffer, those who presume to seize the priesthood of Christian grace for themselves by worldly means, if those who contumaciously attempted to seize the priesthood of the Mosaic Law for themselves were punished with such great severity;
