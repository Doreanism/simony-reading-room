For God bestows his Holy Spirit upon human beings, but through the prayer and invocation of those who are known to have received the ordination of this ministry or office from Catholics without charge — even if they appear to men of corrupt conduct. In this faith and ministry, heretics have no part or lot, because they are made ministers not of Christ but of Satan through a price, and the Holy Spirit is not given through their prayer or invocation, because their ministry — even if they appear to be of upright conduct — is not acknowledged by God. For as the blessed apostle says to the Corinthians, such men are false apostles, deceitful workers, disguising themselves as apostles of Christ \[2 Corinthians 11:13\]. And no wonder. For Satan himself disguises himself as an angel of light \[2 Corinthians 11:14\]; it is therefore no great thing if his ministers also disguise themselves as ministers of justice \[2 Corinthians 11:15\], whose end will be according to their deeds. And just as Satan, disguised as an angel of light, cannot cease to be an angel of darkness — all the more perverse the more presumptuous — so too his ministers, disguised as ministers of justice, cannot cease to be ministers of injustice, all the more detestable the more boldly they lie in claiming to be what they neither are nor can become.

## CAPUT XVIII.

### Judgments of the Holy Fathers on Those Who Traffic in Ecclesiastical Dignity.

Also from the foregoing, from the Apostolic Canons, chapter 30 \[*Canones Apostolorum* c. 30\]:

If any bishop or priest or deacon has obtained this dignity through money, let him be deposed along with the one who ordained him, and let him be entirely cut off from communion, as Simon Magus was by Peter.

Also from the same canons, chapter 31:

If any bishop, using secular powers, obtains a church through them, let him be deposed, and let all who communicate with him be expelled. Against which canon, whoever procures powers for himself at a price — even if those powers be ecclesiastical — they without doubt become secular, as the blessed Gregory says: “If money has any place in ecclesiastical offices, what was sacred becomes secular.”

Also from the same. From the Council of Chalcedon, chapter 2 \[*Concilium Chalcedonense* c. 2\]:

If any bishop has performed an ordination for money and has reduced to a price the grace that cannot be sold, and has ordained for money a priest or deacon or anyone among those numbered in the clergy, or has promoted for money a steward or defender or anyone subject to a rule, for the benefit of most shameful profit: he against whom this is proven to have been attempted shall be liable to the peril of forfeiting his own rank. And he who was ordained shall gain nothing from this ordination or promotion — which was made through trafficking — but shall be a stranger to the dignity or responsibility which he sought by means of money. If any person has been an intermediary in such base and wicked gifts or receipts, if he be a cleric let him fall from his own rank; if he be a layman or a monk, let him be anathematized.
