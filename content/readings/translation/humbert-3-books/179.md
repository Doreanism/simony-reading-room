By this contagion a handful of diseased sheep infect the whole flock, as some carnal souls, touched by the sickness of ambitious avarice, grow weary of the pastures of heavenly teaching and slip away from the eyes of the shepherd, hiding themselves in the byways of vice. Thus when they are found by the plundering simoniacs, like stray sheep, they quickly agree with those men’s will, overpowered by the severity of their own malady. For the latter wish to kill, the former to die; the latter seek to slay, the former not to live; the latter to destroy, the former to perish. So both parties alike, to their own ruin and the corruption of the whole Church, devise and engage in carnal and worldly trafficking in the Holy Spirit. Their persecution is more grievous to Christ than that of any pagans, who rage against Christians with open savagery and passing blows, compared with these men, who by secret conspiracy under the guise of peace inflict everlasting deaths. And if Christ could not endure the persecution of his members without calling down from heaven upon Saul — who was ignorant of him yet had zeal for God, though not according to knowledge — and taking from him his common sight, what do we suppose will befall those who profess to know God and to be rulers of his Church, yet inflamed with diabolical zeal do not cease knowingly to persecute and slaughter innumerable souls of the faithful? And if Saul is struck with such force for bodily persecution, with what force, do you think, are these struck for spiritual persecution? Hence Saul deservedly lost for a time the eyes of his body, while these men have lost the eyes of their heart, so that, having henceforth become blind leaders of the blind, they fall together with their followers into the pit of eternal destruction. If they knew themselves to be blind, as the Lord says of those like them, they would have no sin, because they would be diligently seeking to bring forth fruits worthy of repentance. But because they say “We see,” their sin remains; to them the Lord cries out from heaven as often as, through his servants in whom he dwells as in heavens, he rebukes their presumption with the words of holy Scripture. Since he is not heeded now, he will afterward go forth from his chamber like a strong and mighty one, and will rouse his zeal like a warrior; he will cry out and shout, and will prevail over his enemies, saying to them then: “I have long held my peace, I have been silent and restrained myself; like a woman in travail I will speak” — “These things you did and I was silent.” Therefore, because you have scattered and consumed my flock little by little and in part, I will scatter and consume you all at once.

## CAPUT XXXI.

### On the malice of the simoniacs, and that they are worse than robbers and have no faith in God whatsoever.

Against these false witnesses — whom the now-impassible Jesus, yet still suffering such things in the sufferings of his Church as he once suffered in himself, pleads with the Father, saying through the Psalmist: “Deliver me not over unto the will of them that persecute me, for unjust witnesses have risen up against me, and iniquity has lied to itself” \[Psalm 26:12\] — it is impious to believe that they retain even the smallest spark of love for God and neighbor. For love of God and neighbor is the proper and distinctive virtue of the pious and holy, while the other virtues can be common to good and evil alike. How can it dwell in those who so despise God as to labor to sell him for any price, and so hate their neighbor as to close their hearts to him entirely, lest he freely receive what divine generosity willed and commanded to be given to him freely through them? If even those who possessed the substance of this world were to close their hearts to a neighbor suffering need and give nothing of their own freely, how could the love of God — which is inseparable from love of neighbor — remain in them? But these men not only give nothing of their own; they despoil their wretched neighbor of all the goods of body and soul in order to give what they received freely from God to be given freely, so hardened against charity that they do not open their mouths to God for the sake of his love or their neighbor’s except with the intervention of money paid or promised. And oh, how much worse than the most wicked robbers, who tear apart at once both the soul and body of their neighbor, while other robbers kill the body in such a way as to be unable to kill the soul.
