The first three Jude the brother of James sets out in order, saying: “Woe to them! For they have walked in the way of Cain, and for the sake of gain they have abandoned themselves to Balaam’s error, and have perished in Korah’s rebellion.” Of the middle one of these \[Balaam\] blessed Peter makes mention in this way: “Having followed the way of Balaam the son of Bosor.” The last two blessed Paul uncovers thus: “Just as Jannes and Jambres resisted Moses, so these men also resist the truth.”

For this reason, if one looks closely, presumption, greed, and obstinacy together are found to be condemned in these five figures. These three are indeed the seeds, the fuel, and the bulwark of all heresies alike. The presumption of Cain, among its other manifestations, consisted in this: that, as the blessed apostle John states, he, being of the evil one and his works being evil, out of envy toward his most holy brother whose works were righteous, presumed to take the priestly office upon himself by offering sacrifice to God. This priestly prerogative was conferred on the patriarchs before the Law by devotion alone, so that they, as priests under the Law, might build altars to the Lord and offer sacrifices — as did Noah, Melchizedek, Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, and blessed Job. His greed, moreover, drove him so far that he was the first to strive to reduce common and public goods to private and exclusive possession, by measuring out land and fixing boundaries. Bent greedily and busily over this pursuit, he brooded over it and anxiously stored away whatever it yielded to him, so that when at last, with his avarice grudgingly consenting, the single sheaf lifted from the earth could only be offered up from sheer envy of his brother. And truly wretched and remarkable was the greed of that treacherous man, for whom, when there were only two other men at the time — namely his father and his brother — the entire world with all its parts seemed small and narrow. Finally, obstinacy bound him inextricably to himself, when he even despised to heed the Lord himself restraining and deterring him from his sins and his premeditated fratricide. For Genesis records that the Lord said to Cain: “Why are you angry, and why has your face fallen? If you do well, will you not be accepted? And if you do not do well, sin is crouching at the door; its desire is for you, but you must rule over it.” Or, according to another translation: “If you offer rightly, but do not divide rightly, you have sinned — be still.” Balaam, in turn, is marked by the following presumption: that, being a diviner and an idolater, he believed himself to be a prophet, one who blessed those he blessed and cursed those he cursed, and who built altars as though a priest of God. His greed is also clearly recognized both from the book of Numbers and from the epistle of the blessed apostle Peter, where he foretells false teachers who will go out from the church — those who entice unstable souls, having hearts trained in greed, children of a curse, forsaking the straight path, going astray having followed the way of Balaam the son of Bosor, who loved the wages of unrighteousness. Agreeing with this, his fellow apostle Jude the brother of James threatens them: “Woe to those who have abandoned themselves to Balaam’s error for the sake of gain.” His obstinacy is most convincingly shown by the fact that, even having been rebuked and warned four times by the Lord, he did not at all turn his mind away from cursing and destroying Israel. Indeed, as sacred history recounts, the Lord twice forewarned him while he was still at home: “Do not go with these men, and do not curse the people, for they are blessed.” Yet the Lord afterwards commanded him: “If these men have come to call you, rise and go with them — only do what I tell you.” Then, when he was already on the road, an angel appeared to him and warned him sternly: “I have come to oppose you, because your way is perverse and contrary to me.” And yet, permitting him to go, the angel said: “Go with these men, but take care to speak only what I command you.” But that impious man, addicted to savage greed, secretly prepared snares of destruction for them, even while publicly wishing himself to die a death like theirs.
