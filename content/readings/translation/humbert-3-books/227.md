For as Solomon says, he who says to the wicked “you are righteous” shall be cursed by the peoples and detested by the nations. Against such the prophet warns: “My people, those who call you blessed are deceiving you and have wrecked the way of your steps.” The Lord stands ready to contend, and stands ready to judge the peoples. “The Lord enters into judgment with the elders of his people and their rulers. For you have devoured my vineyard; the spoil of the poor is in your houses.” This most wicked vice of flattery we must pursue all the more vigorously, the more ruinously we understand it to exceed nearly all other vices. For it destroys priestly authority itself, it destroys royal power and restraint, and it confounds what is right and wrong so that what is owed to God and men is not rendered. The flatterer is indeed a smooth enemy — a perjurer toward the princes to whom, contrary to his oath, he is faithless, when he either persuades them to do evil or praises their evil deeds with wicked approval, so that unrepentant they lay up wrath for themselves in the future; most destructive to the peoples, whom he allows neither to be wise nor to repent; hateful to God, against whom he works as an effective helper of the devil in turning men from good and inciting them to evil. Whence the venerable Prosper, outstanding in prose and verse alike, says of such men: “The tongues of flatterers bind souls in sins. For men delight in doing those things for which they not only fear no reproach, but even hear praise.” His epigram on this is as follows:

The sycophantic tongue heaps up the sin of the transgressor,
And binds with praise the one delighted by his crime.
So that the fallen has no care to recover his salvation,
While harmful honor flatters the guilty man.
Let rather a friend’s free voice of rebuke be heard,
And let it not permit blind poison to creep into the vitals,
Nor let the sick man, trusting a deceitful physician’s words,
Love the wounds of the praised pestilence.

## CHAPTER XXIII.

### On the peril of priests who keep silent wrongly and those who speak wrongly, and on the inseparable unity of the divinity and its own.

Therefore let these and very many similar things be patiently heard and wisely weighed; and at last, if the ministers of Christ and above all the priests wish to avoid their own ruin and that of others, let them beware — whether from human fear or human love — not only of speaking ill, but also of keeping silent wrongly; otherwise they will die if they make no sound either as they enter the tabernacle of God or as they leave. Likewise, let the ministers of the devil — parasites, ventriloquists, and above all erring clergy — cease from pleasing their superiors or inferiors while they favor the wicked and slander the good. Let the former presume to speak; let the latter learn to be silent. Otherwise the Lord will lay waste to the tongue of the Egyptian sea, and those who call \[men\] blessed will be deceivers, and those who are called blessed will be cast headlong. The psalmist sets forth their wickedness and punishment in a psalm against transgressors, saying: “They have spoken vanity every man to his neighbor; with flattering lips and a double heart they speak. May the Lord cut off all flattering lips and the tongue that speaks great things, who have said: We will magnify our tongue; our lips are our own; who is lord over us?” As therefore he is accursed who withholds his sword from blood, so also woe to him who flatters his friend and leads him in a way that is not good.
