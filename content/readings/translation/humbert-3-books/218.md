## CAPUT XVI.

on the zeal of holy kings for divine worship, and on the reward and chastisement of their peoples.

Likewise, wherever the ecclesiastical order and divine worship has been diminished or annulled, let \[princes\] gird themselves with the zeal and manner of King Josiah to restore and vindicate it, and let them approach the priests with pious devotion and humble, pious complaints after the example of Hezekiah — even if those priests do not fear to neglect their own office. For thus it is reported of him in the book of Chronicles: “Hezekiah, in the first year and first month of his reign, opened the doors of the house of the Lord and restored them, and he brought in the priests and Levites and gathered them in the eastern square and said to them: Hear me, Levites, and sanctify yourselves. Cleanse the house of the Lord God of your fathers, and remove all defilement from the sanctuary. Our fathers have sinned and done evil in the sight of the Lord our God, forsaking him. They have turned their faces away from the tabernacle of the Lord and presented their backs. They closed the doors of the portico and extinguished the lamps and did not burn incense and did not offer burnt offerings in the sanctuary to the Lord God of Israel. And so the wrath of the Lord was provoked against Judah and Jerusalem, and he gave them over to commotion and destruction and hissing, as you see with your own eyes. Behold, our fathers have fallen by the sword, and our sons and daughters and wives have been led away captive because of this wickedness. Now therefore it is my will that we make a covenant with the Lord our God, and he will turn away from us the burning of his wrath. My sons, do not be negligent. The Lord chose you to stand before him and to minister to him, to worship him and to burn incense.”

Whose devotion was not satisfied with correcting only those subject to him, but moved him also to admonish those who were strangers, as is added shortly after in the same place: “Runners went out with letters by the king’s command and that of his princes, throughout all Israel and Judah, and in accordance with what the king had commanded, proclaimed: Sons of Israel, return to the Lord God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, and he will return to the remnant that escaped from the hand of the king of the Assyrians. Do not be like your fathers and brothers, who departed from the Lord God of their fathers, and he gave them over to destruction, as you yourselves see. Do not stiffen your necks as your fathers did; give your hands to the Lord and come to his sanctuary, which he has sanctified forever. Serve the Lord God of your fathers, and the burning of his wrath will turn away from you; for if you return to the Lord, your brothers and sons will find mercy before their masters who led them away captive, and they will return to this land. For the Lord your God is merciful and compassionate, and he will not turn his face away from you if you return to him.”

And what more need be said? The deeds and saving words of this king accomplish much for the salvation of themselves and their own, for those who desire to reign well and prosperously — by whose industry God was so pleased that with a single prayer he destroyed 185,000 of his enemies and, when he was about to die, obtained fifteen more years of life for himself; whereas men of blood and deceit do not live out half their days. David finally, by his piety and justice, acquired for himself the throne of his kingdom in his offspring forever. But neither Jeroboam nor any of his successors, except Jehu, reached the third generation on account of their impiety and injustice; rather, not one of the posterity of any of them who urinated against the wall was left. Let the counselors and intimates of princes attend to this, and let the peoples of those princes attend to this as well, and not think themselves exempt from their princes’ presumption, but let them know that they are in every respect sharers in their guilt — indeed its very authors. For a hawk, however it may try, does not prey without feathers and plumage. Hence, when a ruler — who is but one — goes astray, countless multitudes of the people are cast down and scourged repeatedly, because nothing of this sort could have been accomplished without their favor or support. For those who fear or love man more than God not only favor their lords in their evil deeds, but moreover take boldness from their masters’ malice and lord it over and rise up against whoever lies in their power.
