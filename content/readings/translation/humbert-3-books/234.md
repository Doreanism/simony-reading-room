It was he who removed from the Gentiles the burden of circumcision and Jewish ceremonies, Peter declaring: “It has seemed good to the Holy Spirit and to us.” This Spirit of Jesus did not permit the apostles to go to Bithynia or to preach the gospel in Asia. He commanded Paul and Barnabas to be set apart for the work of the gospel. He forewarned the same Paul to cross into Macedonia, and afterwards foretold that he would be bound in the chains of the Jews and handed over to the Romans. The same Spirit bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God, and heirs — heirs together with Christ. Through him who searches all things, even the deep things of God, God has revealed to his own what no eye has seen, nor ear heard, nor has entered the heart of man. He through Paul gives counsel to virgins, and to the married and to widows as well. We have him as the pledge of Christ, crying in our hearts: “Abba, Father.” In him we are sealed on the day of redemption. Whoever does not have him does not belong to Christ. Finally, this same Spirit, as the same apostle attests, helps our weakness. For we do not know how to pray as we ought, but the Spirit himself intercedes for us with groanings too deep for words; and he who searches hearts knows what the mind of the Spirit is, because he intercedes for the saints according to God. From the mouth of Christ after the resurrection the apostles received him, so that they might forgive and retain the sins of men. What more need be said? He himself taught the rites and solemn celebrations of catechesis and scrutiny, of baptism itself, of Masses and the canonical hours. He himself instituted holy chrism; he himself arranged the various orders and offices of clerics and ministers in the Church. He himself distinguished people of different callings not only in their dwelling places and sustenance but also in their dress. He himself set over each calling rulers to be gradually perfected in unity. He himself dictated whatever is fitting for the service of the Deity and whatever is honestly and usefully expedient for human conduct. He himself preordained all the sacraments of the Church that have ever been, are now, or will be. He himself adorns the paradise of the Church with the roses of martyrs and the lilies of confessors. He himself, finally, imbued, guided, and directed the pens and minds of the evangelists and apostles and of all the orthodox, so that wherever his impulse led, they too followed in his steps. He himself is the spirit of life in the wheels — that is, in the canonical scriptures — so that when the living creatures go, they go; and when they stand, they stand; and when they are lifted from the earth, they are lifted, because his words are spirit and life. He himself so vivifies those scriptures that through them he also vivifies men. Without him not only does their letter profit nothing, but it even kills — as does the flesh of Christ when understood in a carnal sense. He himself promulgated through his own authority all the decrees of apostolic men and all the canons of the holy fathers in their various councils, issued generally and specifically for the correction and discipline of the churches of God — as blessed Pope Damasus attests, writing to Aurelius, archbishop of Carthage: “Those who willfully violate the canons are severely judged by the holy fathers and condemned by the Holy Spirit, by whose impulse and gift they were composed, since they who act or presume to speak willfully and not under compulsion against those same holy canons — or who freely consent to those who wish to do so — are not unfittingly seen to blaspheme the Holy Spirit. For such presumption is plainly one form of blasphemy against the Holy Spirit, since, as was said above, it acts against him by whose effort and grace those same holy canons were issued. For the devil’s wickedness is wont to deceive many with subtlety, and very often deludes the imprudence of certain persons under the guise of piety, so as to persuade them to embrace what is harmful instead of what is wholesome. Therefore the norm of the holy canons, which are founded in the Spirit of God and consecrated by the reverence of the whole world, is what we must faithfully know and diligently handle as authority, lest in any way we transgress the decrees of the holy fathers without unavoidable necessity — which God forbid — but rather, faithfully walking in them together with those who established them by divine inspiration, we may deserve, with the Lord’s help, through their merits to share in the glory of their reward and the fullness of their labor,” and so on.
