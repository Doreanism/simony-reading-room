But if this is granted even to those whose use and service he leaves and permits even to his own enemies, namely that they cannot be possessed without their inherent qualities, how much more is it granted to those things which by his sanctification he has decreed to belong to the sole support of his holy Church and of his holy ministers — not only from his enemies but also from his lay friends? Beyond doubt, against schismatics of this kind it is not unfitting to direct that complaint of Christ: “They divided my garments among them.” \[Psalm 21:19\] For are not these men also like profane soldiers who, for the wages of their mockeries and injuries which they persistently heap upon Christ in order to please his enemies and turn away his friends from him, continually divide his garments whenever they presume to take for themselves and divide among themselves things dedicated by the devotion of the faithful to him alone, and sanctified to feed and clothe his members still in want here below — bringing themselves not any sanctification but only a curse and everlasting damnation? Moreover, they feed him with gall and give him vinegar to drink, because by withholding from his poor the food owed to them, they fill their lives with intolerable bitterness from hunger and thirst; and even if they are seen sometimes to give something of their own to these members, it is like the scarlet cloak placed upon the more dishonored — so it is reckoned — members of Christ. For they do not thereby consult Christ’s honor, but their own, since they come to the aid of the miserable in their calamities for this sole reason: that they may not appear inhumane, or at any rate that they may not blush to be accused of not being embarrassed by those who importune them.

## CHAPTER XXVI.

### On the punishment of those who divide, and that only the followers of the apostles’ faith receive the Holy Spirit.

Since these things are so, what God has joined let not man put asunder — for he will do so at great peril — according to what Christ himself forbids in the gospel when questioned about divorce between spouses. For just as those two, who before marriage were two, are after marriage no longer two but one flesh, as the Lord attests; or as a rational soul and flesh make one man: so too the clerical order and the Church’s possessions are one sanctification of the Lord, and whoever divides them in a worldly manner produces nothing but spiritual destruction — just as if by adultery one dissolved a marriage, or by killing dissolved a man. Once this is done, it is inevitably the case that in dividing one thing you make two parties aggrieved: namely God and the husband, if you \[wrong\] the wife; and likewise God and the inner man, if you divide the outer. So you sin against God and against his servants when you separate their sanctified possessions from the sanctification that belongs to them. You do this more dangerously here than in adultery, because there you scandalize one person, but here you scandalize many thousands of men. And if whoever scandalizes one of God’s little ones is to be drowned in the depths of the sea with a millstone hung about his neck, what do we suppose he deserves who by his ambition and usurpation scandalizes so many of God’s little ones together with their elders? By this scandal he dissolves the marriage of Christ and the Church through his adultery, homicide, and sacrilege as well, when by his malicious zeal he drives from the Church the pledge of the divine marriage — the Holy Spirit — by whom it was sanctified, vivified, and adorned. The Holy Spirit, just as he fills the whole world and contains all things as creator, so that they may be able to subsist, also fills and contains the Church and all her things as sanctifier, so that they may be able not only to subsist but also to be holy. This sanctification a good will or devotion and prayer invites to sanctify whatever the faithful offer to the holy Church — those who, keeping the Lord’s commandments, lift earthly patrimonies from the earth to the heavenly treasuries and transfer them through the hands of the poor in spirit, of whom is the kingdom of heaven, and whose advocate and comforter is the Holy Spirit, from whom all sanctification comes, or rather who is sanctification itself. By him the whole body of the Church is sanctified and governed; he serves as vicar of Christ for his disciples and all the faithful everywhere in place of Christ’s bodily presence.
