In order to show that all sellers and buyers of heavenly grace must be expelled from the fellowship of the holy Church, the Lord thereupon began to drive out those who bought and sold in the Temple, and overturned the seats of those who sold doves — thereby teaching that the priesthood of such merchants is destroyed. For although there are some who are not physically driven from the fellowship of the holy Church, they are nonetheless spiritually rejected before God and stripped of their rightful honor.

## CHAPTER XIX.

Refutation of those who say that simoniacs possess grace, from which they are deposed.

But because to weighers of words and disregarders of arguments it seems that a sufficiently full answer has already been given above by reason of certain expressions which in the aforesaid canons are used improperly and figuratively according to the trope of catachresis — such as “obtained a dignity for a price,” “performed an ordination,” and the like — there is nevertheless one point now to be examined which they strive in vain to press against us. They say:

### Objection.

Behold, those ordained for money are ordered to be deposed. It is therefore established that they have obtained spiritual grace and been promoted to a rank of honor, from which they are commanded to be deposed. For deposition is the loss or deprivation of something already held.

### Refutation.

To these objectors it has already been shown above that a pool may be called a pool even when it contains no fish; and so by the same figure of speech, if they are unwilling to remain obstinate, they can readily perceive that deposition may likewise be properly said of one who has never had any promotion or rank of honor, except insofar as it is demonstrated by some outward resemblance on the surface — especially since the aforementioned canon of the Council of Chalcedon clearly supports our position, at the opening of which it is written: *If any bishop shall perform an ordination for money and reduce to a price the grace that cannot be sold.* Therefore, having turned back to their senses under the resounding and unanimous thunder of six hundred and thirty holy bishops, let the transgressors learn that grace cannot be sold. For those same holy fathers are recorded as having professed themselves ready to defend their synod even unto blood. Finally, though in an unusual yet proper manner of speaking, we call a deposition of this kind a revelation, just as the Lord Jesus is proclaimed by Simeon as set for the fall and rising of many. For one who falls rises again, whenever, forsaking evil, he girds himself toward the good. Hence, since it is manifest from so many testimonies of divine authority that the Holy Spirit cannot be sold, let those dark and nebulous arguments — or rather phantoms, containing nothing of truth but all of vanity — by which they argue for such things, vanish away like dispersing smoke.

### Objection.

If the one who receives money for the laying on of hands does not bestow the Holy Spirit upon the one he consecrates for a price, then surely he has not sold the Holy Spirit, nor has the other purchased it. But priesthood is neither conferred nor received without the gift of the Holy Spirit.
