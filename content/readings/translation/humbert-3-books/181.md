As great as is the difference between my company of horses — I, the Lord, who submerge in the waves Pharaoh and his anointed ones and his riders and his horses and his chariots — as great, I say, as the difference between my horsemen and the horses of Pharaoh, so much better are you than all daughters, you, the bride, you the ecclesiastical soul, better than all souls that are not ecclesiastical. Therefore you are an ecclesiastical soul and better than all souls; if you are not better, you are not ecclesiastical.” For which reason — woe is me! — how greatly I fear lest, in accordance with the judgment of so wise a man, that heavenly bridegroom, exasperated at his bride — namely our catholic Church — who has now long been wandering shamelessly through the heretics’ stalls, may give her a bill of divorce and say to her irrevocably: “Go forth and follow after the footsteps of the flocks and pasture your goats by the tents of the shepherds.” For she seems so to be ignorant of herself that she does not scruple to cling and dwell with profane shepherds in the byways, those who are known to pasture nothing but goats, from which they moreover traffic. Therefore one must take the greatest care lest her chastity be endangered by the lascivious frolicking of goats and the wantonness of he-goats through constant cohabitation, and so, as adulterers are admitted little by little into the bridegroom’s chamber, she be defiled to the crown of her head and no longer pasture lambs — whom by consenting and communicating with heretics she turns into goats to be placed on the left — from which meanwhile grow up leaders and their fathers, the wanton he-goats, who will come by lot, with many curses and the sins of all the catholic people imprecated upon their heads and brows, to the portion of the deserted devil, in whose hell there is none who confesses to the Lord. They will therefore receive with their goats the portion of the accursed desert, who refused to inherit the portion assigned to them, the paradise of blessing. They will become the clergy of the devil and the synagogue of Satan, because they refused to be the clergy of God and his Church. While this incomparable evil is not vigilantly guarded against, it inflicts such unceasing defects upon the Church of God that, like the moon in her monthly wane or at the dark of the moon, however whole she may be, she appears diminished or not at all. And this befalls her for the very reason that she is not ashamed to be ignorant of herself even when corrected and admonished. Hence by the just judgment of God, she who knows not herself is so unknown even to her own that it is now doubted by many whether she exists at all.

## CAPUT XXXIII.

### On the diminution of the Church and the multiplication of heretics.

Thus, with the deadly humors of the simoniac heresy embedded in her breast, the catholic Church is weighed down, burdened by its sentence, confounded and worn away by her internal and domestic war, so that the bride, now scarcely half-alive, barely flickers toward her last breath, and like a vast ship about to go under she already springs her groaning planks, or like a populous city that has been attacked and overcome by its own members at last labors and can scarcely hope for any of her people’s salvation. Because she despises small things, she has little by little fallen to the point of now hardly knowing where she is or whose she is, God alone knowing among so many thousands of the worst buyers and sellers who are his own — to whom also daily he speaks sweetly and intimately in the ear of the heart: “Fear not, little flock, for it is your Father’s good pleasure to give you the kingdom; sell what you have and give alms. Make yourselves purses that do not grow old, a treasure that does not fail in heaven, where no thief approaches nor moth destroys. For where your treasure is, there also will your heart be \[Luke 12:32–34\].” But oh, good shepherd Jesus, how small now appears the number of your lambs among the countless gatherings of goats! So greatly now has your gentle Jacob been overcome by his rough and hairy uterine brother that, according to Isaiah, briers and thorns grow for the feeding of goats throughout all the land, and for the few little lambs scarcely any pasture remains.
