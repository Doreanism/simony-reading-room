For otherwise, if the world — which consists of such people — can, while persisting in its infidelity, receive the Spirit of truth, either the Church does not exist, or if it does, Truth itself is convicted of falsehood for asserting that the Holy Spirit cannot be received outside of it. For if heretics and the rest of the faithless can receive the Spirit of truth, it follows that Catholics either do not receive him, or that they are no different from heretics — indeed that Catholics and heretics are the very same thing. But let this perversity be far from Christian minds! For the inviolable Truth confirmed the sentence of its inviolable promise with inviolable attestation, adding at once: “Because it seeth him not, neither knoweth him.” \[John 14:17\] Since then it is more to receive something than merely to see it or know it, it is established that the lying world does not receive the Spirit of truth, seeing that it is said to neither see nor know him. He who does not merit even the least — is it doubtful whether he merits the greatest? And he who is judged unworthy even of the last spark — is he worthy to receive the whole flame? But the same Truth, assuring his own concerning his Spirit, added: “But ye know him; for he dwelleth with you, and shall be in you.” \[John 14:17\] By this it is proven that the Holy Spirit is known by the disciples of Christ, and that he dwells among them and within them forever. And lest any caviller still prevaricate, let him recall what we took as an example a little above, where Christ, praying to the Father to sanctify his disciples, said: “Neither pray I for these alone, but for them also which shall believe on me through their word; that they all may be one.” \[John 17:20–21\] Furthermore, sanctification is neither given nor held without the Holy Spirit. It is therefore established that the apostles and their successors, who believe in their hearts and confess with their mouths the apostolic faith, know the Holy Spirit, receive him, and have him dwell among them and within them. Hence — to leave aside the other plagues for the moment — let simoniacs prove that, by following apostolic faith and teaching, they are selling and buying the Holy Spirit: and without controversy let it be conceded to them that they receive and possess the Holy Spirit, and can obtain or give him to whomever they wish, whether for payment or free of charge. But if they are unable to prove this — as in truth they cannot — especially since they know from the very founders of the primitive and whole Church, namely Peter the prince of the apostles and John the beloved apostle and evangelist of the Lord, and cannot deny, that their own progenitor was irrecoverably struck down for the mere estimation of so wicked a transaction: let them acknowledge that they are of that world which cannot receive the Spirit of truth, and repenting, let them hasten to become of that world which Christ chose out of their world.

## CHAPTER XXVII.

### On the grace of the Holy Spirit poured upon the disciples after the resurrection, and on the punishment of Ananias.

Now the Father sent this Paraclete in the name of his Son, so that he himself might teach the faithful all things and suggest to them all things whatsoever the same Son had said to them. This Spirit of his — who proceeds from the Father — Truth sent to his own, that he might bear testimony through them concerning them; he would neither be sent from the Son to them nor would he come, if the Son himself had not departed from here. Which was of the greatest benefit to them, so that they would not remain carnal, if they cared to know Christ only according to the flesh. Coming then, he convicts the unclean world of the sin of unbelief, of the righteousness of his own, and of the judgment passed on the devil who has been cast out. This Spirit of truth also, coming, teaches all truth to the disciples of Christ, so that having been made spiritual through him they may learn many things that Christ had to tell them — but because they were still carnal, they were not able to bear them. Nor does he speak of himself, but whatsoever he hears he speaks, and he announces to them what is to come.
