The blessed Ambrose likewise, in his commentary on the Gospel passage: “Many lepers were in Israel in the time of Elisha the prophet, and none of them was cleansed except Naaman the Syrian” \[Luke 4:27\], says after a few words: “Learn the precepts of proper virtue. He will prove his faith who refuses a reward. Learn both things from the teaching of words and deeds — but follow \[the example\]. You have the Lord’s commandment and the prophet’s example: to receive freely, to give freely, and not to sell but to offer the ministry. For the grace of God is not taxed at a price, nor is profit sought in the sacraments, but the service of the priest. It is not lawful, however — even if you yourself seek no profit — to fail also to restrain the hands of your household. Nor is only this required, that you alone keep yourself chaste and unspotted. For the apostle did not say ‘you alone,’ but ‘keep yourself chaste.’ What is sought, therefore, is not only your own chastity from such trafficking, but also that of your household. ‘For the priest must be blameless, managing his own house well, keeping his children subject with all chastity. For if a man does not know how to manage his own house, how will he have care for the Church of God?’ \[1 Timothy 3:2–5\]. Instruct your household, exhort them, watch over them. If a servant should go astray — for I do not exempt the capacity of human frailty — let him be repudiated when found out by the prophetic example: leprosy quickly follows shameful reward, and money wickedly obtained corrupts both body and soul. ‘You have received money,’ he says, ‘and from it you will possess a field and vineyard and olive groves and flocks; but the leprosy of Naaman shall cling to you and to your seed forever’ \[2 Kings 5:27\]. Do you see that because of the deeds of the originator, the succession of the heir is condemned? The guilt of a sold mystery is inexpiable, and the vengeance of heavenly grace passes to posterity. Finally, the Moabites and others shall not enter the Church of God unto the third and fourth generation — that is to say, speaking more plainly, until a succession of many generations should blot out the guilt of their ancestors. But since those who sinned against God through the error of idolatry appear to have been punished unto the fourth generation, it seems a harsher sentence indeed by which, on prophetic authority, the seed of Gehazi is condemned to perpetuity on account of his greed — especially since our Lord Jesus Christ has through the washing of regeneration granted remission of sins to all.”

## CAPUT XVII.

### Recapitulation of the foregoing, followed by the Judgment of Blessed Augustine.

From the foregoing assertions of so great and prudent a man, we are taught in many ways that episcopal grace cannot be received for a price — only leprosy can. And yet does anyone dare to open his mouth in objection, growling that he or anyone else can receive from such leprous men — that is, simoniacs — some grace of the holy orders, which they do not have, rather than solely the leprosy which they received and do have? And when that most illustrious doctor declares without hesitation that the guilt of a sold mystery is inexpiable and that the vengeance of heavenly grace passes to posterity — so that the example of Gehazi clings to the seller and to his seed in perpetuity — of what weight are those who contradict him from the opposite side? “The soul that sins, that soul shall die, and the son shall not bear the iniquity of the father” \[Ezekiel 18:20\], especially since the Lord himself says: “I will visit the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth generation” \[Exodus 20:5\].
