## CAPUT XLI.

### On the savagery of this beast and on the mark of Christ and of Antichrist.

But this beast, which at first fawned with signs as if domestic, in order to seduce, at the end rages like an untamed wild animal, terrifying all with its roar and fury, so that there is no one left who would now oppose its savagery for fear of death. This can be clearly understood from the single heresy of the simoniacs, which in earlier times appeared so mild that its wickedness could scarcely be perceived. Now, however, it has burst forth into such ferocity that — just as in the time of the Arians — men of our own time are said to have been publicly beaten, stripped of their possessions, exiled, and put to death by the simoniacs merely for having admonished them with kind and gentle words to come to their senses. Thus this beast, which first extended its head as mild and lamb-like, gradually raises its tail like a cedar, so that it now spares no age, no sex, no condition, no profession, no order, no power. From the greatest to the least, it drives peoples in throngs to battle against the Lamb and his army. And before it, to be sure, it sends many; but behind it, it drags innumerable others — so many that, along with those who savored earthly things, the dragon’s tail, having become its follower, also drags down to earth the spiritual ones who were seen to shine in the Church like stars in the sky. Thus from the ambitious and thorny bramble, once the fire went forth, it burned not only the barren forests — which, having been rejected by the olive, the vine, and the fig tree, had chosen it as their king — but also devoured the very cedars of Lebanon \[cf. Judges 9:15\]. That it might accomplish this malice, it marks the right hands and foreheads of all who consent to it with the mark of Antichrist; and whoever does not have this mark will be able neither to buy nor to sell. One may ask what this mark is: it is surely perfidia — which is also called properly impietas \[“impiety”\]. For just as the Catholic faith, which is also called pietas \[“piety”\], is the mark of Christ, so too heretical perfidia is recognized as the mark of Antichrist. And just as Catholics bear the mark of Christ on their right hand and forehead, so likewise heretics bear the mark of Antichrist. For we carry the stigmata of the cross of Christ on our right hand, which we make for ourselves and for others with the slightest motion of the right hand whenever it is appropriate, which we also bear impressed on our foreheads with the saving chrism. We are not ashamed of it, but rather glory in it, so as to profess with the apostle: *I bear on my body the marks of Jesus Christ* \[Galatians 6:17\]. And with Jerome: “I carry the standard of the cross on my forehead.” And how fittingly is it affixed to the forehead, which is the principal and more honorable part of the head and the place of shame — as upon the lintel of our outer person, to forewarn the principal part of our inner person, which is reason, that the cross of Christ is to be set before it as an eternal sign and protection. Thus we should so suffer with him that we may reign with him, and count it our highest glory to suffer shame for his name, and be ashamed of nothing for his sake but sin. So too on our right hand, which after the head holds first place among the limbs and is the industrious imitator of the arts and the administrator of the various services of its body and of others, there is the mark of the cross — by which we are reminded that in all our actions we must attend to the deeds and words of the crucified Christ, as the apostle says: *Whatever you do in word or deed, do all in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, giving thanks to God the Father through him* \[Colossians 3:17\]. When we do this, nothing will be sinister but all will be rightly done, and the left hand of evils will not know what our right hand is doing.
