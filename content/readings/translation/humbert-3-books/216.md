For obedience is better than sacrifices, and to listen is better than to offer the fat of rams; because to rebel is as the sin of divination, and to refuse to comply is as the crime of idolatry. Because therefore you have rejected the word of the Lord, he has rejected you, that you should not be king.’ And Saul said to Samuel: ‘I have sinned, because I have transgressed the commandment of the Lord and your words, fearing the people and obeying their voice. But now, I pray, bear my sin and return with me, that I may worship the Lord.’ And Samuel said to Saul: ‘I will not return with you, because you have rejected the word of the Lord, and the Lord has rejected you, that you should not be king over Israel.’ And Samuel turned to go away. But Saul seized the hem of his cloak, and it tore, and Samuel said to him: ‘The Lord has torn the kingdom of Israel from you this day and has given it to your neighbor who is better than you. Moreover the Strength of Israel will not lie or repent, for he is not a man, that he should repent.’ And Samuel saw Saul no more until the day of his death. In this most severe divine sentence against both kings, let Christian princes take note: neither devotion nor necessity was accepted as an excuse; both were divinely punished on account of the priesthood they had usurped for themselves. And indeed Uzziah lost the health and cleanness of his body and his kingdom; while Saul, handed over to an evil spirit, lost the Holy Spirit and his kingdom, and found the death of his soul and body, slain by his own hand, and the destruction of his entire house. And certainly neither of them presumed to take for himself the breastplate \[the rational\], or the sacred stole, or the golden plate, or any of the priestly vestments; only the first offered a burnt offering, while the other seized a censer to offer incense. It should also be noted how much more excusable and tolerable is what necessity compels, compared with what the will presumes. For although Saul offered the burnt offering under the compulsion of necessity, he nonetheless did not so provoke the Lord or the prophet that he was entirely abandoned by them; for afterward the Lord glorified him with many victories, and the prophet visited him many times, greeted him, and at the Lord’s word commanded him to go and destroy Amalek. But when he presumed the same thing of his own will, immediately the good spirit of the Lord departed from him, and the evil spirit of the Lord began to trouble him, and thereafter he had no victory and saw Samuel no more.

## CAPUT XV.

on the great scourges that afflict princes together with their peoples on account of presuming upon the priestly office.

Let Christian princes attend to what great scourges afflict them together with their peoples on account of presuming upon the priestly office, and let them cease presuming anything of it for themselves, lest they put on not only a simple, but a double confusion, as if donning a double cloak. For the Lord God of vengeance, the strong and jealous God of vengeance, is not accustomed to leave such wickedness unpunished, but avenges it either in the present age or in the age to come, or certainly in both.
