But because, as goodness advances by its own degrees, so too does malice, this pretense of somewhat excusable trafficking clung to the Church as long as some degree of Christian decency or honorableness remained rooted in our forebears. But when, with sins prevailing, they lapsed entirely into ignorance of God, they cast off every coloring of excuse or any semblance of honorableness and no longer troubled to cloak their avarice, but allowed it to rage openly with bare arms; and it, having at last attained its most wicked freedom, drove those greedy for ecclesiastical dignities to the point of now not deducting or subtracting anything at all from their own purses or those of their associates or from their resources, but scraping together the entire price given or promised from the revenues or possessions of the churches over which they are set. Hence throughout and especially across the whole of Italy one sees the churches of God and monasteries and other religious houses — some destroyed and overthrown from their foundations, some excavated, some still hideous with half-ruined roofs and walls threatening to collapse, some deserted by men and left only to noxious beasts and unclean birds, some filled with brushwood and nettles; and some, though they still appear outwardly standing in walls and buildings and inhabited, are found stripped of all their decor and interior ornament alike in books and in the vessels and vestments of ecclesiastical ministry, so that of the many things which devout antiquity had acquired and prepared for pious places, not so much as some little psalter or earthen Samian vessel or linen corporal remains. And certain houses, once renowned for many and various estates, castles, towns, households, and goods, now retain not a little field, not a little hut, not a little slave, not a little donkey or kid, nor anything at all of what they possessed — to such a degree that the very courts of the sanctuary and the burial grounds of Christians are plowed and cultivated by a stranger farmer for himself and filled with crops or vines.

## CAPUT XXXVI.

### How the simoniacs sell not only the property of the Church but the princes themselves together with their peoples to the devil.

In such fashion are the bones of many saints and devout Christians indiscriminately dragged from their very tombs and left lying bare and abandoned on the face of the earth like the bones of irrational animals. Wretched me — I recall frequently seeing, within those very walls, the pavements of what were once noble basilicas being plowed and sown, or cattle stabled in them. And for such great devastation, it is not the Vandal, the Goth, the Hun, the Lombard, or the Hungarian who is so much the author as the simoniac enemy, who dissipates and squanders the moveable and immoveable and self-moving goods of venerable places by selling and donating them to relatives and strangers alike, so as to leave nothing for himself or his successors but tears. Would that he at least only plundered what he found prepared by his predecessors and has at hand during his own time, and did not also dispose of what he does not yet have. For the tithes and offerings of the living and the dead, and all the revenues that the Church would have received after him even to the end of the age, he sells or donates to secular men by diabolical contracts, and by whatever means and conditions he can, he alienates them in perpetuity — so that those who will serve God in future generations and work out their own salvation and that of others shall find nothing left to them, neither for the food of a single hour nor for clothing nor for lodging through the night. Hence there are bills in the hands of secular men, hence diverse documents and instruments of charters, ill-secured by the subscriptions of such men, by which the ancient chirographs and canonical privileges of the churches of God are emptied; and all ecclesiastical possession is consigned and confirmed to lay right until the end of the age. The decrees and privileges of catholic pontiffs can no longer resist the writings of these heretics; no canons or synods of the holy fathers can any longer prevail; no edicts and precepts of emperors or laws of devout princes can any longer stand in the way.
