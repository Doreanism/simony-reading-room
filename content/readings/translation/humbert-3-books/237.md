There is also another thing asked for in that prayer, namely that heretics and schismatics may return to the unity of divine truth. In the preface to that prayer we are also enjoined to pray for them, that God our Lord may deign to recall them from all their errors to the holy catholic and apostolic mother Church. Hence it is clearer than light that heretics are not present in the unity of the catholic and apostolic Church, to which the universal Church prays that they may be recalled and to which they may return. Therefore either simoniacs are not heretics and are within the Church, or they are certainly heretics and are outside the Church. Now if they are within the Church, they are Catholics and can receive the Holy Spirit, and so they are no longer to be reckoned or called heretics. But if they are outside the Church, they are certainly either heretics or pagans and therefore cannot receive the Holy Spirit, because he cannot be received outside the Church — as the Lord protests above, whose sentence without doubt all the orthodox have followed; and the distinguished Prosper, following the same, says: “Just as one must consider what and to whom you vow, so too must one consider where you vow, because the place of true sacrifice is nowhere outside the catholic Church.” And declaring this also in verse he adds:

He who renders pious vows to the Lord who thunders knows

That there is one place for divine worship

Throughout the whole world, which the true Church alone provides,

And without which there is no merit of righteousness.

In which words it is plainly declared that neither the true Church, nor the true sacrifice, nor divine worship exists or is found outside the catholic \[Church\]. And so either simoniacs will be not merely proclaimed but actually held to be Catholics, or they will certainly be convicted of possessing neither the true sacrifice, nor the place of the true sacrifice, nor divine worship — just as other heretics do not. What then do they equally possess? Only falsehood and the worship of demons. But let it be granted, one says, that among them there is sacrifice, priesthood, and the other sacraments and insignia of Catholics. And oh, how many things are said to exist which are demonstrated to contain within themselves absolutely none of the realities whose names are used to indicate them! Since this has been shown abundantly above, it can also be shown from the words of the prophet Ezekiel, where he says of those who prophesy from their own heart: “Woe to the foolish prophets who follow their own spirit and have seen nothing. Like foxes in the desert, your prophets have been, O Israel. You have not gone up into the breaches, nor built a wall for the house of Israel, that you might stand in battle on the day of the Lord. They see vain things and divine lies, saying: ‘The Lord has said,’ when the Lord has not sent them; and they persist in confirming the message. Is it not a false vision you have seen, and a lying divination you have uttered? You say: ‘The Lord has said,’ when I have not spoken. Therefore thus says the Lord God: Because you have spoken vanity and seen lies, behold, I am against you, says the Lord God, and my hand shall be against the prophets who see vain things and divine lies. They shall not be in the assembly of my people, nor be enrolled in the register of the house of Israel, nor shall they enter the land of Israel; and you shall know that I am the Lord God, because they have led my people astray, saying: Peace — and there is no peace. And when the people built a wall, they smeared it with whitewash.” \[The prophet continues:\] “Say to those who smear it with whitewash that it is about to fall; for there shall be a flooding rain, and I will send great hailstones crashing down upon it, and a stormy wind shall scatter it.
