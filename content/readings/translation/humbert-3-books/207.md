as I have confirmed by hearing and sight, and moreover learned from the mouth of the Emperor Constantine Monomachus of orthodox memory, when I was stationed in that royal city \[Constantinople\] as a representative of the apostolic see for its responses — neither the emperor himself nor any layman ever presumes to exercise any authority over the disposal of churches, ecclesiastical ordinations, or ecclesiastical property, but all such matters, once relinquished, are left entirely to be administered by metropolitans and ecclesiastical persons, from the time of the greatest and first Constantine, who issued a memorable sentence on these matters that is worthy of reverence by all Catholics. For when presiding over the holy synod gathered at Nicaea \[325\] and observing that a complaint from certain persons had been brought before him, he said: “You cannot be judged by anyone, for you are reserved for the judgment of God alone; for you have been called gods, and therefore cannot be judged by men. Go and settle your own causes among yourselves, for it is not fitting that we should judge gods.” Whereupon the holy synod itself promulgated: “It is the ancient custom that the bishop of the city of Rome should hold the primacy and govern with his care the suburban territories and the entire province. Let those who are in Egypt, Alexandria’s bishop, have care over all. Likewise let the proper privileges be preserved for the metropolitan churches in the vicinity of Antioch and in the other provinces.” Furthermore, what is found in the decrees of the Roman pontiffs concerning ecclesiastical affairs and business, we judge should be inserted here as well. It is as follows: “No one who reads the divine scriptures is ignorant that in the beginning of the nascent Church, when the disciples were gathered together with the multitude of believers — among whom there was one heart and one soul, who sold their fields and possessions and brought the proceeds, which were distributed to each as need required — the apostles foresaw the future Church among the Gentiles, especially because the Lord had foretold them: ‘Go into all the world and preach the gospel’ \[Mark 16:15\], or because they knew they were to be expelled from Judaea and dispersed among the Gentiles, and that the Church was to be gathered from an unformed people. For this reason they did not acquire lands in Judaea, but only their proceeds, to support the needy. But when the Church grew and increased amid the storms and adversities of the world, it reached the point where not only Gentiles but even the Roman princes, who held the monarchy over nearly the entire world, came running to the faith of Christ and the sacraments of baptism. Among these, a most devout man, the first Constantine, openly embraced the faith of truth and granted permission throughout the whole world, to all dwelling under his empire, not only to become Christians but also to build churches, and decreed that lands should be given \[to them\]. Finally this same prince also established immense gifts and the building of the temple of the first see of the blessed Peter, prince of the apostles, to such a degree that he relinquished the imperial residence over which the Roman princes had presided and granted it to benefit the blessed Peter and his successors.
