Whoever hears the voice of simoniac promoters saying: “I will not give you baptism, I will not give you the sacrament of the body and blood of Christ, I will not give you the Holy Spirit, I will not give you any ecclesiastical grade, nor will I perform the consecrations of churches or any other administrations of the divine sacraments, unless you give me a price” — that person is beyond all doubt not a sheep of Christ, against whose voice he hears and acknowledges the voice of strangers. For this reason he beyond all doubt now follows a stranger, not Christ, by whom he is no longer acknowledged — even if he is heard crying out to the one whose voice he has ceased to acknowledge: “Lord, Lord.” If he had retained that voice, he would flee as quickly as possible from the stranger uttering a voice unknown to him.

## CHAPTER XLII.

### With what vigilance the sheep ought to resist thieves who have broken into the sheepfold.

If, therefore, the sheep cannot resist simoniac thieves who break into the sheepfold, let them at least resist those who have entered, in the only way they can: namely, by being unwilling or compelled to hear them in such a way as to follow them as a shepherd, or even to hear them as one would hear a hired hand. But — as has been said — let them do what alone is in their power: flee from them, avoiding their communion, their services, and their establishments gathered from the blood of the sheep they slaughter and destroy. Whatever terrors from men, or damage to their bodies or possessions, Christ’s sheep are besieged or oppressed by, let them strive above all to escape injury to their souls, lest they be devoured alive by robbers, and let them not fear those who kill the body in such a way that they cannot kill the soul, but rather him who can destroy both in the gehenna of unquenchable fire. Let them consider that if those who at the end of the world follow the antichrist, seduced in the time of the greatest collapse of the Christian faith — seduced not only by great signs and wonders but also compelled by torments of universal persecution — will be without excuse, then far more without excuse are those who now, in the great glory of the Christian name, follow thieves and robbers, seduced by blandishments and terrors. For just as it was foretold to those that false prophets and false christs would arise, so also to these it was foretold that thieves and robbers would come and enter the sheepfold, the Lord thus forewarning his own in the Apostles: “Take heed that no man seduce you. For many will come in my name saying, I am he, and will seduce many. Then if any man shall say to you: Lo, here is Christ, and lo, there: believe it not; go not, neither follow. Behold I have foretold you. If therefore they shall say to you: Behold he is in the desert, go ye not out; Behold he is in the closets, believe it not. For behold, the kingdom of God is within you. You therefore take heed: behold, I have foretold you all things.” As if to say: “It is already in your power to be seduced or not seduced, and therefore you will be without excuse if you neglect or despise taking care, because I have foretold all things to you.” For he has shown beforehand where the kingdom of God is — namely, in the Catholic Church, that is, within you, who keep and retain the apostolic faith and teaching in heart and mouth. Hence where this is shipwrecked or corrupted, let not Christ nor his kingdom be believed to be there, even if it is called and appears to be a desert or a secret chamber. O how many hermits, in the time of the Arians, declared that Christ was in their desert — when, infected with the Arian plague, they harbored the antichrist among themselves! O how many, who were thought to be ecclesiastics at that time, cried out and proclaimed that Christ was in their inner chambers — when they nonetheless concealed the antichrist among themselves — such as Eusebius of Caesarea, that fiercest champion of Arius, as blessed Jerome attests; such as Didymus and Eutyches, and very many others, both from the cities and from the solitudes. What was said, therefore, concerning the aforementioned heresy, let the same be understood of the others.
