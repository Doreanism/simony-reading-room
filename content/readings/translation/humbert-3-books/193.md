This sum is found in several Greek words reckoned in Greek, among which is “Teitan,” written among us as “Titan” — whose plural is “Titanes,” understood as the giants who in legend once attempted to storm the heavenly ones, and named after the sun, which is said to have previously rejected its accomplices; whence the sun is still called Titan, and also “Apollon” in Greek, meaning “the destroyer,” because by cooking with its heat all the moisture of green plants it destroys herbs. By this name the Apocalypse names the king of the abyss, who, as it is added there, has in Latin the name “Exterminans” \[the Destroyer\] \[Revelation 9:11\]; and the Greek derivative “APOLLYEIN,” meaning destruction, if you calculate it, likewise yields 666. Whether then you call antichrist a giant or destruction, it matters not: for he is a giant who opposes God and “exalts himself above all that is called God or that is worshiped” \[2 Thessalonians 2:4\], and he is destruction, because he is the man of sin and son of perdition who, though he promises salvation to all, does not save even himself — as the psalmist cries: “The king is not saved by great might, and the giant shall not be saved by the abundance of his strength” \[Psalm 33:16\]. Against whom one must gird oneself also from behind, as a giant equipped for battle prepares against a force opposing him — as the psalmist also testifies: “He exulted as a giant to run his course” \[Psalm 18:6\]. To which blessed Ambrosius responds: “He proceeds from his bridal chamber — the royal hall of modesty — a giant of twofold substance, eager to run his course.” By whose grace just as we are saved through seeing, so too our earlier fathers merited to be saved through hoping, as the apostle Peter says: “Through the grace of Jesus Christ we believe we shall be saved, just as they also were” \[Acts 15:11\]. Whom those fathers prefigured under the name of salvation-to-come in the number eight, as Noah was the eighth in the ark \[1 Peter 3:20\] and Abraham was circumcised in Isaac on the eighth day \[Genesis 17:12\]. Whom we also retain under the name of salvation given and to be given in the same number — believing, through his resurrection on the eighth day and through the coming of his Spirit from that resurrection on the first day of the eighth week, that Jesus rises in our souls now and will rise in our bodies on the last day — in the number eight. He is not without reason signified by the number that contains six and seven, so that, in the image of the world’s Creator, he shows that after the perfection of good works, as after six days, we are to have the seventh of the soul’s rest, and finally to receive the eighth of the resurrection of bodies as well.

## CAPUT XLV.

### On the Number of Antichrist’s Name

Hence antichrist also, so that nothing less than the devil should be thought of him, strives as much as he can — as one supremely skilled in deception — to twist the mystery of Jesus Christ toward himself in signs and numbers, even appropriating his names, so that he too may be called Messiah or Christ, giant or strong one, though in truth he is the son of perdition who comes to destroy everything that the Lord Jesus, according to his name, had come to seek and save, because they were lost \[Luke 19:10\]. To his injury and to conceal his own darkness, antichrist desires to be called Titan or Apollo, as though he were the principal sun — which Malachi thus foretells: “To you who fear my name, the sun of justice shall rise” \[Malachi 4:2\] — when in fact he is utterly the slave of pitch-black, sulfurous Gehenna, a rebel against the unchangeable sun, destroying all the green and germination of faith. He is to be conceived and born in secret from a wicked incest, and from the very moment of his conception he is to be seized by the prince of darkness himself and adopted and held as his son, so that by antichrist’s power and lying signs our first hope — the offspring of a virgin and a man, that is, of the most holy Mary — may be emptied of meaning \[cf. 2 Thessalonians 2:9\], and this bastard \[“manzer”\] may be declared and held to be the Son of Man in his place, and yet be far superior and more powerful than him. And all these things and many more besides he will feign, so as to subvert our king’s humility with pride, his piety with impiety, his clemency with cruelty, his truth with falsehood, through certain of his dodges — because they will contain some semblance of truth, he will more easily subjugate the unwary to himself. For this reason he labors in vain to make the number of his name equal to the number of the name of Jesus Christ, because he does not approach it.
