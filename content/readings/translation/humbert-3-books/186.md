Thus after the Arian Auxentius, the Catholic Ambrose found the income and lodging of the Milanese church left intact for him. So too Nectarius after the Cynic Maximus. So too Proterius after Dioscorus. And if you survey all the other heretical beasts, you will find none more destructive than this one, which so tears apart the sheep of Christ that it leaves neither sheepfold nor pasture for those who will live after. For truly this heresy not only devours and grinds up everything with great iron teeth and tramples the rest underfoot with its feet, like that beast which prefigured the Roman Empire \[cf. Daniel 7\], but moreover, as flame issues from it, it leaves nothing unconsumed before it. Wherefore if the prophet, struck dumb with astonishment at the power and monstrousness of that beast, passed over its name in silence, though he did not omit the names of the others however terrible they were, to what beast shall this one be compared, or by what name shall it be called, since it is found to be more unlike and more cruel than all the rest? Yet although Daniel — whose name means “the judgment of my God” — did not, apart from the teeth and feet, show that beast which was the prophecy of the fourth kingdom either by name or form, by the grace of the Lord, that is, John \[the Apostle\], portrayed this beast — which is the prophecy of heresy — in his Apocalypse, suppressing only the name, as though making it visible to all, saying: *And I saw another beast ascending from the earth, and it had two horns like a lamb, and it spoke like a dragon, and exercised all the power of the first beast, that is, which was like a leopard, whose feet were as a bear’s, and whose mouth was as a lion’s, in its sight. And it caused the earth and those who dwell therein to worship the first beast. And it performed great signs, so as even to make fire come down from heaven to earth in the sight of men. And it seduced those who dwell on the earth through the signs which were given to it to perform in the sight of the beast, saying to those who dwell on the earth that they should make an image of the beast. And it was granted to give spirit to the image of the beast, and to cause all who would not worship the image of the beast to be slain. And it will cause all, small and great, rich and poor, free and slave, to receive a mark on their right hand or on their foreheads, so that no one may buy or sell, except one who has the mark or the name of the beast or the number of its name*, and so on \[Revelation 13:11–17\]. Concerning this, shortly afterward an angel proclaims with a loud voice to men: *If anyone worships the beast and its image and receives its mark on his forehead or on his hand, he also shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is mixed unmixed in the cup of his anger, and he shall be tormented with fire and sulfur in the sight of his angels and in the presence of the Lamb, and the smoke of their torment ascends forever and ever, and they have no rest day or night, who worship the beast and its image, and whoever receives the mark of its name* \[Revelation 14:9–11\]. Furthermore, that second beast he subsequently calls the false prophet, saying: *And I saw from the mouth of the dragon and from the mouth of the beast and from the mouth of the false prophet three unclean spirits in the form of frogs. For they are spirits of demons performing signs, and they will go out to the kings of the whole earth to gather them for battle on the great day of God Almighty. Behold, I come like a thief. Blessed is the one who watches and keeps his garments, lest he walk naked, and they see his shame* \[Revelation 16:13–15\]. And after some further words: *And I saw the beast and the kings of the earth and their armies gathered to make war against him who sat on the horse and against his army. And the beast was captured, and with it the false prophet who had performed signs in its presence, by which he had seduced those who received the mark of the beast and those who had worshipped its image. These two were thrown alive into the lake of fire burning with sulfur, and the rest were slain by the sword of him who sat on the horse, which proceeded from his mouth, and all the birds were gorged with their flesh* \[Revelation 19:19–21\]. And after these things, concerning the glory of the saints even in the present age, he says: *And I saw thrones, and they sat upon them, and judgment was given to them; and the souls of those beheaded for the testimony of Jesus and for the word of God; and those who had not worshipped the beast nor its image, nor received its mark on their foreheads or on their hands; and they lived and reigned with Christ for a thousand years. But the rest of the dead did not come to life until the thousand years were completed* \[Revelation 20:4–5\].
