And if the spirit of the world has already been condemned and cast out along with him, it is plain that the world he rules has already been condemned; nor is it any longer in the world that God loved and — having redeemed it with the blood of his Son — cleansed with the washing of water in the Word, so that God himself might be with them as their God. For *what part has a believer with an unbeliever?* Therefore, whoever holds wrong views about the Holy Spirit should take note of what blessed Pope Damasus, writing among other things to Paulinus, patriarch of Antioch, says: *If anyone has rightly believed concerning the Father and the Son, but has not held right views concerning the Holy Spirit, he is a heretic.* And that all heretics who hold wrong views concerning the Son of God or the Holy Spirit are found to be in the company of Jews and pagans. Indeed, to the Jews saying: *We are not born of fornication* — that is, of idolatry — *we have one Father, God,* the Lord Jesus answered: *If God were your Father, you would surely love me. For I have come forth from God and I came; for I did not come of myself, but he sent me. You are of your father the devil.* And to Simon Magus, who sought to purchase the Holy Spirit for money, Peter said: *You have no part nor lot in this faith.* But lest this sustained refutation — though entirely necessary — if prolonged further should press and assail our adversary so relentlessly that our weary fellow soldier falls in the struggle, the writer’s intention ought to provide the delicate reader with a moment to breathe, and accordingly let this be the end of the first little book.

### I. What the blessed apostle Peter and his fellow apostles thought concerning heretics.

### II. On piety and impiety, and on the presumption of Cain and Balaam.

### III. On the sin of Korah and his followers, and their punishment.

### IV. On the common people who follow the presumptuous, and on the difference between the true and the false priesthood.

### V. On Jannes and Jambres, worse than whom are the simonians.

### VI. Where Christians are warned to avoid the supplanting by heretics, whose evil and punishment are shown therein.

### VII. On the condemnation of those who seek God insincerely, under the example of Ananias and Sapphira.

### VIII. On the fact that what is vowed to the Lord must not only be vowed but also rendered.

### IX. That the Holy Spirit appears to the faithful but is hidden from the unfaithful, by whom he is not even sought.

### X. On the difference between the excesses of those who err upon the foundation of Christ and those who err without his foundation.

### XI. That heretics are to be avoided, after the example of vipers and a corrupt tree.

### XII. That heretics are to be guarded against even more than vipers, and on the nature and typology of vipers.

### XIII. How vipers correspond to simoniacs.

### XIV. That those who subvert the sacraments and the words of Christ are all of one kind, and that they neither ask from him nor obtain from him.

### XV. How the Lord is in one sense not heard, and in another sense heard, by certain persons.

### XVI. Where it is argued that heretics do not hear God within the Church, as criminals do not.

### XVII. On the difference between merchants of the world and those who are called simonians.

### XVIII. On the fact that carnal members of the Church are not without faith, after the manner of the tribes of Reuben, Gad, and half of Manasseh.

### XIX. On the fact that simoniacs are invaded by Satan, after the example of Judas setting out to betray the Lord.
