Whatever such persons do in the divine sacraments by virtue of the office entrusted to them in the meantime is entirely holy — just as Judas \[acted\] before the conspiracy of betrayal, and Nicholas before the invention of his abominable sect. This is confirmed also by the treatise of Paschasius on the Body of the Lord, where one reads as follows: “Even if the priest be guilty, the office of his ministry is sacred, and the rank of priesthood is sustained by Christ the supreme pontiff, so that this mystery has nothing less — as regards the flesh and blood — from any unworthy person, provided that the consecration is performed in accordance with the institution of the apostolic lineage; for it is fulfilled not from that person himself, but from his office, by the supreme pontiff, with the grace that truth itself has promised; and the priesthood and the pontifical office are not diminished in any respect by the merit of any individual. For the power of consecrating resides in what is possessed by him who is ordained. Therefore those who return, having previously been baptized, are not rebaptized; just so, those who were previously ordained are not re-ordained upon returning to Catholic unity, if they have departed from it. But either they minister, if the utility of the Church demands this, or, if not, they nonetheless bear the sacrament of their ordination, and therefore hands are not laid upon them among the laity. There remains in them, therefore, the power of the sacrament — unprofitably for themselves, even when they consecrate while guilty — yet a power and ordination of consecrating sufficiently profitable to those who communicate in faith, hope, and charity in the grace of the blessing and administration received. Nor do these life-giving sacraments flowing from a heavenly source benefit the perverse unto salvation, since there is no fellowship of justice with iniquity, just as there is none of light with darkness. And yet such persons — even if they are adulterers and murderers and have committed many things on the devil’s side — while they sacrifice without yet having been cut off by the evangelical sickle, one is not to consider who and what they are, but what they give, and what those who communicate in faith receive; nor who possesses the power of consecrating, but what he possesses. For just as in the name of Christ, by his own testimony, even by members of the devil demons are cast out and many mighty works done through them, so also by the power of the same Christ, the body and blood are consecrated into a sacrament by the wicked — although it is said that God does not hear sinners — for over the water of baptism, and over the oil, and over the Eucharist, and over the heads of those upon whom hands are laid, all these sacramental acts are celebrated and accomplished. Therefore they are valid even through murderers; and although such persons do not have the Holy Spirit, God nonetheless is the one who gives that same Holy Spirit through their ministry and, from the office, consecrates the gift of his own generosity.” Lest perhaps the objection be raised against us from the judgment of the aforementioned man — because it is said here at the end that the Holy Spirit is given through the ministry of criminals even though they do not themselves have him — those who raise it should take note of what he set out at the beginning, where he speaks hypothetically thus: “This mystery has nothing less from any unworthy person, provided that the consecration is performed in accordance with the institution of the apostolic lineage; for it is fulfilled not from the person himself, but from his office, by the supreme pontiff, with the grace that truth itself has promised.” Whence it is clear that they have the Holy Spirit for ministry — which they received through the institution of the apostolic lineage — yet do not have it for themselves. They have it for administering good things to others, but not for turning evil away from themselves; they have it for believing rightly, but not for living rightly — as has been demonstrated abundantly above — and moreover that the ministry of heretics is not of Christ, nor is it received through the institution of the apostolic lineage.
