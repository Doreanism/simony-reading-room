*This is the first resurrection. Blessed and holy is he who has part in the first resurrection; over such the second death has no power, but they shall be priests of God and of Christ and shall reign with him for a thousand years* \[Revelation 20:5–6\].

### Recapitulation of the foregoing passage from the Apocalypse.

## CAPUT XXXVIII.

Since this apostolic prophecy is expounded in manifold and copious ways by the holy fathers, we think it necessary to indicate only this much briefly: that the first beast, like a leopard with feet of a bear and mouth of a lion, specifically foreshadowed Antichrist, whose dominion will come with marvelous speed and most cunning rapacity, and whose temporal life will end very swiftly, namely in forty-two months. For the leopard is a variegated, blood-thirsty, swift, rapacious, and headlong animal — so reckless that it plunges to its death in a leap — since, as Jeremiah says, it cannot change its spots. Whose feet are the feet of the bear: namely, all the heretics, who have feigned a certain semblance of human wisdom, as if of divine reason, and who the lower they are in the body of Antichrist, the more destructive they are. For it is by them that the joints of all the members are held together and governed; by them the whole mass of the diabolical body is sustained; by them it is carried, by them armed, by them it roams everywhere, by them it is tracked — not to be crushed, but to grow; and it is raised up by them as by its most solid foundations and columns. Whoever follows them, whether pagan, Jew, or Christian, is straightway incorporated into Antichrist and is carried through them to eternal destruction. Furthermore, as the Lord Jesus is called and is the mouth of the Father, so too that blasphemous one is the mouth of that roaring lion who walks about seeking whom he may devour — to whom the dragon, who is that same lion, has given his virtue and power as to a son and arm, so that he may rage with open and hidden malice alike throughout the world. But the heretics, whom John the apostle, the companion of God, foresaw to be incorporated in Antichrist and to be his feet, he perceived in the subsequent vision to be specifically signified in another beast, which ascended from the earth and had two horns like a lamb and spoke like a dragon. For all heretics who call themselves of Christ ascend, as beasts, from the earth, because they depart from the firmness of the Church through the pride of their ostentation and arrogance, into the service of that sea-monster swollen with secular power — namely, Antichrist — according to the words of the present apostle: *They went out from us, but they were not of us* \[1 John 2:19\]. For the earth here is fittingly understood as the holy Church, of which the Psalmist says allegorically: *Who has founded the earth upon its stability; it shall not be moved forever and ever. The earth is the Lord’s and the fullness thereof, the world and all who dwell therein. For he founded it upon the seas, and prepared it upon the rivers. Who shall ascend the mountain of the Lord, or who shall stand in his holy place?* \[Psalm 24:1–3\] For the Lord caused it \[i.e., the Church\] to stand above the seas, that is, above worldly arrogance and power. Above the rivers — that is, worldly lusts — he exalted it, so that it might be at once his singular mountain and his holy place. Of those who go forth from it, all display two horns like a lamb, because they either claim to receive the two Testaments or to observe the two commandments of charity, or they contend that their power pertains to the kingdom and priesthood of Christ, or that they have been sanctified by his baptism and cross. But in these things they show no truth beyond the mere outward resemblance of the lamb’s horns. And so they speak like a dragon, who did not stand in the truth; for when the devil speaks a lie, he speaks from his own, because he is a liar and the father of lies, and just as he spoke and speaks only in order to become a murderer, so too do these speak only in order to become murderers. They also exercise all the power of the first beast in his sight, for they rage against humanity no less than Antichrist himself, whether by hidden or open malice. Yet they exercise this power in his sight, because just as Catholics refer to Christ whatever they do by his authority, so heretics refer to Antichrist whatever they do by the power granted them by him.
