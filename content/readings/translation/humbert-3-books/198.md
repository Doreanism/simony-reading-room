## CHAPTER I.

### Against those who say that they have purchased not a consecration but the properties of the church.

Just as nothing in the nature of things is more solid and powerful than round and open truth, so nothing is more empty and worse than crooked and lurking falsehood. For such is the clarity and at the same time the efficacy of truth that, just as true light not only strikes the eyes of all but also shines in the darkness, so by contrast such is the obscurity and at the same time the feebleness of falsehood that it neither grasps nor perceives the truth by which it is seized and penetrated. That men have fallen into this condition through just condemnation, the author of truth — Truth itself — wished to show in the Gospel: *This is the judgment: that light came into the world, and men loved darkness more than light, for their works were evil. Everyone who does evil hates the light and does not come to the light, lest his works be exposed. But whoever does the truth comes to the light, so that his works may be made manifest, because they have been done in God* \[John 3:19–21\]. It is as if he said: A physician came into the world and found every person sick. Some, choosing rather to remain ill than to appear ill by approaching the physician, acted falsely. Others, choosing rather to appear ill than to remain ill, acted truly by approaching the physician. Whether of one group or the other, they were alike regarded as ill. But the works of the former were evil, in that through pride they resisted the physician and denied their own sickness; whereas the works of the latter were good, in that through humility they yielded to the physician and confessed their own sickness. This is plainly discerned by judgment also in simoniacs and in those ordained through their hands, as it is said, when some of them wisely acquiesce in the sentences of the holy fathers pronounced against simoniacs, and humbly laying bare their guilt seek the fitting remedy; whereas others, foolishly resisting, defend their fault with pride and contend that they have no need of a remedy. Nor is this surprising, since they undoubtedly belong to that generation which, according to Solomon, seems clean to itself yet is not washed of its filth. When it feels its falsehood surpassed and refuted by truth, it never ceases by whatever means it can to dodge and shore it up and cover it with fog, and strives to make void the commandment of God on account of its own invention. From this generation surely were also those whom Truth in the Gospel confutes, saying: *Woe to you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! For you say, “If anyone swears by the temple, it is nothing; but if anyone swears by the gold of the temple, he is bound.” Blind fools! Which is greater, the gold or the temple that sanctifies the gold? And, “If anyone swears by the altar, it is nothing; but if anyone swears by the gift that is on it, he is bound.” Blind fools! Which is greater, the gift or the altar that sanctifies the gift?* \[Matthew 23:16–19\]. They are not unjustly called blind and hypocrites, because they so resist the divine law’s testimony — *Whoever touches the temple and the altar shall be sanctified* \[cf. Exodus 29:37\] — as shamelessly to seek to persuade others that the sanctifier is nothing, while only the sanctified things are to be weighed and attended to.
