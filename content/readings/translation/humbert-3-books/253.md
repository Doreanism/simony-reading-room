There one will find not only these things, but also very many other mockeries, blows, dangers, and losses suffered by Catholics at the hands of heretics. After such evils, who could adequately recount with what constancy the Catholic church in Africa withstood the Vandals, more savage than any beast — compelled to live on for a long while under countless exquisite torments, and barely at last permitted to die? Therefore, woe to the prosperities of the Catholics of our own time, under whom the Christian faith is surrendered and extorted without any, or with hardly any, loss of self and one’s possessions under most Christian princes — a faith which no measure of unspeakable adversity was able to make our predecessors surrender or extort, even under sacrilegious tyrants. As to how long heretics are to be endured, then, and in what manner they are to be deposed: the Lord himself first determined this when he made known that they are not to be heard by the sheep but to be fled; and the God-speaking Paul thereafter confirmed it by deeds and words. For though Paul tolerated even quarrelsome, stubborn, greedy, covetous, and envious hirelings to such an extent that he declared himself to rejoice and to expect to rejoice in their preaching, he nonetheless signals that thieves and robbers — that is, heretical men — are to be avoided after a first and second rebuke \[cf. Titus 3:10\].
