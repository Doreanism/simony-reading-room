## Chapter XIII.

### On the Dignity of the Priesthood of the Old Law and on the Presumption and Punishment of the Kings of Judah and Israel.

Let them also weigh this: although the twelve tribes of Israel then had an equal number of princes — among them Nahshon from the tribe of Judah, from whom the scepter was not to be taken away, nor the ruler’s staff from between his thighs, until the King of Kings, Christ — yet none of them save Moses alone presumed to ordain from among his fellow tribesmen a high priest, priests, and Levites, and to institute and organize all the ceremonies of that time. For he himself, acting in the capacity of supreme pontiff and greatest primate, clothed his brother Aaron and Aaron’s sons, priests of the lower grade, with the priestly vestments, consecrated them, and anointed them. He also erected both tabernacles and, by anointing and sprinkling with water and hyssop, dedicated them together with all their utensils and altars and vessels, and with the same water sprinkled and sanctified the people. For, as the Apostle says to the Hebrews: “When every commandment of the Law had been declared by Moses to all the people, he took the blood of calves and goats, with water and scarlet wool and hyssop, and sprinkled both the book itself and all the people, saying: ‘This is the blood of the covenant which God commanded toward you’” \[Hebrews 9:19–20\]. He sprinkled the tabernacle also and all the vessels of the ministry likewise with blood, and under the Law almost everything is cleansed with blood, and without the shedding of blood there is no forgiveness \[Hebrews 9:22\]. Thereafter, when his brother Aaron died, he clothed Aaron’s son Eleazar with his father’s vestments on the mountain, so that he might discharge the pontificate in his stead \[Numbers 20:28\]. Is it found anywhere that the rulers or peoples of the other tribes designated or established high priests and the other ministers of the tabernacle or of the Lord’s temple, or presumed anything belonging to their lot or right? For the priests themselves conducted their ordinations and affairs among themselves and on their own terms as seemed good to them, and the whole Levitical tribe was subject to the governance of its own pontiffs alone, with no ruler or layman contradicting them, but rather all depending upon their command and direction both in matters of offerings and in other observances. Whoever would not obey them should be cut off from the midst of his people \[Deuteronomy 17:12\]. And this lawful right was inviolably rendered to the Levitical tribe by the others until the worst of kings, Jeroboam, who divided kingdom and priesthood in two by his schism, establishing other altars and other priests for idols — priests who were made not in the manner of Moses but in simoniacal fashion. For, as the history records, whoever wished filled his own hand and became a priest of gods who are no gods \[1 Kings 13:33\]. Judah and Benjamin, together with some part of the Levitical tribe, retained the priesthood as their portion for some time, though deviating occasionally; hence the temple and altar were sometimes profaned by the wickedness of certain priests and kings, sometimes expiated by the devotion of their successors. In the original unity of that kingdom and in its final schism were prefigured above all the concord of Catholics and the discord of heretics — the small number of Catholics in the Jews of Jerusalem, and the multitude of heretics in the Israelites of Beth-aven. Among these it is also to be noted that those who retained for themselves only a false priesthood, along with the Mosaic Law which they read in vain and the Davidic psalms which they chanted for demons rather than for God, were the first to incur irrevocable and perpetual captivity. But those who retained for themselves the divinely instituted priesthood — though falling and rising again — incurred only a captivity of seventy years, and that long after the others; from which captivity they returned as if improved and renewed, with the Law and the psalms, with their priests and prophets, with the sacred vessels themselves and with the gifts of many gentile rulers, to rebuild the temple and the holy city.
