just as there is the greatest possible difference between the sacraments of these two \[priesthoods\], so too must there be the greatest possible distance between the ministers of each — as great, that is, as the distance between a servant and the person of an emperor. For the Mosaic priesthood, devoted to carnal sacrifices and the blood of goats and bulls and other animals — by which, according to the Apostle’s words, it was impossible to take away sins — bore only the shadow of the good things to come; whereas the Christian priesthood, occupied with spiritual oblations, daily pours forth the blood of the spotless and immaculate Lamb Jesus Christ, who takes away the sins of the world, for the remission of sins, through the manifest reality of present and future goods. It is also worth noting that Korah sinned not through money, but through the nearness of his own rank, through kinship — for he and Aaron were of the same tribe, though not of the same family — and because he trusted that he too belonged to the Lord, as is clearly evident from these words of his: “It is enough for you, for the whole congregation is holy, and the Lord is among them. Why do you exalt yourselves above the assembly of the Lord?” It is no less worth noting in what dreadful and pitiable manner Dathan and Abiram descended alive into hell with all their possessions and all their household goods, even though they had not sought the priesthood for themselves — for they were not of the tribe of Levi — but had merely favored those who presumed to seize it. And yet the supporters and defenders were punished more severely than the presumptuous ones themselves. For it is a greater thing to be swallowed alive into the earth along with one’s goods and even the little children who had not sinned, than to be burned by fire alone — those who had sinned. Let laymen and clergy who do not fear to give patronage to wicked and undisciplined men take note of this, and especially those who compel unworthy men to be promoted to holy orders, whether through their own power, favor, or money. Let them also observe that the people were forbidden to touch anything belonging to Dathan and Abiram, lest they be entangled in their sins, while the furnishings of Korah were so far from perishing that even his censers, hammered into plates by God’s command, were affixed to the altar. And as the narrative relates in the following chapters, a great miracle occurred: when Korah perished, his sons did not perish with him. Let the defenders and supporters of the wicked at last come to recognize how much worse they are than the wicked themselves. For just as Dathan and Abiram, who were first in wickedness, were first swallowed by the earth, so afterward Korah with his accomplices was seized and consumed by flame. Above all, let the simoniacs take careful note — those who, after innumerable anathemas, presume to assault the Holy Spirit with money in their ambition for ecclesiastical rank, and who believe that they have overcome or can overcome him — how much more wicked they are than Korah, who, before any anathema, attempted to seize the priesthood of the Jewish synagogue, without any money offered or promised, trusting only in kinship or in holiness. Furthermore, let the supporters and obstinate defenders of simoniacs and of any undisciplined persons take note of what they ought to fear from evangelical truth, if Dathan and Abiram suffered such destruction for their part in the shadow of the Law. For the precept of the Gospel is more severe than that of the Law, since under the Law only the evil deed is punished, whereas under the Gospel even the evil thought is punished. And certainly Dathan and Abiram, as well as Korah, are believed to have been crushed with a twofold destruction — of body and of soul — as Judas the brother of James attests.

## CAPUT IIII.

### On the mob that follows presumptuous men, and on the difference between true and false priesthood.

Jesus, saving the people from the land of Egypt, destroyed those who did not believe by cutting them off.
