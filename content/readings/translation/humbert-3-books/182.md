“And it shall come to pass in that day, the glory of Jacob shall be diminished, and the fatness of his flesh shall grow lean; and it shall be as when the harvestman gathers the standing grain, and his arm reaps the ears; and it shall be as he that gathers ears in the valley of Rephaim. And there shall be left in it as the shaking of an olive tree, two or three berries on the top of the highest bough, four or five on the outermost branches thereof: says the Lord God of Israel \[Isaiah 17:4–6\].” Then the same prophet adds what further diminishment the catholic Church incurs even in that very smallness of hers: “Therefore they shall plant a planting of unbelievers and shall sow an alien seed \[cf. Isaiah 17:10–11\].” Woe, woe, woe, O Lord God, spare, spare your people, and do not give your inheritance over to destruction! Be gracious, I beseech you, be at rest! “Who shall raise up Jacob, for he is little \[Amos 7:5\]?” For we have been diminished more than all nations, and besides, those whom we plant for you become unbelievers, and we sow an alien seed, because according to the lament of Jeremiah \[cf. Lamentations 2:22\], those whom we raise up and nurture, our enemy consumes. And oh, if only those alone were consumed! But what is more grievous is that there are those who were born in the womb of the Church and nourished at her breast, who afterward pass over from her to faithless Simon with their mother’s weapons, and cruelly rise up against her and, having deceived their erstwhile brothers, destroy them at the peril of their mother under the guise of peace. Why then do they who tear apart the Church of Christ without ceasing, for whose sake alone he came in the flesh, not deny that Christ has come in the flesh? For if the blessed father Augustinus asserted that the Arians, Macedonians, Cataphrygians, and Novatians deny that Christ has come in the flesh — in that by holding perverse views about the divinity they rend his Church through their insolence — much more do the simoniacs, who not only by thinking perversely about God but also by trafficking in his Church reduce it and whatever belongs to it almost to nothing, so far as in them lies. And certainly the Arians, though they perversely call the Son and the Holy Spirit lesser than God the Father and creatures, do not yet reckon themselves able to sell either of them; nor do the Macedonians, though they wickedly call the Holy Spirit lesser than Father and Son and a creature; nor do the Cataphrygians, though they assert that the Holy Spirit came substantively into Montanus, the founder of their sect. For which reason it is clearer than the sun that the simoniacs deny more than all these that Christ has come in the flesh — they who indeed say with their mouths that the Holy Spirit is in all things co-equal to the Father and the Son, yet daily believe themselves able to sell him, in that they shamelessly sell his visible sacraments.

## CAPUT XXXIIII.

### How much more pernicious than all heretics are the simoniacs.

How much more excusably would the Arians and Macedonians carry on this trafficking, and believe the Holy Spirit could be sold for a price, since they proclaim him to be a creature? For it is more consistent that a creature can be sold than the creator and sanctifier of the world. Moreover, their fury is far more tolerable — those whom manifest terror rather than pretended love armed against the catholic Church, and whom public confession declared to be avoided — compared with those who are more intolerable than them in proportion as they are more ingratiating and covert. For we regard the simoniacs as catholics by their confession, and as fathers or friends by their smooth or peaceable admonition or greeting, which they are so zealous to use as a cover for their perversity that they are believed to be more ingratiating than the most ingratiating, while they never cease to destroy the people of God — who neither senses nor resists such evils, but rather smiles upon and applauds them — men who, as is openly shown above, are worse than the most wicked Arians, as the holy seventh synod \[the Second Council of Nicaea, 787\] defined by an irreproachable and authoritative judgment. In comparison with them even the Novatians may be thought good, who, puffed up by the sect of wondrous continence and most rigorous discipline, dogmatize that the twice-married and the penitent for crimes ought not to be received by the Church — though in all other respects they in no way contradict the catholic faith. But because by this scandal of theirs they scandalize and divide the Church, they too deny that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh — not by word, but in the same way as nearly all other heretics, by action.
