To these let the concordant judgments of his fellow apostles be appended, as it were by way of subscription. For Peter himself, discoursing at greater length about Simon and those like him who are treacherous and apostate, in his second epistle says: “There were false prophets among the people, just as there will be lying teachers among you, who will introduce sects of destruction and deny the Lord who purchased them, bringing upon themselves swift ruin. And many will follow their licentiousness, on account of whom the way of truth will be blasphemed; and in their greed they will exploit you with false words — their judgment, long decreed, is not idle, and their destruction does not slumber.” And a little later: “These, like irrational animals, naturally born for capture and destruction, blaspheming in matters they do not understand, will perish in their own corruption, receiving the wage of unrighteousness.” And a little later: “Seducing unstable souls, having hearts trained in greed, children of a curse — forsaking the straight way they have gone astray, having followed the way of Balaam the son of Bosor, who loved the wages of unrighteousness. But he received a rebuke for his own madness: a dumb beast of burden, speaking with a human voice, restrained the prophet’s folly. These are springs without water and mists driven by a storm, for whom the gloom of darkness is reserved. Speaking arrogant words of vanity, they entice in the lusts of the flesh and in licentiousness those who have barely escaped from those living in error, promising them liberty, while they themselves are slaves of corruption — for whoever is overcome by something is enslaved to it. For if, after escaping the defilements of the world through the knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ, they are again entangled and overcome, their last state has become worse than the first. For it would have been better for them never to have known the way of righteousness than, after knowing it, to turn back from the holy commandment delivered to them. What the true proverb says has happened to them: ‘The dog returns to its own vomit, and the sow, after washing, returns to wallow in the mire.’” The apostle Jude likewise, warning us about such people, says: “Certain men have crept in unawares, those who were long ago designated for this condemnation, ungodly men who pervert the grace of our God into licentiousness and deny our only Master and Lord Jesus Christ.” And a little later: “Woe to them! For they have walked in the way of Cain, and for the sake of gain they have abandoned themselves to Balaam’s error, and have perished in Korah’s rebellion. These are blemishes at your love feasts, feasting with you without fear, shepherding themselves. They are waterless clouds, carried along by winds; fruitless trees in late autumn, twice dead, uprooted; wild waves of the sea, casting up the foam of their own shame; wandering stars, for whom the gloom of utter darkness has been reserved forever. It was also about these that Enoch, the seventh from Adam, prophesied, saying: ‘Behold, the Lord comes with ten thousands of his holy ones to execute judgment on all and to convict all the ungodly of all their deeds of ungodliness that they have committed in such an ungodly way, and of all the harsh things that ungodly sinners have spoken against him.’ These are grumblers, malcontents, following their own desires; their mouths speak arrogant words, showing favoritism for the sake of gain. But you, beloved, remember the words spoken beforehand by the apostles of our Lord Jesus Christ, who told you that in the last time there will be scoffers following their own ungodly passions. It is these who cause divisions, worldly people, devoid of the Spirit. But you, beloved, building yourselves up in your most holy faith and praying in the Holy Spirit, keep yourselves in the love of the Lord, waiting for the mercy of our God Jesus Christ that leads to eternal life. And have mercy on those who doubt; save others by snatching them out of the fire; to others show mercy with fear, hating even the garment stained by the flesh.” John likewise — whether the evangelist, as is believed, or the elder of the church of Ephesus — writing to a certain holy woman says: “This is the commandment of God: that you walk in it, as you have heard from the beginning. For many deceivers have gone out into the world, those who do not confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh. Such a one is the deceiver and the Antichrist. Watch yourselves, so that you may not lose what we have worked for, but may win a full reward. Everyone who goes on ahead and does not abide in the teaching of Christ does not have God. Whoever abides in the teaching has both the Son and the Father. If anyone comes to you and does not bring this teaching, do not receive him into your house or give him any greeting, for whoever greets him takes part in his wicked works.”
