Or thirdly, as Gerson expounded in *De vita animae*, lect. i: while it pleases, it is a sin insofar as it is offensive to God — apart from any other motive or inducement to sin. For that indeed bespeaks a diabolical obstinacy in the one who fights out of hatred for the one he has offended. Hence briefly: one who perseveres in a venial sin as venial, even deliberately, commits only a venial sin. And although one who sins venially from deliberation abuses the mercy of God — having assumed that sin to be venial and not mortal — nonetheless he does not sin mortally. And this reflects greatly on the mercy of God, that such abuse, even when deliberate, is only venial. And so much for that question and the whole distinction.
