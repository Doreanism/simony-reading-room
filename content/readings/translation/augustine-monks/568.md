For it is one thing to plead a genuine bodily infirmity or to feign a false one; but quite another to be thus deceived and to thus deceive, so that in addition to this it appears that greater righteousness exists among the servants of God because sloth has been able to reign among the ignorant. For one who genuinely shows bodily infirmity is to be treated with human compassion; but one who feigns a false infirmity and cannot be convicted must be left to God. Yet neither of them establishes a pernicious rule, because a good servant of God both serves his manifestly infirm brother and believes the one who is deceiving him — because he does not think him wicked, he does not imitate him so as to become wicked himself — and even if he believes him, does not brand him a deceiver; and nevertheless does not imitate him. But from the one who says: “This is the true righteousness — to imitate the birds of the heaven by performing no bodily labor, since whoever has done anything of the kind acts against the gospel” — whoever hears and believes these things with a weak soul is to be mourned: not because he thus has leisure from work, but because he thus errs.
