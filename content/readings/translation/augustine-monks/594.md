But if, after this admonition — or rather, supplication — of ours, they shall think they must persevere in this practice, we shall do nothing other than grieve and groan. Let them know this; it is enough: if they are servants of God, let them have compassion; if they have no compassion, I do not wish to say anything harsher. All these matters, then, in which I have perhaps been more long-winded than either your occupations or mine would wish — if you approve, make them known to our brothers and sons, for whose sake you have deigned to lay this burden upon me; but if anything seems to need withdrawing or correcting, I shall learn of it from the reply of your blessedness.
