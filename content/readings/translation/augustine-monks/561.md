“as it is written: he who had much did not have too much, and he who had little did not lack.” \[2 Corinthians 8:15\] Thanks be to God, who put the same zeal for you in the heart of Titus; for he accepted the encouragement, yet being more zealous than that, he went out to you of his own accord. And we have sent with him the brother whose praise in the gospel is spread through all the churches — and not only him, but he was also appointed by the churches as our traveling companion in this grace which is administered by us to the glory of the Lord and to show our readiness — avoiding this, lest anyone find fault with us in regard to this bounty which is administered by us; for we take care to do what is right not only before the Lord, but also before men. In these words it appears how greatly \[the Apostle\] wished not only that the care of holy congregations should be to minister necessities to the holy servants of God — giving counsel in this regard, because this was more profitable to those who were doing it than to those for whom they were doing it (for something else was profitable to them, namely to make holy use of this service of their brothers toward themselves, and not to serve God on that account, nor to accept these things except to supply necessity, not to feed idleness) — but also that the blessed apostle speaks of his own care so greatly in this administration, which was then being transmitted through Titus, that he mentions him appointed by the churches as his traveling companion for this purpose, a man of God and of good repute. “Whose praise,” he says, “is in the gospel through all the churches.” And he says that he \[Titus\] was appointed as his companion for this purpose — that he might avoid the reproaches of men, lest, without the witness of holy companions in this ministry, he should be thought to be taking \[the funds\] as for himself
