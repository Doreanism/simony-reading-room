Hence also the apostle, when he charged Timothy to admonish the rich, said: “Let them give readily, let them share, let them lay up for themselves a good foundation for the future, that they may lay hold on true life.” When, therefore, the Lord has directed the eye of those who give alms toward the life to come and toward heavenly reward, so that even those deeds may be full of light when the eye is simple — for that is what avails for that final recompense, as what he also says elsewhere confirms: “He who receives you receives me; and he who receives me receives him who sent me. He who receives a prophet in the name of a prophet shall receive a prophet’s reward; and he who receives a just man in the name of a just man shall receive a just man’s reward. And he who gives a cup of cold water only to one of these least ones in the name of a disciple, amen I say to you, his reward shall not be lost” — lest perchance, when the eye of those who supply necessities to the poor and to prophets and to just men and to the disciples of the Lord was being corrected, the eye of those on whom such things were bestowed should become corrupted, so that they would wish to serve Christ in order to receive those things. “No one,” he says, “can serve two masters.” And a little later he says: “You cannot serve God
