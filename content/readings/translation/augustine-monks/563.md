“He who supplies seed to the sower will also supply and multiply your seed for sowing, and increase the growing harvest of your righteousness — so that being enriched in every way for all generosity, which through us produces thanksgiving to God. For the rendering of this service not only supplies what is lacking for the saints, but also causes it to overflow through many thanksgivings to God. Through the proof of this ministry, they glorify God for the obedience of your confession toward the gospel of Christ and the generosity of your sharing with them and with all; while they also long for you with prayer on your behalf, because of the surpassing grace of God in you. Thanks be to God for his inexpressible gift!” With what great richness of holy joy was the apostle suffused while speaking of the mutual supply of the want of Christ’s peoples — on the one side, things of the flesh flowing toward those \[in Jerusalem\], and on the other side, things of the spirit flowing toward these \[in Corinth\] — so that he broke forth and, as though belching forth the fullness of holy joys, exclaimed: “Thanks be to God for his inexpressible gift!”
