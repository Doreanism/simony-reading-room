# On the Work of Monks

## I. 1.

I was bound to comply with your command, holy brother Aurelius, with all the more devotion the more clearly it became evident to me who it was that had commanded through you. For our Lord Jesus Christ, dwelling in your inner self and inspiring in you a solicitude of fatherly and brotherly charity, took up your will and your tongue as instruments of his own work, and through you commanded me to write something to you on this matter — namely, whether that license ought to be permitted to our sons and brothers the monks, who have neglected to obey the blessed apostle Paul when he says: *he who will not work, let him not eat*. And so he himself is present with me as well, that I may so obey as to understand, through the fruitfulness of profitable labor itself, that my very obedience is a gift from him.
