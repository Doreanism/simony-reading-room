Or has anyone followed the Lord with so swift a course as he who said: “I did not run in vain or labor in vain”? Yet he both commanded these works and performed them. So great an authority should have sufficed for us, both learned and formed \[in the faith\], as an example both of relinquishing our former possessions and of working with our hands. But aided by the Lord himself, we may perhaps in some measure understand what it profits even servants of God who work in this way to have nonetheless left behind their former pursuits. For if someone is converted to this life from wealth and is not impeded by any bodily infirmity, are we so foolish as to lack the taste of Christ — so as not to understand how great a swelling of former pride is healed, when, with the superfluities by which the mind was once ruinously inflated being cut away, even the humility of a craftsman is not in the least refused for the modest necessities that remain and are naturally required for this life?
