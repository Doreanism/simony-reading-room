For if he sought food and clothing through spiritual labor — that is, by receiving these things from those whom he was building up through the gospel — he would not logically go on to say: “If others share in the right over you, do we not \[have it\] even more? But we have endured all things, lest we give any hindrance to the gospel of Christ.” What is the right he says he did not use, if not the right he held over them, received from the Lord, to reap their material goods for the sustenance of this life lived in the flesh? Others also shared in this same right — those who had not first proclaimed the gospel to them, but came later to preach the same gospel to their church. And so, after having said, “If we have sown spiritual things among you, is it a great matter if we reap your material things?” he added: “If others share in the right over you, do we not \[have it\] even more?” And after demonstrating what right belonged to them, he says: “But we have not used this right; rather, we have endured all things, lest we give any hindrance to the gospel of Christ.” Let these men therefore say how the apostle obtained material sustenance from spiritual labor, when he himself plainly declares that he did not use this right.
