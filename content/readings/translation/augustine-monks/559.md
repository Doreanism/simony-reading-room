This is the basis of what he says in the letter to the Romans: “Now,” he says, “I am going to Jerusalem to minister to the saints. For it has pleased Macedonia and Achaia to make some contribution for the poor among the saints at Jerusalem. It has pleased them, and they are indebted to them. For if the Gentiles have shared in their spiritual blessings, they ought also to be of service to them in material things.” This is like what he says to the Corinthians: “If we have sown spiritual things among you, is it too much if we reap material things from you?” And likewise in the Second \[letter\] to the Corinthians: “We want you to know, brothers, about the grace of God that has been given among the churches of Macedonia, for in a great ordeal of affliction, their abundance of joy and their extreme poverty have overflowed in the wealth of their generosity; for according to their means, I testify, and beyond their means, they gave of their own free will, begging us earnestly with many entreaties for the grace and fellowship of this ministry to the saints —
