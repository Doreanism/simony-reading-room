by what delusion these men deceive the unlearned, by what cunning and devices of the enemy they are themselves tossed about, and in their own spinning they try in a manner to make the minds of the weak spin with them, so that together they know not where they are. For they have heard or read what is written: “As many of you as were baptized into Christ have put on Christ, where there is neither Jew nor Greek, neither slave nor free, neither male nor female” — and they do not understand that this was said with regard to carnal sexual desire, because in the inner man, where we are renewed in the newness of our mind, there is no sex of this kind. Let them not therefore deny that they are men, on the ground that they make no use of the male sex. For even married Christians who do make use of it are not thereby Christians, since in this they have something in common with other non-Christians and even with the beasts themselves. For it is one thing that is conceded to weakness or rendered for mortal propagation; quite another is what is pledged by faithful profession to the attaining of incorrupt and eternal life. That which is enjoined upon men concerning not covering the head is figured indeed in the body, but is enacted in the mind, where the image and glory of God resides.
