For why did he say: *or is it only I and Barnabas who have not the power of not working?* — if not because all evangelists and ministers of the word of God had received power from the Lord not to work with their hands, but to live from the gospel, laboring only in spiritual works in the preaching of the kingdom of heaven and the building up of the peace of the church? For no one can say that the Apostle meant by “spiritual labor” the phrase: *or is it only I and Barnabas who have not the power of not working?* For all those men had this power of not working \[in the bodily sense\]. Let him therefore speak up — whoever attempts to corrupt and pervert the apostolic precepts to his own opinion — let him speak up, if he dares, \[and say\] that all the evangelists received from the Lord the power of not evangelizing. But if it is the most absurd and most senseless thing to say this, why are they unwilling to understand what is plain to all: namely, that those men received the power of not working — but of not working with the body, by which they would seek their livelihood — because *the laborer is worthy of his food and his hire*, as the gospel says? Therefore it was not Paul and Barnabas alone who had no power of not working; rather, all alike had this power, which those two were not using, giving more lavishly to the church, as they judged fitting for the benefit of the weak in the places where they were evangelizing. And therefore, lest he should appear to have reproached his fellow apostles, he adds and says: *who ever serves as a soldier at his own expense?*
