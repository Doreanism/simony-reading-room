Thus, if the apostle had said only this on the matter: *he who will not work, let him not eat*, these words could be twisted to another meaning; but since in many other places in his epistles he teaches most openly what he thinks about it, they labor in vain to cast a fog before themselves and others, so that what charity profitably urges they not only refuse to do, but refuse either to understand themselves or to allow others to understand — not fearing what is written: *he would not understand, that he might do good*.
