## X. 11.

He continues and adds, lest anyone suppose that he had not received \[support\] because they had not given it: “But I have not written these things that it should be so done for me; for it is better for me to die than that anyone should make my glory void.” \[1 Corinthians 9:15\] What glory? Surely the glory he wished to have before God, suffering with the weak in Christ, as he is about to state most plainly in a moment. “For if I preach the gospel,” he says, “it is no glory to me; for necessity is laid upon me” — that is, the necessity of sustaining this present life. “For woe to me if I do not preach the gospel” \[1 Corinthians 9:16\] — that is, it will be to my harm if I do not preach the gospel, for I shall be tormented by hunger and shall have nothing to live on. For he continues and says: “For if I do this willingly, I have a reward.” \[1 Corinthians 9:17\] He says he acts willingly — without being compelled by any necessity of sustaining this present life he acts \[thus\], and for this reason has a reward, namely the reward of eternal glory before God. “But if against my will, a stewardship is entrusted to me” \[1 Corinthians 9:17\] — that is, if compelled against my will by the necessity of maintaining this present life I preach the gospel, a stewardship is entrusted to me, so that through my stewardship — because I preach Christ, because I preach the truth, though doing so on occasion, though seeking my own \[advantage\], though driven by the necessity of earthly gain — others may profit, while I have no glorious and eternal reward before God.
