For the innkeeper had heard, to whom that wounded man was brought: *If you spend anything more, I will repay you when I return.* More, therefore, was spent by the apostle Paul, who, as he himself testifies, served at his own expense. For it is written in the Gospel: *And he himself continued journeying through city and village, preaching and announcing the kingdom of God, and the twelve with him, and certain women who had been healed of evil spirits and infirmities: Mary called Magdalene, from whom seven demons had gone out, and Joanna the wife of Chuza, Herod’s steward, and Susanna, and many others, who ministered to them out of their means.* The apostles imitated this example of the Lord in receiving the food due them — concerning which the same Lord speaks most plainly. *Go,* he says, *preach, saying that the kingdom has drawn near —*
