And yet, if we compel them to live according to the very words of the Gospel as they understand them, they will try to persuade us first that the words are not to be understood as they understand them. For they say they ought not to work, because the birds of the air neither sow nor reap — concerning which the Lord gave us a comparison, that we should not be anxious about such necessities. But why, then, do they not attend to what follows? For it was not said only that they neither sow nor reap, but it was added: neither do they gather into storehouses. Now storehouses, or granaries, can be called word-for-word “repositories.” Why, then, do these men want idle hands and full storehouses? Why do they lay up in storage what they take and keep, drawn from the labors of others, from which it is brought forth daily? Why, finally, do they grind and cook? For birds do not do this. Or if they find people whom they can persuade even of this, so that prepared meals are brought to them each day, they at least fetch water for themselves from springs, or draw it from cisterns or wells and store it: winged creatures do not do this. But if this seems good, then let good and faithful subjects, most devoted to the eternal King, be eager to serve his most valiant soldiers even to this extent — so that they are not even required to fill their own water vessels — if indeed they have by now surpassed even those who were then in Jerusalem in some new degree of righteousness.
