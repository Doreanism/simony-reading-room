Certain persons, not understanding this, interpreted the word not as “a sister who is a woman” when he said, *Have we not the right to take about with us a sister who is a woman?* — but as “a wife.” They were misled by an ambiguity in the Greek word, since in Greek “wife” and “woman” are expressed by the same term. And yet the apostle had so stated the matter that they ought not to have been misled, for he said not “a woman” alone but “a sister who is a woman,” and not “to lead” but “to take about.” Nevertheless other translators were not misled by this ambiguity, and rendered it “a woman,” not “a wife.”

## V. 6.

Whoever thinks it was impossible for the apostles to have had women of holy life travel with them wherever they preached the Gospel, in order to minister to them out of their own means the things needful for them, let them hear the Gospel and learn how these women did this very thing following the Lord’s own example. For our Lord, according to his customary mercy, having compassion on the weak, though angels could have ministered to him, also had a money-bag, into which was put the money contributed by his good and faithful followers for their daily needs — a bag he entrusted to Judas, so that we might learn to endure thieves in the church, even if we cannot avoid them.
