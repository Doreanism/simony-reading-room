Let us therefore see from what occasion the apostle came to say: *if any man will not work, neither let him eat*, and what he weaves together next, so that from the very context of the passage his meaning may appear made clear. *We charge you*, he says, *brethren, in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, that you withdraw yourselves from every brother who walks disorderly and not according to the tradition which they received from us. For you yourselves know how you ought to imitate us, because we were not disorderly among you, nor did we eat bread from any man for nothing, but in labor and toil we worked day and night, that we might not burden any of you: not because we have not power, but that we might make ourselves a pattern to you, that you should imitate us. For even when we were among you, we charged you this: that if any man will not work, neither let him eat. For we have heard that there are some who walk disorderly among you, doing no work at all, but being busybodies. Now those who are such, we charge and exhort in the Lord Jesus Christ, that working in silence they eat their own bread.* What can be said to this, since, lest it be permitted to interpret this according to one’s own will rather than according to charity, he taught by his own example
