## XXVIII. 36.

Since this is so, permit me for a little while, holy brother — for the Lord grants me great confidence through you — to address those very sons and brothers of ours, whom I know with what great love you labor over, until the apostolic discipline be formed in them. O servants of God, soldiers of Christ, are you really so heedless of the snares of that most cunning enemy, who by every means desires to obscure your good reputation — that good fragrance of Christ — with his stench, lest good souls say: “We will run after the fragrance of your ointments,” and so escape his traps? He has scattered throughout every region so many hypocrites under the habit of monks, wandering through the provinces, sent nowhere, settled nowhere, standing nowhere, sitting nowhere. Some peddle the relics of martyrs — if indeed they are martyrs’ relics. Others make much of their fringes and phylacteries. Others falsely claim they have heard that their parents or kinsmen are living in this or that region, and that they are making their way to them.
