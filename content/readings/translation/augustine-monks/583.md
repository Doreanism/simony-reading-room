And what follows concerning the birds of the sky and the lilies of the field, he says for this purpose: lest anyone think that God does not care for the necessities of his servants, since his most wise providence extends even to creating and governing these things. For he himself feeds and clothes even those who work with their hands. But lest they bend their Christian service toward the pursuit of these things, the Lord warns his servants that in that ministry which is owed to his sacrament, we should think not of these things, but of his kingdom and his righteousness: “and all these things will be added to you” — whether to those working with their hands, or to those hindered by bodily infirmity, or to those constrained by such an obligation of this same service that we are able to do nothing else.

## XXVII.

For just because God said, “Call upon me in the day of tribulation, and I will deliver you, and you shall glorify me,” it does not follow that the apostle was therefore obliged not to flee and be lowered through the wall in a basket to escape the hands of his persecutor, but rather to wait to be seized, expecting God to deliver him from the midst of the flames as he did the three young men.
