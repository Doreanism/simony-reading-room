“Did I commit a sin in humbling myself, that you might be exalted, because I preached the gospel of God to you free of charge? I robbed other churches, accepting payment for ministry to you; and when I was present with you and in need, I was not a burden to anyone. For the brothers who came from Macedonia supplied what I lacked, and in every way I have kept myself, and will keep myself, from being a burden to you. The truth of Christ is in me — this boast shall not be broken in me in the regions of Achaia. Why? Because I do not love you? God knows. But what I do and will continue to do, I do in order to cut off the opportunity of those who desire an opportunity, so that in what they boast of, they may be found just as we are.” It is this opportunity, then — the one he says here that he wishes to cut off — that he wished to be understood by what he said there: “nor under a pretext of greed, God is my witness.” And what he said here — “in humbling myself, that you might be exalted” — corresponds to what he says in the First \[letter\] to those same Corinthians: “I became weak to the weak,” and to the Thessalonians: “I became a little child in your midst, as a nurse cherishes her own children.” Therefore pay attention to what follows.
