## VII. 8.

But let us return to the sequence of the text and consider carefully the whole passage of that letter. *Have we not*, he says, *the liberty to eat and to drink? Have we not the liberty to lead about a believing wife?* What liberty did he mean, if not the one that the Lord gave to those whom he sent to preach the kingdom of heaven, saying, *eat such things as are set before you; for the laborer is worthy of his hire* — and himself setting the same example, the most faithful women ministered to them from their own resources, supplying such necessities? Moreover, the Apostle Paul went further, so as to adduce his fellow apostles as evidence of this liberty permitted by the Lord.
