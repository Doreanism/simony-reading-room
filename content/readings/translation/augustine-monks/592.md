“The apostle,” they say, “forbade a man to have long hair; but those who have castrated themselves for the sake of the kingdom of heaven are no longer men.” O singular madness! Rightly does he who says this arm himself with the counsel of wicked impiety against the plain testimony of sacred scripture, and persist in a crooked path, and strive to introduce a pestilential doctrine. For he is not the blessed man who has not gone in the counsel of the ungodly, nor stood in the way of sinners, nor sat in the seat of pestilence. For if he were to meditate on the law of God day and night, he would find there the apostle Paul himself, who, having certainly professed the highest chastity, says: “I would that all men were even as I myself,” and yet shows himself to be a man not only by so living but also by so speaking. For he says: “When I was a child, I spoke as a child, I understood as a child, I thought as a child; but when I became a man, I put away childish things.” But why should I mention the apostle, when those who say these things do not know what to think about our very Lord and Savior himself?
