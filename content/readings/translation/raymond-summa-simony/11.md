Extra de simonia, c. *Nobis*.

Having received our mandate to dispense — in accordance with the constitution of the general Council — with monks who, by giving something through simony, obtained a place in monasteries (and further on): in brief answer to your consultation, we say that the Apostolic mandate extends also to abbots, and to resignations of spiritual and temporal things which are acquired on both sides not by any agreement but by a preceding disposition of the heart (in which case it suffices for those who have erred to make satisfaction to their Creator through penance alone) — that they are not held liable for simony of this kind.

*Diversis fallaciis* (and further on): We command that you deprive, appeal removed, the clerics in question of the ecclesiastical benefices they have obtained in your diocese, especially since the substance of ecclesiastical things is wont to be dissipated by such persons — unless perhaps you knowingly conferred ecclesiastical benefices on such persons, in which case we decree they are to be despoiled not by you but by your superior.

But if \[the simony was committed\] with the knowledge of the benefice-holder, the Pope alone can dispense. Extra de simonia, c. *De regularibus*.

Regarding regular canons or monks who, with their own knowledge, obtained entry through simony, you wished to consult us. Since many authorities are found explicitly on this matter, we respond with nothing other than what must be ordained: that they wholly relinquish the place they so obtained, and betake themselves to solitudes or other stricter monasteries, in which they may unceasingly lament so execrable an excess. If, however, the money was given without their knowledge, you shall compel them to renounce that same place, and afterwards you may restore them to it, if they can remain there without scandal, or you may place them in another house of the same order to serve God.

Likewise the bishop can dispense with one who committed simony only by intention or hidden will, because I do not find that it is prohibited to him. Argument from Extra de sententia excommunicationis, c. *Nuper*.

He can also dispense with one who simoniacally conferred an order or benefice, or who was a mediator of simony — provided it is occult — and with one who interposed carnal intercessions, even on behalf of an unworthy person, with no other condition or agreement involved. Likewise with one who simoniacally received an order. In \[cases of\] properly received orders, and with one who simoniacally received a benefice after having resigned the exercise of orders, I believe — saving better judgment — that after final penance his own bishop, if that bishop is free from that sin, or otherwise the metropolitan, may dispense with due discretion in a penitential proceeding, through the legal authorities just cited above.

One who knows or believes that the conferrer granted him the order or benefice chiefly on account of services or carnal intercessions made by himself or another — I believe that if he was unworthy, he must resign, because in that case such intercessions take the place of a price; otherwise, if he was worthy, he is not bound to resign but must do penance for the corrupted intention. 1 q. 1, c. *Sunt nonnulli*; Extra de qualitate et aetate praeficiendorum, c. *Tuam*; Extra de simonia, c. *Mandato*.

In the second question, however, we believe a distinction must be drawn; but attend carefully.

He who communicates with an excommunicate communicates \[in his crime\] if he knowingly communicates in the crime of the criminal by offering him counsel, assistance, or favor, or otherwise in prayer or a kiss, or by praying with him, or even by eating with him. In the first instance, since such a person communicates in the crime and participates with the criminal, and thereby seems, by reason of the condemned crime, to offend against the one who condemned it, absolution must be sought — once they have been duly reported — from him or his superior, \[namely\] when the one who acts and the one who consents \[are both involved\].
