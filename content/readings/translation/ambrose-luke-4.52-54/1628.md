It is not enough, however, that you yourself seek no profit: the hands of your household must also be restrained. Nor do we require this alone, that you keep yourself alone chaste and unstained. For the Apostle did not say, “Keep *yourself* chaste,” but rather, *Keep yourself chaste* (*1 Timothy* 5:22). Therefore the chastity not only of your own person but also of your household is required as proof against this kind of trafficking: *For a bishop must be blameless... ruling well his own household, having his children in subjection with all chastity. But if a man know not how to rule his own house, how shall he take care of the church of God?* (*1 Timothy* 3:2–5). Instruct your household, then; exhort them, watch over them; and if a servant deceives you (for I do not exclude the possibility of human \[failing\]), let him be cast off in accordance with the prophetic example. Quickly (*Decretum* C. 1, q. 1, c. *Cito*) does creeping leprosy follow the wage, and the gain ill-gotten defiles both the money piled up and the soul. *Take, he says, the money, and you shall possess fields, vineyards, olive groves, and flocks; but the leprosy of Naaman shall cleave to you and to your seed forever* (*4 Kings* 5:26–27). You see how the succession of the heir is condemned by the act of its author. For the guilt of selling the holy mystery is inexpiable, and the heavenly vengeance against the abuse of grace passes down upon posterity. Hence the Moabites and the rest are not to enter into the Church of God until the third and fourth generation (*Deuteronomy* 23:3) — that is to say (to take the simpler interpretation), until the manifold succession of generations should wipe away the guilt of those who first incurred it.

Yet since those who failed against God in the error of idolatry are seen to be punished only as far as the fourth generation, the sentence by which the seed of Gehazi is condemned forever for greed of possession by prophetic authority seems harsher by far — especially since our Lord Jesus Christ, by the regeneration of the laver, has granted the remission of all sins; unless you understand “seed” as the seed of vices rather than of bloodline. For just as those who are sons of the promise are reckoned as good seed, so also those who are sons of error are reckoned as evil seed. So too the Jews are children of their father the devil, not of course by carnal succession, but by that of crime. All the greedy, then, all the avaricious bear the leprosy of Gehazi together with their riches; and by their ill-gotten gain they have heaped up not so much an inheritance of wealth as a treasury of crimes — for everlasting punishment, and for fleeting enjoyment. For though riches are perishable, the penalty is everlasting; since *neither the covetous, nor drunkards, nor idolaters shall possess the kingdom of God*.
