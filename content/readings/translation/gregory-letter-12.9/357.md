to urge upon all, that your fraternity should take care to be so diligent that, before the wickedness of the simoniac heresy can take hold there through the vice of any one person, it be severed at the root from that assembly by the cutting blade of your sentence. For whoever does not burn with vehement zeal in the consideration of his office to amend this crime should have no doubt that he will share a portion with the one from whom this transgression requiring expiation first took its beginning. And as we have said, it must be handled with vigilance and diligence, so that your council ensures that what has been preserved until now by God’s protection, without any such corrupt practice, can neither be polluted nor destroyed by this poisonous wickedness. Furthermore, we have directed Hilarus, our notary \[chartularius\], not to delay in joining you, should the matter require it. Therefore, if it shall be necessary, inform him by your letters that he ought to come to you, so that together with him you may be able, with God’s help, to resolve all these matters in a wholesome manner.
