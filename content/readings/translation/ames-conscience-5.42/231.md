11. A. 3. Contracts of buying and selling, and those of the same nature, have no place with respect to certain things — not because such contracts are unlawful or bad in themselves, but because those things are so good that they cannot be valued at a price.

12. A. 4. Hence it is a sin of simony to buy or sell, or in any way to exchange, a holy and spiritual thing for a temporal one. The reason is:

First, because holy things ought to be used as holy, not polluted and profaned — which is what happens when they are brought down, like worldly things, into temporal contracts, contrary to the religion and reverence due to holy things.

Secondly, because it is injustice to sell those things that are to be bestowed freely. Matthew 10:8.

13. A. 5. This sin extends not only to supernatural gifts (which was nevertheless the sin of Simon Magus, from whom it takes its name), but also: first, to the causes of such gifts, such as the sacraments and the like; and secondly, to things annexed to such gifts as such. These are all things so conjoined to the spiritual function and office — whether they precede it, accompany it, or follow after it — that they can in no way be separated from it.
