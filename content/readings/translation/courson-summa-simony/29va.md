## XIII

Likewise, the question is raised: whether anyone can by law be excommunicated after death — which is argued from “Causa XXIV, question \[illegible\], chapter Sane,” following the passage in which \[authorities\] say that heretics are to be excommunicated after death, in whose books the teaching of heresy is found. But against this, the ecclesiastical rule is that as we communicate with the living, we ought to communicate with the dead. Likewise Pope Leo says of one who is placed on trial: “It is unlawful for you to pass judgment on him, for him who \[the last day finds in the condition in which\] the last day found him.” The last day found him without the sacrament of the Church in an unforeseen \[moment\], and after death he is not to be separated from the communion of the dead. Which is argued differently: it is said in “Causa \[illegible\], question VIII, chapter I” that in any case there must be present: a judge, a prosecutor, a witness. This \[requirement\] is not observed where a heretic is excommunicated after death
