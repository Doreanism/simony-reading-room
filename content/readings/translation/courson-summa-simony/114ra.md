## IIII.

And in that chapter *reprensibilis*. The effect of an appeal, once interposed, is that nothing may be altered — the matter remains wholly intact, as the same question provides in that law *monitionem*. And there is a general rule that where someone has appealed jointly with the possessor of an immovable thing, and there is suspicion that the fruits will be dissipated, as is provided in the *Digest* on appeals. ## V.

And of commissions. The same judge ought to reject the appeal interposed, and within thirty days from the time of the appeal or dismissal he must give letters — called apostles \[i.e., apostoli, the formal letters transmitting the appeal\] — containing the summary of the case and the allegations of the parties. Read the law *post appellationem*. And that judge to whom the appeal is brought must consider the prior allegations and may receive new evidence on the same matter, and in accordance with this \[the case proceeds\].

## VI.

And if, however, judgment has been rendered contrary to law, contrary to the seventh instance, or contrary to a res iudicata, then it is necessary to lodge an appeal, as the seventh chapter of the same question, *sententiam*, shows, so that it may be demonstrated that the final judgment is unjust — once the contention and the absolving and the aforesaid proceedings are valid and observed, following due judicial order, claimed by a judge of lawful authority, unless he should fail; if several \[judges pass\] sentence and unless one alone wills and holds it, with such matters threatening imminently, appeal has its place here.

## VII.

Likewise in the same place it is said that a sworn judge will not receive an appeal, nor will an official receive an appeal from an interlocutory sentence of a judge, unless there is a declaration \[of grievance\], since this is indeed the rationale with respect to the first mode of appeal \[before\] a judge who might otherwise be unaware. Appeal is prohibited by reason of the person, along with reasons of the cause, and reasons of the fullness \[of jurisdiction\] that is invoked — which it is permissible to appeal even from the lord pope, as the third canon has it at x.q.iii, the chapter *cunia*. \[Appeal is prohibited\] by reason \[of status\] in multiple ways: on account of age, to protect notorious great criminals.
