Concerning the abuser there is no question but that he must be removed as a punishment \[short\] of death \[if one considers\] what the Old Testament judges: that such a man should be stoned. The Christian law, however, as instituted, \[decrees that he be\] punished with capital punishment, and the Apostle \[says he is\] given over to a reprobate mind; and the same \[law holds concerning\] all those things that make a man’s body effeminate, as the general canon \[states\] \[illegible\] barring a woman from promotion; truly by the Apostle’s instruction — the father has been made effeminate \[and\] \[illegible\] with all ignominy he is to be expelled, being barred solely from promotion within the fold. Even in the other direction the same saying \[holds\], to understand what pertains to the cleanness of life and even \[to the cleanness of\] a name. Augustinus observed that they become one \[body\] of Christ, and that there is made his one bride the Church \[illegible\]; Iohannes says that this bride \[is given\] to the bridegroom made \[flesh\] through the incarnation, that is, every \[member\] of the bride, that is, of his Church, ought to be a counselor \[to it\]: as is said in the Song of Songs: “My dove, my chosen, one,” and \[he\] does not prepare \[another\]. In the Old Testament \[the law was\] that one who delayed should not take a virgin as wife, and \[if his wife\] was already dead, he married another wife. This remains in the canon: the bigamist, who has indeed married \[more than\] one, that is, two wives, or the man found cohabiting with a widow either before or after baptism, may not be promoted, because that man is judged on the ground that he has lost the seal, and therefore the question concerns the one who killed \[another\] whether before or after baptism, because that man, by fulfilling the concept of \[illegible\] our Lord — if he confesses some seal \[illegible\] of the Church, and it has not been found against that commandment, that is, by killing, or by removal of a wife, he acquires some seal \[illegible\]. If no \[such barrier\] remains, the irregularity persists after baptism, and who \[illegible\] will be ordained.
