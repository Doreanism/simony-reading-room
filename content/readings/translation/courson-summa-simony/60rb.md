If anyone understands this to mean that monks, who are quasi-Levites, should pay tithes to other \[monks\] or to priests — if this indeed means that the remaining \[revenues\] of such churches are to be kept by those zealously paying tithes — it often happens that monasteries acquire the fields of interior churches and cultivate them with their own hands and at their own expense, having arranged to direct tithes while driving away the sowers, destroying baptismal churches and altars on the occasion of the aforesaid privileges, and in this \[they sin\] mortally. We concede, saying that if they cultivate new clearings, they are \[indeed\] bound to pay tithes from them, since those lands were never the fields of any baptismal church. But if, either through purchase or in any other way, they acquire fields attached to some church and deny tithes from those fields, they sin mortally, for they grow rich through another’s loss. Nor can \[one be sure that\] if they should obtain some privilege from the lord pope — the legitimate cause being known, \[and\] charity supplying \[the rest\] — since privileges of today \[breed\] dissensions that bind their \[own\]

factions in the church. If however one asks how priests today hold tithes, we reply by saying that all tithes by right pertain to the bishop’s disposal and have been constituted as such in each and every year. If the objection is raised concerning the fourfold division of them in the law xii, q. ii, the relevant chapter, we say that the division holds, but the first obligation of the church \[is\] that one part should be \[given\] to the bishop, another to the poor and the sustaining \[of them\], a quarter to building works, and a quarter spent on the goods of the church — and likewise the superiors divided \[theirs\] — nor is it otherwise as was \[said\] at c. q. in the same \[c.\] “Nos.” If in an enlarged and dedicated and adorned church that division has its place. Likewise that everyone is bound to pay tithes is proven by Abraham, who on meeting Melchisedech gave tithes of all things, as the Apostle recalls in the Epistle to the Hebrews, saying: “Melchisedech, king of Salem, priest of the most high God, offered.”
