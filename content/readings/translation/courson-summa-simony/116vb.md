### Post hec de rescriptis aliqua breviter de rescriptis tangamus.

Rescripts are of three kinds \[issued\] by the supreme pontiff or by the prince-emperor: for deciding cases, or for executing whatever mandates of theirs \[are required\]: some are \[illegible\] — that is, letters following the pattern of rescripts, differing from a rescript in this respect: rescripts bear the apostolic seal or signet, whereas those \[letters\] do not. First, a question arises here: if the lord pope has \[reserved a matter\] or delegated \[causes\] to certain persons by name and has added the names of their dignities, whether those rescripts are valid. It seems that yes: for if they receive \[matters\] for decision or commit \[them\] in letters of commission, those letters are of no effect for deciding the matter — and by the same reasoning a rescript \[drawn up\] \[illegible\] under those names is of no effect. Furthermore, an error in a dignity or a simple \[error\] impedes a rescript, as is provided in the *extra* \[decretals\] on rescripts *ad autres* \[i.e., *ad auditores*\], for the same reason: an error in a name impedes. Universally \[however\] what is clear: that an error does not impede a rescript, as the chapter *lsbre* at xv.q.v. provides — and this universally is \[valid\] so long as the identity of the person is established.
