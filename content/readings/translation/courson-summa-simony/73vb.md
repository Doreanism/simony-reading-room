To the fourth \[argument\], we say that the holder of different lordships, flocks, or tithes is not thereby attributed to different churches as a member of them, and does not have the cure of souls or a voice in the chapter or a stall in the choir; he pays tithes and has earned \[income\] from different churches, and belongs to different chapters that participate in electing the bishop, and the rest who are not \[his\] superiors would not deal with him in a restrained way. The analogous case we resolve because various rights of patronage with the receipt of certain revenues, if the patron is in need, may be held by the same patron — just as the same bishop may retain dignities in several churches constructed under the same see in order to confer prebends in those churches — and therefore it does not follow that someone ought to be a prebendary in different churches, because if the patron \[holds\] prebends in one of his churches, what suffices him is owed to him and he ought not to be made a prebendary in another. To the sixth argument, we likewise say that while it is lawful for a monastery or abbot by reason of one transition to retain several titled churches under that monastery, this is not lawful for that \[abbot\] or any other \[person\] in his own name. To the seventh objection, we say that while it may happen in fact that a monk obtains several portions or abbacies, this is nevertheless not lawful by right. Whence I say in response to the eighth objection: while it is lawful for the lord Pope, with knowledge of the cause, to grant the revenues of another prebend — as a portion or tithe — to someone who holds one titled prebend, for example if such a person is in great need, or if he has been pledged to a pilgrimage to Jerusalem, or if he has been captured by enemies, or for another just cause with knowledge of that cause — he may give him the revenues of various benefices. But it is not thereby lawful, contrary to the ancient canons and their sanction, for him to confer on anyone without knowledge of the cause, and it is not lawful for such a person to hold various prebends or churches or dignities; and the objection of certain persons on this point is of no weight.
