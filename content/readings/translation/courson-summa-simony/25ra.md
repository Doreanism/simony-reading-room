At the same time, the dispensation of grain without rational cause fills the Church with confusion, if we were to hold that it is required. By the same reasoning apply \[this principle\] to the privilege in the case of the said abbot. Likewise, in Exodus the Lord said to Moses: Jethro instituted Aaron and others in charge of \[individual groups and in honor\], and enumerated eight burdens unjustly borne by certain individuals at their boundaries, and sends \[them\] after the scapegoat above their heads. And the abbot commissioned \[is warned\]: lest Aaron or a dean usurp by succession the greater \[office\] that belongs to him — \[lest such a one\] suffer the penalty of Core, Dathan, and Abiron \[cf. Numbers 16\], and the penalty of Nadab and Abihu whom God punished with fire because they kindled alien fire. Because no one established as bishop ought to proceed with display and pomp, nor adorned as Korah, nor wearing a ring. So this one is neither a heretic nor pompous, and nothing should be added concerning horns or vestments or staff — because \[these things\] signify against my meaning and reversal. Thus the angel says the spiritual use \[of these symbols\], and it will please God above the new tomb producing all fruits. The ring signifies worthily ruling the flock with worthy company, spurring the false, alluring \[the true\] — but by the sign of commendation they are \[merely\] symbols, because these things have departed from him; the figure alone remains, incompatible with the purpose of this instrument of conceiving and bearing, yet without fruit. And why does the Church impute to such a father reward and scandal, \[and why do\] clerics on their own account usurp these pontifical ornaments, altogether undeserved? Another monstrosity: the world, placed in evil, is incited to disobey the Roman Church on the occasion of the keys. For the wretched common people see that the sword has been placed in the hands of madwomen, because all around they \[such prelates\] menace with their words. That sword and those keys are in the hands of the greedy and extortionate \[lit.: of Judas and exacters\]. The common people — the assembled congregation of simple lay folk, through whom the household groans.
