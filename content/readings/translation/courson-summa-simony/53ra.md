Although \[the usurer\] sins in this case, yet Hieronymus \[says\]: “It is better that the treasury of the Church be exposed to usury than that a poor person should perish.” Hence the blessed Paula received money at usury to feed the poor. The usurer or another who takes scandal in this case should impute it \[to himself\], as happens most beautifully with the fervent person initially when he is scandalized by the form of it — this is not to be imputed to him, and as if a desert of sinners \[illegible\] of memory were to approach with thieves rushing to plunder, that whole \[affair\] belongs to their account, and what is said \[amounts to\] this: it is better to die of hunger than \[to act\] \[illegible\] or with scandals of the soul \[illegible\]. Thus are such effects caused by \[this\] arrangement to be understood.

Likewise ask whether a prince or prelate, himself or through others, may receive anything from a usurer who brings it from usury or who \[the prince\] strongly believes does not \[do otherwise\]. We know that all his goods are already usury or \[ill-gotten\] residue, and \[ask\] whether a prince may by right of kingdom, if he has constrained the \[usurer’s\] household as is fitting for a usurer, \[take\] all his goods into the treasury. Likewise on the same point, on certain authorities \[cited by\] abbots and those of monastic religion, to whom such \[gifts\] are commended — saying that they may receive something from such people, \[arguing\] that one should despoil them as the Hebrews enriched themselves from Egypt \[Exod. 12:35–36\]. “Make friends for yourselves from mammon of iniquity” \[Luke 16:9\] — asking nothing of the \[sinners’\] share, lest they refuse to accept the offerings made by usurers when they freely offer them. “We are sons of the Scots \[i.e., Irish\]; it is lawful for us to eat something from punishment, but the malice of all the Scots’ greed is easily scourged by the mouth of the Savior.” Those who consent cannot say in the Gospel as Zaccheus says: “If I have defrauded or withheld anything, \[I restore\] fourfold.” \[Luke 19:8\]
