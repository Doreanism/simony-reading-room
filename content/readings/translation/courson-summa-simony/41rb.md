Further, the one holding the priory may, if it is easy enough for him to live there and if he would do so, transfer that person to a monastery; and the priory, insofar as it is set in motion by such a stroke of circumstance, finds its situation like the bitterness of Mara. And since he does not grasp that the principal \[capital\] is not moved by usury or simony — because what is spiritual there is assumed with humility, like the right of patronage, which entails neither simony nor usury in such a contract, and justly so — since perhaps the holder of the priory does not \[count\] among \[the priory’s assets\] the tithes or the offerings of churches that pertain to the grant of the priory: if in this case the goods exchanged there are spiritual things, they simply cannot be sold. Further, one who buys such a priory — if it is bought at so high a price and there are tithes and church offerings within it — buys something that can \[otherwise\] be sold and commits interior simony. Solution: the case is twofold, as implied. The first is if someone wishes to sell the priory itself. The second is when someone intends to sell the fruits of that priory. In the first case he incurs simony. In the second case he incurs usury. The case can arise if, accounting for the time involved, nothing accrues in the sale beyond the mere present value of the yield. The form is as follows: “The fruits of this priory are customarily worth nearly five hundred pounds per year, and a little more; I will give them to you for as long as you shall possess \[it\].” Let the sale be made at a fair overall valuation; let us take care that simony not arise, so that the \[spiritual\] capital is placed at risk. In the case at hand, when the priory is treated as a whole — unless \[calculated\] from the canon portion, the first fruits, or the entire deanery together with the offerings of the churches and other revenues — then \[the spiritual revenues\] are entrusted to the possessor from the tithes, offerings, and other spiritual things; the remaining temporal residue can be sold.
