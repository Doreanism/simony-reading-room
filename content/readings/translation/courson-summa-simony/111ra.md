### XXV.

Item, concerning the distinctions to be drawn regarding servants according to the law: either they are servants of the faithful or of the unfaithful. If of the faithful, either of a private person or of the church. If servants of a private person or the church, either they are fully manumitted or not. If fully manumitted, they can be promoted if they appear in the list of those \[eligible\] today \[illegible\]. If not manumitted and made evident \[as such\], \[illegible\] and \[presumed subject\]. However, unless thereby the unfree servants are \[listed\] in the aforesaid provisions. Servants of the church are frequently manumitted by reason of the right of patronage, and yet they can \[be ordained\], because of the Apostle \[speaking\] of free grace. But against this: before God there is no acceptance of persons. Before whom there is no distinction between Latin and Greek or barbarian. Therefore the conditions of such persons are no more or less to be excluded from promotion. For the Lord chose not the noble or the philosophers, but fishermen, the poor, the ragged, and the base — and those things which were as nothing to confound what was mighty \[1 Corinthians 1:27–28\]. By common right, therefore, all such persons are to be promoted if they are otherwise worthy, for they also \[merit\] promotion. We resolve this: Pope Leo assigns this cause — whoever is to be enrolled in the divine militia must not be bound to any necessity, whether of servants or of courts, because he must serve God freely. One cannot serve God and Mammon at the same time, for “no man who serves God, etc.” \[2 Timothy 2:4\]. Concerning servants of the faithful we thus say and must distinguish. Now if they were servants of Jews or pagans — either of both by some natural \[claim\] — either some of the others did not wish to become Christians. A Christian bought from Jews \[or pagans\] made them public and brought them to freedom lest the name of God be blasphemed \[cf. 1 Timothy 6:1\], as is stated in the canon “Mancipia” at dist. 54.
