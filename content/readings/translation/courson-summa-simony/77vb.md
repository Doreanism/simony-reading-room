And then it is necessary to cry out against such incorrigibles; and if they are manifest usurers, let him excommunicate them anew, denouncing them publicly as excommunicated by sanction of the Lateran Council — and neither his bishop nor his archdeacon will be able to revoke this. But if it is a case of adultery or of some \[offense\] that is manifest yet known only in secret — and \[the offender\] will deny \[receiving\] communion of the Eucharist — if it is a public matter, \[the priest\] shall, on a solemn feast day, forbid such persons to approach the altar under pain of excommunication; so henceforth he shall record the names of those who, by reason of their ecclesiastical status, wish to be bound by the name of the Church — whether prelate or community of the faithful — \[illegible\]; if in secret and before witnesses they are unwilling to repent of such \[sin\], and when the prelate finds them altogether hardened, such incorrigibles should be excommunicated in the face of the Church if they refuse even then to hear; and then they are to be held as pagans and publicans and avoided by all. Likewise Augustinus, at the end of \[his commentary on\] this \[passage\], says that the prelate — by his example and temporal support — keeps his subjects in check and corrects them in their excesses. Hence it is rendered to Peter by the Lord: “Feed, feed, feed my sheep” — as if to say: feed by correction, by teaching, and by true example; feed, feed also by temporal support: in prayer, in the giving of alms, and in other \[works\]. Hence it is clear that the prelate is bound to exercise greater care in sound correction than others, lest the flock perish through him; for unless \[one is\] attentive, Pope \[illegible\] — dist. xi, q. iii, chapter “galdraue” \[illegible\] — prelates deserving of censure who set examples of perdition for their subjects \[illegible\] fear lest the prelate be guilty of the spiritual death of rousing his parish \[to perdition\], and in every way let \[him remember\] that word: if this \[illegible\].
