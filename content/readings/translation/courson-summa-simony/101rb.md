Insofar as someone lies to please men — one who lies in the fifth kind sins in two respects: in that he lies, and in that he desires to please men. For even doing good in order to please men is evil. Hence the Apostle \[says\]: “If I were still pleasing men, I would not be the servant of Christ” — that is, if I were doing these things for the purpose of pleasing men; and to do good with this intent, that I may please men, is evil. Much more does one who does evil and lies from a desire to please sin doubly. But one who lies in the third kind, that is, from the sheer lust for lying, sins less than that one \[i.e., the one who lies in the second kind\], and sins more gravely than one who lies in the fifth kind or in the fourth. We grant this for the case at hand. Yet they can both exceed and be exceeded, because some circumstance can aggravate the sin more than these alone considered — that is, considering the desire to please and the sheer lust for lying — so that the fifth is in fact less than the fourth. Likewise, suppose that someone lies in the teaching of religion, asserting something against histories, deeds, or names — if he says that Abraham departed from Ur of the Chaldeans, or \[that\] Job \[illegible\], or that Christ mounted a donkey on Palm Sunday. Such a one, lying thus, sins in the teaching of religion. Therefore \[it seems that\] one sins less who through a lie persecutes a brother with hatred unto death, than one given \[to lying\] in the teaching of religion, when one ought to know that the other is more serious. Solution. To lie in the teaching of religion is taken in two ways: sometimes it is taken so broadly as to encompass all things that pertain to religion; sometimes it is taken more strictly to encompass the very articles of faith — according to which Augustinus says that this is the gravest sin; for one who, like a heretic, corrupts some article of faith by his teaching sins most gravely, because such teaching kills souls; and therefore one who by his teaching pursues a brother to \[spiritual\] death sins more gravely \[than one who persecutes a brother to bodily death through a lie\].
