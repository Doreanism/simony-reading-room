Those of the evil eye are blameworthy. \[Now the question arises:\] if a legal expert sells advice, or an advocate sells patronage, whether he does so without mortal sin — which he proves in several ways. It is established, indeed, that, as sacred scripture attests, without the charismata of the Holy Spirit — the spirit of wisdom and understanding, the spirit of counsel and fortitude, the spirit of the fear of the Lord — no one among those who excel can sell these gifts. He proves this through Simon Magus, who was condemned by Peter for wishing to buy the power to work miracles by means of money, with Peter saying, “May your money perish with you.” By the same reasoning, an advocate cannot sell \[his service\] when it proceeds from knowledge, nor can a consultant sell what proceeds from the spirit of counsel. He now proves this by the authority of Solomon, who says in Proverbs: “Do not sell doctrine, wisdom, or understanding.” And even if you say in objection, “I wish to sell this” — do you not sin mortally by that very intention? Secondly, \[the advocate draws\] from his knowledge in the doctrine of counsel, as many are given as if from a chair of simony to be sought. If he seeks it in one or two \[cases\] from his chair: “Break your bread for the hungry” — by “bread” he understands all doctrine, whether of counsel or of advocacy. And James says: “Religion pure and undefiled before God is this: to visit orphans and widows in their tribulation, and to keep oneself unspotted from this world.” If great men have a desolate or unjustly oppressed widow and you scorn her unless you sell the bread of your counsel or advocacy for money — or if you have given it in exchange for money without true religion — you scorn her and offend God.
