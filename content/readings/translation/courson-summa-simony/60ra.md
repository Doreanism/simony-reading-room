As to whether such \[tithes\] are to be received — since their solitary labor has been diminished by usury or plunder — these are among the more contemptible \[matters\]. And \[regarding\] what follows: predial \[tithes\] are given, however frontally from feet \[i.e., of the soil’s yield\]; the first concern all fields that belong to the holdings and gardens of the parish, since they renew themselves each year. Of these renewals the parish priest can and must demand tithes, which the priest does not accept from usury — from however great a renewal of crops that are broadly brought in. For we are not able, in receiving tithes from an individual, to distinguish between fields sown with lawful or unlawful seed or otherwise unlawfully acquired. \[The principle is\] like \[receiving\] from a usurer. And it is in this case that Alexander speaks of \[things\] sown with the usurer’s goods and with unlawful \[means\] — especially where it is established that the seed was from usury or plunder, for “one who sows evil reaps with a curse.” If it is asked whether monks of Albi \[Albingenses\], Templars, and Hospitallers, who on the occasion of their privileges have begun to strip churches of the tithes from their fields, are sinning: it seems they are, \[for\] no one ought to grow wealthy through the loss of another, as \[under\] the law \[d.\] Quanto. It seems they are not sinning, because lord Alexander grants them the tithes of new clearings

and of their own \[lands\], as in the \[title\] “de decimis et proveniunt,” \[c.\] “Nobis enim” and \[c.\] “Patri.” And we can say: the donation originally commanded \[them to\] now be compelled to pay tithes in the same title, \[c.\] “Statuimus.” It is better, however, for religious \[houses\] to pay tithes on those \[fields\] which they hand over to laymen and others to cultivate. But concerning new clearings and those \[fields\] which they cultivate with their own hands and at their own expense, and for the feeding of their own animals on their own demesnes — Hadrianus and Alexander together conceded tithes to them, as in the same title, \[c.\] “Literas” and \[c.\] “Ex parte.” Moreover in the law let \[the reader\] know \[of the restoration\]
