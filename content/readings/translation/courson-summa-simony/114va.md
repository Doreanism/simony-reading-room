## IX.

Alexander, on the title concerning appeals, in that chapter, says that the remedy of recusal should be available even in those causes committed without an appeal clause, since if anyone \[exercises\] \[the process of\] recusal, in those \[causes\] all recusal that has been continuously raised by right is removed in the same title, in the chapter *vero*. He says that if any cause was delegated to certain persons and \[also\] to their blood relatives, who have power over the litigants, and also \[anyone\] suspected in the cause — such a delegate may be recused even though appeal has been removed and there is no \[formal\] appeal of the delegation. Recusal occurs when, before joinder of issue, someone says to the judge: “I recuse this judge here in this cause because you are a blood relative or have otherwise taken the side of my adversary,” and that \[judge\] should demonstrate that he is \[impartial\] and will not be recused. We say that what Lucius established in the decretal *postremo* — which superseded what Alexander had established in the chapter *si ulterius nos* — is to be observed: \[namely\] that when litigants in the audience of the supreme pontiff were able to lay their cause before him, and delegates were given there with authority to settle those causes and send them away, and \[the parties\] then resort to the named judges there — in the first mode they \[the parties\] could not recuse them in the pending cause. Likewise, the fifth rule: as you have sanctioned for us, let them do the same, and whatever measure they have applied \[let it\] be applied to us; and what one person knows to be evil will in time rebound upon him. And if appeal has been removed in the counter-claim it may follow that the removal carries over — and conversely, as for example if disputes have arisen and been settled: through some agreement in which an appeal clause was removed, and I afterward wish to bring against you a counter-claim in this cause for the recovery of a cloak, \[appeal\] cannot be made here, because in the agreement appeal was prohibited. As in the decretals, extra on the title.
