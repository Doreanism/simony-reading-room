### Whether Jacob Lied in Saying He Was Esau

This question asks whether Jacob lied when he said he was Esau. First, it asks whether it was lawful for him to confirm this with an oath. Second, it asks whether Isaac blessed against his own intention. Third, whether Jacob sinned by stealing the blessing. Fourth, whether the contract Jacob made with Esau regarding the sale of the birthright is valid. And other difficulties related to these matters we treat briefly in summary. That Jacob lied in saying he was Esau is proved because he neither was Esau nor could he claim to be Esau. In saying he was Esau, he was mocked. Furthermore, when Isaac felt him and said, “The hands are indeed the hands of Esau, yet the voice is the voice of Jacob,” he understood that it was Jacob rather than Esau, and he understood Jacob to be speaking falsely with the words “I am Esau.” He understood at that point that the whole thing about being Esau was false. Isaac himself understood, whenever the occasion presented it, that the statement was false, and understood what Jacob had signified by it, and Jacob had spoken a falsehood. But against this: in the same passage the gloss says, when Jacob uttered the oath “I am he,” this locution was itself the oath sworn in respect of the birthright, as is stated in that chapter. Likewise, dist. xii, q. ii objects that in saying what he said, Jacob made himself heir to the birthright. For he did not say he was the firstborn by birth, but by the right of the birthright, which he did not surrender in claiming it. Moreover, he and the whole person of this oath declared the oath valid in some case in order to confirm \[his claim\] by the oath. And that unless by his statement “I am Esau” it is established that he was intending the business of another equally, then the faithful say: what one who swears this understands is what the other by whom he swears understands. And Isaac understood this in the proposed sense, and God \[knew\] the statement in the proposed sense to be false, and if he intended it as false.
