Likewise, Alexander says in a decretal that where a church is so poor that it does not suffice for a salary, nor can \[its holder\] obtain another church, divided fortune being preserved, someone with insufficient \[income\] may be permitted to hold another church along with it. Likewise, it often happens that an entire cathedral church would collapse if a pillar essential to it were removed, as happened at the church of Reims when the Parisian cantor was taken from it — wherefore he himself, when he protested, was compelled to return that prebend for the utility of that church, even though he was already a prebendary in another church. Was this not lawful where the matter concerned the business of the church’s purse? Because the Apostle says of it: the daily pressure \[of care\] — that is, the solicitude for all the churches \[2 Corinthians 11:28\] — each one man caring for these above all. And similarly, someone who judges himself to be of minor importance may, in \[various\] matters, be of service to many churches, lest anything in them deteriorate through his absence. Likewise, it is permissible indifferently to hold the best revenues, tithes, and excellent properties as wealth. And just as prebends are spiritual things, so are tithes; and the same is to be reckoned for benefices in full and for tithes. Likewise, it is lawful for a patron to hold several rights of patronage, and in those patronages it is lawful to receive portions from various revenues if he is in need. As indeed happened at Liège, where it was established that certain clergy hold in many churches small portions of patronage rights in those churches and receive parts of the revenues, and they retain the cure of souls — and thus one person can hold prebends in different churches. Likewise, just as several abbeys are established according to the holy fathers — which say that a single such church or monastery has several fully incorporated parishes from ancient times — by a stronger argument still, a simple cleric, who is not bound to the same degree of perfection as an abbot, may lawfully hold several churches.
