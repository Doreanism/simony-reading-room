Solution. Religious who buy or seize public pastures or forests, or streams or tithes, to the detriment of adjacent forests, are bound to make good all damages, according to that \[principle\]: “No one ought to be enriched at the loss of another.” Concerning these matters and concerning bishops who minister in wars, we distinguish as follows: either they did so when the destruction of the kingdom was imminent, or \[they did so\] in an unjust war. In the first case, against pagans and heretics lest the kingdom or the Church be overthrown, they may supply expenses to the extent of their ability. In the second case, by no means, because if through the generosity of princes they distribute the goods of the poor as expenses for wars against believers, or if the Church derives no benefit therefrom, they sin mortally and are bound to the restitution of everything.
