Likewise, suppose that in such an extreme case there is a prelate: you are bound to lay down your soul for your sheep, like a good shepherd; and you are more bound to die of hunger than to kill them through scandal. Some say that just as a prelate is secretly harboring a great heresy, he ought rather to eat the food and the bread than to scandalize anyone, or others, unto death; and so the simple person \[*simplex*\]. But it seems to us that both the simple person and the prelate ought to manifest the willingness to cause scandal — that they render no veneration to an idol — and under the compulsion of mortal peril they should eat; and thus if they eat they do not sin, and those fools who are unwilling to believe die in scandal without his sin. Furthermore, truth attests that no one ought to kill himself; and if there is unavoidable peril, let scandal arise rather than abandon this truth. Likewise, the companion —

### ix.

Your companion comes to you to borrow money. If you say “I have it” and do not lend it, he will be scandalized. If you say “I have it” — but do not say “I have it” — if you deny it you will scandalize him to a greater degree. What will you say? In the first case, the risk of death is imminent; in the second, it serves the motive of prevention — which avoids the scandal of scandal, that is, an excessive desire for money. \[Illegible\] lest he scandalize, just as in the case of the one who seeks \[the man\] to kill him and is told his whereabouts — said “he is here.” This \[must be judged\] in the interest of saving his life; by equal reasoning, and much more for the salvation of his soul, you may say “I have it.” If this is a lie, and the reason is iniquitous, he says in sign \[of it\]. For if he \[only\] lies in the sense —. Solution: No one is in need of saying a falsehood in such a case, for he does not lie. From the body of law —

### x.

A priest has fixed and settled intent on Easter day not to repent, and he must of necessity either celebrate \[Mass\] or, if he does celebrate, scandalize his entire parish. What is he to do in this situation? It is established that if he celebrates he receives the cup of the Lord unworthily.
