Moreover, a prince perhaps sends them into war, and all those who follow his camp hold their position and fittingly corrupt all the churches, plundering much; and if such men were nothing in war, they hear the Lord saying in Exodus \[23:2\]: “You shall not follow the multitude to do evil.” And in the Gospel: “to him who has received a command, do not extort, do not use violence; be content with your pay.” \[Luke 3:14\] If they are unwilling or as they knew first, because they count themselves as timber of the Lord, so that neither his command nor fear of him is eternal for them — moreover the lord would not follow them if he disinherited them, cutting off their wives and children. But if they should go with him on their own, they will go with him little by little to gather grain and suddenly plunder monasteries and granaries. However, if the Church enjoins on all the aforesaid that they should not obey their lords and should cease from those labors they exercise for kingdom and church, and there was no iron boundary between king and people, and the palace was thus properly administered, not \[moved\] by forms as by a lord, then no one would be found who in fine goods or in tears or in precious colors of clothing would impede anything of luxury or pride by labor — because if all believed the words of Hieronymus, they would believe them. Every \[soul\] rushes into danger, either moved by luxury or by cruelty; and philosophers say, according to the disposition of right nature, that if something is useful to the human race, it is to be called such through \[right\] reason.
