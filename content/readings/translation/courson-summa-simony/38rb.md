Further, a certain person has simoniacally obtained a prebend or a bishopric. The question is whether, if the lord pope grants a dispensation after a resignation has been made, he could be reinstated in the same prebend or bishopric, and whether the pope could absolve him from \[the obligation to restore\] the fruits of the prebend. This seems so, because the pope has fullness of power; and just as he could grant and concede the fruits of tithes and similar things for a time — since one may be absolved from \[the obligation of restoring\] received fruits — so he can do the same regarding the fruits of a prebend, which in that case are justly \[retained by him\].

### Solution

There is a twofold resignation: one that is verbal and real, the other real and verbal. A \[resignation that is merely\] mental and verbal and real \[occurs\] when someone verbally resigns what was simoniacally acquired while harboring in his mind the hope of receiving it back. Such a \[merely\] real resignation is not valid in the sight of God. But the other kind is valid in the sight of God and of men. Let this full resignation be made in the present case and let him be newly invested.
