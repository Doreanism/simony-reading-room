Likewise, there is a general rule that when a mandate expires, the mandate \[given under it\] also expires. There is a similar general rule that the remedy of appeal is nowhere to be granted in a \[case conducted by\] strict examination. But the lord pope can remove the remedy of appeal that has been granted \[in a case\], just as he has added the opposing clause to another \[letter\], forbidding appeal. And by the same reason, \[one\] who in his letters \[includes\] that general rule about the expiry of a mandate upon \[the principal’s\] death — so that he is bound by his own letters \[illegible\] boldly, so that a mandate thus expires upon \[his\] death. ## Solution.

If the death of the mandators came to \[them\] \[illegible\] before the letter was retracted — if they were able to proceed — if the case was opened and heard — even at the moment of the mandator’s death, \[they proceed\] nonetheless. And we will say generally that the lord pope can establish or \[grant\] a privilege \[during his lifetime\] so that after his death it remains in force. If he wishes to establish this, it should be done in such legal form, saying: “I, while alive, grant cognizance of this cause by my authority even after my death” — because in this way he would derogate from his future successor.

## VIIII.

Likewise there is one of two \[decretals\], one an emendation \[i.e., a decretal of correction\] on rescripts, the chapter *cerium*, and the other an emendation on the office of the delegated judge, the chapter *sane*. The resolution concerns that chapter *cerium*: to say that, amicably, concerning that chapter *cerium* — that they make no mention of competing letters \[of commission\] — the judges should \[set aside\] the petition on its merits and the statement of \[the relevant\] observation says that judges must suffice \[with what is presented\]. Hence it is customary to say that all things that arise and that are more favorable take precedence. \[One might\] say in that chapter *cerium* that \[the date formulation\] which is customarily inserted thus: “given in such a month, such a year” — in the future it could say “the more favorable takes precedence” or they say so in that chapter *sane*. But this is only customary.
