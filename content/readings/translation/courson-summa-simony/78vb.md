The question arises how this conflict of canons is to be resolved. Solution: these conflicting texts speak to distinct cases. The first set of chapters speaks about a crime that is notorious by its own self-evidence. The conflicting chapters speak about hidden crimes. If the examiner poses the case thus: You are a rector of a parish, and it is established by the clamor of the whole parish that a manifest sinner is corrupting your entire parish. He does not heed you reproving him, either in private or before witnesses. What will you do with him — can you excommunicate him without the authority of the bishop who favors him? Likewise: ignorant canons of some cathedral church — the bishop and the dean favor your canons who openly keep concubines. What will you do in such a case? When you enter the choir or oratory, where you know and see such persons ministering there and giving occasion to others to communicate with them — you withdraw; you scandalise the whole church. If you hear the mass of such persons — the canon says that no one should hear the mass of those whom he undoubtedly knows to keep a concubine. And all priests who openly keep concubines are suspended, indeed excommunicated by the canon itself. For thus it says at dist. xxxii, cap. “preter hec”: Holy Timothy ordained under excommunication, saying: Everything concerning priests, deacons, and subdeacons, by the constitution of our blessed predecessors, namely Pope Leo or Pope Nicholas, regarding the chastity of clerics — whoever openly keeps a concubine, or having taken one does not dismiss her, by the authority of almighty God and of the Apostles Peter and Paul, we decree that he shall not sing mass, nor read the Gospel, nor the Epistle.
