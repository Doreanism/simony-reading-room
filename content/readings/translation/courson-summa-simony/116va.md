Witnesses who are present prove what is alleged; those that are illegitimate are to be rejected, while those to be admitted and those to be rejected \[must be distinguished\]. Certain witnesses, then, if they are to be admitted for testimony: they must swear that they will tell everything they know about that matter without admixture of falsehood. And this on the one side that is in the \[court of the\] *palia* \[i.e., the rota\], as is argued at xciii.d.q.im., c. ii, *sane*. Concerning the rest, on witnesses, examination, and \[their\] weighing \[of evidence\], we recite five things chiefly which diminish the credibility of testimony: diversity of matters; diversity of persons; diversity of days; diversity of times; diversity of places. Diversity of matters: to assert something from one side on one matter and something else from another, as the third question, ninth \[distinction\], the chapter \[cited\] provides.

## Diversitas personarum

And \[illegible\] as is read in xxxv.g concerning twins; diversity of places, as is provided in the \[case where one\] said it occurred under the first \[tree\] and another said under a different tree, as the law, iii.q.ix., chapter *melommius*, \[provides\]; and note that in a cause on appeal, after the sentence, new witnesses can be produced — \[before sentence\] the secular laws compelled \[witnesses\] to give testimony, by name on account of old age, or because they are \[infirm by nature\], or because they hold public office; likewise on account of blood relationship or affinity or affection, as a freedman against his patron — according to the canons \[such persons are\] to be admonished, not compelled, unless it is established to the judge’s satisfaction that they are withdrawing from testimony out of love or hatred. On these matters: xiv.q.iii., chapter *quidquid*. In all these matters: always that one witness alone is not sufficient to convict.
