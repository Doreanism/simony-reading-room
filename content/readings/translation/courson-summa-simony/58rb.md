A question is raised concerning the Campagna: when in the hour of death a priest approached a certain man so that he might confess those things which were in his possession — and the priest passed over in silence his common sins, saying nothing of whether those things he possessed were held lawfully or unlawfully — and when the priest wished to absolve him, the sick man refused, saying: “Behold, all that I have came to me from my father — powerful possessions not sought for my salvation — and I could not \[be saved\] unless I make restitution; therefore I wish you to carry me away naked and have all those palaces and the rest restored.” Let usurers follow his example in doing likewise.

## What must be annexed concerning tithes

## On tithes

First it must be noted what is understood by the term “tithes,” why tithes exist, and whether all persons are bound to pay them. \[We shall also ask\] whether certain persons who in some case make use of ecclesiastical property can be held to the payment of tithes. Tithes are owed, as stated in the relevant chapter. Tithes are so necessary for the poor. And the Apostle says: when a contribution is to be given, it ought to be rendered as a right to the needy; they are to be given and stored in the tabernacle of the Lord.
