A similar objection applies to lending: here there is neither the character of plunder nor of usury. For when God says to someone “take a loan from him, give him a gift as sacred,” he \[the lender\] acts in what he gives as a loan or in a contract or in a vineyard, and so the place would bear neither this nor that — you owe nothing there, either in loan or otherwise, in concession or in receipt, nor ought you to commingle anything of ours with his, so that he might grant another loan. One who does this runs with fury, because it is rendered a subvention of mere mercy. ## A twofold corruption is at issue here

The root of the corruption is that such a matter always retains the stain of plunder or usury until it is returned to its true lord. And let us say to one who knowingly participates: if you know that the mass \[of money\] is from plunder, then \[illegible\] my commercial dealings — you shall not receive through contract or celebration or loan anything from them, because this very participation in fairs implicates \[all parties\], since it involves public and notorious complicity with the diligence owed. Such fairs may lawfully operate \[only\] when participants observe this. Those who engage in them and nonetheless claim ignorance err.
