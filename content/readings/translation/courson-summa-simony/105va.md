He sometimes kills in the line of duty, as Augustinus says in the same question, same chapter, “de occidendis” — yet not to cleric nor to layman unless as that question teaches in that chapter, “tu iudicasti.” If you ask how far a cleric may minister in this matter, we shall say: up to the point at which the Lord said to Peter, “Put your sword back in its sheath” \[John 18:11\]. Which saying is: “Behold, here are two swords” \[Luke 22:38\], and he replied, “It is enough.” And it must be said that when someone is simultaneously a count and a bishop, he holds both swords, having the authority and execution of the spiritual sword, but the authority without execution of the material sword — as is evident from blessed Silvester, who received the authority of the material sword from Constantine, who committed all the kingdoms to the church; which authority he \[bestowed\] fittingly upon the secular princes who received the execution of the material sword from the church.
