### V.

“In whatever ecclesiastical judgments, wherever there is an appeal to other ecclesiastical judges, it shall be heard, unless \[one\] is unwilling to come.” And it is to be understood that from any judge one may appeal to the pope, as is held in the same question, c. “In.” And a delegate before whom litigation is conducted can appeal from the delegation, or have recourse to a superior — if someone holds himself to be superior — as to the pope. As is held in that decretal “Nos meminimus,” title “De appellationibus.” But whoever appeals to a lesser judge will be harmed by the error, since \[the appeal\] is made before the opening \[of the hearing\]. But if \[the appeal is made\] to the pope or to the greater \[judge\] to whom it should go, the error will not be harmful.
