## Concerning Ambitious Judges

who belong to a new kind of harming and flattering, \[operating\] in the courts of princes, and among great ladies, and in the chambers of those who derogate from the laws — softening the rigor of the canons and promoting \[causes\] in favor of those persons. Let us say that they are to be struck with a far graver penalty and punished with that punishment, because it happens that such persons, as if promoting certain canons they have read at a price, cause them to be able the more to sin through robbery or exactions; nor can the lord pope sin in taking away \[from them\] or in issuing or remitting authorizations, nor in any way through his plenitude of power. But it is established that they are imbued with all manner of lies and so forth, because where the greater gift of prelacy exists, there the greater transgressor is subject to the greater fault. This is also proved by that poetic saying: “Every mind deserves, insofar as it is conspicuous in itself — he who is greater sins the more \[deserves the greater consequence\].” It also frequently happens in the case of a knight \[illegible\] that he hires out his services to hire other knights and adds them to his following, and sins mortally — because for vainglory he wishes to be more widely known, and he hires out his services insufficiently in that \[enterprise\] while \[illegible\], and sins mortally. We see that a mind regarding a violent physical attack, if it busies itself with curing what it does not know how to cure, so too this \[judge\], ascending to ecclesiastical office unworthily, deceives \[others\].
