Regarding entertainers and jesters \[tornatores et convellos\], where it is established that they are such persons, the sacraments are not to be administered to them, either in life or in death. Likewise, counsel a ruler not to take such persons into service for the defense of the kingdom, since it is established that this class of men is the worst of all and is infected by many laws \[pluries saturatis legibus\]. And since, in order to resolve doubts, we must discuss those who fall under the canon — that canon broadly sown \[late seminate\], which to this day the Church has not resolved in full clarity — Innocentius in Session XVI, third question, final part: if anyone incites, in a general way, so that anyone who lays violent hands on a cleric is by that very act \[eo ipso\] excommunicated. First, certain objections are raised against this. Suppose someone spits in his face, or soils his vestments, or sprinkles on him unclean water or foul urine: it seems this inflicts a greater insult than if he had lightly struck him. It seems then that he falls under the canon. Likewise, a master flogs a pupil and exceeds the proper measure of correction, or initiates him into something for no reason, with the intent to corrupt: does he fall under the canon? Likewise, what if \[illegible\] there are officials and a prince commands that no one shall enter the chamber, and he \[the official\] wants to expel and flog the unwilling clerics without reason: does he fall under the canon? Since the canon states that to repel force, common laws permit common force as a first resort, but if one surpasses the tardiness of discipline and flogs beyond measure, one falls under the canon. Likewise, cleric boys who play and strike each other do not fall under the canon, since they are below the age of puberty: so it appears Alexander \[held\]. By the same reasoning, some who play at ball, or in a game of hurling or in such a procession by foot, \[illegible\] falling under the canon if they exceed measure in the game, as when one knocks another’s peg away.
