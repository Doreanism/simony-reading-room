Furthermore, suppose that in this penitent the contrition was so great that it sufficed to delete every penalty and guilt, and he goes to the priest. The priest remits neither guilt nor relaxes penalty, since there is neither guilt nor any liability to penalty, and \[the priest\] does nothing with respect to remission or absolution. If someone says that in truth the priest does not remit the sin or the penalty but is said to remit it insofar as he shows it to have been remitted — if that is what he does — what does he do more than a mere herald? A herald shows one \[man\] here and another there. For in himself \[the priest\] neither remits nor remits anything of guilt or penalty in the present case. And yet such a showing is of value. Or how does he remit, when a showing is not a remission? Or when what has been shown is stated only to himself and others? Furthermore it seems that such a showing is altogether foolish, because the authority upon that passage “whatever you shall have bound” says: many think they loose and they do loose, and \[think they\] bind as though binding. If therefore the priest thinks he looses with such a loosing, and if loosing is nothing other than showing the loosed, and binding nothing other than showing the bound, then he shows many as loosed or bound whom he does not know he is showing as bound or absolved. Furthermore, if the priest thinks he binds what he does not bind, this happens in some case \[where\] the sentence does not remain everywhere; therefore he does not bind there, and an unjustly imposed sentence does not hold — contrary to which canon says that a sentence imposed by one’s own pastor, whether justly or unjustly, holds. Solution. The opinion of some is that the same \[debt\] can be remitted a hundred times even when there is nothing in the matter. No one can have a debt remitted if — suppose this person owes nothing — you impose upon him \[a charge\] saying he owes something, and you drag him before witnesses and judges, and they finally recognize him as innocent: any one of them would absolve him from your claim, yet they relax no debt.
