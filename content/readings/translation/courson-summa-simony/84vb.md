It seems not — since he had obtained the permission of his metropolitan and bishop, and they contracted \[marriage\] for him with his wife. We say that he must observe continence here on account of scandal, as Augustinus says of his mother: “When you are in Milan, do as is done there; when you are in Rome, do as is done there.” Furthermore, I ask whether a person who was ordained deacon there, coming here, could contract marriage here; or whether one ordained here could contract marriage there. Granting this, and given that he vowed continence here, he ought not to act contrary to his vow, whether here or elsewhere; and yet he ought to contract marriage there. If \[the objection is raised\]: the Eastern man who came from there, being married, would not observe continence here except on account of scandal; and one ordained here, transferred there according to their custom, could and should \[not observe continence there\] — Resolution: The cases are not alike, because a vow — or something equivalent to a vow — must not be broken on account of any scandal. Nor are the cases alike between a young deacon and an old man — because by the sacrament he has been made a companion \[of continence\], namely by daily continence; and therefore dispensation against this is more difficult. If therefore \[the question concerns\] an old man wishing to contract marriage: anyone who is an old man and wishes to contract marriage, if he is free, should be granted \[the permission\] — by the Apostle and with a wife in the Lord, that is, a deacon who is young or also one who is constituted in holy orders — should not be dispensed with unless it can be done without scandal or harm to his neighbor, as in what concerns \[those\] among Gratian’s in the parts of Hungary.
