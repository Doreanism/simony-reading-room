I can pass over, because the testimony of one witness is the testimony of no one. And what is the force of one or two witnesses? \[It is because\] the Lord, imbued with the Gospel, \[gave weight\] to the testimony of one — namely for the sake of \[illegible\] if it concerned the person of John. Furthermore, it is better to say, on that passage, “Bring one or two witnesses” — with the purpose of correcting or of convicting under the witnesses; and from this it follows that multiple witnesses are required. But first we shall say that the aforementioned chapter speaks of cases to be assigned thus: when you alone know that someone has sinned, first seek to constrain him alone. And if he was not willing to hear you, you cannot go further — stop there, for although that person confessed without judicial proceedings to another, you can \[presuppose\] \[illegible\] concerning him by \[illegible\] hearsay; for the one who heard him passes it onto himself alone. If, however, it was committed in the presence of others \[illegible\], then you must first seek to restrain him alone; if at that point he was not willing to hear you, you could then bring in witnesses who were with you in the matter \[illegible\], previously \[having given\] admonishment, together with whom you may rebuke him.
