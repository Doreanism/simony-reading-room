If moreover anyone is called as a witness who ought to know that the case is unjust, and if he has proved it to be unjust, he shall be held infamous and shall not be admitted to advocate in any court. It is now clear what the oath against calumny must contain, as set forth in the decree on the oath against calumny, c. “Qui ex legibus,” and in the following chapters of that same title. Pope Innocentius says that neither a bishop, nor a priest, nor anyone of whatever order is to be compelled to swear, with the admonition and understanding that \[this applies\] unless the Roman pontiff has so ordered or adjudicated, and that one must be supplied with the necessity of the case \[i.e., a compelling reason\]. In the same chapter it says that plaintiffs are often to be compelled to furnish the oath against calumny, as argued from that chapter. Now it is clear from this that in certain special cases such an oath is admitted — namely in all cases except spiritual ones, in the primary case, and in criminal and civil matters, if in \[matters\] concerning birth that can finally be reasonably shown to involve the shedding of blood.

It seems that it can reasonably be shown how in every case and from whatever person the oath against calumny is to be required. For the laws and canons are countless for deciding disputes and bringing cases to an end, so that litigation does not go on forever; and the more conveniently one law subsumes everything, the more sharply it punishes calumny — for however many times in a year disputes were decided, if all matters could be summarized in a single day, because if everyone who \[brought\] a matter into the dispute were required to take such an oath, and if advocates understood that they could not stand in an unjust case, nor take any unjust cases, few or none would be found to prolong litigation. Furthermore, others who unjustly accused or defended an unjust case could easily be decided, and almost all cases could be decided at once and in one \[session\] before the oath against calumny were admitted in every case.
