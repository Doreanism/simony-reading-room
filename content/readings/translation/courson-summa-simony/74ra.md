He approves it. I object: the lord pope sees laymen holding tithes and says nothing, and he approves it. Should you object: the lord pope has often promoted unworthy men to prebends and \[allowed them\] to hold \[positions\] in different churches, and this he would not do unless he considered the holding of cures and prebends in different churches to be lawful. We believe that objections of this kind must be answered as follows: the acts of the lord pope are always to be interpreted in the more favorable sense. For just as the Church may be deceived — so that a boy is believed \[to be of full standing\] — and the lord pope cannot know everything nor be informed of everything, so too he may beforehand have believed the unworthy to be worthy, and would then grant them two churches or prebends, believing them to be revenues, or one as a commendation and another as a title. Nor does it fall to us to expose the shameful things of our father; lest, if critics find something blameworthy in his see — God forbid — we incur the curse of Ham, who exposed the nakedness of his father. To the final objection we say that no one who obtains nobility ought to usurp multiple prebends, since the nobility of no one should be a burden to the poor, but rather a consolation to them, for nobility of soul is the sole and unique fortress of virtue. Let the modern fathers yield their many revenues in the Church \[as the statutes require\]; they stand ready for the churches and the care of souls, and for the many bishops subject \[to the Church\], such that they cannot obey all; moreover, these men hold the places of many prebendaries in which they receive no prebend, and they therefore believe themselves absolved from guilt — but a far greater confusion hangs over them, for blind ambition blinds them, since they hold those positions with a view to being made bishops on account of them. But I ask: what shall we say of those who cannot be removed from this corrupt and troublesome confusion originating from Cambrai?
