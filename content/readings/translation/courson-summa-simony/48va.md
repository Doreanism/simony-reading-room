Therefore he who is the guardian’s client can do nothing in the administration, and if he confers anything to the deception of \[the ward’s\] first and tender appointment, nothing attaches to the one who tremblingly confers \[it\]. To demonstrate this: if someone were to confer a prebend or some dignity upon an ass or a brute animal, the conferral would not be binding — just as no other sacrament would be valid \[in such a case\] — since it is by invention that it would exist, and here nothing is effected. Granting all of this, we concede and say that since the boy is entirely undiscerning and does not even know what pastoral care is, if he is thus \[appointed\] and assigned, nothing is conferred upon him in such a case. Hence, as the Cantor says, since he has nothing in his ear in such a parish or prebend, he can be despoiled of it during \[his\] minority. Therefore if certain judges who have a commission from the lord pope to examine such assignments of cures or archdeaconries made to such small children — they would be bound by law to remove all such children and to place worthy persons in their place. If it is then asked whether adult vassals who are laymen are possessors of such dignities — we say that unless they are newly instructed \[and fit\], they are \[not\], because the original assignment was void in its investiture, whether united \[to a benefice\] or under excommunication.

## Concerning all those who wish to hire out their services to brokers, usurers, or plunderers

Of such persons we say that all incur \[guilt\] with fury, since they are counted among plunderers — because having nothing otherwise, they use brokers to devour the church and the deans, nor do they provide for the simple. We rage against brokers, usurers, and plunderers who are incorrigible and as if it were certain that such persons \[are beyond correction\].
