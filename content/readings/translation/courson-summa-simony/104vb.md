## This Case

This case is cited concerning that great arch-heretic, whose guilt is established by witnesses beyond all exception. He was first tried in a general council, and because heresy was proved against him and he was a lapsed and relapsed heretic, it was judged that he should be handed over to the secular court, that is, to be punished by secular power, according to what is contained in the constitution Lucem et tenebras abolendam in the last book of the Decretals. And it was settled that the wording “he is to be stripped before punishment” must be used. And nowhere is the mode of punishment to be determined, lest the prince himself inquire about the mode — for perhaps the prince, being an ecclesiastical man, says: “You know the law of God; do what penalties are to be inflicted according to human equity and the authority of the fathers. I am ready to hang this evildoer, or to burn him, or to let him go.” And if nowhere it is prescribed what is to be done with him — that is, to confine him in prison for a long time — then by the same reasoning I must have prisons to lock up such persons, or \[have\] a count of such wrongdoers.
