Therefore a \[illegible\] distinction must be drawn: if by legal statute in such contracts the principal is guaranteed safe whatever happens, and by expectation of time something \[illegible\] is expected over and above the outcome, it is plain that it is usurious and \[so\] in some way like \[the case of\] outcome \[illegible\]. But if the \[lender\] commits the principal to the hazard of loss, and yet hopes to receive something above it, this is not usury, since the risk is on both sides, as long as this contract is made according to the established custom of the place.
