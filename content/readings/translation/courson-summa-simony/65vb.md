Moreover, denunciation alone renders the guilty party bound to restitution or satisfaction, and when it makes heretics — as that \[saying\] of Solomon shows: “Six things the Lord hates, and the seventh his soul detests: namely, haughty eyes, a lying tongue, hands that shed innocent blood, a heart that devises the most wicked thoughts, feet swift to run toward evil, a deceitful thing, and he who sows discord among brothers.” On that passage Augustinus says: “The tale-bearer of this kind who sows discord among friends tears apart their unity as a heretic tears apart the unity of many in the Church, and in like fashion he seems prone to \[illegible\] heresy, and ought to be expelled as a heretic and as a plunderer of money, honor, or of what has been taken away.”
