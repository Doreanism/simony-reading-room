...about to be \[rewarded\]. Plainly. Certainly a perfect man falls and afterward rises in a lesser degree of charity, which can indeed happen, according to what is said in \[the Song of Songs\]: “from a thousand first a hundred, then ten,” the denarius signifying \[the one\] who rises more fittingly in the perfection of charity. Hence: this man will rise not worthy of as much as his charity was \[before\]; all the good merits of perfect charity are acknowledged to have returned in him. This man is somehow good, or alternatively so. In him all good works return — namely, there was some good in him, to wit the dignity by which he was to be adorned with a chaplet; but that dignity \[belongs to\] the perfect charity returning in him. Solomon. A greater layman said it is impossible that one who has once attained perfect charity should rise again in a state of imperfect charity; indeed, if he was once in a state of perfect charity he rises in that same state if he is to rise at all. The stronger solution is this: that the less a person subsequently followed, the less \[he merits\]; since each individual evil work deserves individual condemnation, and many evil works produce proportionally greater punishment — the quality and the plurality of works must be weighed with attention to the root of charity. Therefore if a good man in part \[falls away\] — if someone was long in perfect charity and did many works of perfect charity, and now rises again in imperfect or minimal charity, and that \[charity\] does not first rise according to the state of perfect charity, nor do those works of perfect charity that remained in him return — then \[the reward is measured\] according to the quantity of charity with which these persons die, because the Lord judges their ends according to their ends. If you object that every merit that existed in me before the fall returns along with it — that is, the dignity of perfect recompense for perfect charity and perfect works — these are to be counted and rewarded accordingly with the prize of the perfect, and \[he is\] to be cleansed.
