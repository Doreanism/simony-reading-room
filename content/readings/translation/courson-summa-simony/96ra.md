Hence the Lord, who is immutable, is frequently read to have changed his sentence, as may be read in that same distinction, chapter “in commutabili.” Objection: no one is bound to something good, and no one is bound in any case to commit any sin, and no one is bound in any case to commit the lesser evil, \[so the lesser is chosen\] only between a greater \[evil\]. Solution. No one falls into a moral dilemma \[perplexitas\] except the one whose conscience tells him that whichever \[option\] he chooses first, he sins. Hence those who thought they were rendering pious service to God by crucifying Christ were in a dilemma whether they did it or not; and similarly the ignorant man who does not know what he ought to say in the matter, believing or fearing

that he would commit perjury in everything — he is in a dilemma. And all authorities that speak of a dilemma are to be understood thus, namely while such a conscience persists.

### X.

But here someone asks you: a man sought \[as a fugitive\] to be killed asks you where he can be found or whether you saw him in a certain place. See how you ought to behave if it is possible to save him thus. You know that he is impenitent and that if he dies in his wickedness he is damned. You, caring more for his soul than for your own flesh, and knowing what your conscience dictates — you should conceal him lest he die thus, and bring him to a place where you can by your conduct \[or teaching\] change him; and you would prefer to change him rather than to let him come to death, whether by silence or by an oath — at least what is licit in intention. If you hide him in your house and they give him up \[betrayed from within\], furthermore one may affirm something and truly, and in strict and formal terms assert to both \[parties\] what will — with or without them — show him. For although it is \[in principle\] lawful to keep silent rather than to confirm by oath, yet Augustinus says on that passage “\[Blessed are\] all those who speak,” that it is not lawful for the strict man to swear to save another’s life, lest while saving another’s life he lose his own.
