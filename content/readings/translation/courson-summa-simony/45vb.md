We put in place of this \[the formula where\] we ask, or something of the sort, or: “If you give \[it\], I shall obtain this” — that is, setting aside all pretexts, “I will give ten” — no one doubts that this person is \[thereby\] contracting simony. Similarly, if upon a promise someone offers to inscribe his name in the register or in the list of those ordained, it is simony. Regarding citations also, make a distinction. If someone sells a citation for use in a secular court — \[this is\] another sin. \[If for use\] under the ecclesiastical sword — simony. Let us see: what is sold \[illegible\] in the secular court begins to be either simony or sacrilege. Similarly, selling \[letters to\] justice \[made\] publicly known: for justice is spiritual, since it is a virtue, and a hired executor is acting simoniacally — as is apparent from the case of Simon \[Magus\], who wished to buy the capacity for working miracles.
