If, however, princes move in some other manner, or in a mixed war, to attack and occupy the lands of others, and having expelled the heirs expel them wrongfully — as Alexander the Great did, and even those \[illegible\] would have been — their \[illegible\] sentence \[illegible\] and they are bound to restitution. If, however, they take up arms in a just war against habitual enemies, they can acquire \[land\] lawfully, and their soldiers can honorably serve under them for stipends, provided that they do not usurp anything beyond their stipends — as the Gospel says: “Do not trouble anyone, nor cause harm. Be content with your wage.” For if \[illegible\] the Apostle’s apostles were \[in error\] they are bound to restitution, as is argued in dist. xv, q. i, *Milita ego*, chapter. Concerning those built by Charles \[Martel\] and Girart: we say that if it is established that the heirs have protested because of the time elapsed, we say without distinction that the present holders of those goods are still plunderers and cannot be saved in that state. We say this and it can be established from the \[sources\]. If, however, ignorance is invincible, it perhaps excuses simple persons in such a monastery, though it is still dangerous to remain there. Concerning the prince who substituted the son of Jeremiah’s birth for another: the solution is given above in the treatment of penance. Concerning tolls and transit taxes and road duties and road charges and similar \[illegible\] \[levied\] for making exactions and for \[illegible\] and for porterage under the yoke: we say that all those \[persons\] are condemned in \[such\] a judgment and cannot be saved in any state so long as they are resolved to make such exactions. Hence we say without distinction that prelates sin mortally who publicly swear and argue for \[these princes\] and who do not oppose them according to the distinction they are required to maintain, and who communicate with them in the sacraments of the Church, with those who appear incorrigible. We, however, exclude from this \[condemnation\] just tolls in their respective cases.
