The course of evangelical teaching must not be impeded — he ought to press on untiringly out of zeal for souls. Concerning the advocate, we say that since a just cause is to be defended, he ought not to abandon his justice, that is, the judgment, on account of scandal. And by way of example: if he is in need, it is lawful \[for him\] to accept a moderate salary for it. In reply to the objection in the third case, we say that the Apostle declares it more expedient for a preacher to die of hunger than to have his glory emptied by accepting support from subjects, if those subjects are thereby scandalized unto death. For it is better to die in body than to infect some in mind. Yet when dogs, pigs, and false prophets hear the preacher, and some others present are truly good, \[and the preacher is\] in extreme need, then no one \[illegible\] — no one \[illegible\] — he can afterward set forth the proper arguments to those who would scandalize \[him\] lest they scandalize \[him\]. If he has \[support\] from elsewhere, let him by no means accept anything. Likewise, concerning this matter it often happens that powerful persons of ill repute, whether through violence or through rapine, or through secret \[charges\] or through public accusation of notorious infamy — if anyone \[illegible\] dares to publish their power and bring them before the court in public accusation — and you as judge have often the sword of both parties drawn to bring such a powerful man before your tribunal; on account of his great infamy you have cast him into prison until he purges himself or until he is purged by someone else. What will you do in this situation concerning such a person? Will you release him under a double bond? For you knew by law that he is a heretic, or such that if you release him the whole region will be scandalized by him, \[people\] believing you to have freed him out of mercy; and if you release him you scandalize all, since you are able to release him — and likewise you cannot condemn him, since he has been neither refuted nor confessed in law.
