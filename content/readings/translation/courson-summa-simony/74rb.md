If they still object: But consider — \[someone\] holds a large bishopric; let him divide it in two halves so that two bishops may be made from it. Likewise it is lawful to divide an entire bishopric; by the same reasoning it is lawful to divide a half; because similarly, if someone possesses a large prebend, it is lawful to have two prebends constructed from it. By the same reasoning it is lawful to divide two prebends out of two dignities.

### Solution

Just as no man should be abbot of different monasteries, so no one ought to be bishop of different cities; and \[while\] remaining in kingdoms or provinces, bishops are permitted to derive \[jurisdiction\] from the one \[see\] and not otherwise by right. But different arrangements are made unlawfully, since the purpose is to divide them so that more can be possessed by more people.
