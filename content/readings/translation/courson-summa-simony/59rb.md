So that clerics, in place of tithes, are bound to distribute to the poor and to the church whatever remains after providing for themselves and their households, since if they retain more than what is necessary, the words of Hieronymus hold: they are thieves. Hence whoever attempts to retain what is necessary for themselves while taking from others communicates with robbers. Already gathered: when we distribute goods to the poor, in \[what would otherwise be\] such losses we return their own goods, \[showing\] mercy in judging; for those of us who distribute goods to the poor, \[we act\] in justice. Hence in the Psalm: “He has distributed, he has given to the poor; his justice endures.” And since by right we clerics are bound to give to the poor all that exceeds what is necessary for ourselves and our households. Hence in the Gospel: “What remains, give as alms, and behold all things are clean for you.” Likewise \[it\] says in \[dist.\]

q. i, \[c.\] “Decimas.” You inquire whether clerics should pay the remainder of their goods to the lord pope as the Levites paid the tithe of tithes to the high priest. It replies: that it is indeed permitted for us to give such \[offerings\] to the intentions of the high priest. But what shall we say of those clerics who, enriched with patrimonial goods, yet holding prebends in churches, devour the goods of the poor? On this matter Hieronymus, xvi, q. i, last chapter, speaks thus: “But it is fitting for those clerics to be supported by the stipends of the church who are not aided by any adequate family inheritance or resources. Those, however, who can be supported by ample family means, if they accept anything from the poor, undoubtedly deserve judgment as sacrilege, for they eat and drink the judgment of such a manner of \[unworthy\] reception.” Likewise, the Lord had ordained \[for the Levites\]: “\[You shall\] not possess \[land\], nor shall you have a portion among them. I am your portion and inheritance.” And further: “I have given all the tithes of the children of those \[who dwell there\] as a possession, and to those serving in the tabernacle bearing the sins of my people — others shall possess the tithes.”
