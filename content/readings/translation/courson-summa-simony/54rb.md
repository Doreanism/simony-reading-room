Again, since usurers commonly offer gifts to churches — building them entirely or in part from usury or \[accumulated\] treasures, or \[constructing\] houses for nuns or hermitages or hospitals or leper-houses — the question is what should be done: should these be torn down? A prelate who understands that such structures were built with the proceeds of usury should understand that these temples are like temples of Baal. Those who fear the unclean contagion of such tabernacles of sinners — \[knowing\] that they were built in whole or in part from plunder, and that no one has ever had the right to consume such plunder — may tear them down, scatter their \[stones\], and consecrate \[the site\] anew, or \[those dwelling there\] may seek alternative lodging if they have another place to live, or they may move.

### Solution

It does not seem to us that so great and universal a purge of the church could be accomplished except in a general council, convened by the motion of the bishops and promulgated under the supreme pope, with the church and the prince simultaneously admonishing all under pain of excommunication and condemnation that whoever labored at this — whether in body or in spirit — would be eating their own bread, as the Apostle says one must do without living off the labor of others; and so that none would be idle among us if all usurers and seditious robbers were removed, and thus alms and church building could proceed, and all things be restored to their original state. But in the meantime \[the prelate\] should simulate and administer. \[illegible\] What shall an impious prelate do? We say that the churches are either secure, or if they have been immediately given back by those who despoiled them \[they are cleansed\].
