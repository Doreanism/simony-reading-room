It often happens that one monk retains several prebends and an abbacy with many prebends to which are attached parishes and tithes; by the same reasoning, this is likewise permissible and not absurd for a simple cleric. Likewise, the canon says that to dispute about the seat of the supreme pontiff is like the act of a sacrilegious man and a soothsayer, and that it is the sin of idolatry to refuse to yield to him. For he and his see are nonetheless over the lowly, and he retains \[jurisdiction\] and with his own hand is accustomed to rule the world and all its fullness — because he has determined the matter of prebends and churches, and he confirms \[them\] for many, and his institution is ecclesiastical instruction. No one, indeed, shall say that the curia acts without reason, since it seems today to suffice for nearly all; and if the lord Pope should declare that something is done in accordance with reason, that approves it. Likewise, the Apostle: “Render to all their due: to whom honor is owed, honor; to whom tribute, tribute” \[Romans 13:7\], and elsewhere: “Anticipate one another in showing honor” \[Romans 12:10\], and those who are of greater utility to the defense of the Church’s affairs and necessities are to receive more. Just as one who is of noble stature and most useful to the Church \[receives more\] when one church cannot suffice, \[while\] others of less consequence receive little enough even as an abbot in a cloister furnishes richer meals to him who labors more and eats more than to him who labors little and eats little — so the Church ought to bestow more on such a man of noble standing.
