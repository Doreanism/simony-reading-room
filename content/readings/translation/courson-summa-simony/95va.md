The more holy the thing by which one swears, the more penal is \[any\] perjury. Nothing is holier than God, and perjury committed against God is far more severely punished than what is done against any other holy thing. For in him all treasures are hidden. And an oath made by God binds more than if made by any other holy thing, because God alone is he who bears witness to others. Solution. Every oath is to be understood as the one who swears understands it, as is stated at dist. xxii, q. v, chapter “Ad cumque.” Furthermore, an oath is to be judged more or less holy in accordance with the intention of those receiving the oath, having regard also to the custom of the kingdom. Hence an oath made in France upon the body of the blessed Dionysius binds more than an oath tendered among strangers who know nothing of St. Dionysius. From this it appears that whoever swears upon everything is the more guilty; yet the religious weight of an oath is judged according to the understanding and intention of the one for whom the oath is made, and according to the custom of the kingdom, which \[those present\] understand to be more binding upon the swearer.

## \[¶\]

Even if someone swears thus: “I swear I will give you a wax candle or a hundred denarii” — the question is what he is bound to; it seems he is bound to give a hundred, because he is so bound; and yet he is bound to reduce it and is also bound to give a hundred. Similarly the objection is raised: if I swear that I will render \[something\] and give a wax candle or \[show\] mercy — also the authority says that what we call the “sworn thing” among us we owe as a religious obligation; if by “I swear” we owe a religious debt, we owe the sworn thing — and it is awkward to distinguish between the religious obligation and the oath itself. Solution. In an oath containing a disjunction it is necessary to include some member that contains a \[possible\] unlawfulness; if that inclusion is not present, \[the listener\] should understand \[the meaning\] from the total expression, as though that \[unlawful\] member had been appended.
