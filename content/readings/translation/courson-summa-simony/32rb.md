## On the return of sins. I.

Next follow the question of the return of sins \[de reditu peccatorum\]. Let us treat it, since without it the penitential forum cannot be safeguarded. \[Chapter\] f., on the return of sins. Therefore, since it is far more miserable \[to fall\] into sin after marriage \[matrimonium\] has been contracted \[i.e., after having been joined in spiritual union through penance\], all \[sins\] that had been remitted return in full. That all previously remitted sins return on the occasion of one \[sin\] is proved by several authorities and examples. First there appears that Gospel authority in which the father of the household remitted the debt of eleven to a servant as a gift; and when the servant, of his own accord, refused to remit a debt, the lord \[therefore\] held him in prison until he should repay the full debt — “each one the peril of his own \[debt\]” up to the very last farthing \[quadrantem\]: by the same reasoning in any other case, all \[sins\] return as one against \[the sinner\]. Likewise, on that passage: “So shall also my heavenly Father do unto you,” the gloss says: “remitted things shall be demanded back.” And another gloss in the same place says: “beware of things committed lest things remitted return.” Likewise, on that passage of the psalm “let the iniquity of his father be remembered,” it is said that that sin which Judas committed is reckoned from his first sin, of which he had nevertheless been absolved in the soul by faith — because the authority says that even original sins return together with sins \[renewed\] to their summit, and far more \[so with sins\] renewed. Likewise, the canonical epistle of James: “offending in one thing he is guilty of all.” But here one asks: is one who offends against one \[commandment\] condemned as guilty of all? Likewise on that Gospel passage: “he returns with seven spirits more wicked than himself.” See Beda, where, as if nothing of pardon remains of the punishment, yet for him who relapses at the same time — nothing of full pardon remains of the punishment.
