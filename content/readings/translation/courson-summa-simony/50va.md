Likewise, as the precentor is accustomed to put forward, a threefold excuse may arise in certain cases: piety, \[the bond of\] matrimony, if one is a palace official, and if it is a matter of primary scandal. For instance, if he so commands them that we should all follow and yet cannot endure any serious fault. Similarly, fools who rely too much on flatterers should despise \[such\] judgment. And it is to be tolerated; hence Hieronymus: “A pilgrim of delicacy.” Now if the scandal of adultery arises among those who live first and foremost for outward splendor, these move the flatterers to be first. For three men set in private are to be assigned according to Augustinus, who says: “I will tend my light; follow the common custom.” Among these, however, we cannot determine where to fix boundaries firmly, because if excess arises in wantonness in such cases, or in their desire for praise immediately at the occasion \[of flattery\], as one may say of Gregorius — for whoever is imbued and enticed solely by it, just as he is above and bound to the world as he was, still remains. The middle path in meriting \[reward\] is as Hieronymus attests, saying: “Neither seek the strong nor pursue delicacies — little praise.” Moreover, today \[the Church\] judges the sign of the coal as what \[must be\] tolerated, and the sign of a Christian.

## On the following \[matters\] regarding stipendiary soldiers

Proceeding to the following matters, we speak of stipendiary soldiers, of hired crossbowmen, and of dagger-men, \[saying\] that they ought always to keep before their eyes the commandment that in every instance is to be preferred, because one must obey God rather than men. If one perpetrates \[something\] and counsel for another course is available to adhere to, one should adhere to it; for to be unwilling to obey is a kind of idolatry, as Gregorius rightly \[says\], as is expressly held. Furthermore, to the argument made from the book of Kings, because he did not obey Saul’s command before \[the appointed time\] — their fathers were moved to act upon it and the response stands for the whole day.
