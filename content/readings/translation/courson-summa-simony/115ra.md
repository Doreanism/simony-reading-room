## III.

Moreover, infamy is multiplied, as you have it at vi.q.i., *infames*. In sum, the apostate, every heretic, every one who corrupts the scriptures or the canons or \[illegible\], every wicked person, every \[illegible\] depraved person, robber, evildoer, poisoner, adulterer, every fugitive who has been returned or a thief, the ambitious, every one who seizes the goods of the church, every calumniator, all violent impeachers who maliciously attack evidence, all \[illegible\] notorious persons, all bigamists and those holding curial offices, all who are stained in body, all who lack sound mind or understanding, all who are \[illegible\] of their own words, all the insane, all who exact usury, and every woman who within the prescribed time after a first marriage remarries — whoever \[illegible\]. Whoever commits a mortal sin \[illegible\] if it has been publicly known, asserted, or proven by law \[incurs the legal penalty of\] infamy \[illegible\]. As Stephen himself shows in the said chapter, these matters being in accord with those said above. And all these persons, whether freely or fully, are barred from sacred orders and from the right to accuse and bear witness — they are to be repelled \[from such acts\], except those whom we excuse therein, concerning whom you have at vi.q.i. under that \[heading\] \[illegible\] *ennem sanctorum*. ## IIII.

After these matters, let it be inquired whether someone is condemned on infamy alone without any accusation. It seems \[he is\], from the example of \[the Gospel of\] John, because \[there\] one exposed to attack from public report \[is condemned\], and thus often if \[he sleeps in\] or \[the condemnation\] is delivered as a health-giving warning in various ways. Likewise, Augustinus says in \[his work\] *De doctrina* \[illegible\] that there the evidence of a committed crime lacks the need for a formal cry of accusation, and Ambrosius says that manifest \[crimes\] have no need of accusation. And in \[illegible\], in the same q.v., \[illegible\] says that where those defamed by their own people are ordained, the judge ought often to demand canonical purgation from them, so that they purge \[themselves of\] \[illegible\] their office. But if they cannot purge themselves and \[prove\] they were required to undergo purgation, they are to be condemned as convicted. And so \[illegible\] the same confirmed.
