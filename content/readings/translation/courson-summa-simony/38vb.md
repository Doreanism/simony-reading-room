If some place is new, where there are neither brethren, nor a chapter, nor established custom, any number of people may each contribute his own portion and endow a prebend for those entering from his own goods, without any special obligation, since neither any spiritual life nor any strictness has yet been established there. If, however, a community has been established in which there is a prelate to whom obedience is owed and the subjects are bound by common obedience, then if anyone endows a prebend for himself from his patrimony, or from tithes, or from elsewhere, he commits simony in so doing. In the church of Reims a young nobleman was turned away who offered revenues of three marks from his inheritance in order to be promoted to \[clerical\] orders. Further, the question is asked whether a simoniacally received canon ought to be called a canon. It seems not, because he does not hold a prebend but merely thrusts himself upon it as a despoiler. For he does not hold by right either a stall in choir or a voice in chapter, since those who come after him bear witness — and the canon attests — that every simoniac is a heretic; and if he is a heretic, he is expelled from the church and excluded from all the church’s \[privileges\] and from the canonical body of the church. We grant this, saying that he is not a canon by right but only in name, and therefore he can be stripped of that prebend and of the fruits which he has wrongfully occupied, once it is established that he is such. A thief cannot give anything from stolen goods, nor a robber from plunder, nor an extortioner from what he has extorted; by the same reasoning a simoniacal prelate cannot give anything from simoniacally acquired \[property\]. And one who dispenses anything from the prebends of a bishopric which he usurps through simony — to give a prebend from such a source is just as if a robber were to give from his plunder or a usurer from his usury. Thus one who gives on behalf of a robber is not bound \[to keep what he receives\], nor is a usurer; and the canon \[installed\] by the hand of a simoniac is likewise no canon but merely one corrupted \[by simony\].
