For Augustinus says at \[dist.\] lxxxv, chapter \[illegible\]: the Apostle and the canons that follow \[declare\] that the following alone stand as irregularities that impede one being promoted: bigamy, infamy, bodily mutilation, spurious birth, and all those moral \[offenses\] that were punishable by death in the Old Testament’s rightful memory — as \[in the case of\] one who did not observe \[the law\] against gathering sticks on the Sabbath, or \[the law\] that those who with the iron of justice punished with death \[whoever committed such acts\] were not made an impediment to promotion. Yet the moral \[offense\] of the one who gathered sticks on the Sabbath — if he did this not out of contumacy, \[in order\] to create a scandal in the synagogue, and was stoned by God on account \[of this reason\] — \[is different\]: if anyone commits an act in manifest contempt of the divine law or the Roman law, despising delegated judges, or to the scandal of the Church, this is an impediment to promotion and one so \[offending\] is to be barred from promotion.

Concerning the rest we speak with instruction. For just as \[certain women\] enter \[into marriage\] — yet \[women’s husbands\] ought to abstain \[from conjugal relations\] since however they conceive, even on forbidden days. But if \[the wife\] at the \[proper\] time publishes \[this\], Hieronimus says in his \[commentary on\] Ezekiel that then neither the wife should conceal this, nor \[should her husband lie\] with her, lest there follow the danger that commonly arises. For the birth of \[illegible\] \[illegible\] elephants is wont to occur, and \[the woman’s body\] is monstrous \[illegible\]; a woman can rightly deny him this on account of the imminent danger. \[A man\] who can receive no one and wishes to receive no one in hospitality out of avarice is judged inhospitable. And such an omission is a mortal sin. Wherefore clerics who abound in temporal goods ought greatly to fear those who serve bulls’ flesh to the poor, and close \[their doors\] — because this pertains by right to \[illegible\] material sufficiency — and one who has not yet been \[found guilty of\] such an impediment to promotion would be promoted to major orders \[despite it\], \[as\] the saying goes: “Feed the one dying of hunger, and.”
