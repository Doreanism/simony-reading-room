## After These Matters, the Question Arises Whether an Excommunicated Person Can Sue Another in a Legal Action or Be Sued by Another

That he can sue another and bring an accusation is shown as follows: anyone can pursue a wrong done to himself or his own. As in “Causa I, question \[illegible\], Causa III, question \[illegible\], \[the chapter beginning\] Hapr,” against another decretal. \[By\] the \[decretal that begins\] “Accipe,” the excommunicated is \[said to be\] able to stand in judgment and \[take legal\] action. Yet certain \[cases\] are excepted, as they evidently are — \[namely charges\] of \[illegible\], heresy, and the crime of simony, in which anyone is admitted to bring an accusation even in the case of the most unworthy person. Gregorius says, “Causa II, question \[illegible\].” Solution: in many matters, at the end, the law of lèse-majesté, with all \[parties\], gives permission, which is here stated at “Causa XV, question III, the great decretal \[called\] tanta.” The simoniac keys — swiftly run to the imitation of souls \[seeking\] the testimony \[illegible\] — they are admitted to accuse in cases of lèse-majesté; anyone is admitted to \[bring\] an action for simony and to be heard in the forced \[extorted\] accusation, and \[in the decretal numbered\] 539 \[it is noted\]. For when it cites that saying, it says that no excommunicated person is to be heard in a case before he is first absolved; while the excommunication persists he can maintain nothing. Yet there remains the additional observation that it is customary to observe in the decretal that before one enters into it everyone must swear an oath against calumny, because there one rightly owes the Gospel, or the relics of the saints, for taking the oath. Whoever does this gives holy things to dogs — that is, to the excommunicated — which is against the Gospel precept: “Do not give holy things to dogs.” Therefore this is to be directed and we recommend \[this approach\]: if it is established that such a person is such, he cannot enter into it and cannot shelter behind such \[protection\].
