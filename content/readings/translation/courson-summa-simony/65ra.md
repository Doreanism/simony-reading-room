Toll-collectors are bound to restitution. The duke or prince who issued an edict that if anyone should set down a drove of animals \[illegible\], and \[it\] is not \[recovered\] or \[illegible\] broken, so that \[the owner\] is required to pay the lord sixty shillings — and yet the lord is more closely bound to come to their aid — such a prince is bound to the restitution of every such exaction. Likewise those who seize from merchants so much money for wine or more, along with \[illegible\] or for similar \[illegible\] pretexts, are bound to restitution in every case where they \[ought to have provided an\] escort to those passing through. Solution.
