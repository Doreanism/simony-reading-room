## Finally let us ask

what the people, if harassed in many ways by such exactions on account of the keys, ought to do: they should have recourse to the superior, such as the metropolitan or the legate of the lord pope, or his representative, or \[seek\] him himself or a proctor, to induce them to restrain the unbearable exactions of their prelates. So that if in Augustinus’s time such things were found to be happening, \[we find\] one who could benefit us too. But if at the level of superiors \[the harassed people\] find any defense at all, a further distinction must be made.

For either the said \[exacting\] superior is compelled \[by them\] to give the sacraments without which there is no salvation of the soul — namely the sacrament of baptism — and to avoid the peril of money; nor should the bridegroom be deprived of his bride whom he has contracted in public betrothal, \[if\] they request to confess and do penance, as requested according to Augustinus and, we believe, the Father — let him who receives baptism render what is owed to his spouse as requested; but if he were to do this for money, neither by granting matrimony nor by extreme unction, nor by the other sacraments without which there is \[still\] salvation \[should he act thus\]. If moreover, these having been dismissed, \[the superior\] excommunicates, let \[the people\] have recourse to the \[next\] superior and endure all things in the remedy of penance and the highest lamentation with compassion.
