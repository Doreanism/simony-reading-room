It also frequently happens that between two good bishops there intervenes one simoniac, or conversely between two simoniacs there appears one good bishop, so that these two seem to have given different orders to a single person. In these ways and in others too tedious to enumerate, such a bewildering confusion is stirred up from every direction day by day, that unless the divine mercy intervenes through the most blessed Pope Leo, who now holds that office, a destructive error will spread lethally through the Church of Christ. Add to this the fact that if it is once admitted that a sacrament given through such persons is void, then all the basilicas that have been dedicated up to this point, together with their altars, must be torn down. And finally — what is supremely impious — hope is taken away from the faithful who have died, so that it would have to be believed that the past age perished utterly without any remedy of human redemption. For by what reasoning are we to believe that those who, as is claimed, were never permitted to receive the sacraments of the Christian faith now reign with Christ — especially when Truth itself declares: “Unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink his blood, you will not have life in you” \[John 6:53\]? For if those priests were not priests at all, and therefore could neither offer nor administer the sacraments of the Lord’s body, how are we to believe that their followers died into the life they never possessed while living? And if we grant this, we must also hold that they died without penance — namely all those who were weighed down by grave offenses. Indeed, since lepers were of old commanded to show themselves to the priests, and the penitent must now by canonical decree pass under the bishop’s hand, how could anyone have been bound or loosed through priestly office where priests themselves could nowhere be found? But why do we lament that they were deprived of the remedy of penance, when on the logic of this perverse argument they appear never to have been Christian or even baptized? For by the authority of the sacred canons, baptism is decreed valid even when administered by any faithful layman. But how could simoniacs or those ordained by them have conferred baptism, they who, as these critics charge, had no Holy Spirit? For they themselves appear not to have been Christians, if they lacked the spirit of Christ — since, according to the judgment of Paul, “he who does not have the spirit of Christ does not belong to him” \[Romans 8:9\]. And if they were not Christians, they could not give Christianity. This is indeed the reason, as stated above, why those baptized by the Paulianists must necessarily be baptized again: because, since it is established that they have no Holy Spirit, they are altogether incapable of baptizing. Thus by the pernicious inventions of such disputants, all those among our predecessors who appeared to be Catholics have been condemned to the same sentence as Arians and all other heretics. This conclusion is so manifestly absurd and so savagely hostile to orthodox faith that it not only destroys the mysteries of the age past but altogether tears up the very foundations of the present and future Church. This can easily be shown as follows. Since the Roman see — which is assuredly the mother of all churches — lay subject to venality, it spread the poison of the simoniac plague through the branches of all the other churches as well, like an infected root. For it was at the example of that see that the coinage of artisans began to be minted in the surrounding churches everywhere. Yet those men, whether they were good or evil, were of necessity bound to pour out into others the sacraments of ordination, so that these might flow down to us as well through the succession of those who came after. But it is said that the ordainers at that time did not possess what they were transmitting, and that the ordained could receive nothing from those who had nothing. Suppose, then, that in the present time nothing is given in exchange for the grace of consecration — but what does it avail, since we ourselves are promoted by those who were ordained by simoniacs, or by their successors however far removed?
