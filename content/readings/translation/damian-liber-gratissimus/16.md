This little work of Peter’s, called the *Liber Gratissimus* \[“The Most Welcome Book”\] “because it is composed on behalf of those who were ordained gratis by simoniacs,” was written approximately in the summer of 1052, while Pope Leo IX was visiting the city of Padua. Against Humbert’s position, Damian endeavored in this booklet to demonstrate with many testimonies that those ordained gratis by simoniacs ought neither to be removed from their offices nor to be ordained again. Beyond papal decrees and records, he cites as witnesses Eusebius, Rufinus, Jerome, Augustine, Cassiodorus, Gregory the Great, and Paschasius.

The first recension of the work, composed in 1052, we have restored from two manuscripts. These are:

A1) Vatican Codex no. 5075, parchment, late eleventh century, quarto, written out neatly on fifty-six folios, though it contains certain errors, many of which the scribe corrected; occasionally, however, he altered some passages on his own initiative. The ending is missing, the final folio having been lost. This manuscript was transcribed by the late G. Waitz.

A2) Codex Audomarensis \[Saint-Omer\] no. 228, parchment, late twelfth century, small folio, formerly belonging to the monastery of Saint Bertin, contains several opuscula of Peter Damian, among them the *Liber Gratissimus*, which our colleague O. Holder-Egger collated.

After Pope Nicholas II, at the Roman synod held in April 1060, had decreed “that those who up to that time had been consecrated gratis by simoniacs should remain in the orders they had received, but that henceforth, if anyone should permit himself to be consecrated by one whom he does not doubt to be a simoniac, both the consecrator and the consecrated shall each be deposed, do penance, and be permanently deprived of their proper dignity,” Peter Damian revised the booklet he had written eight years earlier, made minor changes, omitted several chapters (cc. 16–18, 29–31), and added some material pertaining to that Roman synod of 1060 (c. 41). This revised form of the booklet is presented by:

B) Codex Cassinensis \[Monte Cassino\] no. 338, parchment, twelfth century, large folio, written in two columns; its chapters 1–12 were collated by the late G. Waitz, cc. 12–31 (the middle portion) by the distinguished Fr. Cauer, and from the middle of c. 31 to the end by the distinguished Nicolaus Müller.
