And indeed, who does not know that the priest Eli was a reprobate, condemned by the sentence of divine judgment both for his own laxity and for the unbridled license of his children? Yet the womb of Hannah was not barren under his blessing, when he invoked fruitfulness for her. For Scripture says: “Eli blessed Elkanah and his wife and said: ‘May the Lord give you offspring by this woman for the gift which you have lent to the Lord’” \[1 Samuel 2:20\]; and immediately adds: “So the Lord visited Hannah, and she conceived and bore three sons and two daughters.” This blessing was made efficacious not by the merit of the man but by the office of the priest; and what the cause of the minister did not possess, the virtue of the ministry supplied.

## XIX. That sinful beginnings often come to blessed ends.

What harm, then, does a simoniac do if he lays hands upon those to be ordained, when he can neither take away what is already held nor prevent what is divinely increased? For that gift of heavenly grace is brought forth not from the purse of one who strives from without, but from the treasury of one who presides invisibly. For even if the one who sows is a thief, the earth nonetheless, under God its author, supplies the power to germinate the grain. Even if the hand that plants is dirty or leprous, the fruit is certainly plucked clean when it has come to full ripeness. Often a beautiful child is born of an adulterous union, and from the shameful pleasure of parents a comely appearance arises in the children. Some of Jacob’s sons he fathered by his lawful wives, others he received from the servile condition of concubines. Yet when both come to take possession of the inheritance, they are divided among themselves by an equal right of distributing the allotted portions. Perez is born of Tamar, who was exposed at the crossroads, yet he is found to be not the least among the princes of the clans of Israel. Solomon is known to have had his beginning through her who was Uriah’s wife, yet he obtained without compare the sovereignty of the royal scepter. Obed traced his origin from Ruth the Moabite, yet he merited to have as his grandson the distinguished king David. Rahab the harlot bore Boaz, yet Boaz merited to be numbered in the royal catalogue of the Lord’s genealogy. And why mention others, when even the Mediator between God and men himself drew his origin from sinners and from a leavened mass received the unleavened bread of his purity without any infection of the old leaven? Indeed, to speak more precisely: from the very flesh of the Virgin, which was conceived in sin, flesh came forth without sin, which moreover freely blotted out the sins of the flesh. What wonder is it, then, if the mighty hand of the Lord — effective in whatever it wills — accomplishes in the Spirit who gives life that which it is found to accomplish even in the flesh which profits nothing? For he who brings it about that sinful beginnings come to happy endings even in earthly matters — how can it be believed that he does not do the same, and with all the more providential care, in spiritual sacraments? Surely he who commands the fruits of trees not to bear the taint of a corrupt planter — why should he be thought to refuse likewise to command that all guilt of the wicked consecrator be wholly absent from those who are consecrated?
