But if they are entangled in the errors of perfidy together with the Arians or Paulianists, then just as those emerging from the latter are by no means permitted to be reconsecrated — as we have just learned a little earlier from Pope Innocent’s categorical prohibition — so too those who have been promoted by simoniacs can by no reason be consecrated again. It remains, therefore, that whether they believe rightly or do not believe rightly, they ought by no means to be consecrated again. It is necessary, then, according to what has been said, that those ordained by simoniacs either be declared to believe in catholic fashion and ought to remain in their offices, or be clearly convicted of subjection to perfidy and thus lose what they have received in such a way that they ought not to be consecrated thereafter. But who does not know that they not only persist in the solidity of orthodox faith, but that many of them, as truly catholic men in every respect, flourish additionally in the pursuit of holy conduct and the dignity of all the virtues? It follows, therefore, that they may rightfully remain in the honors they have received, in which holy conduct indeed accords with integrity of faith. Let the blasphemous audacity of rashness be cast aside, let the unlawful presumption be curbed, and let those who argue that the rebaptized strip off Christ also execrate the reconsecrated for denying the Holy Spirit with equal impiety.

## XXXIII. That just as no one can be rebaptized, so neither can anyone be consecrated anew.

Come then, let those who contend that persons consecrated in any manner whatsoever can receive a second laying on of hands not rest content with their own views, but take care to confirm it with testimonies of canonical authority. Let them say who among the holy fathers has dared to assert this, or who has even taught that it ought to be dared. And when authentic writings of the ancients fail them as evidence of proof, let them confess themselves to be the necessary authors of a new doctrine. For since it is found nowhere prescribed by the sanction of teachers, it is found moreover to be expressly prohibited. For the most blessed Pope Gregory declared to John, bishop of the church of Ravenna, that just as no one is to be rebaptized, so neither can anyone be consecrated again for the same purpose, in these words: But as for what you say — that one who has been ordained should be ordained again — this is quite ridiculous and appears foreign to the judgment of your intelligence, unless perhaps that example is brought forward in which the man who is said to have done such a thing was himself judged. But may your fraternity be far from such thinking. For just as one who has been baptized once ought not to be baptized again, so one who has been consecrated once in the same order cannot validly be consecrated again.

By these words it is clearly shown that, just as in rebaptism Christ is stripped off, so too in reconsecration the grace of the Holy Spirit is evacuated from one who has been improperly advanced.
