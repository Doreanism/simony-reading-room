in whom not only your evil deeds are evil, but even your good deeds have been turned to evil for you — in whom blessing has been turned into curse, the gifts of grace into heresy, sacraments into sacrilege, honor into shame, and advancement into ruin. Add to this that you are accountable to render an account for as many souls as you are now seen to be set over as rulers of the faithful. Overflow now with riches; crowd together, swelling with the press of your attendants — it is near at hand that you will see all the armies of angels fiercely wrathful against you, the apostles, the martyrs, and the hosts of all the saints rising up against you in terror and joining together in unanimous and equal judgment upon the sentence of your condemnation. For you have offended him, and therefore you have consequently made an enemy of one whose offense is remitted neither here nor hereafter. Furthermore, in the enormity of your bloody and most wicked crime, you surpass adulterers, exceed murderers, outstrip plunderers, sacrilegists, the incestuous, parricides, and the offenses of almost all criminals. And this is not yet enough: for if the matter is weighed as it deserves, Jewish perfidy itself and every heretical wickedness are not equal to your transgressions. Night has come upon you at midday, storm in fair weather; heaven has become iron and earth bronze for you \[Deuteronomy 28:23\], because you neither rise up to heavenly things under the weight of your wickedness nor conceal among men what has been committed, as conscience bears witness. Indeed if even then, as the authority of the fathers attests, blessing is turned into a curse for you when you are seen to be blessed by the mouth of a priest — what will happen to you when the very divine voice calls you accursed and repudiates you, when the fury of the judge casts you away from his sight and delivers you to torturers? Then assuredly you will learn at what cost the bitter purchase of transient honor comes, when it will now be necessary that the boundless gaping pit of Tartarus swallow you up and the cruel cauldron of Gehenna enclose you. Then the crackling and hissing flame will feed upon your bones richly marbled with fat; then the devouring fire, like a furnace belching out, will not cease to belch its fumes upon you through your mouths, your eyes, your ears, and your nostrils. So, so it will then be fitting that you share a portion with that leader of yours, the heresiarch Simon; and let Hell, purchased by the devil for money, satisfy those for whom Paradise freely promised by Christ was not enough. Though I also judge you not incongruously to be more damnable than the very author of your error and the president of the pestilential see — so that you may fittingly precede in punishment one whom you wickedly surpassed in guilt. For he wished only to buy a heavenly gift, but did not attain the fulfillment of his blazing desire; but you both wished and accomplished, you drew near and crept in. The structure was built, the coin was produced. He finally, while pretending to be God, is shown to be a dog; while elevated, he is overwhelmed; while seeming to scale heaven, he plunges into the pit. Likewise you, while seeking high things, fall down; while you are not content to appear equal to other men, you are despised by divine and human judgment alike and hasten to the likeness of unclean dogs. But we are wasting effort with trifles if we attempt by rebuking to restore dry bones to life, as it were by prophesying. The only recourse is prayer: that, laying aside their honor, they may be reclothed in flesh and sinews, and so come back to life again through the breath of the Holy Spirit.
