## XII. That the gift of God is not polluted by any contamination of its ministers.

For if the ray of this visible sun suffers no darkness from any murky pit, is stained by no filth of sewers, what wonder is it if the highest and uncircumscribed Spirit illumines with His splendor the dark and sordid hearts of certain men, while He Himself nonetheless remains in His own cleanness and purity? Therefore, however great the guilt of the one who consecrates — be he proud, or lustful, or a murderer, or even a simoniac — he himself is indeed polluted and without doubt suffused with a deadly leprosy; but the gift of God which passes through him is defiled by the corruption of none, fouled by the contagion of none. For what flows through him is pure; it passes clean and clear to the fertile earth. For holy Church is a garden of delights and a spiritual paradise, watered by the streams of heavenly graces. Let us suppose, then, that wicked priests are in some manner channels of stone: in stone channels water germinates nothing, until flowing through them it pours itself out into the fruitful garden beds. For even though the succession of times may have brought forth many reprobate priests in sequence — so that both those who consecrate and those who are consecrated are found equally unworthy — still that living fountain is not constrained from flowing through the grove of the Church until the end of the age, so that not the priestly order alone, but all who are reborn in Christ, may draw the cup of their salvation. For through priests baptism and chrism are administered, and the entire ministry of the ecclesiastical sacraments is performed.

If therefore the wickedness of the priests could hold back the gifts of God, then surely, with them transgressing, it would be necessary for the entire human race to be cut off from divine benefits. But if, with them standing firm, men receive heavenly gifts, yet with them falling they could in no way obtain them, then men would necessarily ascribe the cause and the whole power of their salvation not to God but to the priests.
