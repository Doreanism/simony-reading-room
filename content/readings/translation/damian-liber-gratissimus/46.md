This assuredly comes about not at all through the merit of the one consecrating, but rather through the word of the living and abiding God. For by the word of God the Father it was said: “Let the earth bring forth green vegetation bearing seed, and fruit-bearing trees producing fruit according to their kind” \[Genesis 1:11\]. And behold, this word remains inviolate to this day, so that the yield of fruits in no way imitates the variety of the farmers, but according to the proper kind of each plant, its proper seed is found in its grasses and its proper seed in its fruits. This seed is committed again to the earth, and afterward it is produced not as something different but according to the form of its own kind. If, then, by the power of the divine word, the earth returns what it has received in the same likeness: what wonder is it if that priestly blessing, which is cast like a seed into the soul of the one approaching, works the priesthood through the breath of the Holy Spirit invisibly? And therefore the image of the one who sows ought no longer fittingly be sought in the fruit, but only, if I may so say, the likeness of the seed should come forth? For God said to the animals: “Be fruitful and multiply” \[Genesis 1:22\]. By the efficacy of this word all living creatures are still created to this day — not as something new in the primary sense, but propagated from the same original seed by succession. Since, then, those words which were once uttered remain so immovably in force, what ought one to think of the words of priestly blessings, which are wholly sacred and divine, and which are therefore always repeated externally in the promotion of candidates, so that they may work the mystery internally? For when it is asserted that the words of enchanters possess such power that, cast like stones, they break a serpent when struck: what wonder is it if the priestly blessing contains within itself the sacrament of divine power, whereby the priest who is presented is made what he is? Surely the words of enchanters are not of greater power than those words that are altogether efficacious, mystical, and hallowed by the invocation of the divine name?
