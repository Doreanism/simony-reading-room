You have but newly received the priesthood by God’s appointment, and I believed that no gift would be more fittingly offered to you first of all than one composed on behalf of priests. Regarding those who have been consecrated gratis by simoniacs, therefore — how great a controversy has raged for three years now across three Roman councils, and how tangled and confused the uncertainty continues to be debated daily in these regions — I do not suppose that your holiness is unaware, especially since, as the ambiguity of the dispute has grown, matters have proceeded to the point that certain bishops are known to have consecrated anew clerics ordained by those men. On this account a great many brothers press upon me with a kind of presumptuous urgency of charity, and compel me — to use such a word — with importunate entreaty not to be reluctant to set down something concise in writing to address this point of such urgent necessity, and to explain what seems to me the right judgment on this question. I put this off for a long time, hoping, that is, that I would first receive permission from the most blessed bishop of the apostolic see. For he was said to be passing through this region shortly. I had thus determined that it was the proper course to treat any ecclesiastical matter whatsoever only if the authority to treat it were conferred on me by the very prince of the Church. But when it came back to my mind that the venerable pope at the most recent synod had already besought all the bishops under a divine oath that they should together implore God’s mercy to reveal to those who were wavering what ought to be decreed in this delicate matter, I judged that I would be entirely obedient to his command if I were able, by praying or by writing, to untie so difficult a knot for the Church — with heavenly grace shining forth. Therefore, trusting faithfully in him who opens the book and looses its seals \[cf. Revelation 5:2–5\], I shall set out on the path toward resolving this question; and since the abundance of words is not at my command, I shall at least attempt, in the effort of obedience enjoined upon me, to make my meaning known by gestures where I do not know how to speak.

### Here begin the chapter headings.

I. That Christ, though he distributes his gifts through many, yet retains in himself the fullness of all grace.

II. That the priest ministers outwardly, but God consecrates inwardly.

III. That just as there is one who baptizes, so there is one and the same who principally consecrates.

IV. That the Lord received both baptism and the rights of priesthood together.

V. That since no one dares to be rebaptized, there is no reason why one to be consecrated should be consecrated again.

VI. That an ordination, if it is catholic \[i.e., in conformity with the universal Church\], is also valid.

VII. That Balaam was a simoniac and yet did not lose the spirit of prophecy.

VIII. That the prophetic spirit came upon the reprobate Saul and his followers.

IX. What the faithful believe concerning baptism and the eucharist of the Lord’s body.

X. That the Holy Spirit is given not by the merit of the priests but through their ministry.
