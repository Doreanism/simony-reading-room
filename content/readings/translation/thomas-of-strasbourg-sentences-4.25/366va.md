Hence, granted that someone under age, say not yet having fourteen years, were ordained a priest, although both the presenter and the ordainer would sin gravely, nonetheless when that boy came to the age of discretion, he would not need to be reordained; but only after obtaining a dispensation for the offense committed regarding him, he could lawfully minister in the order of the priesthood.

To the fourth it must be said that this determination of time was not introduced by law on account of the necessity of the sacrament, because the sacrament is still conferred even if that requirement is not observed — since according to that determination, a priest to be ordained ought to be thirty years old, yet this is not commonly observed.

The second conclusion is that the ordinand must be of the male sex; this is required of the necessity of the sacrament. For that person, or that sex, to whom it has always been forbidden by apostolic ordinance to teach in the church, and who is commanded to have the head veiled in church when praying, is not capable of the sacrament of order; the female sex is of this kind; therefore it seems to be required of the necessity of the sacrament that the ordinand be of the male sex. The major is clear, because it belongs to one ordained to teach in the church, either in act — as in the case of a priest or deacon, since even to a deacon it belongs to preach the Gospel — or in capacity, if one is in the other orders below the order of the diaconate.

Furthermore, it belongs to no one ordained always to have the head veiled in church when praying, as is self-evident. The minor can also be proved from the words of the Apostle, because a woman ought to pray with veiled head, as the Apostle says in 1 Corinthians 11; nor ought she to teach, because the Apostle says in 1 Timothy 2: I do not permit a woman to teach in the church.

But the opposite of this conclusion was held by the sect of certain people called the Cataphrygians, who, to cloak their error, adduce authorities from both canons — that is, from law and from sacred scripture.

1. From law indeed, because in Decretum C.27 q.1 it is prescribed that a deaconess should not be ordained before she is forty years old; therefore the order of the diaconate can sometimes be conferred upon a woman. And in the same question it is prescribed that no one should seize or solicit a deaconess. Likewise, in dist. 32, a presbytera is instructed how she ought to govern her household; therefore it seems that the order of the presbyterate can be conferred, and that it has in fact sometimes been conferred upon, a woman.

2. Furthermore, if order were incompatible with the female sex, this would be above all because the power of judging could not belong to a woman; but this is no obstacle, because as is clear from Judges 4, Deborah judged the people of Israel and presided over them for many years; therefore according to sacred scripture order is not incompatible with a woman.

3. Furthermore, they confirm this by the following argument. With respect to the soul, there is no distinction between man and woman; but order does not regard a person with respect to the body, but rather with respect to the soul, since it is a kind of spiritual power; therefore with respect to the capacity to receive orders there seems to be no distinction between man and woman.

4. Furthermore, whenever there are several patients of the same species, then by the same reasoning that the same agent can produce a given effect in one of those patients, it can produce a like effect in another; but man and woman are of the same species; therefore by the same reasoning that a bishop can confer the sacrament of order upon a man, he can confer the same sacrament upon a woman.

### Against

But this opinion cannot stand, because Christ did not confer the sacrament of order upon his Mother, notwithstanding that she was the most noble and most holy creature; therefore he did not wish any woman to be capable of this sacrament.

The antecedent is known, because as is clear from what has been said above, Christ conferred the sacrament of order only \[upon men — continued below\].
