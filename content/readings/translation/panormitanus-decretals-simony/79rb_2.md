## Capitulum XXX.

### A person simoniacally elected is not harmed when money was given despite his prohibition.

Namely, in \[rubric\] 7, *c.*, whether \[one must\] relinquish the benefice obtained, at the end. Alfonsus can be promoted to the rectorship of a secular church, or \[the question of\] one excommunicated, and whether to inferior churches — treated by cross-reference. One elected as abbot must be confirmed by the bishop to whom the monastery is subject.

### One who pays money for another’s promotion also commits simony.

The chapter \[holds that\] during a vacancy the \[cathedral\] chapter has the power to confirm an elect, but not to confer benefices. On the reason for this distinction, see the cross-reference. If a prelate commands someone under pain of excommunication to do something, and \[the command\] has not been obeyed, no one thereby incurs excommunication, because this is rather a kind of admonition.

### Whether I can ratify that which was not done in my name — \[this is relevant\] to anyone.

The confirmation is not to be imputed to the elect if, despite his prohibition, money was given in connection with his confirmation. This is the summary, in three parts. The first sets out the consultation. In the second, the response, at \[the word\] *In*. In the third part a certain determination is set out, at \[the word\] *illos*. The literal case: when the monastery of Aulne was vacant, the monks elected one of their own — a prudent and discreet monk — as abbot. And because the church of Liège, which is a cathedral, was without a pastor, he went to the church of Cologne, which is its metropolitan, and with its permission he administered the monastery of Gembloux in the interim. Afterward a bishop was installed in the church of Liège. When the elect requested confirmation from him, the bishop was unwilling to confirm him. But when it was discovered that this was being done for money, \[the elect\] pronounced an excommunication that no money should be paid on account of this matter: whoever acted to the contrary would be excommunicated.
