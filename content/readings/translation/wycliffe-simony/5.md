— they receive a price in the Church for the laying on of hands, through which imposition the Holy Spirit is given from heaven. A dove is therefore sold when the gift of the Holy Spirit is offered for a price. But our Redeemer overturned the seats of those selling doves, because he destroys the priesthood of such traffickers. Hence it is that the sacred canons condemn the heresy of simony and even command that such persons be deprived of their priesthood. And because the saint knew that many sophisms are employed in this matter, he immediately adds: “That day will certainly come, and it is not far off, when the Shepherd of shepherds shall appear and bring the deeds of each one before the public; and he who now chastises the fault of subjects through their superiors will then, raging against the evils of the superiors, condemn them himself. Hence, entering the temple as if he had made a whip of cords, and casting out the wicked traffickers from the house of God, overturning the seats of those selling doves, he strikes the faults of subjects through their pastors, but strikes the vices of pastors himself. For that Judge is certainly coming before whom no one can hide himself by silence, and whom no one can deceive by denial.” In this passage the saint seems to say that, since by the doves the Church is signified, as in Song of Songs 5, or the gift of the Holy Spirit who appeared in the form of a dove, those who sell doves are those who sell churches or any spiritual gift. And according to Crisostomus and that saint, Christ as king, and other secular lords in the name of the supreme king, ought to destroy them; and therefore the decree of Pope Pelagius says they are to be suppressed by external powers — the Gloss says by laymen, who have power outside the clerical Church — as is clear from Distinctio 17, “Nec licuit,” and 23, questio 5, “Principes.” And here it is fully evident, as I have often said, that temporal lords may lawfully take away temporalities from a delinquent Church.
