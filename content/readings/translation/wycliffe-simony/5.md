And the same is set down in canon 1, questio 2, “Sunt nonnulli” \[*Decretum* C.1 q.2 c.7\]. The saint wishes to say that whenever anyone in a disordered way exchanges a spiritual gift of God for himself or another, tacitly or expressly, simony is committed — because this is contrary to the command of the Lord in Matthew 10: “Freely you have received, freely give.” Can the Truth be deceived in this matter because the contract, which in fact is illicit, is arranged through an intermediary? Secondly, from what is gathered from the fact itself, simony is so widespread as a heresy that there are few or no beneficed clergy who have not been promoted by some simoniacal means.

From this it appears that the greater part, and especially the leadership, of the Church is heretical — and this is no surprise, because as regards that matter, those who hold simoniacally obtained benefices more persistently, more extensively, or more lucratively are the more heretical. The truth of this conclusion is established from the final questio by the decree of Pope Pascalis, chapter “Patet”: “It is evident,” he says, “that simoniacs, as the first and foremost heretics, are to be rejected by all the faithful, and if after admonition they do not repent, they are to be suppressed even by external powers; for all other crimes are reckoned as almost nothing in comparison with the heresy of simony.” And Blessed Gregorius testifies the same in Homily 17 on the Gospels: “For to you,” he says, “priests, I speak with grief, because we have learned that certain of you perform ordinations for reward, selling spiritual grace, and heaping up temporal gains at the cost of sin from the iniquities of others. Why, therefore, does it not return to your memory what the divine voice says: ‘Freely you have received, freely give’? Why do you not call to the eyes of your mind the fact that when our Redeemer entered the temple, he overturned the seats of those selling doves and poured out the money of the money-changers? For who are they in the temple of God today who sell doves, but those who in the Church receive a price for the laying on of hands, through which imposition, as everyone knows, the Holy Spirit is given from heaven? A dove is therefore sold when the gift of the Holy Spirit is offered for a price. But our Redeemer overturned the seats of those selling doves, because he destroys the priesthood of such traffickers. Hence it is that the sacred canons condemn the heresy of simony and even command that such persons be deprived of their priesthood.” And because the saint knew that many sophisms are employed in this matter, he immediately adds: “That day will certainly come, and it is not far off, when the Shepherd of shepherds shall appear and bring the deeds of each one before the public; and he who now chastises the fault of subjects through their superiors will then, raging against the evils of the superiors, condemn them himself. Hence, entering the temple as if he had himself made a whip of cords and casting out the wicked traffickers from the house of God, overturning the seats of those selling doves, he strikes the faults of subjects through their pastors, but strikes the vices of pastors himself. For that Judge is certainly coming before whom no one can hide himself by silence, and whom no one can deceive by denial.” In this passage the saint seems to say that, since by the doves the Church is signified — as in Song of Songs 5 — or the gift of the Holy Spirit who appeared in the form of a dove, those who sell doves are those who sell churches or any spiritual gift. And according to Crisostomus and that saint, Christ as king, and other secular lords in the name of the supreme king, ought to destroy them; and therefore the decree of Pope Pelagius says they are to be suppressed by external powers — the Gloss says by laymen, who have power outside the clerical Church — as is clear from Distinctio 17, “Nec licuit,” and 23, questio 5, “Principes.” And here it is fully evident, as I have often said, that temporal lords may lawfully take away temporalities from a delinquent Church.
