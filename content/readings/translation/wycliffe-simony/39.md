As for the decree, it is said that it is necessary for jurists and all the faithful to understand it as speaking of illicit seizure. The decree well notes this in calling such persons plunderers and sacrilegious — for one who removes temporalities from a pseudo-pope or heretic is thereby commended by Christ and the law of the Church. And I do not see how it could be lawful to remove temporalities from a man against his will unless he were a heretic — and then it is lawful. Moreover it is lawful and obligatory under the old law, by which the burden of one’s neighbor’s beast must be relieved \[Exod. 23:5\]; much more so in the new law ought the spirit of a brother not to be burdened with the load of a so-called alms by which the religion of Christ is destroyed and dragged to hell by the devil’s rope — nor should credence be given in this matter to the appetite of the infirm, who on account of their slothful torpor \[*ditrasiam*\] have embraced the dung of the sense of Scripture. If therefore temporalities are necessary for us, let them be granted to us according to the measure and manner that the Gospel has fitted; for then temporalities would be scattered in the world fruitfully like rain, and dissensions and wars and sects and the other works of the flesh which the apostle enumerates \[Gal. 5:19–21\] would be stilled, and the word of God would water the hearts of men differently \[than it does now\]. Hence I believe that the Holy Spirit understands by this law that those who despoil the Church — which is \[constituted by\] persons excellent in state and virtue — through blind endowments arising from diabolical instigation, and who endow \[only\] that their name may be perpetuated on earth: these, I say, are sacrilegious and accursed by the Church Triumphant. For the devil is king over all the sons of pride, and as the faithful person knows, such persons rob good things immeasurably beyond their own measure; nor does hypocrisy or blind intention excuse them, because Paulus was smitten with the charge of blasphemy out of his faith in Scripture, blinded by the like hypocrisy of the priests,
