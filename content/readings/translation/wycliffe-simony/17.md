And so whoever — whether bishop, curate, or priest — accepts that status with temporal honor or gain as the preponderant motive is a simoniac and heretic, remaining in that heresy for as long as he — being unfit for his office — retains that status. This is clear from the definition of simony, and this appears to be the judgment of blessed Gregorius in the Register, Causa 1, questio 1, cap. *Si quis*, set forth in these words: “If anyone who is neither distinguished by holy and polished character, nor called by the clergy and people, nor compelled by their urging — who has already shamelessly polluted the priesthood of Christ by any crime — with an unusual affection of heart, or by sordid prayers of mouth, or by retinue, or by manual service, or by a fraudulent little gift has accepted episcopal or priestly dignity, supported not by the profit of souls but by the greed of vain glory, and has not voluntarily relinquished it in his lifetime, and death finds him in harsh penance — he will perish in eternity beyond doubt.” From these things it is further clear that whatever religious person has assumed the dignity or order of abbot or prior, of established prelacies, or the endowed or proprietary religious grades, more for worldly dignity or prosperity than for devotion in a life of poverty — so as to forsake the world — is anathema, because he is a simoniac and heretic upon entry; for every such person makes some illicit exchange, either by himself or through another, giving a man food, a spoon, prayers, or the hope of temporal advantage in return for the spiritual grace of meriting blessedness — which is the most contemptible simony.
