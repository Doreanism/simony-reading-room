For every bishop must hold whatever he occupies as the common goods of the poor, as is clear from the cited law of Gregory, *Pervenit*, causa 1, quaestio 2, chapter *Pastor ecclesie* \[*The pastor of the church*\] and the following chapter *Si quis non vult* \[*If anyone is unwilling*\], with many similar ones; and the reason of the religion of Christ compels this. By these arguments it is shown that it would be safer and more perfect for a bishop to possess purely food and clothing, as the Apostle prescribes in 1 Tim. 6, than any abundance whatsoever of the temporalities with which the Church is endowed, because as regards the person of the pastor it is equally his duty to feed his body sparingly and moderately; and as regards the alien and secular office of defending and distributing the goods of the poor, it is clear that not only does great danger attend it, but the greatest error, from which the entire human race is infected simoniacally. And this frequently moved me to say that temporal lords ought to take temporalities away from the offending Church. For Gregory teaches that prelates incur the antichristian heresy on account of the improvident distribution of the goods of the poor to a burdensome household, to soldiers, and to kinsmen; and in causa 1, quaestio 2, the Decree teaches Jerome’s ruling in the chapter *Clericos* \[*Clerics*\]: “Those clerics,” he says, “it is fitting to be sustained by the stipends of the Church in whose case the goods of parents and relatives do not provide sufficient support. Those, however, who are able to be supported from the goods of their parents and their own resources, if they accept what belongs to the poor, they assuredly commit sacrilege and eat and drink judgment upon themselves through the abuse of such things.” Whence the fact that all things of the bishop ought to be common is written in the same quaestio, final chapter: “All clerics who faithfully and vigilantly serve the Church shall receive the stipends due to their holy labors according to the merit of their service
