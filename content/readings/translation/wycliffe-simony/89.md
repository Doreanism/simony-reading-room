As regards progress in these propertied orders, it is plain that they are led by simony in procuring \[benefices and revenues\], in dispensation, and in the governance of temporalities. In procuring \[these things\], not only by lying and hypocrisy in petitioning secular lords for revenues and incomes in perpetual alms — and not only in procuring such lordships together with the goods of the poor, which is altogether simoniacal — but most especially in the fact that by false representations churches are appropriated to such convents. For whereas the alms of parish churches sustained the secular clergy, who freely taught and preached the evangelical truth in their parishes and elsewhere, through such appropriations the necessary instrument is taken from those secular clergymen, and the aforesaid fruit is without doubt suspended from our mother \[the Church\] by the cunning of the devil. Hence the Bishop of Lincoln says, in a certain sermon to the Pope, after enumerating the defects in the care of the pastoral office: “When,” he says, “the appropriation of parish churches is made to religious \[houses\], this is the firm perpetuation of the evils already described” — because men who would have been able to succeed bad temporal pastors and carry out the works of the pastoral office can no longer do so. Not only does this spiritual harm befall the Church, but whereas rectors would have multiplied the people, fed the flock, and spiritually begotten the Christian people, the aforesaid despoilers, lurking in their enclosed retreats, withdraw the people from the kingdoms, devour the poor who dwell in the places of the parish church, and bring forth sons of the devil destructive to kingdoms and temporal lords.
