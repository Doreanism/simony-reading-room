— Christ Jesus, mediator between God and men, as is clear from Luke 2; but this vice is most contrary to good will toward Jesus, therefore it is most opposed to the peace and salvation of men. The minor premise is evident from the deeds of Christ, who came to suffer most humbly at the hands of Jews and Gentiles, and yet was severely enraged not only against this sin but against its very figure, when with a whip made from cords by his own hand he drove from the temple those prefiguring simony, as it is said in John 2. And that whip signifies that those who commit this heresy — on account of the cords by which they are ensnared in it — are to be flogged without reprieve at the second coming of Christ. Likewise, in proportion as a sin sounds to a greater contempt of God, it is to be punished the more; but such is simony, therefore, etc. For simoniacs sell not only justice and grace for a base price — as if someone were to lead to market a beast of burden and a slave to be sold for a base price — but Christ himself, who is the most just of persons, they consume and devour most dishonorably, as far as in them lies. And as regards the first point, Parisiensis notes how simoniacs surpass Scarioth \[Judas Iscariot\], who sold the Truth in secret to important persons while \[Christ\] was in a mortal body; but simoniacs do the exact opposite, both as regards the Son and as regards the Holy Spirit. It would have sufficed for the simoniac that Christ was once sold for us and led to his Passion; but he is consumed by them still, because it is said after this event in John 2: “Then the disciples recalled that verse of psalm 68 which is said of Christ: ‘Zeal for your house has consumed me.’” Here it is evident that Christ addresses God the Father on behalf of the wicked priests of his Church, whose burning envy consumes Christ when it gnaws at his life by lying, or when it consumes Christ — who according to the Apostle in 2 Corinthians 5 is “sin,” that is, an offering for sin — in the rich offerings of the faithful and renders to the people the viaticum of the law of God.
