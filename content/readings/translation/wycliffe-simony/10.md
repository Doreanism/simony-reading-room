— it is said: “Whoever wished to fill the hand of Jeroboam became a priest of the high places.” And it follows: “For this cause the house of Jeroboam was overthrown and wiped from the face of the earth.” He must therefore avenge the selling of the priesthood of Christ who thus avenged the selling of the priesthood of idols. The same consideration applies to religious orders, to universities of study, and to the disordered exchanges of private colleges. For in all of these there must be simony whenever they deviate from the law of Christ, and thus the simoniacal wickedness in them is the harbinger of simoniacal weakening or future ruin. Among religious, it is said that spiritual gifts of God are exchanged illicitly; in universities, it is said that there is even more deceitful trafficking in the purchase of graces, in halls, and in other means, so that money is more subtly drained from poor students under the pretext of selling; and in private colleges, it is said that acceptance of persons and the sin of those who build Zion in bloodshed are actively at work. And since all these things dissolve the order of the Holy Spirit and thus peace, they must, according to the testimony of Christ in Luke 11, be desolated. “Every,” he says, “kingdom divided against itself shall be desolated, and house shall fall upon house.” For since the spirit of discipline will flee the deceitful, it is evident that kingdoms thus divided against themselves must be desolated by the absence of the Comforter. And since everyone who is at discord with God must also be at discord with himself, it is evident that one family shall collapse upon another and they shall mutually destroy each other. And so nothing destroys contracts and kingdoms — indeed Christianity itself — more than simoniacal heresy, because it removes the grace of the Holy Spirit through which the members would be held together among themselves and with Christ. Therefore it is the same to sow heresy in a good community as to withdraw moisture from a grassy garden and thereby destroy its greenery at the root; and so woe to the nation, woe to the place, and woe to the man who strives to sow, defend, or foster simoniacal heresy in a community.
