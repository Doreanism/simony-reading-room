“Those who sell or buy holy orders cannot be priests. Hence it is written: ‘Anathema to the giver and anathema to the receiver — this is the simoniacal heresy.’ How then, if they are under anathema and are not holy, can they sanctify others? How can they deliver or receive the body of Christ? One who is cursed — how can he bless?” Indeed, it would be more expedient that such prelates neither bless nor administer the ecclesiastical sacraments, because the council in the same questio subsequently says: “We ought not to receive the blessings of heretics, for they are curses rather than blessings.” For in Malachi 2 the Lord promises: “I will curse your blessings,” that is — as the Decretum says — whatever you bless will be cursed by me. Which seems to be capable of being soundly understood thus: whoever knows or ought to know that you are simonists and on that account receives from you approvingly a blessing or any other episcopal ministry deserves thereby the curse of the Lord, because contrary to the commandment of the Lord in Matthew 18 — “Let him be to you as a heathen and a publican” — he fosters and anoints such a one with the oil of sinners. How then would so evil a deed not rebound mortally to the detriment of such a person, especially when through one of the six kinds of consent he consents to the heresy of such a prelate? And it is plain how bereft our kingdom is of spiritual suffrages on account of simoniacal wickedness.
