And this appears to be the judgment of the venerable Bede \[Gratian’s Decretum\], Causa 1, questio 3, cap. *Non solum venditores*, where he says thus: “Not only are those sellers of doves who make the house of God a house of commerce — who in distributing holy orders seek the price of money, or of praise, or even of honor — but also those who exercise a grade or spiritual grace in the Church which they received through the gift of the Lord not with a simple intention but for the sake of any human recompense, contrary to that word of the apostle: ‘If any man speak, let him speak as it were the words of God; he that ministereth, as of the power which God administereth; that in all things God may be honored.’ Therefore let all such persons, if they do not wish to be driven out of the Church at the coming of the Lord, remove these things from their deeds, lest they make the house of God a house of commerce.”

And from these things the discerning person can reflect how intoxicated and excommunicate are kingdoms through heretical prelates — \[that is,\] simonists — and through all kinds of priests. For concerning the prophet Aggaeus, Hieronymus says, as recorded in \[Gratian’s Decretum\] Causa 1, questio 1, cap. *Sic*: “Thus is this people of all heretics in my sight, says the Lord: everything they have done, which they have offered to me — whether vows for salvation or peace offerings for sin, or holocausts, or alms, or fasts, or bodily chastity — shall be contaminated in my sight. For although what is offered may appear holy in its outward form, yet because it has been handled by one who is polluted in soul, all things are polluted.” And the same is clear in the following chapter, *Odit deus*.
