Now since there are many laws and arguments for destroying this heresy, and since it cannot be eradicated at its root until its mother and nurse has been destroyed, it is clear that — as in the time of Christ — its sustenance must be withdrawn in order to destroy it. For while the endowment stands, which is the nurse of this heresy of the Church, it would be an extraordinary miracle for simoniacal heresy to be extinguished. As a sign of this: from the time of Symon Magus until the endowment of the Church, this heresy lay dormant, nor does it become rife among religious who possess nothing, nor does it flourish around meager benefices, but around wealthy ones such as bishoprics and other Caesarean dignities; the reason for this is that the working of miracles, in which the Holy Spirit is assumed, is not now seen as it was in the primitive Church — by which the faithful recognized the faithful from the faithless — and neither the working of miracles nor the dignity of our prelates merits this for others. Therefore, through the cunning of the devil, the religion of the clergy has been overturned from the Christian to the Caesarean; and having abandoned the title of receiving from the people freely given alms as a subsidy, certain antichristian laws arose to extort money — and so the venality of simoniacal heresy was introduced. And these are the perilous times that Christ and his Apostle had foretold.

Hence Parisiensis \[William of Auvergne\], in his treatise On Avarice, enumerating eight things that make for the detestation of this sin, says in horror of it that it is spiritual sodomy. For just as through bodily sodomy the seed is everlastingly destroyed from which an individual of the human race would be formed, so in that \[spiritual\] sodomy the seed of the word of God is cast down, by which a spiritual generation in Christ Jesus would be created.
