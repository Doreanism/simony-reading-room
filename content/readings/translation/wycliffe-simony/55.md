But hear my words and counsel, and God will be with you; be thou for the people in those things that pertain to God, that thou mayest bring their causes to him, and show the people the ceremonies and manner of worship, and the way in which they ought to walk, and the work they ought to do. \[Exod. 18:19–20\] If therefore under the old law holy Moses lived so poor a life on the counsel of a gentile philosopher, who was in this an instrument of the Holy Spirit, and relinquished the particular judgments of which he had surpassing knowledge, how much more ought the vicar of Christ under the law of grace to be an example of a poor and humble ministry, and not to assign to offices unknown to him persons beyond the measure of the knowledge given to him. For judges in the law of Moses must be powerful men fearing God, in whom there is truth and who hate covetousness; how much more so in the law of Christ, in which men are set over souls. The highest vicar of Christ on earth must therefore live spiritually in the manner of Christ, look upon the universal state of Christendom with contempt for private advantage, and be an example of virtue and contempt for the world to all the people. Otherwise he dissipates both himself and all of Christendom into factions on account of the poisonous example he gives and the private gain he pursues. For these reasons he must be a diligent observer of the law of God and in difficult matters look to the mercy-seat and the other signs of the temple according to the law of Christ; and then, and only then, is he vicar of Christ; and with so great a Lord commanding, we must look to him, believe him, and obey his precepts.
