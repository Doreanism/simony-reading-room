But improvident election appears to me, in ambiguous cases, to rely on election according to human traditions, when the other part is clearly secure according to the law of God. And so it appears to me that every person who, with respect to his state or manner of living or acting in that state, follows human traditions while abandoning the easy and open path according to the law of God, sins gravely — though ignorance arising from prior sin is in many cases excused by the grace of God. And so it appears to me that every election \[made\] either purely according to human tradition and thus unlawfully, or that imperfectly refrains from such matters where one could according to the law of God abstain from them altogether, sins gravely; and so it appears to me that all human inventions beyond the faith of scripture are culpable to a greater or lesser degree. The third point, however, appears to be concluded as true at the beginning of the argument: it would be better for all human nature to be governed purely according to the rule of the gospel, with all added human law removed, because in faith God — foreknowing all future things — delivered through Christ and his apostles the fulfillment of the law for the attainment of beatitude; how then does it not become superfluous, with respect to this end, to add a new law? Hence those appear to be disciples of Antichrist who say that it is as necessary to add a new law to the law of Christ as it was necessary for the law of Christ to be added to the old law.
