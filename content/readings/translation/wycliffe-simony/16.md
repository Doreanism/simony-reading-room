Causa 1, questio 1, cap. *Ordinaciones*. For since the kingdom of God is worth as much as you have, God forbid that it could be purchased for money — not, however, by money as such, but by a good will. Nor does anything appear to be purchasable in itself for money; yet some can illicitly both buy and sell the kingdom of God, as Christ, who is the kingdom, was sold by Scarioth and bought by the priests for thirty pieces of silver, as is clear in Matthew 26; he himself is the kingdom of God that is within you, as it is said in Luke 17 — and therefore it seems that the kingdom of God was bought and sold among those simoniacal traders. Nor is there any doubt that it can be studiously and most meritoriously bought from a man and sold by God — but not commercially; indeed it seems that those who buy all the sins of the wealthy at their gates sell the kingdom of God to simonists, just as every person acting meritoriously through prayers does so according to present justice. For a man can sell and buy as much as he has; indeed it seems that every mortal sinner buys the right to condemnation or perpetual punishment, and since this is something highly spiritual, it is clear that he buys it most illicitly. From which it appears that every simonist is a heretic, and vice versa. For if it is lawful to seize the kingdom of heaven, all the more can one unjustly buy or sell the kingdom of heaven. And the antecedent is clear from Matthew 11: “From the time of the Baptist the kingdom of heaven suffers violence, and the violent seize it.” And in this simony appear to be those who illicitly sell indulgences and absolutions from punishment and guilt along with other spiritual suffrages.

Secondly, the definition appears to apply to every buying or selling which, though remitted, is not spiritual or annexed to the spiritual, and yet by faith we believe that the apostles, who bought food at the Lord’s command as is clear from John 4, did not thereby commit simony.
