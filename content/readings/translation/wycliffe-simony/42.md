as is shown above by the chapter Si duobus \[Decretum, C.7 q.1 c.14\] cited earlier, human rule would be equally deficient in choosing a superior; how then could a priest of Christ set aside the law of God and proceed according to elections governed by human law, which is full of the devil’s snares? Or how could the pope annul such elections, if they are just? Likewise, the apostles by common decree, in the absence of revelation, chose Matthias as apostle by lot, as is plain from Acts 1. But their action ought to be a more evident model in this matter than the frequently cited words or deeds of the Roman pontiff; therefore the Church should still attend to this law. And this seems to be the position of the decree of Hieronymus placed at C.8 q.1: “If,” he says, “that great and mighty Moses was not permitted to choose by his own judgment a prince of the people or to appoint his successor, who is it that asserts that the election of the people — customarily stirred to \[seek\] office by clamor and outcry, or perhaps even by money — or even from among the priests themselves, who among them will judge himself qualified for this, unless it be the one to whom, as he prays and does penance, it is revealed by the Lord? As God said to Moses, Numbers 27: ‘Take to yourself Joshua the son of Nun, a man in whom is the spirit of God, and lay your hands upon him, and set him before Eleazar the priest and give him commandment and before all the congregation, and charge him in their sight, and thou shalt put some of your honor upon him so that all the children of Israel may obey him.’ Do you hear clearly the ordination of a prince of the people so plainly described and almost expounded?” And also that Petrus the apostle always appointed Clemens \[as his successor\], as is evident from cap. Symon Petrus in the same question cited above, and thus generally when the apostles appointed presbyters; so that, if the inspiration that ought to be known to the people is wanting, we must be directed \[by God\]; for it is not in the power of the pope or of man to destroy the laws of scripture.
