It is conceded, however, that it happens that many proceed in this matter in a disordered fashion; therefore, that I may lean upon an ineffable rule, I set forth 13 conditions of a bishop which the Apostle declares to Timothy \[1 Tim. 3:1–7\], conditions which must belong in the highest degree to the pope. And from the same Epistle I set forth 20 conditions of pseudo-bishops, which must belong in the highest degree to an anti-pope or Antichrist. Nor is it lawful for a believer to prohibit this faith, or to prevent a Christian from treating, discussing, or moderately preaching it with what follows. And from this faith I assert boldly that whether the Roman pontiff or the Avignonese or any other has in the highest degree these 15 good conditions, this man is pope; and if he has in the highest degree these 20 conditions, then he is anti-pope and the greatest Antichrist. Nor is it lawful for a believer to deny, flee from, or gloss over this faith beyond the sense that the Holy Spirit has imposed on it, as the school of Antichrist says: that it was not lawful for the Apostle to write a law that would bind the pope or the Roman pontiff, since an inferior has no power over his superior. Therefore the Apostle’s law concerning bishops only begins to apply to bishops subject to the papacy, while it is blasphemously said to be lawful for \[the pope\] himself to wander above every law of God in whatever manner he wishes — because the four Evangelists with Paul and the other Apostles do not bind the pope, since an equal has no authority over an equal; and that is said to be the source of authority for the old law. Therefore, as they feign, no new or old law of God binds the pope; whereas Christ willed to be subject to the law of God as a man, and thus it was necessary for him to fulfil it by virtue of the obedience owed to the Trinity. If, I say, the pope has apostatized from the Apostle’s law concerning bishops, no one is more to be fled, disbelieved, or pursued in opposition, because he is then the greatest Antichrist.
