This is indeed the greatest cause against which the faithful ought to fight. And if it is objected that one ought to believe those elders who ought to govern the people by their authority, and not private, humble, and ignorant persons who were unacquainted with the dignities and laws of the Church — all the faithful ought to believe that God would not in vain ordain the way of the Lord in conformity with his own law. Therefore all the faithful ought moreover to recognize your simoniacal sin as \[the sin of\] the greatest heretics, when for temporal gain they exalt idiots in order to have them in greater numbers; and in this third \[matter\] the whole people ought to rise up against such persons — indeed, they themselves ought to rise up against themselves. And it is well known throughout many kingdoms that the pope, on account of such deference, still fails to recognize those pastors whom they appoint who are manifestly failing in the cure of spiritual governance. Therefore, since God has given to each person freedom of will so that he may care for his own soul — even though in part those who blind the people with regard to the sacraments \[impede this\] — therefore according to the freedom of will, after manifest notice \[of the evil\], the whole people ought to rise up against such persons.
