How can they hand over or receive the body of Christ? How can one who is accursed give a blessing?” Therefore, as I said above on the testimony of Jerome and Gregory, the blessing of such persons is harmful. For just as they blaspheme in saying that Christ, by blessing the bread, utterly destroyed it into non-being, so they bless the people in an ironic sense — that is, in the sense of the wife of Job (in the second chapter thereof): they curse the people. And the same judgment holds of any simoniacal pope with respect to every consecration of a bishop or every other act that he then performs.

### If therefore, according to the laws of the princes, when a higher prelate fails — since there is an essential order between him and the subordinate prelate or priest who ministers — it is necessary to annul the office of the subordinate, then there would be few or no priests at all, because they would have been generated by simoniacal prelates, and thus the priestly sacraments would perish from the Church.

Nor does the fiction avail whereby one says that God alters the law of his operation at the instance, or on account of the failure, or at the request of such accursed priesthood — for he gives grace freely, not for temporal gain, but he takes it away. As Ambrose says, as quoted in the Decreta in the passage cited above: “When a bishop was being ordained, what he gave was gold; what he lost was his soul. When he ordains another, what he received was gold; what he gave was leprosy.” We are therefore compelled to deny that essential order between our prelates and their subjects; indeed, with those \[prelates\] failing like curled locks, Christ ordains whomever, in whatever manner, and whenever he wills. And hence, knowing those errors that were to come in the Church, he ordained his apostles as bishops at a time and in a manner unknown to us — so that we may thus learn not to prize overmuch the solemnities that we have devised for the sacraments, nor to be too pompous about the dignities by which, on account of human institution, we exceed our brothers. Let the priest therefore follow Christ in \[his\] conduct, and he may confidently rely upon Christ —

that by Christ’s own authority, when any imperial prelate \[whatsoever\] fails him, he has been legitimately ordained by Christ and approved in whatever sacrament or work he has rightly performed. From these \[arguments\] is gathered the antidote without which, as is credibly believed, the Church receives no remedy from the simoniacal heresy — namely, that the lord pope, the bishops, all religious \[persons\], and those endowed with the title of perpetual possession as secular clergy, ought to renounce those \[possessions\] into the hands of the secular arm of the Church; and if they have pertinaciously refused, they ought to be compelled \[to do so\] by secular lords. The first part is proved by the \[text from\] Luke 14: “Every one of you who does not renounce all that he possesses cannot be my disciple.” And the second part is clear from Matthew 5: “But if the salt has lost its savor, it is good for nothing, but to be cast forth and trodden underfoot by men.” The heavenly king therefore awaits these meritorious remedies from the kings of the earth — may he, who reigns over the whole Church forever, himself bring this to pass. Amen.
