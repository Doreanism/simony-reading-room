Bishops are therefore compelled to defend the claim that all those to whom they appropriate or have appropriated churches in their diocese assist the pastors of the sheep more fruitfully, and do not diminish the care of the sheep which the rectors previously discharged — otherwise they themselves would be pseudo-pastors and agents of antichrist, bearing on that account a heavier burden upon the Church. It is conceded, however, that it is lawful and meritorious in a parochial situation to give alms beyond what suffices for the private curate’s own needs, from tithes, offerings, or other alms freely given to a community or to a person to whom one freely chooses to make them — but this is outside the determination of one’s bishop, lest perhaps custom be drawn into an execrable law and voluntary almsgiving into servile exaction.

But finally it seems that this opinion would so greatly disturb the Church that the consecrations of popes, the blessings of bishops, and the celebrations of Masses would fail, in that all such ministries in simoniacs are infected.
