For one who becomes entangled with them both in intellect and in affection will inevitably be seduced, since they seduce, if it be possible, even the elect \[Matt. 24:24\] — so great is the blindness that has been brought into the church by Antichrist and his accomplices regarding the sacrament of the altar, such that many believe not only that sacrament to be an accident without a subject, but to be their God by identity. And among all the blindnesses that have ever been permitted by God to fall upon the church, the most remarkable is this: that not only the people but those called the subtler holy doctors believe that a quantity, quality, ponderousness, or bundle of accidents is, without an underlying substance, that sensible sacrament — nor are Avicenna, Algazel, Hali, and the other philosophers so greatly deceived by the verses of Mohammed. It is as if Antichrist were to argue thus: ‘If I can seduce the foremost Christian philosophers to such a degree that they believe the mode of a thing, or an accident without a substance, to subsist of itself — since this is supremely impossible and unintelligible except in the outer shell \[of words\], contrary to Scripture, to the holy doctors, to the law of the church, to natural reason, and to the senses — if,’ says the devil, ‘in Antichrist I can make them believe this, I shall make them believe whatever I wish about the power of the pope and his works in consequence.’ And I say this is unintelligible, because all the philosophers of this sect are ignorant of the proper terms when speaking of an accident or of its nine genera variously explained, so that just as they adore what they do not know, so they preach what they do not understand; and hence they are ashamed to reveal their opinion to the people. But at God’s instigation the people eagerly ask what the sacrament of the altar is, since it is not identically the body of Christ; and neither the people nor the teacher understands that it is an accident without a subject.
