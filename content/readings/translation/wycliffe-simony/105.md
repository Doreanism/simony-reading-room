How, I ask, will princes answer in the day of judgment for the Church, which they received from Christ to defend and especially to defend against simoniacal heresy, when they could so readily and so profitably to themselves expel that poison, yet on account of love for the devil — since it is on account of the pomp of the world — they consume themselves and their own in a manner entirely contrary \[to their duty\]? But whatever counselors of princes either oppose or remain silent before their princes concerning this sentence of destroying and striking down \[simoniacal heretics\], are as the greatest heretics, since they are the most cunning simoniacs and the most subtle disciples of Antichrist. The watchman, moreover, according to the teaching of God in Ezekiel 34, ought to free his soul and the people’s from danger, considering that no small but very great danger or persecution arises from thence. Thus therefore the Church — but especially the watchmen — ought to denounce such simoniacal heretics loudly and continuously, converting perilous worldly wars into that office; and whatever confessor, counselor, or corrupter says the contrary, let him beware on account of the sentence of that Lord who, from the testimony of Ezekiel, will require the lost soul from the hand of the silent guardian. Nor does it appear that such blasphemous simoniacs are today, as under the old law, to be killed in general by the people, but according to the teaching of the Apostle in Titus 3 they are to be avoided by every Christian.
