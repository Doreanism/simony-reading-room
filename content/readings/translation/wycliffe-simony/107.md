But this invention of the father of lies is at once refuted by the fact that obedience is owed to a private superior only to the precise degree that God commands it in conformity with his law. Therefore in Acts 5 the apostles say: “We ought to obey God rather than men.” It therefore seems to me that those sent to such an office should expose the lies of Symon and procure in those matters the dissolution of simoniacal wickedness; for so did Thomas Didimus consume the treasury of king Gundoforus. Now, however, it is blasphemously said that the pope, by appropriating or by confirming or by tacitly approving, thereby licenses what was previously unlawful. For to say this would be equivalent to exalting him above God; for on the day when each one shall bear his own burden, the blasphemous error of a pope or another prelate by which he leads himself and his subjects into the pit will not excuse \[them\] — but the faith of scripture, which is the last will of God’s testament, and the duplicity of their rash consent, will condemn such persons on the last day. For they falsely suggest and diabolically incite the pope or a prelate to wickedness, and then, as heralds of that crime, they weigh upon him with manifold toil in the performance of it; and that this is not an excuse but an accusation is known to the judge from whose sentence no one is permitted to appeal.
