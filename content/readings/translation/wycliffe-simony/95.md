Therefore the third remedy is one in which I place greater confidence: that the people withhold tithes, offerings, and other private alms from the unworthy disciples of Antichrist whom they see trafficking in this way around the sacrilegious plunder of their goods; since they are bound to do this under the law of God, the curse or censure inflicted by the disciples of Antichrist is not to be feared but joyfully accepted. Let us therefore pray to God that this, practiced individually, may so grow that the clergy return to the primitive state, content with food and clothing according to the apostle’s rule, and that the people may elect for themselves charitable spiritual leaders best suited to the salvation of their souls.

But an objection is raised that, according to this view, there would be no appropriation of churches, and consequently colleges would be extinguished and thus the clergy. But as regards that objection it has been said at length elsewhere that it would be advantageous for the Church if there were no appropriations of this kind to cathedral churches, or the chapels of princes, or the university colleges of secular clergy; the clergy would then stand more abundantly, more virtuously, and more actively than they stand now. Nevertheless, it would be consonant with justice in equal cases that in a parish church which of itself would be sufficient for one of the clergy, the college itself should reside there as long as it stands worthy through lawful ministry. And as regards colleges in the schools of clerics, it is plain that they proceeded from evil — the evil indeed of a bishop who ought to promote and assist theologians, and from the evil of the appropriation of parish churches from which the school of theologians in the universities ought to be continuously maintained everywhere.
