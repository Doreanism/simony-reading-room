For this endeavor would be motivated by the reward of blessedness, by the peace of kingdoms, and — to speak in human terms — by temporal advantage. For nothing in our ministry would seem more pleasing to Christ than such a charitable direction of his bride and such great honor paid to our mother \[the Church\]; and it is beyond doubt, as regards the peace of men, that nothing disturbs the peace of kingdoms more than the aforesaid simoniacal heresy. For through it, kingdoms are deprived of the instruction to love enemies, to despise worldly things, and to live by word and example in good will according to the law of Christ. For through simoniacal heresy, precisely the opposite has been introduced. Therefore Blessed Gregorius says in his Register, Book 3, chapter 29, to the queen of France: “Since it is written, ‘Justice exalts a nation, but sin makes peoples miserable,’ then a stable reign is raised up when the fault that is recognized is the more quickly corrected.” Therefore, since the cause of the people’s ruin is wicked priests, and since in our regions priests conduct themselves wickedly, we must ardently rise up to avenge these things, lest the wickedness of a few bring about the destruction of many. And he continues: “Provide, therefore, for your own souls, provide for your grandchildren whom you desire to reign happily, provide for the province, and before our Creator extends his hand to strike, give most diligent thought to the correction of this wickedness.” As regards the third point, it is evident that certain moveable goods of heretics would be confiscated to the princes who would destroy their heresy, and all their immoveable goods — revenues and landed estates — would return to the hands of the secular \[lords\], just as it was in the beginning, because this would bring the state of the Church to fulfillment, as it was in the primitive Church by the ordering of Christ. And although ancestors, superstitiously and blindly and irrationally, deprived themselves of true dominion before God in matters of Christ’s religion, nevertheless their posterity, contrite over the folly of themselves and their forebears, will by correcting the errors acquire the title of justice both before God and before men; and this title would be far more just than the title derived from conquest by the sword.
