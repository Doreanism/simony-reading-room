Wherein it is evident, as I have often argued, that the following consequence results: if in such a case the person who first presents his process to the bishop ought to be preferred in grace, but Petrus, unworthy before God and foreknown as a reprobate, presents his process to the bishop before Paulus, who is predestined and capable before God — then Petrus himself ought to be preferred in grace. Now since the minor premise of that argument commonly comes to pass, because Judas does not sleep but works deceitfully and keeps vigil for the sake of gain, I grant the truth of the minor. And then it is evident that the conclusion is heretical, and the minor is supposed to be true, and the form is also sound — which leaves the major, namely the papal law, as heretical. For it does not accord with the law of blessed Gregorius, C. 1 q. 6, c. *Sicut is* \[C.1 q.6 c.1\], but manifestly conflicts with it, since it follows that because Petrus pursues \[the dignity\] more importunately than Paulus, he is indeed to be preferred. A bishop who consequently prefers Petrus, deceived by the fallacy of treating a non-cause as a cause by conferring \[the benefice\] on one who is unfit, manifests himself as a disciple of antichrist. For such laws are manifestly \[perverse\], since all human traditions that are not originally founded in Scripture are worthless — nor are the evasions in this matter of any avail — as these men blaspheme, \[saying\] that by the very fact that Petrus has first presented \[his letters\] by virtue of papal law he is more capable, when often by virtue of God’s law he is less capable. And since God’s law in such a case is contrary to papal law, that \[papal law\] must yield as heretical, because it has departed from the truth.
