in all these places and many similar ones you will find that the pope can be simoniacal, heretical, and tainted with every kind of sin. Therefore I boldly assert with the Apostle that the pope is antichrist, and one who exalts himself above everything that is called God — if he cannot commit simony; for then he cannot sin, since he is the more prone to this particular sin, and thus cannot be separated from the divine ordinance; it remains that he is exalted above God, for then he could impeccably oppose the divine ordinance, which he could not do unless he were superior to our God. And according to the Apostle, 2 Thessalonians 2, antichrist is exalted in this blasphemy; therefore someone says that he is the chief member of simoniacal heresy, and from his school and seat as from a source the simoniacal heresies stream widely throughout the world.

Supposing therefore that he together with his college can be simoniacal, it must be seen in what three ways he could commit simony. In the first way, by aspiring to that monarchical eminence for the sake of pomp and gain: no state is accustomed to be more dangerous, if \[one holding it\] does not more closely follow Petrus and Christ in \[his\] life, but departs \[from them\] — such a one is to be held not apostolic but apostate. One therefore who aspires to that pinnacle on account of worldly honor or gain is, in the first mode, struck in his own person with blasphemous heresy; but since this could be hidden from the world, therefore negligence in ministering to the Church after the manner of Christ and the Apostle Petrus, and deviation toward a secular way of life, are the manifest indication of that papal heresy.

The second kind of simoniacal heresy springing from this is the will to multiply traditions that smell of profit, to the burden of the Church, and which are implicitly contrary to Christian law; for whoever does this most imprudently commits a spiritual dignity for the sake of desire for temporal advantage.
