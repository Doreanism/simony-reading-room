The folly of kings, then, is the origin of this evil, since certain popes have sought relief from our kingdom by means of a subsidy, as is apparent in the tract *De Papa*; but now it is said to be established as law that the pope should have first fruits from those promoted by him. There is no doubt that unless kingdoms resist these practices of the chief Antichrist, they will suffer a worse outrage at his hands; not only because the long-established harvest \[of bad precedent\] duly ripens into the despoliation of those goods whose kingdom God has committed to them as their chief charge, but also because they consent to such great simoniacal heresy against the deed of Christ, and it is inevitable that they will be deceived by the pseudo-clergy, the domestic enemies whom they exalt through simony.

The second fiction says that when the pope, without an intervening agreement, promotes a worthy candidate, he does not commit simony, even if he afterwards receives freely from his own. For thus he grants to bishops and archdeacons in England the first fruits of their entire diocese; why then could not the pope as supreme lord by the same authority appropriate to himself the first fruits? Here it is said that it makes no difference before God whether a tacit or express, implicit or suggestive condition intervenes, because God who looks into the hearts of men cannot be deceived; but the pope can conceal the condition he holds in his mind, that the one promoted should pay him his first fruits, because he can demand money refused through excommunications, deprivations, and spoliation-grants \[conferring benefits on others\] according to mental reservations and countless other diabolical stratagems; therefore, to disguise his lordship and hypocrisy, he does not expressly agree beforehand.
