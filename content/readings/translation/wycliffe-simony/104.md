Nor are those to be heeded who blasphemously stammer that it belongs to the pope to interpret whatever laws of the Church there are, to suspend them at his pleasure, or to abrogate them entirely — as seems to follow from the law at C. 25 q. 1 c. *His itaque* — for, as I have often said, nothing would more thoroughly nullify the laws of the Church nor more blaspheme the pope than that a fully Antichristian pope should dispense and prescribe against God; indeed, nothing would more generate against the pope — as Lincolniensis \[Robert Grosseteste\] says — a cataclysm of distrust and disobedience. For who then would believe or obey papal law or bulls? Yet according to this same opinion it would follow contradictorily that one is bound to obey the pope’s will in those three aforementioned fictitious matters. But it is not clear from papal bulls, however recently they may have come forth concerning the pope’s interpretation, suspension, or abrogation; therefore, lest the pope’s ultimate will be offended, no one ought to act in any way according to his laws or bulls. Therefore blessed be the pope whose will is inflexibly just, most fully known to all in eternity. But since according to the whole Church such a notorious heretic ought to be struck down by the people, and it belongs to kings and secular lords — with respect to this — to govern, lead, and encourage the people, it is clear that they ought by the duty of their governance, more boldly setting aside private fear or gain, to take the lead in these matters. And I assert this boldly: that before this spring which flows from the fountain of simoniacal heresy has been stopped up in \[their\] kingdoms, they will never be undisturbed in God’s sight and among men, because those princes themselves and their whole people are otherwise struck by simoniacal heresy through consent, and the more gravely so in proportion as, by their office, they could more persistently and more readily expel from their domains those lepers who are thus spreading infection —
