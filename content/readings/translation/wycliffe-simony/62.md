Nor is there any doubt that from these things there follows, in the third place, the proportional conclusion of Elizeus. ‘Now,’ he said, ‘thou hast received money and garments to buy oliveyards and vineyards and sheep and oxen and menservants and maidservants; but the leprosy of Naaman shall cleave also to thee and to thy seed for ever. And he went out from his presence a leper as white as snow.’ \[2 Kgs. 5:26–27\] And proportionally the same appears to be the case with the first simoniacal pope before God. Leprosy, I say, signifies simony mystically according to the saints, because just as no disease is more infectious, more dissolving of continuity and deforming of form, or more horrible to live near on account of stench or the sound of the voice, so it is with simoniacal heresy. For no sin is so infectious, since the simoniac infects first himself, second those above him by the act of conferral, and third the people below him to whom he ministers the ecclesiastical sacraments — indeed he infects the people on every side through consent. And as to the second point, it is plain that this all too thoroughly dissolves the continuity of the members of the church, since those whom it ought to have joined to Christ according to the form of the gospel it severs and deforms according to the law of the devil. And this is the reason it is called heresy, which is interpreted as division. Hence there is no monster more deformed before God, though such men may appear to be the head of the church. Hence Aaron is expressly reproved and his sister Maria is struck with leprosy, as is clear from Numbers 12: ‘Behold,’ said Maria, ‘the gleaming leprosy appeared white as snow.’ \[Num. 12:10\] And consequently Aaron prays to Moses for her, that she should not be as one dead and as an abortive that is cast forth from the womb of its mother. \[Num. 12:12\] For such persons are cast out before God from the mother church, even though they appear to govern it. And as to the third point, it is plain that every believer would shudder at this leprosy more than at the devil himself; indeed their reputation infects many, they emit the voice of the asp and not of the trumpet; and therefore they are ordained in the old law, as is clear from Numbers 5, to remain as intolerable persons outside the camp. \[Num. 5:2–3\]
