And if one asks with whom they are trading, it is said without doubt that they trade with the devil, for they make the office or occupation of so spiritual a duty their most foolish merchandise.

In the second way a bishop commits simony with extreme gravity when he consumes in a disorderly fashion, for himself, an excessive and luxurious household, or — thirdly — for his external associates, such as retaining knights or kinsmen, the temporalities that should be distributed to the poor; for in all such cases the episcopal office is disorderedly exchanged for the most shameful gain. And this is the judgment of Pope Gregorius, causa 1, quaestio 3, *Pervenit* \[*It has come to our notice*\]; which law, because it is necessary for the instruction of the Church, is repeated in causa 16, quaestio 7: “It has come to our notice through ill report that certain bishops do not confer tithes and the oblations of Christians upon the priests of their own diocese, but rather upon lay persons — namely soldiers or servants — or, what is worse, upon their own kinsmen. Wherefore if any bishop shall henceforth be found to be a transgressor of this kind of precept, let him be counted among the greatest heretics and antichrists as not the least of them; and, just as the Council of Nicaea decreed concerning simoniacs, both the bishop who gives and the laymen who receive from him, whether for a price or by way of benefit, shall be devoured by the flames of eternal fire.” Many, I say, are of the opinion that according to that most holy decree and in fullest accordance with the faith of Scripture, the greater part of bishops, since the endowment of the Church, has infected itself and its associates with the leprosy of simony to such a degree that if any bishop in England
