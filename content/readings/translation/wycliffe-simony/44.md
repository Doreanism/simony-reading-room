From this moral saying of this holy \[father\] you can perceive how greatly the Church is corrupted today — for formerly there was a law that the child to be governed should choose for himself a priest or bishop. But now the hands of simple folk are excluded and the Roman pontiff is sought, from whom merchants buy the worst \[provision\], as thoroughly corrupt as simple folk \[are destined\] to be perpetually tormented by the devil; Judas indeed bought \[the betrayal\] of the person of Christ, who was designated and given a price, but these merchants trade more foolishly — for marked altars \[drawn\] from the people, and a price they do not understand. And there is no doubt that it would be infinitely less evil for the pope to sell an ox or a beast of burden, because the danger lies infinitely more hidden \[in the trafficking of souls\]; a blinded layman would resist anyone taking away his ox or cow, but in the trafficking of his own soul through antichristian seduction, it is permitted to deal with it in whatever way one wishes. For just as Christ entrusted to Petrus the care of souls, so they implicitly hold that antichrist has entrusted to the pseudo-pope the capture of souls; and the whole people in this matter is so forgetful of their own salvation that they would stand more boldly unto death for temporal human law, but for the divine law of their own twofold nature would not dare raise their head — to such a degree has the agent of Lucifer blinded the people of God. For it is plain that the pope strives so inordinately for provisions over kingdoms that he neither knows nor loves, with regard to curates, not on account of the love he has for souls, but on account of the ineffable domination by which he seeks to oppress them and on account of the gain of his own temporal goods — which \[he pursues\] with supreme stupidity, the consideration of salvation set aside; for otherwise, according to the teaching of the blessed Gregorius, C.1 q.6, cap.
