The whiteness of leprosy \[see 2 Kgs. 5:27\] signifies the whiteness of the hypocrisy by which they simulate sanctity in the eyes of the faithful; and from the first simoniacal pope to the last of this generation all are struck, as if irremedially, by this leprosy. And so \[the pope\] first renders the worthy unworthy; he does not promote but condemns — and more grievously than Gesi, inasmuch as, like a second god, he blasphemously justifies sin more dangerously against Christ, and so steals most sacrilegiously from the goods of the church, which are not his own, as the foremost Antichrist.

And as regards the first fruits of our superiors whose collation proceeded from the same source: those who wish to excuse them from simoniacal wickedness in *Causa* 1, questio 3, cap. *Si quis obiecerit*, strive beyond their powers; and, what is most detestable, so long-standing and far more openly manifest simony regarding patrons and their clerics has been defended in England. This vice has, however, some excuse in their case: first, because they do not sell a benefice for first fruits as their predecessors do, but as lawful men receive confirmation from a heresiarch prelate. Second, they suggest to the people, falsely though, that without those first fruits they could not perform the office assigned to them; and so they do not simoniacally hand over a spiritual benefice, but rather offer preaching, correction, and other spiritual gifts as humble servants of Christ and the people. But I leave to the people the task of refuting that lie. The form established by Petrus and the other apostles must therefore be considered, according to which they ought to labor among the people; and according to the need of that labor let them accept for themselves the voluntary contributions of the people — not for carnal pleasure, not for lavish worldly display nor for superfluous household exchange, but let them apply their study to performing the office of Christ as fruitfully as possible for the spirit and as lightly and humbly as possible before the world.
