Nor does it matter whether that gift is given to the principal party, to the intermediary procuring it, or to the agent laboring on his behalf. For from the corrupt root the whole consenting accompaniment is infected; so when patronage is pre-arranged with the community for whom the appropriation is to be made, it seems that one who acquires the appropriation by reason of such an end is a simoniac, because for the sake of that appropriation as the principal end he prepares costly means and consequently buys that appropriation inordinately. For so one buys the kingdom of heaven inordinately when, without the oil of devotion, he performs for the sake of this a work that is good in its genus, as is plain in Matt. 6; and again the person of the community gives to itself the first prelacy in the church on account of a suspected simony. For although an aggregate person might present from among itself the more capable member for the benefice of which it is patron, and indeed a simple person might in some cases present himself to the bishop for a benefice — if this is possible in a case where he is more capable than all others — yet that the same aggregate person should perpetually and successively present itself for its own benefice seems unreasonable, most especially because such prolonged and constant conferral requires a great and fully prudent knowledge of the person who ought to be thus presented; but neither the bishop nor the community knows even approximately the capability of the person who is to be so presented in future, nor the capability of all the members of the aggregate person as presently constituted, because often in such convents a Judas lurks.
