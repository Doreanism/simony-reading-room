# TREATISE ON SIMONY

## CHAPTER I.

After the general treatment of heresy, it remains to deal with its particular forms. There are three kinds of heresy that are most notorious: namely, simony, apostasy, and blasphemy. These are not distinguished as mutually exclusive, since the species both of virtues and of vices are interconnected. Nevertheless, their defining principles are distinct: apostasy consists in the general principle whereby a man deviates from the religion of God; blasphemy in the principle whereby it imputes false accusation against the virtue of God; but simony in the principle whereby it strives to rupture the ordering of God. And in this way, through this threefold offense, the entire Trinity is wronged: through apostasy, God the Father, who according to pure and undefiled religion powerfully binds to himself the bride of Christ; God the Son, who, being the power of God and the wisdom of God, is daily blasphemed by the second heresy; and God the Holy Spirit, who, out of the highest goodness, wisely and peacefully orders his household, is provoked to anger by the perversity of simony that acts contrary to his ordering. For the simoniac, resisting the Holy Spirit, strives to dissolve this benign order blasphemously, and thereby the peace. Therefore, since according to the testimony of Truth the sin against the Holy Spirit is unforgivable both in this age and in the age to come, it seems fitting to begin with that sin, since simony is a leprosy which, by reason of the nature of the disease and its persistent hardening, it is impossible to cure except by an extraordinary miracle of God —
