The reason, however, why the greater part of the people more willingly associate and do business with such persons is that blind foolishness and simoniacal heresy are the cause, for it is written in 2 John: “If anyone comes to you and does not bring this teaching, do not receive him into your house, nor say ‘Hail’ to him; for whoever says ‘Hail’ to him shares in his evil works.” Now that teaching is the preeminence of the pastoral office in Christ and in the members who follow him — which the simoniacs not only do not bring, but utterly confound. If the greeting is forbidden \[toward them\], how much more the administration of the poor’s goods?

But it is objected: since the greater part of the Church, and especially its leading members, are infected with this heresy, there remains nowhere for the faithful to flee — since one seeking out deserts would be struck by \[the heresy of\] senseless consent and the denial of assistance. To this it is said that every Christian is commanded to flee from following the simoniacs in their practices, and the weak are commanded to flee from bodily association with them for fear of contamination by the leprosy; but the difficulty seems to apply to those in the cloister, since almost the entire multitude of them is infected with this leprosy, and those who flee will be imprisoned as apostates. But it is established that, since God is immeasurable while Simon is a wretched creature, narrowly condemned to punishment, no one can flee from God but anyone can flee from Simon everywhere; for each person can from freedom of will prepare a dwelling for his God, into which Simon does not dare to enter — so that devout cloisterers grieve over the simoniacs and, guarding against consent to them, suppress those stains as far as they can, yet they are tempted by a thousand devious paths
