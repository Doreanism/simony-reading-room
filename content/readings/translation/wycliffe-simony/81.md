Whence, to exclude such sophistries of the devil, a thousand laws have been written by popes and holy doctors; as in Book V of the *Decretals*, *De Symonia*, chapter 1, it is prescribed: “For ordination, therefore, we wholly forbid the giving of anything whatsoever for letters and tablets by the person being ordained.” And in \[*Decretum*, causa\] 1, quaestio 3, chapter 5, *Si quis obiecerit* \[*If anyone should object*\], this sophism is removed, where it is said “that although the Church cannot exist here without temporalities, just as the soul cannot exist without the body, yet whoever sells either of them will not escape God’s judgment. Let no one therefore buy or sell anything ecclesiastical, nor let the people or sustenance be paid for this, before or after.” And a grave penalty is imposed upon the bishop who falls into this vice, either personally or through his minister, as is clear from causa 1, quaestio 1, chapter *Quicquid* \[*Whatever*\]. And the penalty is the reward mentioned above — namely, \[the loss of\] what he exacts in his capacity as a minister of this kind, or what a cleric in his capacity as a cleric administers to the people — since in all such matters he ought to be a minister of God, who gives grace beyond human operation to the one who ministers and acts in the ministry of his allotted office. How blasphemous a presumption it is to sell so greedily so spiritual a ministry which Christ in Matt. 10 commanded to be given freely! Whence from a council of the Roman Church, causa 1, quaestio 1, it is written: “It has been noted that in certain places coins are given for the reception of chrism, and likewise for baptism and communion. This simoniacal heresy has been condemned and anathematized by the synod; and it has decreed that henceforth nothing shall be required for ordination, for chrism or baptism or balsam, nor for burial or communion; but that the free gifts of God shall be dispensed freely.” Such laws are countless, but through the ignorance of the people and the sloth of the bishop — with the devil’s cunning — they have been suppressed.
