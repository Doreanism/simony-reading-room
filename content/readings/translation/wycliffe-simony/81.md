In a third form, it is said that a summoner or some other lackey will purchase from the bishop or his cleric the office of conveying such matters, and he then demands, by the devil’s fraud, a larger or smaller sum of money for the service of conveying; and so with the pennies of confession, with gifts artfully introduced for baptismal rites, so that just as a blessing is turned into a curse, so the gathering of alms is turned into an exaction, and the episcopal endowment is turned into a preparation for the plundering of the people. And this is necessary when they, boasting of their spiritual power beyond that of the holy apostles, prefigure — like another Roboam — the division of the Church through simoniacal heresy, just as Roboam, through a display of pride infinitely less evil, prefigured the schism of Solomon’s kingdom by Jeroboam, as Crisostomus notes in Homily 1 of the *Opus Imperfectum*. How, I ask, does a bishop richly endowed with the goods of the poor hire at great expense servants to care for his superfluous horses, and innumerable meddlesome attendants quite irrelevant to the episcopal office, and yet does not care that he negligently commits simony in a thousand ways in the ministry of his province? Let him either have ministers whom he fully supports from the common goods, or let him cease to infect the people with the simoniacal leprosy through which he ought to heal them — lest he incurably defile diseased sheep not previously cleansed. How, I ask, would a faithful bishop demand from his minister a profit compounded of worldly service owed to himself, while he cares less about the spiritual ministry of the common Church — indeed, he permits his ministers, either out of favor to the world or to have them at a cheaper price, to stray in manifold simoniacal ways?
