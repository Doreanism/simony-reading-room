Here it is clear that a false conclusion is drawn contrary to what was said above, since it stands that a man may lawfully be promoted by the intercession or payment of a friend without his own knowledge; for otherwise no one would be persuaded to accept an ecclesiastical dignity. In that case he would be receiving by intercession or payment a spiritual gift — yet this is lawful given the presupposition of fitness, since otherwise the apostles, who received the apostolate at the command of the Lord, would thereby have sinned through simony. But in this matter both intellect and affections must be purified on each side: the intellect must wholly be informed by the house of God, so that the superintendent may know according to the law of scripture to lead his sheep through Christ’s pastures; secondly, every sin that hinders from this office must be emptied out, and especially pride, avarice, and carnal sins; and thirdly, he must be of such character and conduct that he may discharge the curate’s office efficaciously and virtuously — but who is such a one, and we shall praise him? The reason why a man ought not to seek that office for himself is this: that office carries as much peril to the soul as any other bodily danger, and without such a burden he could advance more, even for the benefit of all those over whom he presides. Therefore it is an act of inescapable presumption that in seeking that office for himself one permits the idolatry of simony. Hence according to the laws, a man who might otherwise be competent for governing disqualifies himself by seeking that office. And this appears to be the judgment of Gregorius set forth in \[Gratian’s Decretum\] questio 6, cap. *Sicut*: “Just as one who when invited refuses remains excused and is to be brought to the holy altars, so one who of his own accord seeks it or thrusts himself forward importunately is beyond doubt to be rejected. For one who strives to climb to higher things — what else does he do but grow by diminishing, and ascend outwardly while descending inwardly into the depths?
