Concerning these superstitious Traffickers in Prayers who were to come, St. Peter prophesied, calling them lying masters in 2 Peter 2, saying: “And there shall be among you lying masters, who shall bring in sects of perdition.” And below: “And through covetousness shall they with feigned words make merchandise of you.” Note that he says “make merchandise.” Glossa: Not for the sake of Religion, but out of covetousness, so that they may gain something, they invent new doctrines; nor do they purchase you for God, but rather acquire, or strive to acquire, some advantage for themselves from you. The text continues: “Whose judgment now of a long time lingereth not, and their damnation slumbereth not.”

Since, therefore, the aforesaid Seducers do the aforesaid things, and many other such things, to the flocks of others in the ways just described, they are deservedly called Wolves in the Divine Scriptures; and by this also they are distinguished from the true Apostles. Hence on Acts 20, upon that saying of the Apostle “I have coveted no man’s silver, or gold, or apparel,” the Glossa says: By this are Wolves known, that is, Pseudo-Apostles, because they covet such things. And below, upon that saying of the Apostle “For such things as were needful for me and them that are with me, these hands have ministered,” the Glossa says: The example of working, even for Bishops, is a sign by which they are distinguished from Wolves — that is, from Pseudo-Apostles. These superstitious Pseudo\[-Apostles\], painting over their covetousness with the color of Religion, furnish to the Devil the arms of Christ, when they make their superstitions (which are surely iniquities) to be esteemed as piety. Hence upon Matthew 23, “Woe to you Scribes and Pharisees, Hypocrites, who devour the houses of Widows, praying long prayers,” Chrysostomus says: You who paint your covetousness with the color of Religion, as it were furnish to the Devil the arms of Christ, so that iniquity may be loved while it is esteemed as piety.
