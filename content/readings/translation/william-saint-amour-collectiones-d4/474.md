\[As if to say\]: *The preachers of Christ ought to lodge with those whom they can reasonably judge will not consider themselves burdened by their expenses, but rather will consider that a favor is being done to them.*

If, therefore, any preachers (whose canonical calling or mission is not established) habitually seek lodgings that are not more honorable but more luxurious, running from house to house in order to be better lodged or better provided for; or seek or accept lodgings or other gifts from unworthy persons — that is, from those whom they can reasonably judge not to consider that a favor is being done to them in this matter, but rather to consider themselves burdened by them — they do not appear to be true apostles, but pseudo-apostles.

## Sign XLVII

The forty-seventh sign may be this: that true apostles, unless compelled by the utmost necessity, do not accept the alms or gifts of those who are not yet reformed and still persist in their sins; for such people give rather in order not to be rebuked than for the sake of God, and therefore they have no fruit from their giving. Hence in 2 Corinthians 8 the Apostle says, *But they gave themselves first to the Lord, then to us by the will of God.* Gloss: *Because, correcting their former errors and vices of conduct, they devoted themselves to God; then they offered their goods to the brethren. For otherwise it was not to be received from them, because gifts blind the eyes and bend the force of authority. But those who give in order not to be rebuked have no fruit from their giving.* Likewise in 1 Corinthians 6, on the words *All things are lawful for me, but not all things are expedient,* the Gloss says: *If he were to accept support from those whom he was rebuking for such great vices, he would weaken the authority of the teacher. For one cannot be rebuked insistently by one from whom one receives — especially when the giver is prompt to give in order to soften the teacher’s resolve against him.*

If, therefore, any preachers (whose canonical calling or mission is not established), without the greatest necessity, knowingly and willingly receive as a matter of custom the gifts of usurers, or of robbers, or of other sinners (who still persist in their sins and refuse to be reformed, but give only in order not to be rebuked) — since they receive from those from whom nothing should be received (namely, those who have no fruit from their giving) — they appear to seek the gift more than the fruit, and therefore they do not appear to be true apostles, but pseudo-apostles.
