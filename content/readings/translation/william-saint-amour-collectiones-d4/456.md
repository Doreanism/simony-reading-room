# Collectiones, Distinction IV: Signs XL–XLVIII

## Sign XL

The fortieth Sign may be that true Apostles do not preach for the sake of gain, but solely for the salvation of souls. Hence in 2 Corinthians 12 the Apostle says, “I seek not the things that are yours.” The Gloss: that is, your riches in gold and silver; “BUT YOU.” The Gloss: to save. Likewise the Gloss: the Apostle was not seeking the gift, but the fruit; lest he be thought, as it were, a vendor of the Gospel. But the pseudo-apostles preach for the sake of gain, though they feign to preach for the salvation of souls; for they are eager for profit, as has been shown above in dist. 1, §. *Quidam autem canonice*. And in 1 Timothy 6 the Apostle, speaking of the pseudo-apostles, says, “Supposing gain to be godliness.” The Gloss: who therefore do a work of pi\[e\]-
