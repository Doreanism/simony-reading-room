Just as birds that feed by tearing bodies apart scan from nearby for bodies that have collapsed in sickness and are about to fall, so this man, who looms over death, also flies around cadavers. Then, once those sick people have died, under the pretext of his own voluntary poverty and his feigned sanctity, they turn the money entrusted to their execution or disposition to their own uses, or to those of their associates, trying to show by self-devised arguments that the money cannot be spent on better uses; and thus, contrary to the teaching of Ambrose, *They become Procurators of secular affairs, who ought to serve the worship of Religion.* And contrary to the teaching of Hieronymus, *They become Dispensers of other people’s goods, who are thought to have despised their own resources*, as was shown above in dist. 2, §. *But perhaps the Defender of Curiosity will object.*

Those, however, who entrust their goods to be distributed by such men on account of the appearance of sanctity which they display outwardly, as though they would dispense them more faithfully, would act more prudently if they restored with their own hand, while yet living, those goods which they may have extorted from the Faithful of Christ by robbery or usury; and if what has been justly acquired they were to distribute, with their own hand — or at least by their own express will, through someone rightly not under suspicion — to the helpless Poor who cannot labor, or to those who for legitimate cause are ashamed to beg. For they ought to consider that Judas, although he was an Apostle, and — according to present justice — was temporally chosen by the Lord, who cannot be deceived, and on that account, because of the appearance of sanctity which he outwardly displayed, was reputed most holy, nevertheless carried off and stole the money that was committed to him to be distributed to the Poor, as is read in John 12, and as was shown above in dist. 2, §. *But perhaps someone will say.* Whence Hieronymus, *To Nepotianus*: *Distribute with your own hand to the brethren the refreshments of your expenditures; for faith among men is rare; call to mind the purses of Judas.*
