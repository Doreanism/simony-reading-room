Whether it be raised of turf or of variegated stone from a foreign people makes no difference. Know that a man is sheltered just as well by thatch as by gold.*

But perhaps the aforesaid men will feign an excuse, saying that those precious and costly buildings (since they are not the buildings’ lords) they do not construct for themselves, but for the lords of those places, who have granted them merely the use of them, as was said above in this same distinction, at the eleventh Sign, § *Item ex eo*.

But since the lords of those places, granting them only the use \[of them\], as they say, are rich and not needy, how do the aforesaid Pretenders dare out of begged alms (by which the hungry Poor ought to live) to construct so many and such great buildings for rich lords? Can they not on this account deservedly be said to be crueler than all Robbers? — since Hieronymus says *Ad Nepotianum*, *To have received what is to be distributed to the hungry Poor, who are many, and* (*which is a manifest crime*) *to subtract something from it, surpasses the cruelty of all Robbers.*

Likewise, not content with their old Basilicas, which are sufficient and of ample beauty, they rashly tear them down under the pretext of the honor of God and of the Saints, and they build for themselves, from begged alms, greater, more ornate, more precious, and more costly Basilicas, importunately requesting superfluous expenditures from the Faithful of Christ; not heeding that Moses, on discovering that the people were offering more for the expenses of the Tabernacle than was necessary, ordered it to be proclaimed by the Herald’s voice that no Man or Woman should offer anything further for the work of the Sanctuary, as is read in Exodus 36.
