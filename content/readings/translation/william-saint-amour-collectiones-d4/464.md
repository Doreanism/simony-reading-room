They likewise suggest to their aforesaid benefactors that it is an excellent thing to expend their money (even if ill-gotten) upon such uses (which, on account of a feigned veneration of God and the Saints, they call Pious), and, as it were, to establish perpetually enduring memorials of themselves for the remembrance of others in time to come; whereas it would be better either to restore it to those from whom it was wrongly taken, or, with their consent, at least to bestow it upon the hungry Poor of Christ, upon those unable to labor, or upon those who from reasonable cause are ashamed to beg. Against their superstitious assertion Chrysostomus inveighs upon that passage of Matthew 23, “You who build the sepulchers of the Prophets,” etc., saying thus: “Foolish one, what does this memorial profit you after death, if you are tormented where you are, while you are praised where you are not?” Likewise there Chrysostomus: “What kind of justice is this, to give gifts to the dead and to strip the living? to take from the blood of the wretched, and to offer it to God? For this is to offer to God, but to wish to make God a partner of one’s violence; so that, when He has willingly received money offered to Him out of sin, He may consent to the sin.” Likewise there Chrysostomus: “Do you wish to build a house of God? give to the Faithful and the Poor that by which they may live; for in buildings men dwell, but God in holy men.” Hence blessed Stephen in Acts 7 says: “He does not dwell on high in things made with hands, as He says through the Prophet, HEAVEN IS MY THRONE, AND THE EARTH IS THE FOOTSTOOL OF MY FEET.” Hence also Paul in Acts 17 says: “God, who made the World and all things that are in it, since He is Lord of heaven and earth, does not dwell in Temples made with hands.” It would therefore be better to expend the money offered by Christians, or extorted from them by importunate solicitations, on alms for the Poor of Christ, than to place it in the building of Temples, or in other immoderate, sumptuous, and superfluous things.
