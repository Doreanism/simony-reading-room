For to give otherwise (that is, to give because of present embarrassment, or because of the importunity of the one asking, or for some other unbecoming cause) is to lose both the thing and the merit. Whence in 2 Corinthians 9 the Apostle says: *Every one.* Gloss: *Let him give.* AS *he has determined.* Gloss: *That is, has foreordained.* IN *his heart.* Gloss: *That is, in the counsel of reason. For he who gives unwillingly on account of present embarrassment has no reward; and therefore let each give as he has purposed in his heart to give.* NOT *out of sadness, or out of necessity.* Gloss: *That is, sad or compelled, but willingly.* For *a cheerful* Giver, Gloss, *who gives from a good will,* the Lord LOVES. Gloss: *That is, he approves and rewards him, not the one who rewards sorrowfully. For he who gives in order to be rid of the tediousness of the one interrupting him, not in order to refresh the bowels of the needy, loses both the thing and the merit.* And below: *If you do it with sadness, it is done from you, it is not you who do it.* Note what the aforesaid Gloss says: *Let each give as he has disposed in his heart.* From which it appears that one ought not to beg from anyone, at least not importunately, except in the greatest necessity; but one ought to wait until a man gives as he has disposed, and as much as he has disposed in his heart to give, so that by thus giving willingly he may acquire merit with God — lest perhaps, not willingly but because of the importunity of the one asking, or because of embarrassment, or for other non-meritorious causes, he give more than he had disposed to give, or give when he had not disposed to give, and thus lose both the thing and the merit. Likewise in 1 Corinthians 16, the Apostle, speaking of the collections to be made for the work of the Poor, says: *Let every one of you lay by him in store, putting aside what shall please him.* And he says this so that his gift may be gratuitous; for, as Seneca says in book 2 *De Officiis*: *He has not received freely who has received after asking.* And below: *Nothing costs more dearly than what is purchased by prayers.*
