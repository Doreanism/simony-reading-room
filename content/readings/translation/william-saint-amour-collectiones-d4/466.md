Such men, moreover, who construct the aforesaid buildings, appear to turn loaves into stones — that is, they turn the loaves of the Poor (namely, the begged alms, from which Christ’s loaves ought to be distributed to the Poor) into heaps of stones, that is, into costly and superfluous houses of stone. And for this reason they seem to be crueler and more merciless than the Devil himself; since the Devil, on the contrary, asked that stones be turned into loaves, saying to the Lord, “Command that these stones be made bread” (Matthew 4).

If, therefore, any Preachers (whose canonical calling or mission is not established) persistently and importunately beg from their Hearers — even from those not subject to them — under the pretext of alms, or of the veneration of God and His Saints, and indiscreetly receive not only the expenses of preaching, but also grain, wine, money, and other gifts, for their own and many others’ yearly maintenance, and even for constructing superfluous and costly buildings and for satisfying other superfluities or pleasures (although they profess the highest poverty and mendicancy), and do so generally with scandal to those from whom they beg or receive, or to others as well — they do not appear to be true Apostles, but rather can be suspected of being False ones.

## Sign XLII

The Forty-second Sign may be this: that true Apostles evangelize not unwillingly, but willingly — that is, they do not preach compelled by necessity of livelihood; for otherwise they would not have the reward of eternal glory for their preaching. Whence Augustinus, in the book *De Opere Monachorum*, chapter 10, reciting and expounding the words of the Apostle Paul in 1 Corinthians 9, speaks thus: “FOR IF I PREACH THE GOSPEL,” says the Apostle, “IT IS NO GLORY TO ME, FOR NECESSITY LIES UPON ME” — namely, of sustaining this life. “FOR WOE IS ME IF I PREACH NOT THE GOSPEL.” That is, to my own harm I shall not preach the Gospel, because I shall be tormented with hunger, and shall have nothing to live on. “BUT IF I DO THIS WILLINGLY, I HAVE A REWARD.” He says he does it willingly if he does it compelled by no necessity of propping up this life; and on this account he has a reward with God — namely, everlasting glory. “BUT IF UNWILLINGLY,” he says, “A DISPENSATION IS COMMITTED TO ME.” That is, if by necessity of carrying on this life
