# Question One of the Twenty-Fifth Distinction.

Because the Master speaks about simony in the second and third chapters of this twenty-fifth distinction, I therefore raise several questions concerning it. First, what is simony? For a definition of terms is the starting point of any inquiry. In the questions that follow I shall speak in particular about cases suspected of the corruption of simony. Simony is commonly defined as follows: Simony is the deliberate will to buy or sell a spiritual thing, or something annexed to a spiritual thing. But Hostiensis says this definition is incomplete and that one must add “with a subsequent act,” because simony does not consist in mere desire alone. He cites canons to this effect, namely C.1 q.1 c. *Qui studet*. And the canonists now commonly follow him in the final chapter of this title. But the definition of the others does not entirely satisfy either, because the one he himself constructs is even less adequate, and he adds that clause badly.
