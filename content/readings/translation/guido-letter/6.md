To those I say: Where is what you gave? These wretches have wretchedly made exchange with one another — the one to serve his love of money, the other to satisfy his own wickedness. Therefore neither does the one possess the price, nor do the others possess Christ. So too those who sell and buy the Paraclete neither possess the price in perpetuity, nor — unless they repent — will they possess Christ without end. Rejoice, O Christian! For what Judas sold and the Jew bought, you have acquired; both have worked great good for us at their own cost. If he who sold Christ was delivered to such destruction, what will become of his fellows who buy and sell the Paraclete? Are those who are equal in crime not equal in punishment? He sold the Son; these, insofar as they are able, endeavor to sell the Holy Spirit.

Can one who is accursed transform bread into the body of Christ by his blessing — especially when the Lord declares that he will curse whatever such a one blesses? If heretical priests cannot even speak words of exhortation, how could they be able to turn wine into the blood of Christ? And if the Lord has commanded that his body and blood are by no means to be given to murderers, adulterers, robbers, and others entangled in grave sins until they have made satisfaction — how will he himself grant the priesthood to one who usurps it through the simoniac heresy, which is condemned everywhere?
