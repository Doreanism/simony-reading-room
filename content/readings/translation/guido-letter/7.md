\[Are we to\] hear the Masses of such men, when even praying together with them subjects us to excommunication? Indeed, to believe them to be priests at all is altogether erroneous, since Peter says to Simon: May your money perish with you, because you thought that the gift of God could be possessed by money \[Acts 8:20\]. When he says “you thought,” it is clear that he is condemned not for what he did but for what he believed himself able to do — and yet to “think” is a lesser thing than to “believe.” Since therefore the damnation of heretics, of whatever kind they be, is certain and beyond doubt, what has been gained by driving Arians, Sabellians, Photinians, and the unclean Manicheans from the whole world, if the simoniac and neophyte heresy — which, as blessed Gregory says, crept in ahead of all others into the holy Church through the devil’s fraud — defiles, as it were, the primary and greatest chastity of the Church with most foul pollution? Therefore in God and for God’s sake we beg and implore your Excellency and all the faithful of Christ: if you desire to have a share with Christ, press forward with all your might so that so savage a pestilence — which has already punished countless peoples everywhere with eternal death by its rampaging — may be utterly destroyed by your authority and example, lest henceforth, God forbid, the sin against the Holy Spirit be committed, which is forgiven neither in this world nor in the world to come \[cf. Matthew 12:32\].
