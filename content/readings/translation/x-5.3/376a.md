## CAP. IX.

Taken from above in the preceding chapter.

From the Lateran Council.

Since in the body of the Church all things ought to be conducted with charity, and what has been received freely should be freely given, it is altogether horrible that in certain churches venality is said to have found a place — so much so that for installing bishops, abbots, or any other ecclesiastical persons in their seat, or for inducting priests into a church, as well as for the burial and funeral rites of the dead, for the blessing of those to be married, or for other sacraments, something is demanded, and one in need cannot receive these things unless he has taken care to fill the hand of the one bestowing them. Many persons suppose this to be lawful to them because they believe that the law of death has prevailed through long-standing custom — not sufficiently attending, blinded as they are by greed, to the fact that sins are all the graver the longer they have kept the unhappy soul in bondage. Lest, therefore, these things be done hereafter — whether for installing ecclesiastical persons in their seat, or instituting priests, or burying the dead, or blessing those to be married, or for conferring or having conferred other sacraments — we forbid any exaction whatsoever most strictly. If anyone presumes to act contrary to this, let him know that he shall have a portion with Gehazi, whose deed he imitates by the exaction of a shameful gift.

## CAP. X.

For the consecration of churches nothing beyond procuration is to be demanded.

To the Bishop of Vich. \[*Lat.* Vigiliensi; Vic, in Catalonia.\]

Since the Roman Church, over which, though unworthy, we preside, is the mother and teacher of all churches, we are compelled by the duty of the governance we have undertaken to respond, as the Lord shall grant us, to the consultations of each individual, so that doubtful questions may be fully resolved by the foresight of the Apostolic See. You have asked, brother bishop, that we write back to your fraternity whether you ought to receive anything for the consecration of churches beyond procuration. And we respond to your consultation as follows: you can and ought to demand nothing for the consecration of churches beyond procuration. But a moderate procuration — which the Apostle speaks of when he says, “No one is compelled to serve at his own expense” — you may demand from that same church.
