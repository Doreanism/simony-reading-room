# TITLE III.

ON SIMONY, AND THAT NOTHING SHALL BE DEMANDED OR PROMISED FOR SPIRITUAL THINGS.

## CAP. I.

Gregorius, presiding over a general synod.

In ordaining a bishop, the pontiff lays on his hand; a minister reads the gospel lesson; a notary writes the letter of his confirmation. Just as the bishop ought not to sell the hand he lays on, so neither the minister nor the notary ought to sell his voice or his pen in the ordination. We therefore prohibit entirely that the person being ordained give anything for the ordination or for the use of the pallium, or for charters and documents.

## CAP. II.

From the Council of Mainz.

Whoever expels by payment of money a priest lawfully ordained in a church, and thereby seizes it for himself in such a manner, shall be deposed in every way.

## CAP. III.

Pope Deodatus.

If the Lord and Master of all — who was without sin — gave to one person the right to bring accusation, how much more must one who is known to be defiled with the stain of Simon \[Magus\] be convicted on the testimony of a single witness? Therefore no Catholic should be dismissed in such a case; rather, he should be urgently entreated with earnest prayers to speak the truth and lay bare the simoniac frenzy.
