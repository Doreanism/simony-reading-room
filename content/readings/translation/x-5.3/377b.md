## CAP. XVI.

Money ought not to be exacted from churches for chrism, even if custom has established it.

### The Same.

Those things that proceed from the root of avarice and cupidity and burst forth into the appearance of simoniacal wickedness must be utterly eradicated; and what springs from thorns and briars must be cut back by the sickle of apostolic governance. We have heard, however, that with reckless presumption you extort money from churches for chrism, calling it by various names — now a cathedraticum, sometimes a paschal payment, and sometimes an episcopal custom. Because this is recognized as simony and engenders peril to souls, we command you by apostolic writ and in commanding we order, that you exact from the churches no further such payments under pretext of any custom or prelacy, knowing for certain that if you presume to do this with reckless boldness, you may not without cause dread peril to your order and dignity. Moreover, etc.

## CAP. XVII.

To do homage in order to obtain spiritual things is simony.

### The Same.

It has been made known to us by your diligent report that H., archdeacon of Canterbury, enticed you with many promises and, by the urging and solicitation of certain great men, induced you to do him homage and swear fealty, on the condition that he would furnish you an ecclesiastical benefice each year. Since, therefore, this sort of obligation is sufficiently illicit and contrary to the advancement of your salvation and his — as your discernment is well aware — you have most urgently and humbly besought us to grant you absolution from it, adding that you would accept nothing further whatsoever from him under the said benefice arrangement. Therefore, giving heed to your petition in this matter as honorable and worthy of favor, and especially since this homage and fealty as thus given are known to be contrary to divine and human law, we absolve you from the homage and fealty of the aforesaid archdeacon by the authority of Blessed Peter and our own, on the condition that you receive from some bishop or discreet priest a penance fitting to the offense, and that you endeavor to be reconciled to God in accordance with his counsel. And lest you should be tainted with any mark or infamy on this account, we have thought fit to grant you the letters of our absolution, by which you may stand safe and fortified against the bites of slanderers, and which you may produce in testimony of our indulgence.

## CAP. XVIII.

An ordination or consecration is not deemed simoniacal even if something was given to the ordaining minister, provided it was not given by agreement.

### The Same to the Archbishop of Esztergom.
