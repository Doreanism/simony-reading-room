## CAP. XLIV.

Once received as a canon, a prebend and income are owed, notwithstanding custom, so that one should not observe the corrupt custom of giving something temporal to the canons themselves.

Gregorius IX to the Prior of S. Thomas of Ascoli.

Jacobus, a canon of Ascoli, has set forth that, though he has been received as a canon and brother in the church of Aprutino, the canons of that same church refuse to assign him his share of the revenues and his prebend, alleging a certain custom by which a newly received canon is obliged to provide them a meal. We therefore direct that, if this is so, you compel the said canons, any appeal set aside, to provide for him in revenues and prebend just as for any one of the others, notwithstanding such a custom.

## CAP. XLV.

One who has ordained someone under a promise not to seek provision from him: the ordainer is suspended from conferring orders for three years, the presenter likewise from exercising orders for three years; the person ordained is suspended from the order in perpetuity, and dispensation is granted to them only through the apostolic see.

Idem.

If anyone has ordained or presented someone for ordination, receiving from him a promise or oath that he will not trouble the ordainer regarding his provision, let the ordainer know himself suspended from conferring orders, the presenter from exercising orders, for three years, and the person so ordained from the order thus received, until they shall have merited to obtain dispensation in this matter through the apostolic see.

## CAP. XLVI.

This canon contains two rulings: the second beginning at the words “and regarding resignations.” On account of its brevity and the variety of readings it is not summarized in any other way.

Idem.

Upon receipt of our mandate, directing you to dispense — in accordance with the constitution of the general council — with monks who through simony, having given something, obtained places in monasteries: (and further on:) Replying briefly to your inquiry, we declare that the apostolic mandate extends also to abbots, and to resignations of spiritual and temporal things that are acquired in such manner not by any pact but with mutual preceding disposition of mind; in which case it suffices for those who have transgressed to satisfy their Creator through penance alone, they not being held guilty of simony of this kind.
