## CAP. XI.

One convicted of simony is to be deposed; likewise one who is under ill repute and cannot purge himself.

To the Archbishop of Toledo.

Regarding the matter in which the king and his princes are said to have received money from Bernard, formerly bishop of Osma, so that they would give their consent to his election, and in which the same bishop of Osma is said to have promised certain revenues in benefices to an archdeacon and a priorship to a certain cleric before his own election, so that both of them — namely the archdeacon and the cleric — would give their consent to him: to your prudence we respond as follows. If it is manifest that the same archdeacon and cleric received the promised things for that iniquitous and detestable cause, or if they have confessed this in court or been lawfully convicted thereof, they are to be perpetually deposed from the ministry of the altar. If, however, it is not manifest, and they have not been convicted or confessed, but are only burdened with public ill repute, canonical purgation is to be imposed upon them. If they fail in that purgation, they are to be punished as the authors of so great a crime. The king and his princes, moreover, you ought to admonish carefully and diligently persuade that, if they have received anything from the aforementioned bishop so that they would give their consent to his election, they restore it without diminution to the church of Osma, since they cannot retain it without grave peril to their souls.

## CAP. XII.

It is simony to bestow an ecclesiastical benefice in return for temporal service.

To the Archbishop of York.

When R., a cleric of Pontefract, and V., a nobleman, son of Godric, were present before us in a dispute that had arisen between them over a certain church which the same R. claimed had been given to him by the aforesaid knight, and when the same V. was unable to remain any longer, upon departing he appointed our beloved son Master Miles, your messenger, as his sufficient attorney in that case. And when we learned from the mouth of the aforesaid R. that the said knight had promised to give him the aforementioned church if he could advance some business of that knight before our presence — and that this promise therefore contained simoniacal wickedness — we judged that silence should be imposed upon the same R. regarding that church. And therefore we command your fraternity by apostolic letter, directing that you by no means permit the said knight to be burdened by the aforesaid R. or by others regarding the aforesaid church on account of the said dispute.
