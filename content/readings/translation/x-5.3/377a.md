## CAP. XIV.

It is simony to conceal a sinner for payment or favor, to reconcile one who has not done penance, or to exclude from reconciliation one who has done due penance.

### The Same.

Let no priest receive any gift or any temporal emolument — indeed, to his spiritual detriment — from any public sinner or person guilty of incest, in order to conceal that person’s sin from the bishop or his ministers; nor, out of regard for any person’s rank, kinship, or familiarity, making himself a sharer in the sins of others, let him refuse to make the matter known to the bishop. Neither let anyone, by favor or partiality, bring to reconciliation a penitent or one insufficiently penitent, and bear testimony of reconciliation to that person; nor by any malice remove from reconciliation another penitent who has done due penance — for both are simony.

## CAP. XV.

A church cannot be bought or redeemed. Dist. II, regarding the letter.

### The Same to the Bishop of Vich and the Prior of Acra.

We have received the complaint of the monks of Acra transmitted to us, that when they had obtained a certain manor called Vella, with its appurtenances, and the church of Genen. from the monks of Cardona under an annual rent of fifteen pounds, and had received a charter from those monks, a certain prior of theirs, without consulting the chapter, resigned the said manor, church, and charter to the monks of Cardona in exchange for money received. Wherefore, since the aforesaid monks of Acra complain that they have been unjustly aggrieved, and it falls to us to correct whatever has been attempted contrary to reason, we commit the case itself to your experience, and by the authority of the present letter we command you that, having summoned the parties before your presence, you hear more diligently what is put forward on each side, and bring the aforesaid case to a conclusion by agreement or judgment, setting aside all recourse to appeal, knowing that just as that church could not be bought, so neither can it be redeemed. But if the monks of Cardona, being lawfully cited, should disdain to appear before you and to stand to your judgment, you shall induct the monks of Acra themselves into possession of the things in dispute, all objection and appeal ceasing, and shall not permit them to be molested therein without due judicial process. And if either of you cannot be present to carry out these matters, the other shall nonetheless proceed.
