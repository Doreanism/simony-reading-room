those prelates who have been introduced and consecrated \[in succession from the Apostolic See\], distinguished only by their mitres and croziers, represent the Church and ought to be received as the Church — and therefore they cannot err. How so? Because they are pastors of the Church and consecrated to the Lord. But were not Aaron and the other rulers of Israel pastors? Yet Aaron and his sons, though already designated priests, nevertheless erred when they fashioned the calf. Why, by this reasoning, would those four hundred prophets who lied to Ahab not have represented the Church? Yet the Church stood on the side of Micaiah — solitary and contemptible, yet from whose mouth the truth proceeded. Did not the prophets bear before them the name and face of the Church when they rose up against Jeremiah with one impulse and threatened menacingly that it could not be that the law should perish from the priest, counsel from the wise, or the word from the prophet? Against the entire nation of the prophets, Jeremiah alone was sent by the Lord to announce that the law would perish from the priest, counsel from the wise, and the word from the prophet. Did not such splendor shine in that council which the chief priests, scribes, and Pharisees had gathered, taking counsel how to kill Christ? Let them go now and cleave to external pomp, so as to make Christ and all God’s prophets schismatics, and to make Satan’s ministers in turn the instruments of the Holy Spirit. But if they speak sincerely, let them answer me in good faith: in what region and place do they think the Church resides from the time that Eugenius was deposed and stripped of the papacy by the decree of the Council of Basel, and Amedeus was substituted in his place? They cannot deny — even if they burst — that the Council, as far as external rites are concerned, was legitimate: it was convened not by one pope alone but by two. Eugenius was there condemned for schism, rebellion, and obstinacy, together with the whole herd of cardinals and bishops who had conspired with him to dissolve the Council. Yet afterward, raised up by the favor of princes, he recovered his pontificate. That election of Amedeus, duly carried out by the authority of a general and sacrosanct synod, went up in smoke — except that he himself was placated with the cardinal’s hat thrown to him like a bone tossed to a barking dog. From the bosom of those heretics, rebels, and contumacious men came forth all who afterward were popes, cardinals, bishops, abbots, and priests. Here they are caught and stuck fast. To which side will they assign the name of the Church? Will they deny that it was a general council, wanting nothing for external majesty — one solemnly convened by two papal documents, consecrated under the presidency of the Legate of the Roman see, well ordered in all its proceedings, and persevering in the same dignity to the very end? Will they confess that Eugenius was a schismatic along with his whole cohort, from whom all \[their clergy\] have been consecrated? Either then let them define the form of the Church otherwise — or all of them, however many they are, will be held by us to be schismatics, who knowingly and willingly were ordained by heretics. But if it had never been established before that the Church is not bound to external pomp, they themselves could furnish us with a lengthy proof of it, who for so long arrogantly sold themselves to the world under that specious title of the Church, while in reality they were deadly plagues upon the Church. I do not speak of their morals and those tragic crimes with which their whole life abounds, since they say they are Pharisees who should be heard but not imitated. You will recognize clearly enough — if you give some time to reading our writings — that that very doctrine itself — that very doctrine, to which they say they owe their standing as the Church — is a deadly slaughterhouse of souls, a firebrand, a ruin, and the destruction of the Church. Finally, they do not deal candidly enough when they spitefully recount how many disturbances, tumults, and contentions the preaching of our doctrine has dragged in its wake, and what fruits it now bears in many places. For the blame for these evils is unjustly shifted onto the doctrine itself, when it ought to be turned against Satan’s malice.
