We ourselves would certainly experience this, were we not corrupting so singular a benefit of God by our ingratitude and turning to our destruction what ought to have been our one refuge of salvation. But I return to you, O King. Let not those vain accusations move you, by which our adversaries strive to cast terror upon you — that nothing is sought or aimed at by this new Gospel (so they call it) but an occasion for sedition and impunity for every vice. For our God is not a God of division but of peace; and the Son of God is not a minister of sin, who came to destroy the works of the devil. And we are unjustly charged with such desires, of which we have never given even the slightest suspicion. We, forsooth, are plotting the overthrow of kingdoms — we from whom no factious voice has ever been heard, whose life under your rule has always been known as quiet and simple, and who even now, exiles from our homeland, do not cease to wish all blessings upon you and your kingdom. We, forsooth, are hunting for an unpunished license of vices — we in whose conduct, though much may be criticized, there is nothing worthy of such insults, nor have we so unhappily (by God’s grace) advanced in the Gospel that our life cannot be to these detractors an example of chastity, kindness, mercy, self-restraint, patience, modesty, and every virtue. That we truly fear and worship God is openly evident, since both in life and in death we seek to have his name sanctified; and envy itself has been compelled to render testimony of the innocence and civil integrity of some among us — those in whom the very thing that deserved singular praise was being punished with death. But if there are those who make tumult under the pretext of the Gospel (as some such have heretofore been found in your kingdom), if any use the freedom of God’s grace as a pretext for the license of their vices (many such I know), there are laws and the penalties of laws by which they may be severely restrained according to their deserts — only let not the Gospel of God be ill-spoken of on account of the wickedness of wicked men. You have now, O King, a sufficiently full exposition of the virulent malice of the calumniators, that you may not lend too credulous an ear to their accusations. I also fear it has been too lengthy, since this preface has already grown to the length of almost a proper apology — in which my aim was not to compose a formal defense, but only to soften your mind toward hearing the cause itself, a mind now indeed hostile and estranged from us, inflamed besides — yet one whose favor I am confident we can recover, if you will once read this our confession (which we intend as our defense before your Majesty) in a calm and composed spirit. But if the whispers of the malevolent so fill your ears that no place is given to the accused to speak for themselves, and if those furies, with your connivance, continue always to rage relentlessly against us with chains, scourges, the rack, dismemberments, and burnings — we who are as sheep appointed for slaughter and shall be driven to every extremity — yet may we in our patience possess our souls and await the strong hand of the Lord, which will without doubt come in due time, armed both to rescue the poor from their affliction and to take vengeance on those contemners who now exult in such great security. May the Lord, the King of kings, establish your throne in justice and your seat in equity.
