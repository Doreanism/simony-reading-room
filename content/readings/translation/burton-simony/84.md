many ecclesiastical chapmen of every degree live and die as beggars, leaving their beggarly families and friends behind them, themselves cut off by untimely death before they could recover their simoniacal debts — to consider those many examples, it is no wonder if some have been minded to take the trouble of making a collection of them throughout the land and compiling them into one book, which would be a worthy work. And as the plague did not cease among the Philistines until the Lord’s Ark was sent back (and that not empty — even by the advice of their own Priests and Soothsayers — but with a sin-offering) to Beth-shemesh: so let no simoniacal or sacrilegious withholding Patron ever expect immunity and freedom from plagues and punishments, until the Lord’s tithes belonging to the Lord’s Ark his Church are, without any simoniacal contract, freely and faithfully rendered to the Church, and — with a sin-offering of repentance and restitution — brought home to Beth-shemesh, to the house of the Sun of Righteousness, to the Church of Jesus Christ.

But to leave the common examples of divine vengeance in this kind to the diligent collection of others, and in the meantime to the sad consideration of those who feel them, and to the rest, who may as justly fear them (for God is no respecter of persons); and it is the Apostle’s admonition immediately appended to his exhortation on the paying of tithes, Galatians 6:7, “Be not deceived, God is not mocked, for whatsoever a man sows, that shall he reap” — suffice it to keep within the verge of Scripture examples. Wherein, when I shall name sacrilege for simony, let no man wonder, or impute irrelevance to this Discourse, since all \* simony is a kind of sacrilege, and they are so intertwined with each other that they may easily be taken one for the other — yet without any great mistake, at least in those examples which I shall allege.
