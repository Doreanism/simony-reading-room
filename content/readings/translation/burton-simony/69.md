Do not tell me of those who are ambitious for favor, who love to sleep, who come to their episcopal office as to a bed of rest. I speak not of these: but of those who watch for your souls, who prefer the salvation of their people before their own safety. Tell me, if a man has but ten children under his governance, living continually with him, he is forced without intermission to take care of them: what then shall not he suffer, who has so many — not members of his own household, but such as have others also under their authority? But he is in great honor, they say. What honor? Three-halfpenny wretches rail upon him in the market place. Why does he not then stop their mouths? A proper reason! For now you are far wide of the office of a Bishop. Moreover, unless he gives himself to please all — both those who live idly and those who follow their business — he bears a thousand accusations on every side. None is afraid to accuse and traduce him. For they are afraid to speak evil of princes, but not of bishops, because the fear of God carries no weight with them. Who can express their solicitous care in preaching and teaching? Who can tell what difficulties they are put to in their elections? His mind is no different from a ship tossed by waves. He is pressed on every side by his friends and by his foes, by his own kindred and by strangers. If he is vehement and zealous, they say he is cruel; if not vehement, they say he is cold. But these two contraries must be reconciled, so that he may be neither despised nor hated. How is he thwarted, how pressed with business? How many is he forced to offend, *volens nolens* \[willing or unwilling\]? How many must he rebuke, will he or no? I speak not unadvisedly, but as I am affected, and as I think. I do not think that among priests there are many who shall be saved, but far more who shall perish. The reason is, because this office requires a high and excellent spirit. For there are many occasions to drive him from his own disposition, and he has need of many eyes. Do you not see how many things are needful for a bishop to have — that he be apt to teach, that he be patient in the bearing of evils, that he be constant and faithful in the doctrine of the Word?
