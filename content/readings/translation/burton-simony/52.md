And Ambrose says: *Inexpiabilis est culpa venditi Ministerii, et vindicta gratiae caelestis transit in Posteros.* — The sin of selling the ministry is unpardonable, and the vengeance of divine grace passes along to posterity, like Gehazi’s leprosy.

But as it is objected, in the same place: *Ventum est. Simoniaci*, etc. — Simoniacs, though they think the grace of the Holy Ghost is vendible, yet they profess the faith and true religion, and consequently are no heretics. It is answered: *Simoniaci*, etc. — Simoniacs, although they seem to hold the faith, yet are enthralled to the perdition of infidelity: as Gregory says, *Cum omnis avaritia*, etc. — Since all avarice is idolatry, whoever does not carefully avoid it — and especially in the bestowal of ecclesiastical dignities — is subject to the perdition of infidelity, though he seems to hold the faith in words, which in deeds he denies. And after: *Cum omnis avaritia* — Those who ordain for money are accounted more intolerable heretics than the Macedonians \[who denied the full divinity of the Holy Spirit\], who, denying the deity of the Holy Ghost, grossly affirmed him to be only the servant of the Father and the Son; but these make him to be their own servant. And also worse than those Jews who blasphemed the Holy Ghost by ascribing Christ’s miracles to the power of Beelzebub. And to speak truly: *Iudae comparantur Proditori qui Iudaeis occisoribus Christum vendidit* — they are compared to Judas the traitor, who sold Christ to the murderous Jews. And therefore, *Ex Concilio Bracharensi: Placuit* — it seems good to us that for the ordination of clerics, bishops take no rewards, and that the grace of God by imposition of hands be not sold for money, but given freely, as the Lord has commanded. For it is an ancient decree of the fathers: *Anathema sit danti et accipienti* — Cursed be the giver and the receiver.

*Ob.* But some object: it was customary in times past to bring the reward of soothsaying to the prophet — as Balak sent to Balaam, and Saul came to Samuel with a gift in his hand. Now prophecy is a gift of the Holy Ghost.
