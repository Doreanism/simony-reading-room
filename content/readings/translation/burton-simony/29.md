### CHAP. IX.

### Of the kinds of simoniacs in general.

We have seen what a simoniac is; now let us see who they are, and how many sorts there be. It was a wicked and impious decree of Pope Hildebrand, Gregory the Seventh, to draw all kings and princes and laypeople into the rank of simoniacs, merely for presenting to bishoprics and benefices, though they did it most freely and honestly. But the main drift of this grand founder of Antichristian usurpation over Christian princes was both to enrich his coffers and to fortify his papal see, by obliging the whole prelacy and clergy to his pontifical chair. But all simoniacs are either buyers or sellers. The common report indeed runs only upon the buyers, as if they alone were the simoniacs; but if the buyer is one, the seller must needs be another, by the law of relatives. Of sellers, Esau is reckoned the first, who sold his birthright, and with it the priesthood. For the birthright had annexed to it dominion, a double portion, and the priesthood. So that by selling his birthright — and with it the priesthood, that most sacred prerogative — he is justly called profane Esau.
