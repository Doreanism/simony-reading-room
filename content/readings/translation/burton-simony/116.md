When you bestow a benefice at the earnest suit of any of your household, when you cannot be ignorant of some base corruption, you give your consent to the betraying of the lambs of Christ into the wolf’s power. And where does simony begin but at this source? At this gate is let in some alphabetical homebred pedant \[one who knows only his ABC’s\] — the very spawn of simony. Such a one will be content to exchange his schoolmaster’s rod for a broken, shortened shepherd’s crook, and to accept not what the Church may claim, but what the patron will leave; and perhaps will cite authority out of the grammar school for it, if he has so much Greek: *πλέον ἥμισυ παντός* — *Dimidium plus toto* — half a loaf is better than no bread, nay than a whole loaf which he cannot obtain. For it cannot be that a man of ingenuous and liberal education will ever seek to enter Christ’s fold on any base and unworthy terms. If there are any simoniacal ministers in the Church (as I charitably hope there may be none) who go about to purchase dignities, it is most probable they first learned to traffic in this market. O shut your gates against such peddling merchants. The way to keep the coasts clear is for you to be no less careful in providing a worthy incumbent beforehand than the illiterate pedant is watchful to catch the preferment before it falls. But your giving way to such persons opens a wide gate to every farmer, teaching him by this means to provide a sufficient maintenance — perhaps for the most untowardly and most deformed of all his sons — by laying out his money for the next vacancy, which lay in your power to prevent. Perhaps you will say: you bought the perpetual presentation or patronage at a great price; or, if not you yourself, your father or predecessors. If your predecessors bought it and left it freely to you, you may the more freely bestow it.
