Patrons, then, having a power conferred on them by the Church to present and commend a person — not simply to bestow and collate the benefice, this being a distinct thing from the right of presentation; indeed, though presentation is sometimes called a collation, yet this collation is not a donation, because donation is an act of free liberality without compulsion, whereas collation is joined with compulsion, in that the collator must present within six months or else his power is lost — patrons therefore have no power to sell that which, strictly speaking, they have no power to give. Hereupon Aquinas says: any act is naturally evil when it falls upon an unsuitable subject. Now a spiritual thing, he says, is an unsuitable subject for buying and selling, and for three reasons. First, because a spiritual thing cannot be made equal to or equivalent with any earthly price: “Your money perish with you, because you thought the gift of God could be obtained with money” (Acts 8:20), as was said to Simon. Second, because that cannot be a proper matter for sale over which the seller is not master or owner; for a prelate of the Church is not lord of spiritual things, but only a steward and dispenser: 2 Corinthians 4. Third, because selling is contrary to the original nature of spiritual things, which proceed from the free and pure gift of God: “Freely you have received, freely give” (Matthew 10:8). *Talia ergo emere vel vendere est peccatum irreligiositatis*: therefore to buy or sell such things is a sin of irreligion. So he. Whereupon Zanchi \[Hieronymus Zanchius\] adds: *His rationibus Luce claris efficitur, Simoniam admitti non posse, sine maxima in spiritum Sanctum, ejusque dona, adeoque, in res omnes spirituales iniuria, & irreuerentia; eoque, Simoniam ad ἀσεβείαν pertinere*: By these reasons, he says, it is made clearer than daylight that simony cannot be committed without the greatest injury and irreverence toward the Holy Spirit and his gifts, and therefore toward all spiritual things — and thus that simony belongs to the category of impiety.
