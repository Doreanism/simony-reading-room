### The Author’s Conclusion, Containing His Candid Protestation and Zealous Congratulation.

As it is in the natural body, so also in the body politic. The fairest and best-constituted body may have some bad inbred humors, or abscesses, or ulcers, which, as they have grown to greater height, require the more bitter pills and potions, the sharper lancets, the hotter cerate plasters, and more caustic corrosives. Now although the remedy is applied only to the ill-affected part or member, yet such is the mutual sympathy of all the parts that they all jointly suffer as one; all complain alike of the bitterness, sharpness, and smarting of the medicine, impatient of it, as if each part were the patient.
