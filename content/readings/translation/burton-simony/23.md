The words of the Oath. I, N.N., do swear, that I have made no simoniacal payment, contract, or promise, directly or indirectly, by myself, or by any other to my knowledge, or with my consent, to any person or persons whatsoever, for or concerning the procuring and obtaining of this ecclesiastical dignity (to wit, the bishopric of, etc.); nor will at any time hereafter perform or satisfy any such kind of payment, contract, or promise made by any other, without my knowledge or consent. So help me God, through Jesus Christ.

Have you now duly and seriously weighed the substance and circumstances of this most solemn oath? Then let it summon and assemble your gravest thoughts in council together, all moving you thus to argue with yourself: I am now in a sacred place, in the open view of men and angels, all spectators, all expecters of what I am to do. Here I come to be consecrated a bishop — a great honor, but a greater burden — which taking upon me, I must surpass many here present, and many more absent, all deserving this honor far before me; so that I draw upon myself, and that justly, the most insupportable burden of envy. Yea more: if this bishopric had fallen to me by lot, as that fell upon Matthias, or had been cast or forced upon me, as bishoprics were once wont to be upon good men in the time of the Church’s innocence, I might better bear and brook it — yea, it would diminish all the envy.
