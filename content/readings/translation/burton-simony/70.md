For if the salvation of one soul was so precious that for its sake the Son of God became man and suffered so great things: consider then what punishment the destruction of one soul will bring. Do not tell me therefore, the priest has sinned, or the deacon: all their sins redound upon the head of those who chose them. Therefore if any shall approach the chief priesthood (that is, a bishopric) as to a function full of care and trouble, no man will easily undertake it. But now we desire this dignity no differently than civil magistracies — namely, that we may be magnified and honored before men, while we are cast out of favor with God. What gain does this honor bring? How does it appear to be nothing? When you shall desire the priesthood, set before you on the one side those accusations which it must necessarily suffer; on the other side a life free from public employment, and compare the measure of the punishment. For if you sin the sin of a private man, you shall not suffer so great a punishment in comparison: but if you sin in the priesthood, you are undone. A bishop cannot sin in private. For it goes well with him if, not offending, he can be free from slanders — much less if he does offend can he escape. If he is angry, or laughs, or desires a little sleep to refresh him, there are many ready to taunt him, many that will be offended, many that will censure him and prescribe laws to him, many that will revive the memory of former bishops and despise the present — and they do it not to praise them, but to sting him. War is sweet to the inexperienced. For we fight not against those who by might oppress the poor, nor have we the courage to stand in defense of the flock: but after the example of those shepherds whom Ezekiel mentions, we kill and devour. Which of us is so careful and solicitous for the sheep of Christ as Jacob was for Laban’s flock? Who can express what cold he suffered by night? And do not tell me of any night-watchings or any such care taken. It is altogether contrary. Princes and rulers do not enjoy so great honor as he who is a prelate of the Church.
