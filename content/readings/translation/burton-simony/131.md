So far are they from expecting that any scholar’s modesty should be made to blush by being a suitor, still less his honesty blamed for presenting his suit sealed up in a bag. In this I think I see a noble emulation between the laity and the clergy of England, and (let me say it in the Apostle’s sense) between the children and the fathers. You are the gracious sons; the sacred persons, the patrons, are your spiritual fathers, who have begotten you through the word of truth. O blessed emulation, free from the least envy — save that it may well be the envy of other nations! Emulate still, you sacred fathers of the Church, and you noble heaven-bred sons of such fathers and such a mother.
