A sin so detestable that the Church of Rome itself (though now the mother and nurse of abominations) has cried out against it. For we must draw a great distinction between the present Church of Rome and what it was formerly, even within the last three or four hundred years. In former times, though that Church hatched and fostered many enormities both of doctrine and practice, which crept in by degrees until Antichrist should come to his full stature, yet there was still room for reproof: devout and learned men might speak and write freely of its abuses. But now, within less than the last hundred years since the Council of Trent, this Church has grown to such superciliousness and height of pride that no man may so much as mention the least speck or blemish of that foolish virgin — or rather, filthy whore. Indeed, those who have already in their writings left any record or monuments of Rome’s sin, and in particular of this of simony, it must pass through the fire of their *Index Expurgatorius*. Take one example among others. Claude Espencaeus, in his commentaries upon the Epistle to Titus, where he touches on the corruptions of the Church of Rome in the matter of simony — there he must be purged.
