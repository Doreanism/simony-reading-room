And in his twenty-fourth tract upon Matthew: some attempt many ways to be called by men bishops — that is, Rabbi — when in the meantime they should understand that a bishop must be above reproach, and so forth as follows in the Apostle’s description, so that although men do not call him a bishop, yet with God he is a bishop. And Cyrillus says, speaking of the office of ministers: if we execute this office rightly, we shall receive a reward, yes and praise too; but if otherwise, or perversely, what flames shall be sufficient to punish us? We shall hear that voice: “You have destroyed my heritage, you have slain my people.” As for laymen, they shall indeed render each a particular account for his own life; but we who bear the burden of sacerdotal offices shall answer not only for ourselves but also for all believers in Christ. And Saint Augustinus, writing to Valerius, whom he calls his loving father, says: *Ante omnia peto, ut cogitet religiosa prudentia tua, nihil esse in hac vita, et maxime hoc tempore, facilius, et laetius, et hominibus acceptabilius, Episcopi, aut Presbyteri, aut Diaconi officio, si perfunctorie atque adulatorie res agatur: sed nihil apud Deum miserius, et tristius, et damnabilius.
