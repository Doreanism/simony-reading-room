Gratian gives the reason: because the right of patronage is neither simply temporal nor spiritual. A layman may have it — that is, the *ius patronatus* — and leave it to his heirs; yet not temporal, because it cannot be sold. Saint Jerome, writing to Damasus on a point bordering upon our present purpose, says: *Quia Beatitudo tua quaesivit, utrum usus Decimarum & oblationum secularibus provenire possit: novit vestra sanctitas omnino non licere: Protestantibus hoc Divinis Authoritatibus Paternorum Canonum* \[Because your Blessedness inquired whether the use of tithes and oblations might be derived to laymen: your Holiness may know it is altogether unlawful, the divine authorities of the ancient councils attesting the same\]. Gratian adds the reason: *Non licere: Hoc est certum, quod Ius Decimarum Laicus possidere non potest, cum sit Spirituale* \[It is unlawful: this is certain, that a layman may not possess the right of tithes, seeing it is spiritual\] — distinguishing between the right of the patronage and the right of the personage. And for this cause, *de sola gratia* \[of favor alone\], not of mere lay-right or claim, the *ius patronatus*, or right of patronage, was conferred upon the lay founders, endowers, or builders; according to that verse:

*Patronum faciunt Dos, Aedificatio, Fundus.* \[A patron is made by endowment, construction, and land.\]

Gratian says moreover: *Sunt autem tria, &c.* \[There are three things\] which the patron attains — Honor, Charge, and Profit: honor in presenting; charge or burden in defending the church from dilapidations; profit, because if he falls into poverty the church shall provide for him, and in a more ample manner and measure than for other poor.

As also the Fourth Council of Toledo, canon 37, has so provided. Further, the Ninth Council of Toledo decreed: *Ut quamdiu, &c.* \[That so long as the founders of churches (as the patrons) shall live, they should have a solicitous care of those places\], so that *rectores idoneos in eisdem basilicis iidem ipsi episcopo efferant ordinandos* \[they themselves present fit rectors to the bishop for ordination in those same churches\].
