Gratian also records a saying of Gregory to this purpose: *Vulnerato Pastore, quis curandis ovibus adhibet medicinam? Aut quomodo populum orationis clypeo tueatur, qui iaculis hostium sese feriendum exponit? aut qualem fructum de se producturus est, cui grani pesteradix infesta est? Maior ergo metuenda est locis illis calamitas, ubi tales intercessores ad locum regiminis adducuntur, qui Dei magis in se iracundiam provocant, quam per semetipsos placare debuerant.* \[That is: “When the shepherd is wounded, who applies the medicine to heal the flock? Or how shall he protect the people with the shield of prayer, who exposes himself to be struck by the darts of the enemy? Or what fruit will he bring forth, whose very root is infected with the blight of grain? Therefore a greater calamity is to be feared in those places where such intercessors are brought to positions of governance, who provoke God’s wrath against themselves more than they ought to appease it through their own intercession.”\] To this purpose Bernard says to such men: *Soli non potestis perire, qui praire debetis docendo et operando. Multi sunt Catholici praedicando, qui Haeretici sunt operando. Quod Haeretici faciunt per prava dogmata, hoc faciunt plures hodie per mala exempla: Et tanto graviores sunt Haereticis, quanto praevalent opera verbis.* \[That is: “You alone cannot perish, you who ought to go before others by teaching and working. Many are Catholics in their preaching who are heretics in their practice. What heretics do by corrupt doctrines, many today do by evil examples; and they are so much more dangerous than heretics, as deeds prevail over words.”\] So Bernard. *Simoniaci autem haeretici sunt, in quibus quid impedit, quo minus omne genus peccati dominetur.* But simoniacs are heretics, in whom nothing prevents every kind of sin from holding dominion. For as *Senectus est omnis Morbus* \[old age is every disease\], so simony is every evil. It is the Dead Sea in which are buried — indeed, bred — more than all the sins of Sodom and her confederate cities. From whence come all those sins of bribery in the commonwealth, if not from this stinking lake? From whence comes all that lay-simony in buying and selling every sort of office, great and small, of public justice and private service, if not from the authentic precedent — indeed, the spawn and seed of simony?
