For as Nazianzen says: *Quos Christus eiecit de Templo, nunquam Spiritus Christi sibi faciet Templum* \[Those whom Christ cast out of the Temple, the Spirit of Christ will never make his Temple\]; as being destitute and void of the grace of the Holy Ghost. As Saint Jerome says: *In Simoniacis nulla spiritualis gratia operatur* \[In simoniacs no spiritual grace has any operation\]. *Non enim operatur Spiritus Sanctus spiritualiter in perfidis mercimoniis avaritiae* \[For the Holy Spirit does not work spiritually in the treacherous merchandise of covetousness\]. And Innocentius has a sentence no less fearful: *Simoniaci etsi fidem tenere videantur, infidelitatis tamen perditioni subiciuntur* \[Simoniacs, although they seem to hold the true faith, are nonetheless subjected to the perdition of infidelity\]. And Saint Ambrose: *Simoniaci integritatem, et conscientiam puram fidei non habent* \[Simoniacs do not have the integrity and pure conscience of faith\].

What shall I speak of Ananias and Sapphira? How fearfully and dreadfully were they struck for withholding part of that which they had dedicated and devoted to God and to his congregation, yea to the maintenance of the Apostles? And what should I speak of Simon’s curse — exemplary indeed, but such as the conscience rather than the senses apprehends, being spiritual rather than temporal, and yet both: for such ecclesiastical traffickers draw upon themselves a universal curse — upon their souls, by a deprivation even of common grace and ministerial abilities; upon their bodies and goods, by a diminution of strength and sustenance, health and wealth, life and livelihood; nothing thrives with them — all contained in this: “Thy money perish with thee; you have neither *μερὶς* \[part\] nor *κλῆρον* \[lot\] in this matter.” A simoniac is no minister, by Saint Peter’s doctrine.
