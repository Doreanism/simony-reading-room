Because the Spirit of truth — Truth itself being witness, from whom it proceeds — is received freely; then doubtless it is proved to be the spirit of lies which is not received or given freely. And Gregory, in the same place: *Quicunque studet per pretii donationem sacrum ordinem accipere, Sacerdos non est: sed nomen tantummodo dici concupiscit.* — Whoever is eager to receive holy orders by the gift of money is no priest, but covets only to be called by an empty title. And a little after: *Quisquis per pecuniam ordinatur, ad hoc, ut fiat haereticus, promovetur.* — Whoever is ordained by money is thereby promoted to become a heretic. And Pope Gelasius: *Quoscunque stiterit*, etc. — If it shall appear that any unworthy person has bought the sacred dignity with money, being convicted, let him be deprived. But what if some worthy man buy it with his money? I answer: that cannot be, if he is worthy. But let him be who he will — if he buys, it argues unworthiness.

Also Saint Ambrose is alleged: *Reperiuntur quamplurimi, negotiatione muneris mercari velle gratiam Spiritus Sancti, dum illi pretium donant, ut Pontificalis Ordinis sublimitatem accipiant*, etc. — There are found many who by the merchandise of money would buy the grace of the Holy Ghost, while they give money to receive the dignity of the pontifical order. Whereupon a fearful anathema is decreed against such as so give or take. Likewise it is cited out of the Council of Chalcedon: *Si quis Episcopum per pecuniam Ordinationem fecerit*, etc. — If any bishop shall ordain for money, and shall purchase the grace of the Holy Ghost at a price — which indeed cannot be sold — and shall for money ordain a priest or deacon, or shall promote any degree or order within the verge of the clergy for filthy lucre, he that shall attempt this, let him upon conviction be deprived of all. *Si quis vero mediator*, etc. — And if any shall be a mediator or agent for such foul and nefarious corruption in giving and receiving: if a cleric, let him be degraded; and if a layman, accursed.
