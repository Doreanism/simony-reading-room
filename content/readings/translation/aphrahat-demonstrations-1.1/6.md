# Demonstration I, §1

1. I have received your letter, dearest friend, and when I read it I greatly rejoiced that you had set your mind upon such inquiries. For these things which you have asked of me are freely given and freely received; and if anyone who has them should wish to withhold them from any who ask, whatever he refuses to give will be taken away from him. For he who receives without cost ought in turn to give without cost \[as the Lord said, “Freely you have received, freely give” (Matthew 10:8)\]. Therefore, dearest friend, concerning the things you have asked of me, I will write to you so far as my smallness has attained; and at the same time, calling upon God, I will instruct you concerning other matters which you did not ask. Hear then, beloved, and open to me the inner eyes of your heart and the spiritual senses of your mind to the things which I tell you.
