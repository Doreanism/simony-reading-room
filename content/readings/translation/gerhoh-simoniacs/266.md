We, however, eat the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth \[1 Corinthians 5:8\]. Augustinus to Boniface: “Let heretics not seek the Holy Spirit except in the body of Christ, whose sacrament they have outwardly, but the thing itself — of which that is the sacrament — they do not hold inwardly.” The same author: “The eunuch who had bought Joseph had no living seed in his dried genitals; so likewise one who purchases the grace of the Holy Spirit has no living seed. Similarly, the fire of the sacrifice that had lived under water for seventy years of the Babylonian captivity was extinguished when Antiochus sold the priesthood to Jason. This signifies that the fire of the Holy Spirit does not shine upon simoniacal sacraments.” Origenes in his exposition on Leviticus: “Such a man is sought who can eat the sacred flesh — not in any place, but in the holy place within the court of the tabernacle. Let those who rend the churches hear this, who, introducing foreign and depraved teachings, think they can eat the sacred flesh outside the temple of God and outside the court of the Lord. Their sacrifices are profane, being conducted contrary to the command of the law. They are commanded to be eaten in a holy place, within the courts of the tabernacle of testimony, which the wall of faith encircles, the pillars of hope support, and the breadth of charity enlarges. Where these are not present, the sacred flesh can neither be had nor eaten.”

If, moreover, authorities promulgated by the Apostolic See consonant with our assertions are required, the authorities cited above, excerpted from the writings of Leo the Great and Pope Paschal, will suffice: the former asserts that in the church of Alexandria, on account of the excommunicate, the offering of sacrifice was cut off, the sanctification of chrism failed, and the light of all the heavenly sacraments was extinguished; the latter, in his letter directed to the Milanese, confirms that saying of blessed Augustinus: “There is no place for a true sacrifice outside the catholic Church.” Since we have set forth the words of these two Roman pontiffs above, we have judged it superfluous to repeat them here, though opportune to recall them. But if anyone demands further authorities of the Apostolic See, let him hear what follows. Pope Pelagius says to Viator and Pancratius, among other things: “It is not the body of Christ that a schismatic confects, if we are guided by truth as our leader; for no one, without incurring the apostle’s condemnation, can pretend that Christ is divided. The Church, as has often been said, which is the body of Christ, is established to be one, and cannot be divided into two or more parts. For as soon as anyone departs from it, he ceases to be the Church. There is one temple of Jerusalem; one who has divided himself from it must needs sacrifice to idols.” The same author to John the Patrician: “Do not defile your catholic mind at any time by communion with schismatics. One body of Christ, one Church — this is established. One who is divided from unity cannot consecrate the truth of the body of Christ. For if we examine the very name of ‘consecration’ with rational and living understanding, to ‘consecrate’ \[*consecrare*\] means to ‘hallow together’ \[*simul sacrare*\]. But one who is divided from the bowels of the Church and separated from the apostolic sees rather desecrates than consecrates. Do not therefore, as if there were no difference between schismatics and the Church, wish to be indifferently associated with the sacrifices of both.” Gregorius in his Register: “We can in no way call a consecration that which is celebrated by excommunicated persons.” The same author in the Moralia, book 35: “It is greatly to be noted that the friends of Job are commanded to offer the sacrifice of their conversion to the Lord not on their own behalf, but through Job.
