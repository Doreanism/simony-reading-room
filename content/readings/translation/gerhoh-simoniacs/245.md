Thus, although by the authority of this Roman pontiff and council we may denounce the aforementioned unchaste and presumptuous men alongside the other heretics, yet because — as I said — they can scarcely be examined and judged, we are content that they be called wicked and under interdict, and that only this be observed concerning such men: that no one obedient to the Roman see should dare to hear offices that have been terribly forbidden by that same see. For, as is written in the First Book of Samuel, whoever calls himself a Christian but holds the apostolic see in contempt incurs the sin of divination. But this obedience can easily be maintained by those who have a more favorable situation and freedom, being obedient in all things to the apostolic see — namely, by avoiding, and teaching others to avoid, those whose offices that see forbids to be heard. But where bishops, like the angel of Thyatira, are unwilling to expel from their territories that woman Jezebel who calls herself a prophetess, but permit her instead to eat forbidden sacrifices, to commit fornication, and to lead astray the servants of God — there, Catholics must either endure persecution from that Jezebel, or flee as Elijah fled the ancient Jezebel, or else conceal their caution and their obedience to the Roman see from that Jezebel, just as in former times the seven thousand men who had not bowed the knee before Baal concealed their caution while Jezebel reigned. Let these words concerning unchaste clerics suffice.

But if someone has made himself so notable and conspicuous in the leprosy of simony that he can be recognized by “the gall of bitterness and the bond of iniquity,” I do not hesitate to declare that he must be shunned as a heretic and enemy of the Church — since he has been proscribed and condemned in the very person of Simon Magus by the blessed Petrus and by many of his successors. For, as Pope Gelasius attests, when the originators and teachers of any perversity whatsoever were condemned with anathema, all their accomplices and followers were likewise anathematized and cut off by the sword of the Spirit, and were pre-condemned even before they were born — just as Canaan, the son of Ham, was cursed before he was born, when Noah said: “Cursed be Canaan; he shall be a slave of slaves to his brothers.” Why was Canaan, still unborn, cursed because of his father Ham’s impious mockery, if not because in any condemned heresiarch the entire posterity of that heresiarch must be understood as accursed? Read, if you have time, the letter of Pope Gelasius to Euphemius, and you will find that for each heretical perversity once condemned, not only the originators but also their successors, and those in communion with those successors, are to be avoided in equal measure. For blessed Pope Gelasius himself says among other things in that letter: *If any right-thinking person communicates with heretics or their successors regarding the Catholic faith, it is not permissible for him to be named among the altars of Catholics.* Likewise to the Eastern bishops: *All accomplices, followers, and communicants of a perversity once condemned are held in the same lot.* Again to all the bishops of Dardania: *Our fathers judged it sufficient that the originator of any error, together with the error itself, be condemned, so that whoever at any time should be a communicant of that error would be condemned by the principal sentence against it, when any such person could be clearly recognized either by his own profession or by his communion.* And a little later: *The rule of faith and Catholic communion set forth at the Nicene council excludes without appeal all Arians, or any who have fallen into that plague whether by consent or by communion.* Again in what follows: *The error once condemned along with its author bears both the condemnation and the punishment upon any participant in that corrupt communion.* Is it not through this Roman pontiff, speaking thus, that the Spirit of truth reproves the world concerning judgment —
