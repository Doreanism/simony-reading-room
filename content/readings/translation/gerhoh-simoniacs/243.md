When, therefore, Judah and Israel were so alienated by the wickedness of such kings and queens that they could not, at least for the sake of the word of God, even have the freedom to teach under them — a freedom whose removal is attested by the killing of the prophet Zechariah between the temple and the altar \[cf. Matthew 23:35\] — many of the prophets, of whom Elijah and Elisha were the chief, wandered in mountains and in caves of the earth \[cf. Hebrews 11:38\]. You, father, and those like you seem to be following their example by dwelling in the forests and inviting those you can to do the same. For what is found to be lacking to you from the ancient example of Elijah, if one considers the austerity of your clothing and the wild diet you use — unless perhaps it is that excessive zeal with which he was so relentlessly hostile to Jezebel, killing in a single day eight hundred and fifty \[of her prophets\] — yet not before he had first shown that the fire of God was absent from their sacrifices? For the king and people, bewitched as they were by those false prophets, would not easily have allowed so many priests to be slain unless it had first been demonstrated how far their sacrifice fell short of the sacrifice of Elijah. Once it was shown, however, that the divine fire did not work in their sacrifice, neither king nor people defended them, and Elijah slew them utterly forsaken of all aid. Would that you had his zeal — first in annulling the sacrifices of heretics, and then in slaying them with the sword of the word, with which you are so powerfully armed! For to deal with one standard-bearer of the schismatics or heretics: how do you plan to separate from Petrus Leonis the people who cling to him, so long as he, cut off from the Church, is believed to offer the same sacrifice that we offer, and to differ from us in the act of offering no more than bad and good priests differ within the Church — priests who, apart from the sole efficacy of salvation, are agreed to be in no way different from the good in their sacrificing? For it does not suffice for the refutation of heretics that we distinguish them from bad Catholics by this — that their sacrifices benefit neither themselves nor those who communicate with them — since within the Church wicked priests not yet under interdict, though they do not work salvation for themselves, yet do work it for those who communicate with them and hear their Masses. This, I say, does not suffice, since the heretics glory in this alone in which the magicians of Pharaoh once gloried — namely in signs altogether similar \[to those of God’s servants\], except insofar as the finger of God alone distinguishes them. For what are the sacraments but signs of sacred realities, by which Pharaoh and all Egypt — that is, all this world lying in evil — is bewitched through heretics, simoniacs, and others separated from the Church? For it does not yet appear that the invisible finger of God, which many assert to be present in all sacraments whether celebrated inside or outside the Church, does not everywhere cooperate with those signs and sacraments, however altogether similar they may outwardly be. You, however — thanks be to God — do not attempt to establish this, but customarily hold yourselves as mediators between the opposing parties, so as not to be found either affirming or denying the aforesaid view; perhaps because, in hiding from this Jezebel in the cave of silence on this question — like Elijah evading the snares of Jezebel — you seek a certain security for yourselves and yours, a security you could not have if, just as you rebuke the world concerning sin and concerning righteousness, you were also willing to rebuke it concerning judgment, by asserting that the prince of this world has been judged not only in himself but also in certain of his members.
