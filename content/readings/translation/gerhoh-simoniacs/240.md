To Bernardus, venerable abbot of Clairvaux, brother Gerhohus sends greetings in Christ.

At the court of Bamberg I could not enjoy your longed-for presence as I had wished. For, hindered by the tumult of affairs, out of many matters only a few came into question — matters on which I had desired your fatherly response, so that by comparing my questions with your answers and relating them together to the one final purpose of charity we might think and speak the same thing, until God should reveal to us, if in anything we thought otherwise.
