### Fourth.

Item, the right of burial is annexed to spiritual things — for it is a right belonging to the church, 13 quaestio 1, Ecclesias \[Decretum C.13 q.1\] — but as is recorded in Genesis 23, Abraham bought from Ephron the double cave for burial, and was not rebuked for this.

### Fifth.

Item, the right of the firstborn was in ancient times annexed to spiritual things, for as is recorded in the gloss on Genesis 14, “And Melchizedek, king of Salem” \[Gen. 14:18\], the Hebrews say that all firstborn from Noah to Aaron were priests; and yet as is recorded in Genesis 25, Jacob bought from his brother Esau the right of the firstborn, and was not rebuked by God for this.

### Sixth.

Item, bodily labor is annexed to spiritual offices, but as was established above, it is sometimes lawful for a man to sell his labor —

### On the other side.

ON THE OTHER SIDE, Extra de iure patronatus, De iure \[X.3.38.11\] and Extra de simonia, Querela \[X.5.3.28\]: it is said that what is annexed to a spiritual thing can neither be bought nor sold by right, and the same is expressly stated in the gloss on both decretals.

### Second.

Item, to buy or sell tithes is simony. 1 quaestio 1, Presbyter \[Decretum C.1 q.1\]. But tithes are annexed to spiritual things, since they are owed to clerics for divine worship. Extra de decimis, Tua nobis \[X.3.30.7\]. Therefore by analogy it seems that to sell or buy other things annexed to spiritual matters is also simoniacal.

### Conclusion.

Things annexed to spiritual matters can be bought and sold without the vice of simony, provided nothing is exacted on account of the spiritual things themselves.

I ANSWER that, as was seen above, some things are spiritual in relation to the Holy Spirit, and some in relation to the human spirit.
