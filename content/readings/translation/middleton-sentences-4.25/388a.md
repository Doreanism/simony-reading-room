Or \[the annexum may be joined\] concomitantly, as in the labor annexed to spiritual offices — such an annexum can be rented out, if the person is not otherwise obligated to perform those offices: as is evident in the case of a cleric who reads the psalter for the dead, and similarly in other cases.

Regarding things annexed to spiritual matters by comparison to the human spirit \[i.e., intellectual gifts\], it may be said that things annexed to the teaching of natural philosophy — such as schools, books, and the like — may be sold under the common law, unless the church provides such things to a teacher for the instruction of clerics; for in that case he would not be permitted to exact any payment for the aforementioned.

### Reply to the First Three \[Objections\]

The replies to the first three objections against \[the conclusion\] are clear from what has been said in the question.

### Reply to the Fourth

To the fourth it must be said that Abraham purchased unconsecrated land, and this is lawful.

### Reply to the Fifth

To the fifth it must be said that the right of firstborn was owed to Jacob by divine ordinance, and therefore it cannot rightly be said that he purchased that right so much as that he redeemed himself from an unjust vexation. Or it may be said that what is to be said about things annexed to spiritual matters in the Old Law is not the same as in the New Law: for just as the priesthood of the Old Law ought to be called a figure of the priesthood rather than priesthood itself, so too it ought to be called a figure of spiritual office rather than spiritual office.

### Reply to the Sixth

To the last objection it must be said that bodily labor annexed to spiritual \[offices\] can be sold in the manner stated above; but not that labor which consists in the administration of the sacraments.
