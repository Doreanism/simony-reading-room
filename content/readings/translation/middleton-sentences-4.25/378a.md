# DISTINCTION XXV.

It is also customary to ask whether heretics cut off and condemned by the church can confer holy orders, and whether those ordained by them, upon returning to the unity of the church, ought to be reordained. The words of the doctors render this question perplexing and nearly insoluble, for they appear to disagree at great length. For certain doctors seem to hold that heretics cannot confer holy orders, and that those who appear to be ordained by them do not receive grace. Innocentius \[I\] says that the clergy of the Arians should not be seen as deserving reception with the dignity of the priesthood or any ministry, permitting them only baptism conferred in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit to stand as valid. He also says that they cannot give the Holy Spirit, which they have lost; and that those ordained by heretics have a wounded head who, having lost honor, cannot confer honor — nor has such a person received anything, since there was nothing in the giver that he could receive. He also holds that those coming from heretics are to be granted only lay communion by the laying on of hands, and that none of them should be elevated to even the slightest degree of clerical honor. Gregorius \[I\] likewise says that the consecration of the Arians is sacrilegious, when communion is received from their hand. Cyprianus too says that everything heretics do is carnal, empty, and sacrilegious — their altars false, their priesthoods illicit, and their sacrifices sacrilegious; like apes who, though not human, imitate human form, they claim for themselves the appearance and authority of the Catholic Church, while they themselves are not within the church, and, being sacrilegious, they administer priesthood and erect altars, when no oblation can be sacrificed where the Holy Spirit is not present, and the Lord profits no one through the prayers and supplications of one who has violated the Lord himself. Hieronymus likewise asserts that all things offered by heretics are defiled in the sight of the Lord, because even if they appear holy in outward form, yet because they have been touched by one who is polluted, all things are polluted. The same doctor writes: God hates the sacrifices of heretics, and casts them away from himself, and as often as they have gathered in his name, he abominates the stench of them and closes his nostrils. The same: Those men offer sacrilegious bread, etc. Leo also testifies that outside the church priesthoods are put to death and sacrifices are not true. The same doctor also says: By the most cruel and wicked madness the light of all the sacraments has been extinguished in the see of Alexandria. The offering of sacrifice has been intercepted, the sanctification of chrism has ceased, and all the mysteries have withdrawn themselves from the murderous hands of the impious. By these and other testimonies it appears to be established that the ecclesiastical sacraments — chiefly those of the body and blood \[of Christ\], of ordination, and of confirmation — cannot be administered by heretics.
