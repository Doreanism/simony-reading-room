By the sin of Iuda is understood the sin of clerics, who ought to devote themselves to the confession of divine praise. Likewise, simony places the idol of cupidity in the temple of the Virgin alongside the Son of God, so that what is said in Daniel 9 seems fulfilled: “And in the temple there shall be the abomination of desolation.” Simony shows no deference to God nor to his angels, for it is present when the divine mysteries are celebrated, even though Christus is present both as God and as man together with his angels. In this the simoniacs are worse than the demons, because the demons fear Christus and his angels, but the simoniacs do not.

## The tenth insult is

that simony uses for the damnation of men the very things which Christus instituted for their sanctification. Thus the devil seems to have triumphed over the Church when he attacks the Church with her own weapons and strikes her, as it were, with her own staff.

## The eleventh insult is

that simony causes robbers and sacrilegious men to hold the place and office of God. For the simoniac is a robber. Thus John 10: “He who does not enter the sheepfold by the door but climbs in by another way, that man is a thief and a robber.” And on Matthew 21, “You have made it a den of robbers,” the Gloss says: “A robber is one who pursues gain, who punishes corporally those who do not give, and kills spiritually those who do give. And every robber first selects a place of ambush, like a den.” The simoniac is also sacrilegious, because the things around which he plies his robbery are sacred. For it is absurd to call someone sacrilegious who steals or seizes the chalice of a church and not to call sacrilegious the one who steals or seizes an entire church together with its chalice and everything else belonging to it. Moreover, the simoniac can be called a thief in two respects: with respect to the one whose goods he consecrates against that person’s will, and with respect to the poor whom he defrauds of the goods of the Church. Thus, since a prelate holds the office of God, when a simoniac is made prelate, a robber and sacrilegious man exercises the office of God in the Church.

## The twelfth insult is

that simony fills the Church of God with reptiles, beasts of burden, and animals. For it undermines the wall of the Church, just as a prelate does — especially if he is carnal — according to Genesis 49: “Symeon and Leui are brothers, vessels of iniquity.” And it adds: “In their willfulness they undermined a wall.” And Iacob interposes a certain prayer that ought to be made frequently by prelates of the Church: “Let my soul not enter into their counsel.” For it would be exceedingly dangerous for a prelate of the Church to have demoniacs or simoniacs in his counsel. Once the wall of the Church has been undermined, a multitude of reptiles and beasts of burden enters, according to Ezekiel 8: “Go in and see the worst abominations.” And it continues: “And I went in and saw, and behold every likeness of reptiles and animals — an abomination.” When someone lustful obtains a benefice in a church through simony, a serpent enters the Church.
