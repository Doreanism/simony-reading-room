## On the manifold harm that arises from simony

Fifth, contributing to the detestation of simony is the manifold harm that arises from it to God and to his Church. The first harm is that God has, as it were, lost the Church, since he has lost entry and exit in it. For a castle is not said to belong to someone who has no entry and exit in it. Therefore the Psalmist said: “The Lord guard your coming in and your going out,” etc. \[Psalm 121:8\]. Another harm is that simony converts the Church, which ought to be the dwelling place of the Son of God, into a den of robbers, according to Matthew 21: “It is written: ‘My house shall be called a house of prayer,’ but you have made it a den of robbers.” In this, ecclesiastical prelates appear to be worse than secular princes, because the latter do not tolerate dens of robbers in their dominions, whereas the former permit cloisters into which entry is made simoniacally. In the judgment of God these are dens of robbers: they are plainly robbers and are not to be acquitted from the gallows of hell. Another harm is that where God ought to be honored, idolatry is practiced instead. For those whom gold and silver have introduced serve gold and silver as though it were God. Thus Hosea 8: “They have reigned, but not by me; princes have arisen, and I did not know them. Of their silver and their gold they have made idols for themselves.” He who buys a dignity afterwards wants to sell the things that pertain to that dignity. Thus on Acts 8, concerning Symon Magus, the Gloss says: “He wished to buy the Holy Spirit so that by selling it to others he could receive more.” And as the blessed Bernardus says, he who enters faithlessly and not through Christus — what does he do but act faithlessly and against Christus? Since Christus did not introduce someone into the Church, afterwards he does not recognize him. Thus Petrus, having been introduced by the servant girl who kept the door into the courtyard of the chief priests, said that he did not know Christus. The servant girl at the door is the flesh, which has the five senses as its five doors. Therefore the servant girl at the door introduces the one whom carnality introduces. Simony also takes the Holy Spirit away from the Church. This seems to have been signified in the fact that the sacred fire which had survived for seventy years of the Babylonian captivity under the waters was extinguished when Antiochus sold the priesthood to Iason, as Augustinus says. By that fire the Holy Spirit was prefigured. And this is the greatest harm that can be done to the Church of God, just as the greatest harm that could be done to our body is if the spirit from which it derives all its strength were taken away.
