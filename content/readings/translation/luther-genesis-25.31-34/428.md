For this \[hypocritical\] Church is especially given to boasting in God’s name: it wishes to be and to be regarded as the Church, and it is so before the world. Just as Esau holds the birthright, yet despises it, and through that contempt loses it. For in turn they are despised, rejected, and stripped of it, and therefore their boasting counts for nothing. So too with the Papists, who vaunt themselves as the Church and as successors of the Apostles. For whoever wishes to be the Church must magnify the birthright — not only in this life, but also beyond this life.

And this is a perpetual contest in the world. As it was between Esau and Jacob, so it is for us with the Papists, and before us it was for the Jewish people with the Apostles. The Jews strenuously contended that they were the people of God, and Paul acknowledges them to be the Church in Romans 2 and 9. But because they retained only the name while despising the true substance of the Church, they were therefore cast away. The Gentiles were called in their place. Hosea 1: “And I will say to those who were not my people, ‘You are my people,’ and they shall say, ‘You are my God.’” And Deuteronomy 32: “I will provoke them with that which is not a people; with a foolish nation I will anger them.”

This then is one mark — indeed, the most sinister feature — of the hypocritical Church: that it is supremely arrogant and utterly confident in usurping the name of God, the Church, the Keys, the Ministry, and the Sacraments; it can do nothing more than boast of God. The second mark is that it truly despises and neglects the things that belong to the true Church. It cares little for its own birthright, just as the Pope and the bishops neither teach, nor baptize, nor perform any function of the Church: they have only the name. But they display neither the fruit nor the effect. Therefore Christ says: “You will know them by their fruits.” Whoever — as the Pope does — despises the Church, thereby forfeits the Church. That empty boast — “We are Christians, we are the Church, we are Bishops” — avails nothing. If the substance is absent, you will not long retain the name: indeed you are already rejected; what you have, you do not have. So too the heretics who will come after us will lose it, because the Church is the pillar of truth (1 Timothy 3), which they themselves will cast away, and boasting and the name profit nothing without truth.

When therefore they cry: “We are the Church, we have the authority to command,” and wish to compel us to assent to their decrees — we shall answer: The Church is twofold: by number and by kind, by merit and by truth. I do not recognize you by name and by reality, as even their own teachers acknowledge. Esau is the Church without substance — in name and appearance only — just as all idolaters and heretics are who arrogate the title of Church to themselves. I can tolerate it when someone is a Christian by number only, yet meanwhile performs the external office. Even if he is secretly impious and wicked, provided he baptizes, cares for the sick, and strengthens afflicted consciences, nothing prevents him from being called a Christian by number, or the Church. But not by merit. This the Pope and the bishops do not do. Therefore they are not even to be compared with those who are secretly impious, because they do the opposite of the ecclesiastical office: they blaspheme the Word, they persecute the godly — that is, the true Church.

In our own Church many ministers are Christians by number only, without merit.
