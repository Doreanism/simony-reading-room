But the Pope openly sets himself against the Church: he suppresses the teaching of faith, fills the whole world with idolatry, simony, and endless monstrosities. That is to rage against the Church in both number and merit, after the manner of the Turks. The argument of the Papists is therefore worth nothing: “We are the Church by number, and therefore you must bear with us.” If they wished to be such \[a church\], they would have to agree on doctrine, to teach and to govern — as befits true and godly bishops. But they are the grandsons of Esau, enemies and destroyers of the Church, like heretics.

As for those who feign piety and are hypocrites — as many are mixed in among the assembly of the godly — we bear with them in the number of the faithful, provided they do not teach anything contrary to the profession of true doctrine.

Moses has also depicted a third mark of the hypocritical church in this type. For after Esau had most carelessly and with great confidence neglected the spiritual goods — faith, love, patience, gentleness — which are found in the Church, and had indeed despised all these things, there now follows the appetite and burning desire for that red pottage: that is, because he does not care for the knowledge of Christ, nor for growth in spiritual gifts, nor for eternal life, he becomes greedy and covetous of the pleasures, glory, and honors of this present life.

This is precisely what it means to prefer the pottage to the birthright — that is, he is a member of the Church by number; but he pursues flesh and blood, and seeks gain in godliness. So the false church feigns piety, only to enjoy the pleasures and honors of this world. The true Church, however, seeks eternal life through patience and faith. Those \[of the false church\] are creatures of the belly, and their god is their stomach.

This is the mystery of the red pottage, which holy Scripture sets forth in this place, whereby the dispute over the birthright between the brothers is settled. Lentils are in fact not red but rather yellowish. I therefore think they were prepared with saffron or some other condiment, so as to whet the appetite. Such are the present goods of this world, in which men delight in a wonderful way — preferring to indulge in feasting and dainty fare rather than to eat and drink the flesh and blood of the Son of God, that is, to believe and pursue heavenly goods; they hunt only for the gain and glory of the world, which hold such sweetness and allurement that they stir in men not merely hunger but a certain craving. Hence Esau uses repetition: “Feed me, I pray, of the red, of that red \[pottage\].”

Holy Scripture therefore signifies that all men become more covetous, more arrogant, and more carnal upon the loss of the blessing than they were before. Because they are all flesh and blood, they seek nothing but carnal things, and with terrible greed they hunger for the red pottage — but under the name of Christ and God, under the title of the Church. Jacob too eats of the red pottage, but not so eagerly — he does not repeat “red” twice as Esau does — that is, the godly make use of present things not for salvation but for their necessity.
