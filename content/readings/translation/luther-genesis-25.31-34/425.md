Therefore Jacob was intent upon this present opportunity, and struck a definite price with his brother, so that no further disturbance or trouble might be raised by his brother on account of the birthright.

So today the Pope holds the name of the Church. We do not. Yet we know that we are truly the Church, because we have the Word, the Sacraments, and the keys, which Christ left behind him — not to serve our power and the desires of this life, but to prepare us for the coming of the Son of God. Therefore we are the true Church.

The Pope, on the other hand, arrogates this name and title to himself because he is in possession \[of the outward office\], and boasts that he is the successor of Peter and Paul. Now if I were willing to make a bargain with the Pope in this way — I will give you so many thousands of gold pieces each year, and you will allow me to preach the Gospel freely and sincerely — would that be simony? No. Because I am not buying what I already have, namely the legitimate use of the Sacraments, the sincere doctrine of the Gospel, of faith, of hope, of expectation of the life to come, all of which are granted to me even against the Pope’s will and without his authority. In offering a thousand gold pieces I seek only to redeem the harassment \[I suffer at his hands\]. I am not buying anything, but merely throwing a sop to the adversary — throwing him the red lentils — so that I may deprive the Pope of the title “Church.” I care nothing for his bulls and indulgences. And this is what I would urge upon the princes of our side: that they pay him so many thousands of crowns each year for this purpose, that bloodshed and other scandals might cease, and the Gospel might be spread in peace. This is not simony, but the redemption of vexation.

Therefore when Jacob says to Esau, “Sell me your birthright,” it is not a real purchase — because the birthright already belongs to Jacob, and Esau has been stripped of it. Possession is with Jacob, but without the name. The name is with Esau, but without possession. Now because Jacob sees that Esau understands or seeks nothing in the blessing except his belly — just as the Pope under the pretext of the Church seeks only his own glory and power — he therefore throws a sop to Cerberus, lest Esau be troublesome to him any further.

In the same way I would truly wish that our princes would pay the Emperor twenty thousand gold pieces each year, so that the Gospel might be taught in peace and tranquility. But will you thus give money for sacred things? someone asks. By no means. For what we already have, we have not bought, and they cannot take it from us. We would give that money only so that they might leave us alone and grant us peace — the Pope and the Emperor alike.

Moreover, those spiritual goods, O Pope and Cardinals, are not yours but ours. Even if the Pope were never to grant both kinds \[of the Eucharist\], we have them by divine gift. Nor can they sell them; or if they try, they will be milking a he-goat and holding a sieve beneath it. We have both kinds, with God’s consent and against the Pope’s resistance.
