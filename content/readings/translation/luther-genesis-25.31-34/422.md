But what must be examined is the intention and will to buy or to traffic in sacred things — and that is Simon’s intention, and it is simoniacal: for he desires that it be possible, by paying money, to receive the Holy Spirit. This same meaning underlies our prayers, when we ask that God be glorified and his kingdom preserved. God cannot be confounded or brought to dishonor, because he is substantially glory itself, power, and goodness. But our intention and will is such that we desire God not to exist, and that his glory be confounded. And we declare this in our attitude toward the Word and the Sacraments. If we could inflict upon God the same injury and contempt by which we undermine his Word and Sacraments, we would not refrain even from that. True simony, therefore, is the trafficking that is carried on in the Word of God, in the Sacraments, in the Church, and among the Saints. This the Pope has not understood, nor does he understand it today — even though the entire papacy is submerged in it, and had been so long before it became simoniacal according to the \[canon\] laws, because they were selling heaven, the forgiveness of sins, grace, and the Holy Spirit in exchange for works. Hence the vast multitude of temples, monasteries, masses, indulgences, and sacred orders, by which works we sought to merit divine grace.

In this way they made of God a merchant, who bestows the kingdom of heaven not freely but for a price and for merits. For even if he offers it freely, they nevertheless reject the gratuitous benefit and contribute a hundred or a thousand gold pieces toward the building of monasteries, temples, and the like, by which to purchase divine grace and benefits. Is this not trading in grace and the forgiveness of sins? And such was the Pope even when he was at his best; and with this kind of simony he filled the whole world, so much so that the world is nothing other than a cesspool of true simony. For what else do the Colleges \[of canons\] do but take money and turn piety into profit?

But strictly speaking, it is impossible to buy grace. It is as if one were to try to milk a he-goat. The Pope milks the he-goat; the people hold out a sieve — as the proverb says. Even if it were possible for the Pope to be reformed and purged of gross simony, they still cannot be purged of true simony, because that is the soul of the Pope. Just as bodily substance constitutes the body, so he is a simoniac in body and soul; and once simony is removed, whatever remains of the pomp, splendor, and dignity of the pontiff will be entirely swept away, and there will remain only the Bishop of Rome.

It is evident, then, how great the dangers and evils in the papacy are, what an abyss it is, how horribly the whole world has been swallowed up by the primacy of the Pope. Therefore we ought to hate that beast with perfect hatred.
