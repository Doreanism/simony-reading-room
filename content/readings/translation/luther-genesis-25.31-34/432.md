Third: if the Devil perceives that you possess the Word and trust that your life is pleasing and acceptable to God on account of the Word, he will not rest, but will cast in your way temptations of every kind and the cross, even in the smallest matters. In your household affairs you will encounter the faithlessness of servants, the hatred of neighbors, the death of children or of a spouse. All these things will come to pass so that your faith may be exercised. But if the Word is absent, impatience and resentment over a way of life so burdensome and afflicted follow in its wake — as we hear from many who cry out that they entered marriage not at God’s leading, but at the Devil’s instigation.

The same befalls magistrates who, when they see the ungovernable wickedness of their subjects — usury, greed, licentiousness — and strive in the discharge of their office to correct or punish it, incur the hatred and envy of men. There arise then grumblings and embitterments no less than in marriage. And if such persons were Papists, ignorant of the Word of God, they flee and forsake marriage or the magistracy and withdraw into solitude, there to serve God in peace and tranquility — but without the grace and will of God. For God has set forth his Word before all people and has commanded that each one serve in his own calling.

Therefore, in order that you may overcome those difficulties and hardships — whether of marriage or of the magistracy — take care above all to have the Word of God thoroughly pondered, by which the magistracy is richly confirmed: Romans 12 and 13. Likewise in marriage, whether one is a servant or a maidservant, a teacher or a pupil, \[all\] are certain of their estate and of God’s will. Only lay hold of the Word, and bring forth fruits worthy of the Word, and you will immediately see the cross and temptations follow. After these follows prayer; after prayer, deliverance; after deliverance, the sacrifice of praise. Thus you will be able both to bear the cross and to offer the sacrifice of praise at the same time — which monks neither will nor can do, since they seek only peace, the belly, and pleasure.

In this manner Moses describes the legend of this holy patriarch in this single chapter: that he lived his life amid many tribulations.
