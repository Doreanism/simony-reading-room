But they must not gain the glory of being seen as having given or bestowed it upon us — for that would be to consent to their authority. And so we do not wish these divine and sacred things, which I cannot purchase with money, to be given or donated to us by the Pope.

Thus Esau, just as Ishmael above, undoubtedly made himself greatly troublesome to his brother Jacob, and arrogantly commanded him, since he himself was the lord and the firstborn, while Jacob was regarded as a servant and a fool. Jacob wished to free himself from this persecution and fraternal harassment, so that he might securely obtain the spiritual blessing. So today many Christians yield up cities on account of their confession of the Gospel, to avoid being compelled to bear the tyranny of the Papists. And Paul says in Romans 12: “If it be possible, as much as lies within you, live peaceably with all men.” If the adversaries are unwilling to keep the peace, give them the red pottage, so that they grant you peace — that is, allow you to enjoy in peace those goods which you already possess.

There follows Esau’s response to his brother’s request: “Behold, I am at the point of death, and what profit shall this birthright do to me?” He even reveals by his own testimony a profane spirit. Is this how one should deal with and think about religion, piety, and birthrights? For he openly confesses that he does not understand the birthright, nor does he hold it as the inheritance of the life to come and as a sacred thing. But if the birthright profits nothing except for glory and wealth in this present life, where will faith, hope, and expectation of eternal goods remain? We shall then speak as the impious and the Epicureans, who are accustomed to say when they hear heaven named — which in our language sounds as *Himel* — they answer: “Das Himel? letten wir hie nit” \[Heaven? We don’t need it here\] — *quid coelum? si hac farinam haberemus* \[what good is heaven? if only we had this flour here\]. This is how the Pope thinks about the Church and the keys. What do you say about the keys? I must use them to heap up riches and to bend kings. These are the Esau-like words of the impious, who regard godliness as gain, and who would bid a long farewell to Christ and his Gospel if they could not thereby prosper in temporal things.

Therefore this is a weighty testimony of Holy Scripture, that Esau had already forfeited his birthright. For he is judged out of his own mouth. He undoubtedly thought to himself: I am the lord; I will indeed sell the birthright, but it is only a game. For even if I have sold it, I can still recover it. Thus he played and despised his brother. Just as the Pope with his masks has made sport of the whole world by selling good works and indulgences, and thought it would go unpunished for him, or that he could at last return to repentance.
