26. Fourthly, that parents are to be loved more than children in those good things that ought to flow from effect to cause — such as honor, esteem, reverence, thankfulness, and the like. But children are to be loved more than parents in those things that flow from cause to effect — such as provision, advancement, and care for their welfare.

27. Fifthly, that husbands and wives are to be loved more than parents or children in those things pertaining to the society and union of this life; for that is the greatest closeness, of which it is said that they shall be one flesh. Genesis 2:24: Therefore shall a man leave his father and mother and shall cleave to his wife, and they shall be one flesh. Matthew 19:5.

28. Sixthly, that those who have done well by us are to be loved more than others, and among these, those who have communicated spiritual good things to us are to be loved most: let him who is taught in the word share all good things with his teacher. Galatians 6:6.

29. Seventhly, that a community or whole society is to be loved more than any single member of it, because the bond of a part with the whole is greater than its bond with another part; and therefore a prince whose life and safety is necessary or most profitable for the common good is to be loved more than any or even many of the common people — indeed more than ourselves in temporal things. 2 Samuel 21:17: You shall go out no more with us to battle, lest you quench the light of Israel. Lamentations 4:20.

30. There are two acts of charity toward our neighbor: prayer for his good, and the working of it. Matthew 5:44: Love your enemies, bless those who curse you, do good to those who hate you, and pray for those who hurt you and persecute you.

31. This prayer, insofar as it concerns the honor of God, belongs to religion under the first table; but insofar as it concerns the good of our neighbor, it belongs to justice and charity toward our neighbor under the second table.

32. We must pray for all those good things which religion commands us to wish for our neighbor, whether they be spiritual or bodily.
