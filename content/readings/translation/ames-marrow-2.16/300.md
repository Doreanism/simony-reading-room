Therefore, although we ought equally to will the salvation of others, yet the exercise and care of this will is owed chiefly to those who are closely joined to us in some special respect — just as a soldier, though he ought to wish well to all his fellow soldiers, is bound to take the greatest care of those in his own company who stand next to him in the same rank. This is evident in the example of Paul, who desired the conversion of the Israelites more fervently than that of other nations, and gives as one reason for this that they were his brothers and kinsmen according to the flesh. Romans 9:3.

22. Yet in this prerogative of charity we must wish to those near to us those good things that belong to the kind of bond by which they are near to us: spiritual good things to those who are most closely joined to us spiritually, and natural good things to those with whom we share a natural closeness — not that these kinds of good things are to be separated from one another in our desires, but because the very nature of the connection is, as it were, a signal from God by which he stirs us up to bestow our efforts chiefly in this or that kind.

23. Hence it follows, first, that blood relations, *ceteris paribus*, are to be loved more than strangers in matters pertaining to the goods of this life; and among those who are near in blood, the nearest are to be loved the most.

24. Secondly, that a close friend is to be loved more than an ordinary blood kinsman, at least in matters pertaining to the common duties of this life, because friendship may be such that it creates a closer bond than consanguinity considered by itself. Proverbs 18:24: For a friend is nearer than a brother.

25. Thirdly, that parents are to be loved more than any friend, because the closeness of parents is greater than that of friends with respect to the communication of those things most intimate to us. 1 Timothy 5:4: If any widow have children or grandchildren, let them first learn to show piety toward their own household and to repay their parents, for this is honest and acceptable in the sight of God.
