Proclaims the great need of God’s people — a need even greater for these spiritual supplies than any person can have for bodily ones. The fainting soul stands in more need of being plied with promises and encouragements than the swooning man with cordials; the careless and polluted sinner needs threats and admonitions more than the diseased body needs stronger purges; and the obstinate sinner needs to be cast out more than the gangrenous limb needs to be amputated. All of us need this spiritual food as much and more than any, than all of us need our natural food — and Job called that necessary food (Job 23:12) — and therefore it is no wonder that Paul said, “a necessity is laid upon me, and woe to me if I preach not the Gospel” (1 Corinthians 9:16). God and nature are not wanting to you in necessaries: do not let grace suffer you to be wanting to your charge in their necessities. Christ was willing to bleed, that from his flesh and blood he might make the provisions — will you not be willing to bear the cost, even if it be of your sweat, to set them on the table? Let your people’s hungering desires call forth your pity. It was one of the saddest notes in the Church’s Lamentations that the children asked for bread, and there was none to break it to them (Lamentations 4:4). Or should their desires be silent, yet let their wants cry for your compassion. *Cogita de cibo & potu animalium tuorum*, says Bernard, *nam esuriunt, & non petunt* — they are often hungry, and yet so weak, sullen, or shamefaced that they will not or cannot ask; nay (which is worse) *inedia languent, & tamen non esuriunt* — they are too often half-famished, and yet are not hungry, ready to die not so much for want of food as for want of appetite, as is the case with too many in our flocks today. And therefore (in this case if in any), if there be any consolation in Christ, if any comfort of love, if any fellowship of the Spirit, if any bowels and mercies, let our bowels yearn over them. Starved souls will be frightful ghosts to us when they and we at last stand together to give up our account and to receive our doom. Let this make us think of our charge and mind our work: it is to give food to God’s household.
