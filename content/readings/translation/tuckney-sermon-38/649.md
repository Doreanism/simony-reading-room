1. Hard to hit upon, and therefore the more carefully to be heeded. Much good is lost because the opportunity is missed — for since to every purpose there is a time and judgment, the misery of man is therefore great upon him (Ecclesiastes 8:6).

2. Most successful usually, if observed. The thing is then beautiful (Ecclesiastes 3:11), and therefore compelling; it rides upon wheels, and therefore goes on smoothly. I am no Arminian, to make the efficacy of grace consist in moral persuasions applied tempore & modo congruis — in the fitness and seasonableness of such applications. No: I know that God, to show the freeness and prevailing efficacy of his grace, sometimes works it (as in Paul and others) when the sinner is most indisposed, just as Christ the physician came when the world was most corrupt — who, unlike other physicians, can to purpose give physic in the height of a fit, as he cured Simon’s wife’s mother, In aestu Febris, in the very paroxysm of her disease. Nullum tempus occurrit Regi.
