# Gregory to Virgilius, Bishop of Arles, sent to Gaul through the priest John and the deacon Sabinianus.

How good is charity, which through love presents absent things to itself as present through an image, unites what is divided, orders what is confused, joins together what is unequal, and brings to completion what is imperfect! How rightly does that eminent preacher \[Paul\] call it the bond of perfection, for the other virtues do indeed beget perfection, yet charity also binds them fast, so that they can no longer be dissolved from the mind of one who loves. By this virtue, then, dearest brother, I find you to be filled, as both those who come to me from the regions of Gaul and the words of your letters directed to me bear witness. Now as to your request in those same letters, in keeping with ancient custom, for the use of the pallium and the delegation of the Apostolic See \[vicariate\], God forbid that I should suspect you of having sought in our vicariate and pallium either the summit of transitory power or the adornment of external display.
