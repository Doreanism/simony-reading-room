It is already clear from what has been said what manner of man comes to the priesthood who passes suddenly from a secular life into sacred leadership; and he who was never a soldier does not hesitate to become the commander of the religious. What sort of preaching will such a man offer, who has perhaps never listened to another’s? Or when will he correct the faults of others, who has not even wept over his own? And since the Apostle Paul forbids a neophyte from coming to holy orders \[1 Timothy 3:6\], we must understand that, just as in those days one was called a neophyte who had only recently been planted in the faith, so now we count among the neophytes anyone who is still new to the holy way of life.

We know, moreover, that freshly built walls do not first receive the weight of the roof-beams until they have dried out from the moisture of their newness, lest, if they take on burdens before they have solidified, they bring the entire structure down to the ground at once. And when we cut timber for building, we wait for the moisture of its greenness to dry out first, lest, if the weight of the structure is laid upon it while it is still fresh, it bend under that very newness and, having cracked, quickly collapse — timber that had appeared to be raised up prematurely to a great height. Why, then, is this principle not carefully observed in human beings, which is weighed with such deliberation even in the case of wood and stone?

For this reason it is necessary that your fraternity take pains to admonish our most excellent son King Childebert to drive the stain of this sin utterly from his kingdom, so that almighty God may repay him with all the greater rewards in his own sight, in proportion as he sees him both loving what God himself loves and shunning what he hates.

Accordingly, in the churches that lie within the kingdom of our most excellent son Childebert, we entrust to your fraternity our own authority in accordance with ancient custom, God being the author, with the proper honor of the individual metropolitans preserved according to the ancient usage. We have also sent the pallium, which your fraternity is to wear within the church only during the solemn celebration of Masses. If any bishop whatsoever should chance to wish to travel to a more distant place, he shall not be permitted to pass to other localities without the authorization of your holiness.

If any inquiry concerning the faith, or perhaps some other matter, should arise among the bishops that proves difficult to resolve, let it be aired and decided by a gathering of twelve bishops. But if it cannot be decided \[by them\], let the truth be examined and the matter referred to our judgment.
