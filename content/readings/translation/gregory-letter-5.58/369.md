We therefore grant your request with a willing spirit, lest we seem either to withhold from you any part of the honor due to you or to have shown contempt for the petition of our most excellent son King Childebert. But now the matter requires greater diligence, so that as honor increases, so too solicitude may grow, and vigilance over the care of others may increase; that the merits of your life may come before your subjects as an example, and that your fraternity may seek never its own gain through the favor of the honor received, but the rewards of the heavenly homeland. For you know what the blessed apostle says with a groan: “For all seek the things that are their own, not the things that are Jesus Christ’s” \[Philippians 2:21\].

For I have learned from certain persons reporting to me that in the regions of Gaul and Germany no one attains holy orders without the giving of a payment. If this is so, I say it weeping, I declare it with groaning: for when the priestly order has fallen within, it will not long be able to stand firm without either. We know indeed from the Gospel what our Redeemer did in his own person, for he entered the Temple and overturned the seats of those who sold doves \[Matthew 21:12\]. To sell doves is to receive temporal payment for the Holy Spirit, whom almighty God bestows on men through the laying on of hands, the Spirit being consubstantial with him. From this evil, as I said before, what follows is already made plain: because they presumed to sell doves in the Temple of God, by God’s judgment their seats have fallen. For even the one who is promoted to sacred dignity is already corrupted at the very root of his advancement, and is the more ready to sell to others what he himself bought. And where is what is written: “Freely you have received, freely give” \[Matthew 10:8\]? And since the simoniacal heresy was the first to arise against the holy Church, why is it not weighed, why is it not seen, that one who ordains and promotes another for payment acts so as to make him a heretic?
