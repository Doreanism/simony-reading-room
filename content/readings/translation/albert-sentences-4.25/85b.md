Others, however, say that the sacraments celebrated by heretics and those cut off \[from the Church\], provided they are celebrated according to the form of the Church, are true and valid: because in departing from the Church they did not lose the right of conferring and consecrating; and those who are thus ordained by heretics, when they return, are not to be ordained again. But those things which are performed by heretics otherwise than as in the Church are false and void, and those who seem to be ordained by such men receive no gift but a wound. And it is in accordance with this distinction that the Doctors speak of these matters in various ways. Some indeed say that the same sacraments can be celebrated by heretics who have been cut off as by Catholics, provided the form of the Church is preserved by them, and that when so celebrated they are true and valid in themselves; but as regards their effect, they are false and void both in those who administer them wrongly and in those who receive them wrongly, and therefore null and false: because what they promise, they do not bestow.
