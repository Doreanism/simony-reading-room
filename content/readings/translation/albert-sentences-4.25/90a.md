Another reason is given for the same point: that one who buys in this way gives nothing to God that was not already his, but merely orders toward the Lord what had been disordered through sin; and this is a work of virtue that every person ought to perform.

TO THE OTHER it must be said that what is “annexed to something spiritual” is called annexed to spiritual things because it ought not to be held except by spiritual persons or places—such as the right of patronage. For although a knight is said to hold it, nevertheless he holds it only for the use of spiritual persons and places. And by this the objection fails, because the very same thing numerically is not annexed to itself, but the same in genus or in species.

## ARTICLE III.

## Whether simony is a heresy?

Secondly it is asked, whether it is a heresy, as is said in the Text.

Now it seems that it is not, because:

1. On Titus 3:10, on the passage “A man that is a heretic, after the first and second admonition, avoid, etc.,” the Gloss says: “Heretics are those who attack the law through the words of the law.” But simoniacs are not such: therefore they are not heretics.

2. Again, above, Augustinus says that “a heretic is one who, for the sake of some temporal advantage, and especially for the sake of his own glory and rule, begets or follows false or novel questions.” But a simoniac does not do such things: therefore it seems that he is not a heretic.

3. Likewise, heresy lies in matters civil or credible; but simony lies only in matters operable: therefore simony is not heresy.
