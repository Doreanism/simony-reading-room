\[they\] administer the priesthood and set up an altar — although the offering cannot be sacrificed there where the Holy Spirit is not, nor can the Lord profit anyone through the prayers and petitions of him who has violated the Lord himself. Hieronymus likewise asserts that all things which are offered by heretics are defiled in the sight of the Lord: for although they may appear holy in their outward form, nevertheless, because they have been touched by one who is polluted, all are polluted. The same author: God hates the sacrifices of heretics and casts them away from himself; and as often as they are gathered in his name, he detests their stench and closes his nostrils. The same: They offer sacrilegious bread, etc. Leo also bears witness that outside the Church there are neither valid priesthoods nor true sacrifices. He likewise says that through most cruel madness in the see of Alexandria the light of all the sacraments was extinguished: the offering of the sacrifice was interrupted, the consecration of chrism failed, and by the parricidal hands of the impious all the mysteries withdrew themselves.

By these and other testimonies it seems to be established that the sacraments of the Church — especially those of the body and blood, of ordination, and of confirmation — cannot be administered by heretics.
