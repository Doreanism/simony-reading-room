To the next it must be said that every temporal service which is the cause of giving spiritual goods is base in comparison to spiritual goods, even if it be honorable or useful in an unqualified sense; and it is in this way that “base service” is here understood.

To the last it must be said that in the conferral of a prebend three things seem to me to require consideration, namely the poverty of the place, the liberty of the spiritual goods, and the intention of the salvation of the recipient. If the place is poor, it seems that a prebend cannot be conferred without much burden upon the persons dwelling there, however liberally they might wish to give the spiritual good, if they could, and to intend the salvation of the recipient. And then it seems to me that by themselves or through others looking after their interests they could say that, if their goods were to increase, they would willingly receive him, but otherwise they could not receive him.
