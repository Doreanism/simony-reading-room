# DISTINCTION XXV.

On the dispensation of the sacrament of orders.

## A. On those ordained by heretics.

It is also commonly asked whether heretics who have been cut off from the Church and condemned are able to confer sacred orders, and whether those ordained by them, upon returning to the unity of the Church, ought to be reordained. The words of the Doctors make this question perplexing and almost insoluble, for they seem to disagree considerably. For some appear to hold that heretics cannot confer sacred orders, and that those who seem to have been ordained by them do not receive grace. Innocentius says that the clerics of the Arians are not to be received with the dignity of the priesthood or any other ministry; to them he allows only that the baptism which is conferred in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit be held valid.
