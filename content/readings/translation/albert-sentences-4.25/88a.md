## F. On those who say that they are buying corporeal things, not spiritual ones.

If, however, some should object that they are not buying consecrations but the things themselves which proceed from consecration, they are shown to be utterly out of their senses. For whoever among these sells the one without which the other is not had, leaves neither unsold.

## ARTICLE II.

## What is simony?

Next, inquiry is made concerning the second part, namely what is said there at D, “Concerning simoniacs, etc.”

Here four questions are asked concerning simony.

First, what is it?

Second, is it a heresy, as the Text seems to say?

Third, can an inferior commit simony with a superior?

Fourth, in what ways is simony committed?

To the first, the definition is taken from the Jurists, who commonly define it thus: “Simony is an eager will to buy or sell something spiritual, or something annexed to a spiritual thing.”
