The time for orders is pressing, and I am commanded to receive orders — what ought I to do? For if I receive them from a simoniac, I must be degraded; but if I do not receive them, I am disobedient. Therefore I am caught in a dilemma.

Reply. To the first it must be said that men are called simoniacs in a broad sense whether they buy or sell, because even Simon afterward contemplated selling the grace he had purchased. Nevertheless there is a line of verse marking the difference,

What Gehazi sells, Simon seeks to procure.

To the next it seems one must say that, if he knowingly committed simony, he is bound to restitution. But if simony was committed on his behalf by others without his knowledge, he is not bound so long as he remains ignorant; but once he has come to know of it, he is bound, so it seems.
