Hence Gregorius calls the communion of Arius an execration, and Innocentius calls the ordination of Bonosius a damnation — not because they are such in themselves, but because the wicked state of those giving or receiving makes them so. In the same way Hieronymus calls their sacrifices “the bread of mourning,” not with respect to themselves, but with respect to their effect. Some, however, hold that those heretics who were ordained within the Church retain the right of ordaining and consecrating even after they have been separated from it, whereas those who, while placed in schism or heresy, have been ordained and anointed by them, lack that right, and therefore when they wish to ordain they inflict a wound rather than confer grace.

## Division of the text

“It is also customarily asked, If heretics, etc.”

In this chapter of the distinction two matters are contained, namely, whether heretics can confer holy orders;

and, concerning the diverse kinds of simony, at the passage, D, “Concerning simoniacs, however, there is no room for doubt, etc.”

## Article I

## Whether heretics cut off from the Church can confer orders

Concerning the first point the Master disputes at length in the Text, and there we ask whether heretics cut off from the Church can confer holy orders or not. It seems that they cannot.
