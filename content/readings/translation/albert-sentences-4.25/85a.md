## C. Resolution of the foregoing contradiction among the authorities.

Some, however, resolve these matters as follows. They say that heretics who, after receiving priestly or episcopal unction, depart from the Church do indeed retain the right of conferring baptism, but do not have the power to bestow sacred orders or to consecrate the Lord’s body once they have been cut off and condemned by the Church — just as a degraded bishop does not have the power to bestow sacred orders, though he has not lost the faculty of baptizing. What Augustinus says, they understand to be said of those heretics who are divided from the truth of the faith and the unity of doctrine not by any sentence of the Church, but by the perversity of their own judgment: such men, though they be of this kind, nevertheless still have the right of ordaining and consecrating. And those who are ordained by them before any manifest cutting-off — even if they openly go out with them and are condemned by the sentence of the Church — are not, if they return, to be ordained again. And wherever one reads concerning those ordained by heretics that, with their orders preserved, they may minister, or that they are not to be ordained again, they say this is to be understood of men of this kind. For once the heretics have been cut off and condemned by the judgment of the Church, they assert that the right of ordaining and consecrating has been taken away from them, as from men who have been degraded or excommunicated.
