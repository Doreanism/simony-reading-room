Just as one who withdraws from unity does not rightly have, yet still they do have, and therefore it is not restored to one who returns; so also one who withdraws from unity does not rightly give, and yet gives: and therefore one who receives from him is not re-ordained upon coming to unity. The same, \[says\]: It is one thing not to have something, another not to have it by right, or to usurp it unlawfully. Therefore the sacraments of Christ and the Church are not on that account not sacraments, because not only heretics but also all the impious use them unlawfully; but those persons are to be corrected and punished, while the sacraments themselves are to be acknowledged and venerated. Likewise, Gregorius: What you say, that one who has been ordained should be ordained again, is utterly absurd. For just as one baptized once ought not to be baptized again, so one consecrated once cannot be consecrated again in the same order.

By these and other authorities it seems to be asserted that in all the impious, even in heretics who have been cut off and condemned, the sacraments of Christ remain, together with the right of giving. For they are able to give, but ruinously; and those to whom they have given are not to be ordained again—which seems to run counter to the premises set forth above.
