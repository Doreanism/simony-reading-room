And if someone serves with this intent, so as to acquire a prebend in exchange for his service, that too is simony. The courts of prelates are, however, probationary environments for clerics, as a certain person says. Hence, if someone would not have served a prelate except in the hope of obtaining a prebend from him — yet not in such a way that he wants it as a reward for his service, but rather wants to show himself worthy and well-deserving, so that he may receive \[the prebend\] according to merit and not according to service, and the prelate gives it not as a recompense but as to one who is worthy, on the testimony of his service — though the prelate would not otherwise have given it, this is not simony, because through this he proves himself the more meritorious. 26 To the argument on the other side, an answer was given in the second conclusion of the first article.
