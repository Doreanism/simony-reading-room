This is how the laws understand the provisions concerning those physically defective and concerning a cleric taken ill throughout. 8 As to the second point, namely concerning the time for those to be ordained, there are six conclusions. The first: that from the age of seven and above the tonsure can be conferred; but whoever confers it before that age is suspended for one year from conferring the tonsure \[*de temp. ordi.* c. ult. lib. vi\], whoever he may be who can give the tonsure, unless perhaps \[the candidate\] were to enter religious life, as that canon states. For the child placed there is understood to be seven years old \[*C. de iure deli.* l. *Si infanti*\]. 9 The second concerns minor orders, which ought not to be conferred except on one who already has the use of reason, around nine or ten years of age; yet it is not necessary that he be of puberty, since this does not prejudice the vow of chastity; and minor orders and a simple benefice run in step with one another \[*de filiis presbyterorum* c. 1 lib. 6\]. A simple benefice can moreover be conferred on a ward, as will be said presently. 10 The third concerns the subdiaconate, which should not be conferred except on one who is of puberty and in his fourteenth year, because through the vow of continence he is bound by an obligation that requires an age firm enough that he could have contracted marriage, since contrary obligations are naturally formed around the same \[threshold\]. Those of puberty are indeed apt for vows and oaths \[*C. non pupilli*; *C. si adversus rem*; *Auth. Sacramenta*\]. 11 The fourth concerns the diaconate: a deacon ought not to be ordained before his twentieth year. 12 The fifth concerns the presbyterate: a presbyter ought not to be ordained before his twenty-sixth year. 13 The sixth concerns the episcopate: a bishop ought not to be ordained before his thirtieth year. Indeed, formerly a subdeacon was not to be ordained before his twentieth year \[28 dist. *de his*; 77 dist. c. *In singulis*; c. *Quicumque*; c. *Subdiaconus*\]; but today it suffices that he be fourteen, as the last canon of that distinction states. Formerly likewise a deacon not before his twenty-fifth year, nor a presbyter before his thirtieth, as this canon and 77 dist. *Placuit* and the said canons state; but today it suffices for a priest who is not a bishop that he be twenty-five, and by the same reasoning for a deacon that he be twenty \[*de elec. cum in cunctis*\]; but for a bishop it is required that he have completed \[the age\], whereas for the others it suffices to have reached it. Concerning one ordained to the diaconate at thirteen, it is said in *extra de tem. ordi.* c. ult. that he is not capable \[of executing the order\], and the bishop must be suspended from conferring all orders, and the one ordained there is suspended, namely until the due age.

14 As to the third point, namely concerning the times for \[conferring\] orders, there are five conclusions. The first concerns the tonsure, which can be given on any day, since it is not forbidden; and nonetheless every order, even one conferred outside the proper time, imprints a character \[*de tem. ordi.* cap. *Consultum*\]. 15 The second conclusion concerns minor orders, which can be given on Sundays and principal feasts, and much more so during those times \[appointed\] for major orders; and all four \[minor orders\] can be received together on one day \[*de tempore ordi.* c. 1\], where it also states that on Sundays virgins can be consecrated \[*de diebus festivis*, eod. tit. c. *de eo*\]. 16 The third conclusion concerns sacred orders, which cannot be given except on the Saturdays of the Ember seasons, and on the vigil of Easter, and on Passion Sunday; not, however, on the vigil of Pentecost, nor on any feasts, except that the Pope ordains subdeacons on Sundays \[*de tempori. ordi.* c. 1 & 2\]. 17 The fourth conclusion concerns the simultaneity of orders: two sacred orders ought not to be conferred at once, nor any sacred order together with a non-sacred one, nor one sacred order on one day and another on the next, even with continued fasting. Yet one ordained \[as a priest\] on Saturday with continued fasting may on the following Sunday be consecrated as a bishop \[dicto tit. c. *Ac literas*; c. *Dilecti filii*\]. One ordained outside the due time receives the character but not the execution \[of the order\] according to some; but it seems more correct that he is to be suspended rather than \[already\] suspended \[*extra* c. *consultum*\].
