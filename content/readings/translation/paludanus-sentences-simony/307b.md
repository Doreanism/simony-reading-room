# DISTINCTION 25. QUESTION 1.

Concerning Distinction 25, five questions are raised. First, concerning the minister of the sacrament of orders. Second, concerning the time of ordinations. Third, concerning the impediments to orders in general. Fourth, in particular concerning simony: whether the Pope can commit simony. Fifth, whether mental simony obliges one to resignation.

## On Those Ordained by Heretics.

AS TO THE FIRST, it is asked whether all bishops can confer orders. And it seems that they cannot, because an instrumental agent acts only when joined to the principal agent; but the minister of the sacraments is an instrumental agent with respect to Christ, who is their institutor; therefore no minister can confer sacred things unless he is joined to Christ; but a heretic is not joined to Christ — not through his own faith, since he is an unbeliever, nor through the faith of the Church, since he has been cut off from it; therefore a bishop, if he is a heretic and has been cut off from the Church, cannot confer orders. Furthermore, to promote someone to orders is a greater act than to absolve from penalties or from legal sentences; but a bishop, if he is a heretic or has been cut off from the Church, cannot absolve anyone from sins or from sentences
