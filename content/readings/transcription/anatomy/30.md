### *First,*

If we consider these things in their own Nature, as separate from the Spiritual Office and Use to which they are appointed, they be things Temporal indeed, and so are vendible; yet, by Virtue of the Annexation and Dedication to the Ministery of such an Office, they become things Spiritual, and so not Vendible: For this altereth the Nature thereof. Even as the Bread and Wine in the Eucharist, are in their own Nature, and whilst separate from the Sacrament, things Temporal, and may be bought and sold; but, when by Consecration, they are set apart for that spiritual and sacred Use of the Sacrament, they change the Nature of Temporal Things, and are no more Vendible.
