'That he had not courted that 'Office by indirect Means, but 'that being called, he came 'thereto. And without this Call, and Commission from God, none hath right to Administer the same: For, How shall they Preach (says the same Apostle,) except they be sent? And therefore, although God hath been pleased, for preventing that confusion, which vain pretensions to a Divine Call might introduce into his Church, to appoint an Ordinary way for the confirmation of his own Call, by the Seal of Ecclesiastical Ordination; yet, when that Seal is procured from the hands of Men, where the Call is wanting from the hands of God, it is of small significancy; it is but the setting up of a Man in an Office in the House, without the Master's consent.
