Hands pure, who, to gratifie the Solicitations and Importunities of carnal Friends, inconsiderately advanceth a Man to a Spiritual Office, I see not.

Yet this is not so to be understood, as if all Intercessions in Affairs of this nature, were either Simoniacal or Sinful; but it must be considered, whether the Person in whose behalf Friends do interceed and intreat for, be a Person worthy; that is, Holy, and well Qualified for such a Sacred Function; or unworthy, and unsuitable for it. If he be unworthy, then certainly he sins hainously who interposeth in his behalf: For he sacrificeth the Glory of God, and the Blood of poor Souls, to the interest of his unworthy carnal Friend or Favourite; and so doth he, who, to gratifie Man, is mov'd by Intreaties, to invest him with such an Office.
