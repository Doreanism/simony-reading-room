But if the Person be worthy, then he who interceeds for him, must consider, upon what Principle he doth so: If it be out of a pious Affection to the good of the Church, and a well-grounded Hope of his being a good Instrument for the Glory of God; upon such a ground one may not only without sin, but very commendably recommend such a Person, and even use Entreaties and Solicitations for his Advancement to such an holy Function; nay, and so much the rather, if he be a Person in whom he hath an Interest of natural Relation: (For Religion doth no way weaken, but rather corroborates the Bonds of natural Affection.)
