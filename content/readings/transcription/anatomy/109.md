And in all our Saviour's Life, we read not that ever he took the Rod in his hand, but when he was to express his Indignation against this Sin, and his zeal for the Purity of his Church; and then we find in the Gospel, that he made a Scourge of small Cords, and drove therewith the Buyers and Sellers out of the Temple, and overthrew the Tables of the Money-changers, and the Seats of them that bought and sold Doves: Thereby, intimating, with what Wrath he would set his Face against those who would in his Church make Merchandise of Spiritual Things.
